After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricanes Francis and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place — the Endeavour and Atlantis missions. After the completion of the Shuttle program, a transition to commercial use began. In 2014, launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rocket launches, as well as Dragon crewed spacecraft. Pad LC-39B is being modernized for the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex features a museum and entertainment area with tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours of the grounds. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. A memorial to astronauts, "Cosmic Mirror," is also located on site, honoring the names of deceased cosmonauts.
