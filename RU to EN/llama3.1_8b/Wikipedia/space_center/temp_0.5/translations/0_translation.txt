The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of buildings for launching spacecraft and managing flights, belonging to NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with an area of 567 square kilometers. About 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, while the rest represents a nature reserve including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and Cape Canaveral National Seashore. This area is one of the highest lightning strike areas in the United States, so NASA spends significant funds on protecting launches. The main operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building (VAB) is located. Two launch pads are located about 5 km east and an industrial zone with equipment and administrative building is located about 8 km south. Shuttles were launched from complex 39, while unmanned vehicles were also launched from the US Space Force Base at Cape Canaveral. The center serves approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
