The launch complex number 39 with a large infrastructure including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems was built for it. From 1967 to 1973, there were 13 Saturn V launches conducted. A tragedy occurred during the program involving the Apollo 1 crew. Later manned flights took place, including Apollo 11, in which a man first landed on the Moon in July 1969. The center was later used for the launch of Skylab and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Additionally, Titan and Atlas-Centaur rockets were used to launch satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, it served as the main site for Space Shuttle Program launches. The first Space Shuttle Columbia launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km long runway is also located here. After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
