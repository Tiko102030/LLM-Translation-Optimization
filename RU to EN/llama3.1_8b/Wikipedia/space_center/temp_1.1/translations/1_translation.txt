The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962 when NASA started purchasing land for launch expansion. In that same year the territory was named Launch Center and in 1963 it was renamed John F. Kennedy Space Center after the assassination of President John F. Kennedy. The lunar program included three stages: "Mercury", "Gemini" and "Apollo". The "Mercury" program had goals of sending humans to orbit, studying their work in space, and testing re-entry. The first flights were performed on Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini program used two-seater ships and enabled the first EVA's (extravehicular activities). Twelve missions were completed under this program. The Apollo program used the Saturn V rocket.
