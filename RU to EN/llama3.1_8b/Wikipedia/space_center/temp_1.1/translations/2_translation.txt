For her a launch complex number 39 was built with a large infrastructure, including a building for vertical assembly and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973, there were 13 launches of "Saturn V". During the program, a tragedy occurred with an Apollon-1 crew. In the future, manned flights were performed, including Apollon-11, during which a person first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used to launch the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Also, "Titan" and "Atlas-Centaur" rockets were used for launching satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first shuttle launch of Columbia took place in 1981. This is also where a 4.6 km landing strip is located. After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
