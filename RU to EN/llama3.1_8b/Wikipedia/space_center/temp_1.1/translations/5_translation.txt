All the above mentioned applies only to iterative search algorithms for neural network solutions. For them, it is indeed impossible to guarantee anything and fully automate neural network training. However, in addition to iterative training algorithms, there exist non-iterative algorithms with extremely high stability and allow complete automation of the learning process. Checking the adequacy of training

Even after successful initial training, a network does not always learn exactly what its creator wanted it to learn. There is a case where a network was trained to recognize images of tanks from photographs, but later it turned out that all the tanks were photographed on the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of learning to recognize tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what is required of it, but rather what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training must be conducted using examples that did not participate in its training. At the same time, the number of test examples should be as large as possible, depending on the quality of training. If a neural network's errors have a probability close to one billionth, then it takes one billion test examples to confirm this probability.
