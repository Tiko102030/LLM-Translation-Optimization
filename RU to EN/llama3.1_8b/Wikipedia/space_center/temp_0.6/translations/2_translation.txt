For it a launch complex No. 39 with a large infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems was built. From 1967 to 1973 thirteen Saturn V launches were made. In the course of the program there was a tragedy with the crew of Apollo-1. Later manned flights were performed, including Apollo-11, in which for the first time a man landed on the Moon in July 1969. The center was later used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Also Titan and Atlas-Centaur rockets were used to launch satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, the center served as the main site for the Space Shuttle program. The first Space Shuttle Columbia launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km long runway is also located here. After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
