The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of buildings for launching spacecraft and managing flights, belonging to NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is located near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with an overall area of 567 km². About 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, the rest is a nature reserve including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge and Cape Canaveral National Seashore. This area records one of the highest quantities of lightning strikes in the United States, so NASA spends significant amounts on protecting launches. The main operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building is located. Five kilometers east are two launch pads, and eight kilometers south is an industrial zone with equipment and an administrative building. Space shuttle launches were performed at Complex 39, while unmanned spacecraft were also launched from the US Space Force base on Cape Canaveral. The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
