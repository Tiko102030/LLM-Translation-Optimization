After the Challenger space shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricane Francis and Wilma. In 2011, there were the last shuttle launches - mission Endeavour and Atlantis. After completing the shuttle program, a transition to commercial use began. In 2014, Launch Complex 39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rocket launches as well as Dragon crewed spacecraft. Pad LC-39B is being renovated for the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex is an exhibit area with guided tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours of the grounds. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. There is also a memorial to astronauts "Cosmic Mirror" on the site, where names of deceased cosmonauts are immortalized.
