John F. Kennedy Space Center - a complex of buildings for the launch of spacecraft and management of flight, belonging to NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is situated near Cape Canaveral between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with an area of ​​567 square kilometers in total. About 13,500 people work there. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, the rest represents a nature reserve including the Mosquito Lagoon, the Indian River, the Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge and the Cape Canaveral National Seashore. On this territory one of the highest lightning strikes in the United States is recorded, therefore NASA spends significant funds on launch protection. The main operations are managed from Complex 39, where a vertical assembly building is located. Two launching pads are located about 5 km to the east and an industrial area with equipment and an administrative building is located about 8 km south. Shuttles were launched from complex 39 and unmanned vehicles also started at the US Space Force Base on Cape Canaveral. The center serves approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
