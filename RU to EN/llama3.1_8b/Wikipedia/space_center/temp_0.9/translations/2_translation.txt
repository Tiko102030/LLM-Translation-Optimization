For it a launch complex No. 39 with extensive infrastructure, including the vertical assembly building and transportation systems was built. Between 1967 and 1973 there were 13 Saturn V launches carried out. During the program there was a tragedy involving an Apollo 1 crew. Later manned flights took place, including Apollo 11, which in July 1969 marked the first time humans landed on the Moon. The center later was used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Additionally Titan and Atlas-Centaur rockets were used to launch satellites and interplanetary spacecraft. Until July 2011 it served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first Columbia shuttle launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km long runway is also located here. After the Challenger disaster in 1986 flights were temporarily halted.
