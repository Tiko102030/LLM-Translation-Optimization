John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of buildings for the launch of spacecraft and flight control, belonging to NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is located near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with an area of 567 km². Approximately 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, while the rest represents a nature reserve including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge and the Cape Canaveral National Seashore. This area records one of the highest numbers of lightning strikes in the United States, so NASA spends significant funds on protecting launches. The main operations are managed from Pad 39, where a vertical assembly building is located. Two launch pads are located 5 km east and an industrial zone with equipment and an administrative building is located 8 km south. Shuttle launches took place at complex 39, while unmanned spacecraft were also launched from the US Space Force Base on Cape Canaveral. The center is serviced by approximately 15 thousand employees and contractors.
