For it was built the launch complex No. 39 with a large infrastructure including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. Between 1967 and 1973, there were 13 Saturn V launches performed. During the program, there was a tragedy involving the Apollo-1 crew. In later years, manned flights took place, including Apollo-11, during which a man first landed on the Moon in July 1969. The center was later used for launching Skylab and Apollo-Soyuz mission programs. Also applied rockets Titan and Atlas-Centaur for launching satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site of the Space Shuttle program. The first launch of shuttle Columbia took place in 1981. This is also located a runway landing strip 4.6 km long. After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
