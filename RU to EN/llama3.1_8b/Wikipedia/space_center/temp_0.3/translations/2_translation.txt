For it was built a launch complex No. 39 with a large infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973 there were 13 launches of the "Saturn V". During the program there was a tragedy with the crew of "Apollo-1". Later manned flights were performed, including "Apollo-11", during which a man first landed on the Moon in July 1969. The center was later used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Also, Titan and Atlas-Centaur rockets were used to launch satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first shuttle launch of Columbia took place in 1981. A 4.6 km long runway is also located here. After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
