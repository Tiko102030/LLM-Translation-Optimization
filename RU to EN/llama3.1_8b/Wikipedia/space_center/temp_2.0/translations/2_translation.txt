The site had built a launch complex number 39 with massive infrastructure including an assembly building and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973 there were thirteen launches of Saturn V. Tragedy struck during the program's course, resulting in the crew death on Apollo 1. Prolonged manned flights followed, including Apollo 11, which achieved lunar landing first for humans in July 1969. Later it was used to launch Skylab and Apollo Soyuz Test Project missions with Titan and Atlas Centaur rockets as well, for placing satellites and space probes. Up until July 2011 the center functioned as primary launch facility for the Space Shuttle program. The maiden flight of Columbia occurred in 1981. It features a runway at 4.6 kilometers long.
