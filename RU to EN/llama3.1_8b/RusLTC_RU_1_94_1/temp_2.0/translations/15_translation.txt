Despite constitutional guarantees regarding property, the states continued to redistribute wealth. In fact, the Thirteenth Amendment secures the principle of confiscatory taxation in the U.S. Constitution. Pursuant to this amendment, the unconditional confiscation of slave owners' property redistributed almost 3 billion USD, half of the entire wealth of southern states. As a result, emancipation in America was one of the most significant instances of property confiscation in history. Governmental interference with private property did not end at these confiscations. During colonial and pre-war periods, regional governments and local authorities repeatedly intervened in matters of private property, thus reconfirming a widely held opinion about the moral duty of government to promote economic development and support poor citizens.
