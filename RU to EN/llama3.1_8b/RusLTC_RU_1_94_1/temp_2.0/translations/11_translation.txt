Uniqueness of American law, property rights and police powers Conditioning or even a myth in the study of American history implies that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. In greater measure due to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "Traditions of the Liberal Americans" (1955), the American society is considered as typically liberal in the classical spirit of J Locke, nourishing a special passion for individualism and property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private ownership and personal initiative, undermining the effort to mobilize workers and other people in the name of socialist and collectivist goals.
