As the use of this principle affords access to an examination of the social interpretation of property, Judge Redfield urged that the limitations on property rights be recognized, because, in his words, actions committed by one owner affect others even if they take place on their own territory. However, the "use your own as not to harm another" is merely a norm, and not a rule; it should not guide us in most cases where conflicting interests are at play with regards to property rights, and both sides complain of neighboring interference (see Holmes 1894 p.11). The question of matters of property was revisited in the 19th century in order to encourage entrepreneurial activity, even if this involved causing damage to other people's properties.
