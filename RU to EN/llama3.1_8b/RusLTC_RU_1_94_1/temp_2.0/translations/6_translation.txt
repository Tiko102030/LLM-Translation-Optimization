There exists an "incontrovertible" right of ownership by the working individual because:
Although the land and lower living things belong in common to all people, still ... the work of his body and the work of his hands counts at strictest account as belonging to him. Whatever a man then draws out of that condition which nature created and preserved, he combines it with his own labour and adds something pertaining to himself to it, and thereby makes it his own property... For since this labor is incontrovutable ownership by the working individual, no one other than him may claim rights to what he has once attached them, at least in cases when a sufficient quantity of the same quality remains for common use by others.
