The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient result regardless of property distribution and independent of who has rights to air and water. Coase's article had a huge influence on economic theory, resulting in many economists abandoning income redistribution ideas and focusing instead on evaluating the efficiency of various legal regimes. However, there are questions associated with these assertions that must be considered separately. Price and distribution consideration only exist when income redistribution will affect both prices and production structure. This would happen if subjects had heterogeneous preferences and output increased or decreased economies of scale. Moreover, as Coase noted himself, his theory did not take into account transaction costs or the expenses involved in negotiation and enforcing agreements. This may be extremely important in making a trade agreement involving externalities. It often proves to be problematic to motivate numerous people to bribe those polluting their environment.
