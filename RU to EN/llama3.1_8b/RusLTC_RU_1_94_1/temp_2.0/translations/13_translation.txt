American property was valued even lower: American debt to British creditors were promptly cancelled without much debate; some states, including Virginia, dismissed cases filed by British subjects in court (see Eltis 1998 p. 35). The wide-spread confiscation of property from opponents of independence from England did not bode well for the safety of property in the new republic. The main rebels struggled to restrain what they saw as an "excess of democracy" (see Thomlinson, 1993, p.60). Protecting property, the safeguard of any right became a worry for creators of first state constitutions all.
