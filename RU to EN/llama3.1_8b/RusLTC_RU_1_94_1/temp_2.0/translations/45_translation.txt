Holmes claimed that the government was unlikely to continue its activities if, to some extent, the question of property ownership was not resolved. Therefore, judges should carefully evaluate competing interests depending on a specific case. The gradual withdrawal turned into general flight when support for sacred rights to private property ceased during the Great Depression in the 1930s. United behind President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and his New Deal policies, a new generation of liberals promoted government assistance for those in need and emergence from crisis by means of reformulated capitalism under such pressure even the Supreme Court stepped away from an absolute defense of property rights. Scholars attracted public attention to the conclusions of the Supreme Court rulings as early as 1937, with some calling this "the constitutional revolution of 1937" following after two conservative judges had resigned when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened that he would "pack" the Supreme Court.
