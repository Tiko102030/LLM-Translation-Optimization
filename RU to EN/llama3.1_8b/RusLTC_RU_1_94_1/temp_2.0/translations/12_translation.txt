Such a concept always required careful consideration. Some Americans accumulated property in accordance with John Locke's principle, combining their labor with land holdings and materials. However, most people became rich through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americans became rich by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to take Native American lands. Even the War of Independence in 1776, a war fought in the name of liberal ideals, was an example of massive plundering, including forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs and most landholdings in Queens, Richmond, Kings County and Suffolk (see Flik 1901, p. 153).
