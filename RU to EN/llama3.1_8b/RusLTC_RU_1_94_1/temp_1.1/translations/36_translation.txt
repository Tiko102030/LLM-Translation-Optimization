The bias of judges towards state non-interference in economy was perhaps best illustrated by the West Virginia Supreme Court of Appeals in the case "State of West Virginia vs Goodwill" (1889). The US Supreme Court did not hesitate to criticize state regulation but Justice Stephen Field questioned police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Charitable Association of Butchers of New Orleans vs Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter House" Corporation. The given case appeared after the legislative organ of the Louisiana State had restricted slaughterhouses in New Orleans since public health was not threatened by such regulations, making this law similar to ordinances issued earlier in many other cities.
