Exceptionalism of American law, property rights and police powers The notion that the United States is a uniquely devoted land to private rights and market freedom is conditional or even a myth in the study of American history. To a greater extent thanks to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1955), American society is considered typically liberal in the spirit of John Locke, fueling a particular passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal consecrates private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to mobilize workers and other people in pursuit of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
