Some Americans accumulated property according to the principles of John Locke, combining their labor with landholdings and materials. However, most people became rich through war victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William into England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to take land from indigenous peoples. Even the 1776 War for Independence, a war fought in the name of liberal ideals, served as an example of mass looting, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs, and most land plots in Queens, Richmond, Kings and Suffolk counties (see Flik 1901, p. 153).
