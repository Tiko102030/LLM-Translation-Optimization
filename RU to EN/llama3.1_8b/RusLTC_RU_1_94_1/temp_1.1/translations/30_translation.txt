Iron and steel industries, whose products were subject to preferential tariffs, were the only ones that could ensure a developing network of railways. At the top of the entire financial pyramid was recently centralized banking system established in New York and created during the war (see Benzell 1984 g., 1990 g.). The angry workers and farmers saw the true goals of bankers and owners of ironworking and railway companies, whose property as it happens was sponsored by state authorities. They wanted new laws that would expand public authority over issues related to the regulation of property.
