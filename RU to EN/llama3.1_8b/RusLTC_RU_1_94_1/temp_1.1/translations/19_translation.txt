Local authorities regularly confiscated homes where immoral activities were taking place, filled the sewage of factories that "posed a danger to human health", prohibited the production of hazardous chemicals and put out fires, destroying private housing and warehouses in the process, without paying any compensation whatsoever. Officials gained access to dozens of industries, including healthcare-related ones such as drug manufacture and cattle slaughter, and other economically significant sectors not related to healthcare, like the right to trade and buy, sell and produce (see Novak 1996, p. 84-94). When regulation was unable to achieve public goals, officials would go on strike.
