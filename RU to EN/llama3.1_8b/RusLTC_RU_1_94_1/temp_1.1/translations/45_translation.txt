The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ended during the Great Depression of the 1930s. United behind President Franklin D. Roosevelt and the New Deal policy, a new generation of liberals helped bring about state assistance to those in need and got out of the crisis by means of reformist capitalism measures.

Under such pressure even the Supreme Court retreated from an absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's 1937 conclusions, even mentioning a "Constitutional Revolution of 1937" after two conservative judges stepped down when Franklin D. Roosevelt threatened that he would "pack" the Supreme Court.
