Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania coal

Shortly before Adkins was considered, the Supreme Court made another historic decision that seemed to fully cover the protection of property rights. In the case of "Pennsylvania Coal Company v. Mahon" (1922), the Court declared a Pennsylvania law unconstitutional because it restricted the activities of coal mining companies in order to preserve subterranean land. The Court ruled that a common rule for all is that regulation of property can be carried out only up to a certain point, and if it goes too far, then expropriation of property will occur.... It remains unclear why human failures or needs will justify shifting the blame onto another's shoulders.... and a strong desire by society to improve its position will not be sufficient to ensure achieving this goal through a shorter, constitutional means of getting out of guilt.... This is just a matter of degree. The case of Pennsylvania Coal, mentioned over 100 thousand times in the following seventy-five years, is still in effect today.
