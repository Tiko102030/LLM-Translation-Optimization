A new approach was not a general critique of property rights or their universal confirmation. Instead, by redistributing rights it only affirmed some property in comparison to other property. According to one of the historians of law, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the legal order of the United States - "the liberation of all human creative energy". Although the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have the opportunity to start projects that generate profits. In theory, if a project's cost was above the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice all the burden is shifted onto the injured party, which only contributes to an increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects and puts all the risk and operational expenses on neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were observed to a greater legal extent, high operational costs would hinder entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by saving on the cost of risks and operational expenses, entrepreneurs' new principle has contributed even more to the development of entrepreneurship.
