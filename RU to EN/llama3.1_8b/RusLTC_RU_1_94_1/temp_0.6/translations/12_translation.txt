However, most people became wealthy through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to seize the land of indigenous peoples. Even the War for Independence in 1776, a war fought in the name of liberal ideals, was an example of mass looting, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs and most land plots in Queens, Richmond, Kings County, and Suffolk (see Flik 1901, p. 153).
