Instrumental and competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, nor should they ever become an end in themselves. Since some rights must take precedence over others, new conditions are necessary to consider and accommodate competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to discussing natural rights, but the contemporary judicial conservative society has not changed US legislation in any significant way. Changing this would require courts completely to change the legal justification history, according to which property is viewed as a social institution, rather than an end in itself. It also would have required them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
