Conditional or rather a myth in the study of American history suggests that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent due to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1955), American society is considered typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, nourishing a special passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to mobilize workers and other people in pursuit of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
