A certain form of property as an instrument may be convenient, but it will never be necessary. However, Locke defends property rights not so resolutely as one might understand from the well-known excerpt quoted above (see Horn 1990; Ketznelson 1998, p. 77). In this excerpt he addresses the question of the right of the English Stuart monarchs to tax without parliamentary consent. But besides this particular issue, Locke also recognizes a social definition of property, according to which property rights must be limited by other natural rights. Locke said that each person should have as much as they can use.
