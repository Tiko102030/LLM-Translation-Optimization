The market of free trade will lead to the same effective result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the right to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, as a result many economists gave up the idea of income redistribution and focused on the values of different legal regimes' efficiency. However, there are several questions related to these statements. Separate consideration of price and distribution of income takes place only when the distribution of income affects both prices and production structure. This will occur in cases where economic subjects have various preferences, and when returns to scale increase or decrease. Additionally, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses related to negotiations and enforcement of contracts. This may be extremely important for concluding a trade agreement involving external effects, especially when it is often difficult to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The definition of property rights is often considered quite controversial in liberal democracies similar to how in the United States, people possess both personal freedom and the right of the majority to govern the country (see Horowitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
