American property was valued even lower: English debts to creditors were, without much reasoning, annulled; some states, including Virginia, closed the cases for hearing brought by British subjects (see Eli 1998, p. 35). The widespread confiscation of property from opponents of independence from England did not bode well for security of property in the new republic. The main insurgents fought to curb what they saw as an "excess of democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60). In ensuring property security, the "protector of any right" became a concern for the creators of the first constitutions of all states.
