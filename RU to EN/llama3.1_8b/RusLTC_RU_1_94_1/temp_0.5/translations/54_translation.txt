Instrumental and competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, nor should they ever become the ultimate goal. Because some rights must take precedence, new conditions are needed in order to consider and competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to discussing natural rights, but modern judicial conservatives have not changed US law in any significant way. Changing it would require courts to completely revise the legal justification history, according to which property is considered a social institution rather than an ultimate goal. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
