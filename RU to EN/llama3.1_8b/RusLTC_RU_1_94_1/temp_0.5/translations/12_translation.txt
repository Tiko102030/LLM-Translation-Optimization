Some Americans accumulated property on the principle of John Locke's ideas, combining their labor with landholdings and materials. However, most people became wealthy through military victories and plunder, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to seize native lands. Even the War for Independence in 1776, a war fought for liberal ideals, was an example of mass looting, including forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its surroundings and most land parcels in Queens, Richmond, Kings and Suffolk counties (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
