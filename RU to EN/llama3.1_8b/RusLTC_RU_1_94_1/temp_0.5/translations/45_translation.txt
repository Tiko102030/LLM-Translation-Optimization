The gradual retreat became a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the Great Depression of the 1930s. Joining forces with President Franklin D. Roosevelt and the policies of the New Deal, a new generation of liberals promoted state aid to those in need and emerged from the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights.
