In 1787, the Constitutional Convention limited the powers of regional governments to intervene in transactions (Foner 1976, p. 133; Eli 1998, p. 26). The fears of conservatives led to the addition of the Fifth Amendment to prevent confiscation of property. The process of confiscating property did not follow due process and provided no compensation (see Raykov 1996, pp. 314-5; Eli 1998, pp. 43-54; Jacobs 1954, pp. 4-5).
