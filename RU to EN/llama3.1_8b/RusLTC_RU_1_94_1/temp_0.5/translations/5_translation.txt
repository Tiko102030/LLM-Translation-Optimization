New paradigm of property rights recognizes different definitions of property, however, still considers "property rights" as relations between people and objects (see Barzel 1997; Liebkep 1989). Thus, property rights exist independently of other social circumstances. As relations between people and objects, property exists without human society; for Robinson Crusoe also has property rights. Like relations between people and objects they create, property acquires moral legitimacy and economic value because it is a reward for the work that shapes the non-human material world.
