The Supreme Court of the United States also indicated that the right to compulsorily take private property is based on public utility and instrumental considerations, and in the case of 1875 "Kohl v. United States" determined that the right to compulsorily take private property was "the result of political necessity, and it cannot be separated from sovereign power." The right to compulsorily take private property was one example of the use of government authority to promote development of a well-organized society, to enhance the quality of public goods or "welfare" through legislation regarding encroachment on owners' rights.
