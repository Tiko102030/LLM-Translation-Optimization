INTRODUCTION: the concept of ownership in economic theory and public practice
Society possesses ownership. Economic systems vary as to how they define the concept of ownership, and the boundaries often tend to be difficult to specify. Some consider that property rights extend to such items as land and trees, including even ephemeral objects like living nature and flowing water. Others complement the given notion with the assertion that property rights also extend to such objects as ideas (patents), some others even include the human being himself in this category as a slave. Societies also vary depending on the rights that ownership holders possess; set of privileges concerning responsibility for any actions taken towards other people. These rights prevent us from considering the law as granting either "greater" or "lesser" protection to property, because each use affects the properties of others. Property has dimensions inasmuch as it concerns inter human relations; I have a right to manage my own property which diminishes your complete freedom to act as you wish.
