Fear among conservatives led to the Constitutional Convention in 1787 limiting the power of regional governments from intervening in transactions. Later, they added the Fifth Amendment to prevent confiscation of property. The process of confiscating property itself did not proceed according to due process and offered no compensation (see Raykev 1996 p. 314-5; Eli 1998 p. 43-54; Jacob 1954 p. 4-5).
