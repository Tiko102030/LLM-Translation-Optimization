But after these liberal icons went into retirement in 1990 and 1991, the Court introduced additional limitations on state regulation. For instance, in the case of "Lucas v. the South Carolina Coastal Council" (1992), the Court demanded that a property owner in South Carolina be compensated for damage to their property after a new coastal zone management law suspended construction on their land. Once again expressing distrust in elected lawmakers, Scalia warned in Lucas and Dolan that judges should carefully scrutinize zoning ordinances so as to insulate themselves from "the risk that private property might be commandeered under the guise of reducing significant public harm".
