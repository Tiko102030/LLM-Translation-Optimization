Instrumental competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, they should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, there need to be new conditions for considering and competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed any legislative aspect in the United States. Changing it would require courts to completely alter the legal rationale history according to which property is considered a social institution, rather than an end itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
