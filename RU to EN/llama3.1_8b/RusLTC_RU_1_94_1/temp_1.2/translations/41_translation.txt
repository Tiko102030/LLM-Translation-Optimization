Apparently, the underlying issue of this case is based on John Locke's views regarding absolute property rights. This case also mentioned the final decision in Mann v. Oklahoma, which was rendered by Chief Justice Wade. However, attention should be paid to the previous statement: "it is only a matter of degree." This restriction, written by Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr., consciously re-established an instrumental view of property rights that declared unconstitutional fifty years of prior judicial practice. In Lochner v. New York, Holmes had already expressed his opinion. Beginning with the remark that "the decision was made on the basis of economic theory, which however, does not appeal to most people," he added: The right of each individual to behave in any manner they see fit, provided, of course, other individuals' rights are respected, does not conform to the basic tenets of legal schools, postal management or laws of any state or municipal agency taking their money to achieve goals that appear necessary, regardless whether he wants them or not. The Fourteenth Amendment does not take into account Mr Spencer's Social Statistics. Unlike Lochner who despoticly defends intensive property rights, Holmes considered competing interests needed a balance.
