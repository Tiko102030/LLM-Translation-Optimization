Local authorities used the right of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. Initially conceived by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, the term "right of eminent domain" was recorded in English law through John Locke, who placed it as a basis for "natural rights," as an alignment of the will of the entire population that granted all authority to legislative bodies of the state (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters expanded the concept simply based on instrumental considerations, denying any sanctity of private property rights. The Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
