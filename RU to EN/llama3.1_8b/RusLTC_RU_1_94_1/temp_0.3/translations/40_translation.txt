Just before considering Adkins's case, the Supreme Court made another historic decision that seemed to fully concern itself with protecting property rights. In the case of Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon (1922), the Court declared a Pennsylvania law unconstitutional because it limited the activities of coal mining companies in order to preserve subsurface land. The Court ruled that a general rule for all would be that regulation of property can only be exercised up to a certain point, and if it goes too far, then it will pass into confiscation....
