A new approach was neither a general criticism of property rights nor a general affirmation of these rights. Instead, by redistributing rights it only asserted some property in comparison to other property. According to one historian of law, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the legal order of the United States - "the liberation of all human creative energy". Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have the opportunity to start projects that are profitable. In theory, if a project's cost was higher than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice all the burden is shifted to the injured party, which only contributes to an increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, while all the risk and operational costs fall on neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were observed to a greater legal extent, high operational costs would have hindered entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by saving on cost risks and operational costs, entrepreneurs' new principle has even more contributed to the development of entrepreneurship. How then can we deal with an excess of democracy?
