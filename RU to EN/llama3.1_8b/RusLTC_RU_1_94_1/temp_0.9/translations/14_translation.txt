The fears of the conservatives led to the fact that the Constitutional Assembly in 1787 limited the powers of regional governments from intervening in transactions. Later, the Fifth Amendment was added to stop property confiscation. The process of confiscating property did not go through due process and did not involve any compensation (see Raykov 1996, pp. 314-5; Eli 1998, pp. 43-54; Jacobs 1954, pp. 4-5).
