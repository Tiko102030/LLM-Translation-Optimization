Evidently, the case is based on John Locke's views regarding absolute property rights. The court also mentioned the final ruling in Mann v., which was delivered by Justice Waite of the Supreme Court. However, attention should be paid to the last statement considered earlier: "it is merely a question of degree." This limitation, written by Judge Oliver Wendell Holmes, deliberately reinstated an instrumental view of property rights that declared unconstitutional the preceding fifty years of judicial practice.  In Lochner v., Holmes had already expressed his opinion. Beginning his speech with the observation that "the decision was made according to the principles of economic theory, which however do not satisfy a large part of the population," he added: The right of each person to act as they please for an unlimited period, provided their rights are not violated by others' actions, does not correspond to fundamental postulates of legal schools, postal management, or laws of any state or other municipal institution taking its money in order to achieve goals that appear necessary regardless whether the individual wishes it.
