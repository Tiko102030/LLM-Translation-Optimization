Uniqueness of American law, private property inviolability and police powers are either a myth or rather even in the research of American history implies that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent thanks to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1955), American society is considered typically liberal in the classical spirit of John Locke, nourishing a special passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal consecrates private property and individual initiative, undermining efforts "to coerce workers and other people in pursuit of socialist and collectivist goals."
