A new approach was neither a general criticism of property rights nor a general confirmation of these rights. Instead, by redistributing the rights, it only asserted some property in comparison to other property. According to one of the historians of law, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the legal order of the United States - "the liberation of all human creative energy". Although the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs, they still have the opportunity to start projects that bring profits. In theory, if a project cost was above the amount of damage caused, its profitability would increase anyway, even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice, all the burden is shifted to the plaintiff's side, which only contributes more to an increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, while all the risk and operational costs fall on the neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were observed to a greater legal degree, high operational costs would have hindered the implementation of entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by saving on the cost of risks and operational expenses, entrepreneurs' new principle only further contributed to entrepreneurship development.
