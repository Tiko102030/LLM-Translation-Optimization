The fears of the conservatives led to the U.S. Constitutional Convention in 1787 limiting the authority of regional governments from intervening in transactions. Later, they added the Fifth Amendment to stop the confiscation of property. The process of confiscating property did not follow due process and did not provide any compensation (see Raykov 1996, p. 314-5; Eli 1998, pp. 43-54; Jacobs 1954, pp. 4-5).
