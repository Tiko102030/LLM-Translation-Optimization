Apparently, the basis of this case are the views of John Locke regarding absolute property rights. The Mann case was also mentioned in this case, and it was presided over by Chief Justice Waite. However, note should be taken of the final statement considered earlier: "this is merely a matter of degree." This limitation, written by Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr., intentionally brought up an instrumental view of property rights that declared unconstitutional the previous 50 years of judicial practice regarding the issue. Holmes already made his views known on the Lochner case. Beginning his speech with the statement that "the decision in the case was made according to the bases of economic theory, which however does not please a great part of the country's population," he added: Property rights allowing an individual to do as they please so long as another person's rights are not violated is incompatible with the main principles of legal schools, postal administration laws and state or municipal law that take its money to pursue goals perceived necessary without regard for whether it wishes to or not. The 14th Amendment does not consider Mr. Herbert Spencer's Social Statics. Unlike how Lochner dictatorially defends intensive property rights, Holmes believed competing interests required a balance be struck.
