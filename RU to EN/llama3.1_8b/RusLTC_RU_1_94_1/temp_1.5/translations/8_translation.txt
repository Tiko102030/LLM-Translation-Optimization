However, Locke defends property rights not as strongly as might be expected from a well-known excerpt quoted above (see Horn 1990; Katznelson 1998, p. 77). In this excerpt he addresses the question of the right of Stuart monarchs of England to tax without Parliament's consent. Apart from this issue, Locke acknowledges and recognizes social definition of property according to which property rights must be limited by other natural rights. Locke said that each person should have as much as they can use.
