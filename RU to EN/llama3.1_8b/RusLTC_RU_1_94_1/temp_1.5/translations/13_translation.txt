Property rights of the British were evaluated even less: English debts to creditors were without much reasoning annulled; some states, including Virginia, closed cases related to suits brought by British subjects (see Eli 1998 p. 35). The widespread confiscation of property of independence opponents from England did not serve as a good omen for the safety of property in the new republic. Main rebels fought against that which they considered "excess democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60).
