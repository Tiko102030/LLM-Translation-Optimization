Instrumental competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must take precedence, new criteria are needed to consider and competing claims. Some still believe a return will occur to discussing natural rights, but the contemporary judiciary has not changed U.S. legislation in any substantial way. The change would require courts entirely to alter the history of legal justification, according to which property is viewed as social institution, not an end in itself. It also required that they raise abstract property above the needs of living people.
