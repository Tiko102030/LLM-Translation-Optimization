The United States Supreme Court also indicated that the right to involuntary expropriation of private property is based on public benefit and instrumental considerations, and in the 1875 case of "Cooley v. the United States" determined that the right to involuntarily seize private property is "a result of political necessity, and it cannot be separated from supreme authority". The right to involuntarily expropriate private property was one example of the use of government power to promote the development of a well-organized society by raising the level of quality for the public good or welfare through regulations concerning the exercise of property rights.
