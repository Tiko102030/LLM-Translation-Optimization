Local government authorities have used the power of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. Initially conceived by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, the term "power of eminent domain" was introduced into English law via John Locke, who based it on the concept of "natural law", which equated with the consent of all citizens that granted legislative authority to state governments (see Egle 1996, p. 8). American judges and interpreters of the law have greatly expanded the concept based solely on instrumental considerations. Disregarding any sanctity of private property rights, Vermont's State Constitution in 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity demands it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
