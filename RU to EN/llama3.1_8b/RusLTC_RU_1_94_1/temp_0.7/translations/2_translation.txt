The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who has rights to air and water. The Coase article had a huge impact on economic theory, as a result many economists abandoned the idea of income distribution and focused on the values of various legal regimes. However, there are several questions related to these assertions. Separate consideration of prices and income distribution only occurs when income distribution affects both price levels and production structure. This will happen in cases where economic agents have different preferences and when the return on scale increases or decreases. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses associated with negotiating and enforcing contracts. This can be extremely important when concluding a trade agreement that involves externalities, especially since it is often problematic to persuade a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The definition of property rights is often considered quite controversial in liberal democracies much like in the United States where people have both personal freedom and the right of the majority to govern the country (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
