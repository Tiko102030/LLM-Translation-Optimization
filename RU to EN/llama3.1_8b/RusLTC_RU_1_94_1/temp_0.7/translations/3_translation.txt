Property rights in Horwitz (1992), Demsetz (1964); Dillan (1895). Thus, on the one hand, property protection is considered as an expression of individuality of the owner. On the other hand, the right of the majority to control economic activity, determine public goals and protect property owners from the consequences of external influence of polluters is considered. However, such a general comparison of concepts of property rights can be understood incorrectly, because these rights mainly limit the rights of other people. Property rights give people state-approved privileges for monopolistic management of resources, the opportunity to appeal to authorized law enforcement agencies in order to restrict the activities of other people. Therefore, property significantly limits certain benefits due to the fact that government authorities support property owners more than other citizens. So it's not a discussion about principles of natural freedom and justice, but rather an examination of property regulation that forces us to choose whose property needs to be protected and whose property rights have an advantage in fighting for them.
