Exceptionalism of American law, private property rights and police authority 

The conditional or rather mythological nature of the research into American history suggests that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent due to the merits of Louis Harz and his famous work "Traditions of Liberal America" (1955), American society is considered typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, nurturing a particular passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to encourage workers and other people towards achieving socialist and collectivist goals."
