Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania Coal
Shortly before the consideration of the Adkins case, the Supreme Court made another historic decision that seemed to completely deal with the protection of property rights. In the case "Pennsylvania Coal Company v. Mahon" (1922), the Court declared a Pennsylvania law unconstitutional because this law limited the activities of coal mining companies in order to preserve the subsoil. The Court ruled that it would be generally accepted that regulation of property can only be carried out to a certain extent, and if it goes too far, then there will be confiscation of property.... It remains unclear why the failures or needs of an individual will justify shifting responsibility for damage onto another... and society's strong desire to improve its situation will not be enough to guarantee that this goal can be achieved in a shorter time than through constitutional ways to evade charges.... it is just a matter of degree. The mentioned case of Pennsylvania Coal has been cited over one hundred thousand times over the next seventy-five years and still remains in force.
