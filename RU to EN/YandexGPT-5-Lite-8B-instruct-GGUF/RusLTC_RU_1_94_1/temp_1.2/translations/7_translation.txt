Such views were spread in the American republic, and Locke's arguments continued to be quoted by late nineteenth-century Supreme Court justices opposed to social regulation of property rights (see Elie 1998). Others argue about property from the position of инструментализма. For them, property rights must be protected, i.e., these rights are an incentive for entrepreneurs (see North 1981). However, it is convincing that such an instrumental approach does not consecrate property; it does not make it an end in itself, an indisputable right that transcends all others. Instead, the problem of property is now paramount, and the instrumental approach itself undermines the protection of all property rights.
