 The free trade market will lead to the same effective result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, as a result of which many economists abandoned the idea of income distribution and focused on the values of efficiency of various legal regimes. However, there are a number of issues related to these statements. A separate consideration of price and income distribution takes place only when income distribution will influence both the level of prices and the structure of production. This will happen when economic agents have various preferences and when returns to scale increase or decrease. In addition, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses associated with negotiation and enforcement of contracts. This can be extremely important when concluding a trade deal involving externalities, especially when it is often problematic to encourage large numbers of people to bribe someone who pollutes the environment. The definition of property rights is often regarded rather controversially in liberal democracy, just as in the United States people have both personal freedom and the majority's right to rule the country (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
