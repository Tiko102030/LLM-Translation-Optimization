The natural law of supply and demand is the best law of trade." Judge Stephen J. Field and the interpretation of sacred private property 
The US Supreme Court was in no hurry to criticize state regulation, but Judge Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in Butchers' Benevolent Association of New Orleans v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Co. This case came after the Louisiana State Legislature restricted slaughter in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances previously prescribed in many other cities. Citing the decision of Massachusetts Supreme Court Chief Justice Shaw in Commonwealth v. Algar (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court upheld the law, holding that "the loss of private right in this case is insignificant in view of the general profit and trade turnover of this commercial organization."
