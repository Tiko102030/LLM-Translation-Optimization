 Referring to the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature that grants us property also limits the extent of this property. "God gave us everything abundantly" - this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But why did He give it to us? For enjoyment. A person has the right to turn into his own property as much as he can use for any needs of his life, before this item becomes spoiled. And what goes beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for corruption or destruction. However, to Locke's огорчение, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal ownership of land", to possess power over others. Therefore, restrictions on the accumulation of property are necessary in order to guarantee everyone access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's property theory contributed to the emergence of discussions about natural rights as a limitation on individual accumulation, subsequently other authors, including the well-known American radical Thomas Paine, joined these reasoning.
