Writing an opinion for the Court, Justice Antonin Scalia acknowledged that the presentation of "conditions regarding the granting of land use permits" is not an "appropriation" according to the Fifth Amendment. The general public use requirement "does not serve the intended purpose of protecting people's visual access to the beach" and "thus it is unlawful, and compensation must take place." In conclusion, Scalia spoke about the new standard: because there is no connection between the condition and the root cause of the construction restrictions, the new goal transforms into something different from what it was before. This time justices Brennan and Marshall expressed their dissenting opinions. They rejected Scalia's demand to revise the opinion of members of the legislative body. They argued that "the appropriate criterion for consideration.... is whether the state can rationally address the issue of whether the measure taken can achieve a public purpose."
