The right to forcibly отчуждать private property was one example of the use of governmental power to promote the development of a "well-organized society," to increase the quality of "public good" or "welfare" through regulations concerning interference with the rights of property owners. These regulations reflected principles of common law, such as: "the welfare of the people shall be the highest law" (Latin: salus populi suprema lex est) and "use your own so as not to harm another" (Latin: sic utere tuo ut alienum non laedas). Frequently cited in court rulings, these main principles serve as "programs of common law governance in a well-regulated society," providing a legal basis for comprehensive state regulation of property to reflect the mutual interests and rights of all citizens (see Novak 1996, p. 42).
