In fact, the Thirteenth Amendment закрепляет in the Constitution of the United States the principle of property confiscation. Under this amendment, the безвозмездная confiscation of slave property redistributed nearly 3 billion US dollars, half of all the wealth of the southern states. Therefore, the emancipation of slaves in America became one of the most significant examples in history of property confiscation. Government interference in private property did not end with these confiscations. During the colonial and pre-war periods, regional governments and local authorities regularly intervened in matters of private property, thereby once again confirming the widespread belief that it is the moral duty of the government to promote a prosperous economy and support poor citizens. For example, most local authorities set transportation tariffs and bread prices, limited food trade, regulated wages and job opportunities, and restricted the activities of sellers of alcoholic products, bars, and prostitutes (see Handlin and Handlin 1947; Burdgeon 1989; Nowak 1996; Lory 1999; Levy 2000). And local authorities used the right to forcefully отчуждать private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners.
