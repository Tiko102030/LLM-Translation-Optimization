 The property of the British was even less valued: debts to English creditors were canceled without much ado; some states, including Virginia, closed cases assigned for hearing on claims filed by British subjects (see Eli 1998, p. 35). The widespread confiscation of the property of opponents of independence from England did not bode well for the security of property in the new republic. The main rebels fought to curb what they saw as "an excess of democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60).
