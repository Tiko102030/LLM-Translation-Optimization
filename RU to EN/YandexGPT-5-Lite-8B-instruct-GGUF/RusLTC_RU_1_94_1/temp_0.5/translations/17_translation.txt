The US Supreme Court also pointed out that the right to forcibly отчуждать private property is based on public benefit and instrumental considerations, and in the case of 1875 "Kohl v. United States" it ruled that the right to forcibly отчуждать private property is "the result of political necessity, and it cannot be separated from верховной власти". The right to forcibly отчуждать private property was one example of using government power to promote the development of a "well-organized society", to improve the quality of "public good" or "welfare" through rulings relating to interference with the rights of property owners. These rulings reflected common law principles such as: "the well-being of the people shall be the highest law" (Latin salus populi suprema lex est) and "use your own so as not to harm another" (Latin sic utere tuo ut alienum non laedas). Frequently cited in court decisions, these main principles serve as "common law management programs in a well-regulated society", providing a legal basis for comprehensive government regulation of property in order to reflect the mutual interests and rights of all citizens (see Novak 1996, p. 42).
