In fact, the Thirteenth Amendment закрепляет in the Constitution of the United States the principle of property confiscation. Under this amendment, the безвозмездная confiscation of slaves' property redistributed nearly 3 billion US dollars, half of all the wealth of the southern states. Therefore, the emancipation of slaves in America became one of the most significant examples in history of property confiscation. Governmental intervention in private property affairs did not end with these confiscations. During the colonial and pre-war periods, regional governments and local authorities regularly interfered in matters of private property, thereby once again confirming the widespread belief that it is the moral duty of the government to promote a thriving economy and support impoverished citizens. For example, most local governments set transportation fares and bread prices, limited food trade, regulated wages and employment opportunities, and also restricted the activities of sellers of alcoholic beverages, bars, and prostitutes (see Handlein and Handlein 1947; Burgein 1989; Novak 1996; Lory 1999; Levy 2000). And local authorities used the right to forcibly отчуждать private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners.
