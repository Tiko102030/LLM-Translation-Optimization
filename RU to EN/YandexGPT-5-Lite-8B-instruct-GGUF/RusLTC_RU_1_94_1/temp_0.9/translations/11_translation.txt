Conditional or rather a myth in the study of American history suggests that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. Largely due to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1955), American society is seen as typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, with a special passion for individualism and property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to encourage workers and other people in the name of achieving socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
