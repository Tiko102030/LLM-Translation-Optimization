Some doubt that property rights have a significant impact on income distribution, while others argue in favor of its effectiveness. In his famous 1960 article, Ronald Coase argued that regardless of the distribution of property rights, any clear definition of boundaries will lead to the same effective social result, the same connection between the production of goods and services. Should those who pollute the environment buy these rights from their neighbors, or should the neighbors pay those who pollute so that they stop doing it? Environmental pollution will take place where the cost of reducing its negative consequences is greater than the value of clean air for the neighbors and will be stopped when the costs of reducing the negative effect are less. A free trade market will lead to the same effective result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water.
