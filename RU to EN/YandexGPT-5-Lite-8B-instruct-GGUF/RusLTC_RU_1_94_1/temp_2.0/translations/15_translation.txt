In fact, the Thirteenth Amendment закрепляет in the Constitution of the United States the principle of имущественной конфискации (property confiscation). According to this amendment, the gratuitous confiscation of slave property redistributed nearly 3 billion US dollars, half of all the wealth of the southern states. That is why the emancipation of slaves in America became one of the most significant examples in history of property confiscation. Governmental interference in private property did not end with these confiscations. During the colonial and pre-war period, regional governments and local authorities regularly intervened in matters of private property, thus once again confirming the widespread belief that the government has a moral duty to promote a prosperous economy and support poor citizens. For example, most local governments set transportation fares and bread prices, restricted food trade, regulated wages and employment opportunities, and also limited the activities of sellers of alcoholic products, bars, and prostitutes (see Handlein and Handlein 1947; Bergin 1989; Novak 1996; Lory 1999; Levi 2000). And local authorities used the right to forcibly отчуждать private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners.
