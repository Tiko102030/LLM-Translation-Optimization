However, Locke defends property rights not as решительно as could be understood from the famous passage quoted above (see Horn 1990; Katsnelson 1998, p. 77). In this passage, he addresses the issue of the Stuart monarchs of England's right to levy taxes without the consent of Parliament. But apart from this issue, Locke also recognizes a social definition of property, according to which property rights must be limited by other natural rights. Locke said that everyone should have as much as they can use. Accumulation should be limited in order to ensure that everyone can share the bounty of nature, leaving "enough and as good" for others. Referring to the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature that gives us property in this way also limits the extent of this property.
