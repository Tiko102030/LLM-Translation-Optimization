 Instrumental, competing "property rights" can never be absolute, must never be an end in themselves. Since some rights should have priority, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some are still sure that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but modern judicial conservatism has not changed the U.S. legislation in any significant way. The change would require courts to completely reshape the legal rationale under which property is viewed as a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require them to place abstract property above the needs of living people.
