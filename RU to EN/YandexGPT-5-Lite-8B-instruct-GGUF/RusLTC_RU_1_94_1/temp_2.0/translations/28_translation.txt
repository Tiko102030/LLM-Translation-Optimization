 The new approach was neither a general criticism of property rights nor a general confirmation of these rights. Instead, by redistributing rights, it merely asserted some ownership in comparison to other property. According to one of the legal historians, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of law and order in the United States of America — "the release of all human creative energy." Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have the opportunity to start implementing profitable projects. In theory, if a project was more valuable than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase, even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice, the entire burden is shifted to the пострадавшей party, which only further contributes to an increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, while all the risk and operational costs fall on the neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were respected to a greater legal extent, then high operating costs would impede the implementation of the entrepreneur's plans. Thus, by saving on the cost of risk and operational expenses for entrepreneurs, the new principle only further contributed to the development of entrepreneurship.
