New benefits of the New Deal
The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ended during the Great Depression of the 1930s. Uniting around President Franklin D. Roosevelt and New Deal policies, a new generation of liberals promoted helping those in need by the state and pulling out of the crisis through reformulated capitalism measures. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from the absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the decisions of the Supreme Court from 1937, even mentioning the "constitutional revolution of 1937" after two conservative justices resigned when Franklin D. Roosevelt threatened to "pack" the Supreme Court.
