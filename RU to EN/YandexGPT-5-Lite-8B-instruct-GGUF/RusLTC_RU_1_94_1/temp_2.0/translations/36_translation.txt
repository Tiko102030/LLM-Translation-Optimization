The U.S. Supreme Court was in no hurry to criticize government regulation, but Justice Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Butcher's Benevolent Association of New Orleans v Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Co". This case came after the Louisiana state legislature restricted cattle slaughtering in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances prescribed before in many other cities. Citing the decision of Massachusetts Supreme Court Chief Justice Shaw in "Commonwealth v Alger" (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court upheld the law, finding that "the loss of private right in this case is insignificant in view of the general profit and trade turnover of this commercial organization."
