Such views were spread in the American republic, and Locke's reasoning continued to be cited by Supreme Court judges of the late nineteenth century who opposed public regulation of property rights (see Elie 1998). Others argue about property from the position of инструментализм. For them, property rights must be protected, i.e., these rights are an incentive for entrepreneurs (see North 1981). However, it is convincing that such an instrumental approach does not sanctify property; it does not make it an end in itself, an undeniable right that transcends all others. Instead, the problem of property is now paramount, and the instrumental approach itself undermines the protection of all property rights.
