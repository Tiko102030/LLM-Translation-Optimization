 The Civil War marked the beginning of a new era in the political life of America, demonstrated the full power of the national government, which freed the country from slavery. Reformers wanted more. For example, defender of workers' rights George McNeill warned that America must "inoculate our industrial system with republican principles" or risk losing its democracy. According to train conductor C.F. Bracey: "The Declaration of Independence was not created for the purposes that trust companies are trying to impose on us today. The result of the Civil War in 1861 was the liberation of black slaves, but now workers are nothing more than slaves of объединенных trust companies" (see Foner 1998, pp. 99, 124-5). Moreover, the Civil War contributed to workers uniting and speaking out against these new demands. Large sums of money were required for military operations, and the expansion and centralization of the government apparatus associated with this лишний раз confirmed that significant financial and industrial capital was needed to support government activities. The iron and steel industries, whose products had preferential tariffs, could only provide for the developing network of railroads.
