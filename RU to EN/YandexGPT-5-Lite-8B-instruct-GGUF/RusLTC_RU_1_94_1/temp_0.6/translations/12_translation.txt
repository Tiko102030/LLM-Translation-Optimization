However, most people became rich through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americansthe became rich by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to take Native peoples' land. Even the War of Independence in 1776, a war fought in the name of liberal ideals, was an example of massive plundering, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of the property in New York and its suburbs and most of the plots of land in Queens, Richmond, Kings County, and Suffolk (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
