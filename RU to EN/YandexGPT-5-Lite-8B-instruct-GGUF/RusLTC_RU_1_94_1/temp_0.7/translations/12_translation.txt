However, most people became rich through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americans grew wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to take the land of indigenous peoples. Even the War of Independence in 1776, a war fought in the name of liberal ideals, became an example of mass robberies, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs and most of the land plots in Queens, Richmond, Kings County, and Suffolk (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
