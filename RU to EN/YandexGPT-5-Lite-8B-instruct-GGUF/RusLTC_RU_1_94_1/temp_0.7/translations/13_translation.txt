 The property of the British was even less respected: debts to English creditors were summarily canceled; some states, including Virginia, closed cases scheduled for hearing on claims filed by British subjects (see Ely 1998, p. 35). Widespread confiscation of the property of opponents of independence from England did not bode well for the security of property in the new republic. The main rebels fought to curb what they saw as "an excess of democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60).
