 After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center was damaged by hurricanes, including Hurricanes Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place — missions " Endeavor" and "Atlantis". After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as manned Dragon spacecraft. Pad LC-39B is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion ship. The visitor complex of the center is a museum and entertainment zone with excursions, IMAX cinemas and bus tours around the territory. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on the territory there is the astronauts' memorial "Space Mirror", where the names of deceased cosmonauts are immortalized.
