 All of the above applies only to iterative algorithms for finding neural network solutions. For them, it is really impossible to guarantee anything and it is impossible to fully automate the training of neural networks. However, along with iterative learning algorithms, there are non-iterative algorithms that have very high stability and allow you to fully automate the learning process. Verification of the adequacy of training

Even in case of seemingly successful training, the network does not always learn exactly what its creator wanted it to. There is a known case where the network was trained to recognize images of tanks from photographs, but later it turned out that all the tanks were photographed against the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of "learning" to recognize tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what was required of it, but what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training must be carried out on examples that were not involved in its training. At the same time, the number of test examples should be greater the higher the quality of training. If a neural network's errors have a probability close to one billionth, then a billion test examples are needed to confirm this probability.
