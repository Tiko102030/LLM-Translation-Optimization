 The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and controlling flights, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is situated near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are about 55 km long and 10 km wide, with a total area of 567 km². Approximately 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, the rest is a wildlife reserve, including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island Sanctuary and Canaveral National Coastal Park. One of the highest numbers of lightning strikes in the USA is recorded in this area, so NASA spends significant funds on launch protection. Major operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the vertical assembly building is located. Two launch pads are located 5 km to the east, and an industrial zone with equipment and an administrative building is located 8 km to the south. Shuttle launches were carried out from complex № 39, while unmanned vehicles were also launched from the U.S. Space Force base on Cape Canaveral. The center is serviced by approximately 15 thousand employees and contractors.
