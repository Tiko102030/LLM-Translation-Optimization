 A launch complex No. 39 was built for her with a large-scale infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973, 13 launches of the Saturn V were carried out. The Apollo 1 crew tragedy occurred during the program. Subsequently, manned missions were carried out, including Apollo 11, during which humans first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used to launch the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Titan rockets and Atlas-Centaur were also used to launch satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first launch of the shuttle Columbia took place in 1981. There is also a landing strip 4.6 km long located here. After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
