 After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center was damaged by hurricanes, including Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place — the Endeavor and Atlantis missions. After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, launch site LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as manned Dragon spacecraft. Site LC-39B is being modernized for the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex of the center is a museum and entertainment area with guided tours, IMAX cinemas and bus tours around the territory. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on the territory there is the Astronauts' memorial "Space Mirror", where the names of deceased astronauts are immortalized.
