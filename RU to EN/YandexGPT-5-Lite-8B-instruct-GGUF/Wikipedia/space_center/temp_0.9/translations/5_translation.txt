 All that has been said above applies only to iterative algorithms for searching for neural network solutions. For them, it is really impossible to guarantee anything and it is impossible to fully automate the training of neural networks. However, along with iterative learning algorithms, there are non-iterative algorithms that have very high stability and allow the process of learning to be fully automated. Checking the adequacy of training

Even in case of successful, at first glance, training, the network does not always learn exactly what its creator wanted. There is a known case when the network was trained to recognize images of tanks from photographs, but later it turned out that all the tanks had been photographed against the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of "learning" to recognize tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what is required of it, but what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training must be carried out on examples that did not participate in its training. At the same time, the number of test examples should be greater, the higher the quality of training. If the errors of the neural network have a probability close to one billionth, then a billion test examples are needed to confirm this probability.
