 The center is served by approximately 15 thousand employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962 when NASA started acquiring land for launch expansion. In the same year, the territory was named Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John F. Kennedy, it was переименована into Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program included three stages: Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo. The Mercury program aimed to put a person into orbit, study his work in space, and отрабатывать return procedures. The first flights were carried out on Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini program used two-person spacecraft and allowed for the first spacewalks. Within this program, 12 missions were completed. The Apollo program utilized the Saturn V rocket. For it, launch complex No. 39 was built with a large-scale infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems.
