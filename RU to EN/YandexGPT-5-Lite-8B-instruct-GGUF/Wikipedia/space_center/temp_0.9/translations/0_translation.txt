 The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and managing flights, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is situated near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with a total area of 567 km². About 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, the rest is a wildlife reserve, including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island Sanctuary and Canaveral National Coast. This area has one of the highest numbers of lightning strikes in the United States, so NASA spends significant funds on launch protection. The main operations are managed from Launch Complex №39, where the vertical assembly building is located. Two launch pads are located 5 km to the east, and an industrial zone with equipment and an administrative building is 8 km to the south. Shuttle launches were carried out from complex №39, while unmanned vehicles were also launched from the U.S. Space Force base at Cape Canaveral. The center is served by approximately 15 thousand employees and contractors.
