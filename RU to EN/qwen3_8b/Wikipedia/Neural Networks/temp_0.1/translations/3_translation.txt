On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of learning parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the learning parameters of the neural network. This stage is particularly important for networks that learn with a teacher. The correct selection of parameters not only affects how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers. For example, choosing a low learning rate will increase the convergence time, but sometimes allows avoiding network paralysis. Increasing the learning momentum can lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Given such a contradictory influence of parameters, it can be concluded that their values need to be chosen experimentally, guided by the training completion criterion (e.g., minimizing error or limiting training time). Network Training

During training, the network examines the training sample in a certain order. The order of viewing can be sequential, random, and so on. Some networks that learn without a teacher (e.g., Hopfield networks) examine the sample only once. Others (e.g., Kohonen networks) as well as networks that learn with a teacher examine the sample multiple times, with one full pass through the sample called an epoch of training.
