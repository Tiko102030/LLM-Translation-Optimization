On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of learning parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the learning parameters of the neural network. This stage is particularly important for networks that learn with a teacher. The correct selection of parameters not only affects how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers. For example, choosing a low learning rate will increase the convergence time, although it may sometimes prevent network paralysis. Increasing the momentum of learning can lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Given such a conflicting influence of parameters, it can be concluded that their values should be chosen experimentally, guided by the training completion criterion (for example, minimizing error or limiting training time). Network training

During training, the network examines the training sample in a certain order. The order of examination can be sequential, random, and so on. Some networks that learn without a teacher (e.g., Hopfield networks) examine the sample only once. Others (e.g., Kohonen networks), as well as networks that learn with a teacher, examine the sample multiple times, with one complete pass through the sample being called an epoch of training.
