On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of training parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the parameters of the neural network training. This stage is particularly important for supervised learning networks. The correct choice of parameters depends not only on how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers. For example, choosing a low learning rate will increase the convergence time, but sometimes allows avoiding network paralysis. Increasing the momentum may lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Given this conflicting influence of parameters, it can be concluded that their values should be chosen experimentally, guided by the training completion criteria (for example, minimizing error or limiting training time). Network training

During training, the network examines the training data in a certain order. The order of review can be sequential, random, etc. Some networks trained without a teacher (e.g., Hopfield networks) review the data only once. Others (e.g., Kohonen networks), as well as networks trained with a teacher, review the data multiple times, with one full pass through the data called an epoch of training.
