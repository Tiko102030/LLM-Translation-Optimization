Choosing data for network training and their processing is the most complex stage of solving the problem. The training dataset must meet several criteria:

Original data is transformed into a form that can be presented to the network inputs. Each record in the data file is called a training pair or training vector. A training vector contains one value per network input and, depending on the type of training (supervised or unsupervised), one value per network output. Training the network on a raw dataset usually does not yield good results. There are several ways to improve the network's perception.
