All the above applies only to iterative algorithms for searching neural network solutions. For them, nothing can be guaranteed, and fully automating the learning of neural networks is not possible. However, along with iterative algorithms for learning, there exist non-iterative algorithms with very high stability and allowing for fully automated learning process. Testing the adequacy of learning 

Even in the case of successful, seemingly, learning, the network does not always learn exactly what the creator intended. There is a known case where the network was trained for tank image recognition from photographs, but later it was found that all the tanks were photographed against the same background. As a result, the network learned to recognize this type of landscape rather than to learn recognition of tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what was required of it, but rather what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training should be conducted on examples not participating in its training. At the same time, the number of test examples should be greater the higher the quality of learning. If the probability of errors in the neural network is close to one billionth, then a billion test examples are required to confirm this probability.
