On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the amount of training data. Experimental parameter selection

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the parameters of neural network training. This stage is particularly important for supervised networks. The correct selection of parameters not only affects how quickly the network's responses will converge to correct answers but also the possibility of avoiding network paralysis. Increasing the learning rate can lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Given such conflicting influences of parameters, we can conclude that their values must be selected experimentally, while adhering to the criteria of training completion (e.g., minimizing error or setting a time constraint). Neural network training

During training, the network processes the training sample in a certain order. The order of processing can be sequential, random, etc. Some networks that learn without a teacher (e.g., Hopfield networks) process the sample only once. Others (e.g., Kohonen networks), as well as supervised networks, process the sample multiple times; one complete pass through the sample is referred to as a training epoch.
