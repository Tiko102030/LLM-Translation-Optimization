On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of training parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the network's training parameters. This stage is particularly important for networks trained with a teacher. The correct choice of parameters affects not only how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers. For example, choosing a low learning rate increases the convergence time, but sometimes allows avoiding network paralysis. Increasing the momentum can lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the error surface's shape. Based on this conflicting influence of parameters, one can conclude that their values should be selected experimentally, guided by the termination criterion (for example, minimizing the error or limiting the training time). Network training

During the training process, the network reviews the training dataset in a certain order. The viewing order may be sequential, random, etc. Some networks trained without a teacher (for example, Hopfield networks) review the dataset only once. Others (for example, Kohonen networks), as well as networks trained with a teacher, review the dataset multiple times, with one full pass through the dataset called an epoch of training.
