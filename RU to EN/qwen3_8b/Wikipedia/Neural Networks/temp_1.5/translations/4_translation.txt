Other networks (e.g., Kohonen networks), as well as networks trained with a teacher, repeatedly process the data, with one complete pass through the data being called an epoch of training. When training with a teacher, the set of original data is divided into two parts — the actual training set and test data; the principle of division can be arbitrary. Training data is fed to the network for learning, while test data is used to calculate the network's error (test data is never used for training the network). Thus, if the error on the test data decreases, the network is indeed generalizing. If the error on the training data continues to decrease while the error on the test data increases, it means the network has stopped generalizing and is simply "memorizing" the training data. This phenomenon is called network overfitting or overfitting. In such cases, training is usually stopped. Other problems may arise during training, such as paralysis or the network getting stuck in a local minimum of the error surface. It is impossible to predict in advance the manifestation of any particular problem or provide definitive recommendations for resolving them. All the above applies only to iterative algorithms for finding neural network solutions.
