John F. Kennedy Space Center — a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and flight operations, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km in length and 10 km in width, with a total area of 567 km². About 13,500 people work at the center. Only around 9% of the area is used for its intended purpose, while the rest is a wildlife refuge, including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and Cape Canaveral National Seashore. This area experiences one of the highest numbers of lightning strikes in the United States, so NASA spends significant resources on protecting launches. Main operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building is located. Two launch pads are located 5 km to the east, and an industrial zone with equipment and administrative buildings is 8 km to the south. Space shuttle launches were conducted from Launch Complex 39, while unmanned vehicles were also launched from the U.S. Space Forces base at Cape Canaveral. The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
