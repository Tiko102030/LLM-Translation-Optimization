A launch complex No. 39 was built for it, featuring a scaled infrastructure including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. During the program, a tragedy occurred involving the crew of Apollo 1. Subsequently, piloted flights were carried out, including Apollo 11, during which a human first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Rockets Titan and Atlas-Centaur were also used for launching satellites and interplanetary probes. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first Shuttle Columbia launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km landing strip is also located here. Following the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
