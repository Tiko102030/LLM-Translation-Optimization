Launch Complex 39 was built with a comprehensive infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. During the program, a tragedy occurred with the Apollo 1 crew. Subsequent piloted flights were conducted, including Apollo 11, during which humans first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Rockets such as Titan and Atlas-Centaur were also used for launching satellites and interplanetary probes. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first launch of the Shuttle Columbia took place in 1981. A 4.6 km runway is located here. After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
