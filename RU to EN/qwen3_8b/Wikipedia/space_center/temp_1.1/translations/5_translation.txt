All the above relates only to iterative algorithms for searching neural network solutions. For these algorithms, there is nothing that can be guaranteed, and it is not possible to fully automate the training of neural networks. However, in addition to iterative training algorithms, there exist non-iterative algorithms that possess very high stability and allow for fully automating the training process. Validation of Training Adequacy

Even in the case of seemingly successful training, the network does not always learn exactly what the creator intended. There is a known case where a network was trained to recognize tank images from photographs, but later it was found that all the tanks were photographed against the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of "learning" to recognize tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what was required of it, but rather what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training should be done on examples that were not used in its training. At the same time, the number of test examples should be greater the higher the training quality. If the probability of errors in the neural network is close to one in a billion, then a billion test examples are required to verify this probability.
