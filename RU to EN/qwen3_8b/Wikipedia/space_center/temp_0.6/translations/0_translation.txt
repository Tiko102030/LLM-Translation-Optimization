John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and flight operations, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is situated near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km in length and 10 km in width, with a total area of 567 km². About 13,500 people work at the center. Only around 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, while the remaining area is a wildlife refuge, including the Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and Cape Canaveral National Seashore. The area experiences one of the highest numbers of lightning strikes in the US, so NASA spends significant resources on protecting launches. Main operations are controlled from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building is located. Two launch pads are situated 5 km to the east, and an industrial zone with equipment and administrative buildings is located 8 km to the south. Space Shuttle launches were conducted from Complex 39, while uncrewed vehicles were also launched from the US Space Force's launch site at Cape Canaveral. The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
