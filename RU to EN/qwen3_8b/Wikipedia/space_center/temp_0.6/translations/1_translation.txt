The center is operated by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962, when NASA started acquiring land for expanding launch facilities. In the same year, the territory was renamed the Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John Kennedy, it was renamed the Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program included three phases: Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo. The Mercury program aimed to send a human into orbit, study human spaceflight operations, and test the return to Earth. The first flights were conducted using rockets Redstone and Atlas. The Gemini program used two-person spacecraft and enabled the first spacewalks. Twelve missions were conducted under this program. The Apollo program used the Saturn V rocket. For this, Launch Complex 39 was built with extensive infrastructure, including the vertical assembly building and transportation systems.
