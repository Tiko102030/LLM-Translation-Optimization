A launch complex #39 with scaled infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems, was built for it. From 1967 to 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. During the program, a tragedy occurred with the Apollo 1 crew. Subsequently, piloted flights were carried out, including Apollo 11, during which a human first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Rockets Titan and Atlas-Centaur were also used for launching satellites and interplanetary spacecraft. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first Shuttle Columbia launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km landing strip is also located here. After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
