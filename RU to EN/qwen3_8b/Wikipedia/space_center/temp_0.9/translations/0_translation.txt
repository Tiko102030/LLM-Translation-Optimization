John F. Kennedy Space Center — a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and flight control, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is located near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km in length and 10 km in width, with a total area of 567 km². About 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, while the rest is a wildlife refuge, including the Mosquito Lagoon, the Indian River, the Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and the Cape Canaveral National Seashore. A high number of lightning strikes is recorded on this territory, so NASA spends significant funds on protecting launches. Main operations are controlled from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building is located. Two launch pads are located 5 km to the east, and an industrial zone with equipment and administrative buildings is 8 km to the south. Shuttle launches were conducted from Complex 39, while unmanned vehicles were also launched from the U.S. Space Forces base on Cape Canaveral. The center is operated by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
