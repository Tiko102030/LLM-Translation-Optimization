Launch Complex 39 was built for her with a scaled infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. Between 1967 and 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. A tragedy occurred involving the Apollo 1 crew during the program. Subsequently, piloted flights were carried out, including Apollo 11, during which a human first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Rockets such as Titan and Atlas-Centaur were also used for launching satellites and interplanetary spacecraft. Until July 2011, the center served as the primary launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first Shuttle flight of Columbia took place in 1981. A 4.6 km runway is also located here. Following the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
