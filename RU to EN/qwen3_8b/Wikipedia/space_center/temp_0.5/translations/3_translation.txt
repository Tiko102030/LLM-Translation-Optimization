After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricanes Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches — missions 'Endeavour' and 'Atlantis' — took place. After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the LC-39A launchpad was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as piloted Dragon capsules. The LC-39B pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex of the center is a museum and entertainment area with guided tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours around the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on the site is the astronaut memorial 'Cosmic Mirror,' where the names of deceased cosmonauts are commemorated.
