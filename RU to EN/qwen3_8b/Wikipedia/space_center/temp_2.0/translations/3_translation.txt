Following the 1986 Challenger shuttle disaster, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricanes Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the final shuttle launches — missions 'Endeavor' and 'Atlantis' — took place. After the shuttle program ended, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the LC-39A launchpad was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as for Dragon crewed spacecraft. The LC-39B pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex at the center is a museum and entertainment area featuring tours, IMAX theaters, and guided bus tours around the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Additionally, the 'Cosmic Mirror' astronaut memorial is located on-site, commemorating the names of deceased cosmonauts.
