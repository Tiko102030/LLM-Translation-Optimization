The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962, when NASA started acquiring land for expansion of launch sites. That same year, the territory was named the Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John Kennedy, it was renamed the Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program consisted of three phases: Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo. The Mercury program aimed to put humans into orbit, study their work in space, and test return procedures. The first flights were conducted using Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini program used two-person capsules and allowed the first spacewalks. Within the framework of this program, 12 missions were carried out. The Apollo program used the Saturn V rocket. For this program, Launch Complex 39 was built with scaled infrastructure, including the vertical assembly building and transportation systems.
