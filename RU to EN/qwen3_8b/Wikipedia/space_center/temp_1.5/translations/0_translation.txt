The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a facility complex for launching spacecraft and flight operations, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. It is situated near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions span approximately 55 kilometers in length and 10 kilometers in width, with a total area of 567 square kilometers. About 13,500 people work at the center. Only roughly 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, the remaining area being a wildlife refuge that includes Mosquito Lagoon, the Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and the Canaveral National Seashore. This area experiences one of the highest lightning strike frequencies in the United States, prompting NASA to allocate significant resources for the protection of launches. Main operations are conducted from Launch Complex 39, which houses the Vertical Assembly Building. Two launch pads are located 5 kilometers to the east, while an industrial area with equipment and administrative buildings is situated 8 kilometers to the south. Space shuttles were launched from Complex 39, whereas unmanned vehicles were launched from the U.S. Space Forces base at Cape Canaveral. The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
