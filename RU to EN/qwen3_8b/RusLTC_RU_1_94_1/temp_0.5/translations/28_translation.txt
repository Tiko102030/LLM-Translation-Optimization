The new approach was neither a general critique of property rights nor a general affirmation of these rights. Instead, through redistribution of rights, it merely affirmed certain property in comparison with another property. According to one legal historian, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the order of the United States of America - "the liberation of all human creative energy." Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect damages remains on the shoulders of entrepreneurs, they still have the opportunity to proceed with profitable projects. Theoretically, if the cost of a project were higher than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase, even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice, all the burden is transferred to the victim's side, which only to a greater extent contributes to the increase in the number of projects by entrepreneurs, and all the risks and operational costs are placed on the shoulders of neighbors. If property rights were observed to a greater legal extent, high operational costs would hinder the realization of entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by saving on the costs of risk and operational costs for entrepreneurs, the new principle has even more contributed to the development of entrepreneurship.
