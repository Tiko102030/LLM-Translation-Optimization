The U.S. Supreme Court did not rush to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field cast doubt on the idea of police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Charitable Association of Butchers of New Orleans v. Crescent City Livestock Landing & Slaughter House." This case arose after the legislative body of the state of Louisiana limited the slaughter of livestock in New Orleans. Since there was no threat to public health, this law was similar to ordinances previously enacted in many other cities.
