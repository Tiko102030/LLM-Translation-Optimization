Conditionality or rather, even a myth in the study of American history assumes that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent, due to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1955), American society is viewed as typically liberal in the spirit of John Locke, with a particular passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to motivate workers and other people in pursuit of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
