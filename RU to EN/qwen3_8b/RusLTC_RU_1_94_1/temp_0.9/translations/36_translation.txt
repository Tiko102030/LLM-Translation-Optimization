The U.S. Supreme Court did not rush to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field cast doubt on the idea of police power as early as 1872 in the case "New Orleans Meat Merchants' Benevolent Association v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Company." This case arose after the legislature of the state of Louisiana limited the slaughter of livestock in New Orleans. Since there was no threat to public health, this law was similar to ordinances previously enacted in many other cities.
