Local authorities used the right of compulsory appropriation of private property to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "right of compulsory appropriation of private property," initially conceived by the Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, was incorporated into English law by John Locke, who established it as the basis of "natural rights," as the consent of the whole population, which granted all powers to the state's legislative bodies. (See Igel 1996, p. 8.) American judges and legal interpreters fully expanded the concept, merely based on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the 1791 Vermont State Constitution stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity demands it." (See Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes.)
