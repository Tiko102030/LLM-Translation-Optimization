The notion, or rather even a myth, in the study of American history assumes that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent, due to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "Traditions of American Liberalism" (1955), American society is viewed as typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, characterized by a special passion for individualism and the rights of private property. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to spur workers and others towards the achievement of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
