The concepts of property, instrumentalism, and the Lochner doctrine could logically have been defined based on other judicial decisions affirming property rights. In the case of Adkins v. Children's Hospital (1923), the Court invalidated a minimum wage law for the District of Columbia, ruling that the law unconstitutionally confiscated the property of workers and firms, violating their "freedom of contract," which should be "a general rule that cannot be taken away." Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon  
Shortly before the Adkins case was considered, the Supreme Court issued another historically significant ruling that seemingly entirely concerned the protection of property rights. In the case of Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon (1922), the Court declared Pennsylvania's law unconstitutional because the law, in order to preserve the coalbed, restricted the operations of coal companies. The Court held that the general rule would be that regulation of property may be exercised only to a certain extent; however, if it goes too far, it would constitute taking property.
