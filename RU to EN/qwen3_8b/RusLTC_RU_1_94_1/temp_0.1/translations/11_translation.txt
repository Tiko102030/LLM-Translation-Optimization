Conditionality or rather even a myth in the study of American history implies that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent, due to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "Traditions of American Liberalism" (1955), American society is considered as typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, fostering a particular passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to motivate workers and other people towards the achievement of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
