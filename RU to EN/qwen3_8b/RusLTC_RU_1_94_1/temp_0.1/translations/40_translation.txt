The concepts of property, instrumentalism, and the Lochner era could logically have been defined based on other judicial decisions affirming property rights. In the case of "Adkins v. Children's Hospital" (1923), the Court invalidated a minimum wage law for the District of Columbia, ruling that the law constitutionally confiscated the property of workers and firms, violating their "freedom of contract," which should be "a general rule that cannot be taken away." Shortly before considering the Adkins case, the Supreme Court made another historically significant ruling that seemingly entirely concerned the protection of property rights. In the case of "Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon" (1922), the Court declared Pennsylvania's law unconstitutional because it limited the operations of coal companies in order to preserve the subsurface, asserting that the general rule would be that regulation of property can be carried out only to a certain extent; if it goes too far, it would constitute an expropriation of property.
