A gradual retreat turned into a general exodus when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the 1930s Great Depression. A new generation of liberals, uniting around President Franklin D. Roosevelt and the New Deal policies, helped ensure that the state would assist the needy and emerge from the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights.
