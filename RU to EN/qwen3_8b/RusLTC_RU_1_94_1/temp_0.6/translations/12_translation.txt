Some Americans accumulated property according to the principles of John Locke, combining their labor with land ownership and materials. However, most people became wealthy through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William into England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to seize land from indigenous peoples. Even the War of Independence of 1776, a war in the name of liberal ideals, became an example of mass looting, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs and most of the land parcels in Queens, Richmond, Kings, and Suffolk counties (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
