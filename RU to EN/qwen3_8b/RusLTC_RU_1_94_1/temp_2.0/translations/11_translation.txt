Conditionality, or rather, even a myth in the study of American history, assumes that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to individual rights and market freedom. To a greater extent, thanks mainly to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "Traditions of American Liberalism" (1955), American society is considered typically liberal in the classical spirit of Locke, fostering a special passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining the "efforts to encourage workers and others to achieve socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
