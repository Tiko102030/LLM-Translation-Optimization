In the 1875 case "Kohl v. United States," it stated that the right to compulsory taking of private property is "the result of political necessity and cannot be separated from sovereign power." The right to compulsory taking of private property was one example of the use of governmental power to promote the development of "well-organized society," increase the level of "public good" or "welfare" through regulations regarding intervention in property rights. These regulations reflected common law principles such as: "the welfare of the people is the supreme law" (Latin: salus populi suprema lex est) and "use your own so as not to harm another" (Latin: sic utere tuo ut alienum non laedas). These main principles frequently cited in court rulings serve as "programs of common law governance in a well-regulated society," ensuring the legal basis for comprehensive state regulation of property to reflect mutual interests and rights of all citizens (see Novak 1996, p. 42).
