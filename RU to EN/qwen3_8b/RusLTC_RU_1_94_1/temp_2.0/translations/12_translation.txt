However, most people became rich through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William into England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to take land from indigenous peoples. Even the War of Independence in 1776, a war in the name of liberal ideals, became an example of widespread looting, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs and a large portion of land parcels in Queens, Richmond, King's, and Suffolk counties (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
