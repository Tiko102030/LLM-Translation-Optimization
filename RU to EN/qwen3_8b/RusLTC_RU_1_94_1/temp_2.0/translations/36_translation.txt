The U.S. Supreme Court did not rush to criticize government regulation, but Justice Stephen Field cast doubt on the doctrine of police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Charitable Association of New Orleans Meat Merchants v. Crescent City Livestock Landing & Slater House". This case arose after the state legislature of Louisiana imposed restrictions on livestock slaughter in New Orleans. Since the health of the population was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances previously imposed in many other cities. Citing the opinion of Chief Justice Shaw of the Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court, in the case "State of Massachusetts v. Algar" (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court adopted this law, stating that "losses of private rights in this case are of no importance in view of the general profits and trade turnover of the said commercial enterprise."
