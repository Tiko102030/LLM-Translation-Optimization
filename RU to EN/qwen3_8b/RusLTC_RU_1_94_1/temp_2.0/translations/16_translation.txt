Local authorities used the right of eminent domain over private property to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "the right of eminent domain over private property," initially formulated by the Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, was incorporated into English law by John Locke, who based it on "natural law," as the consent of the entire population, which conferred all powers to the state's legislative organs. (see Igler 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters expanded the concept further, merely relying on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the 1791 Vermont State Constitution stated: "Private property should be subject to public use when necessity requires it." (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
