Instrumental, competing "rights" to property can never be absolute and should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must take precedence, new conditions are required to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that a return to the discussion of natural rights will occur, but contemporary judicial conservatives have not significantly altered U.S. legislation. Such a change would require courts to completely revise the legal rationale according to which property is viewed as a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
