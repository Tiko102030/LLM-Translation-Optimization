The valuation of English property was even less significant: debts to English creditors were annulled without much deliberation; some states, including Virginia, closed cases scheduled for hearing on claims asserted by British subjects (see Eli 1998, p. 35). The widespread confiscation of property from opponents of independence from England did not bode well for property security in the new republic. The main rebels fought to curb what they considered as "excess democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60). Ensuring property security, "defender of every right" became a concern for the creators of the first state constitutions (see Eli 1998, p. /think).
