The gradual retreat turned into a mass exodus when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the 1930s Great Depression. A new generation of liberals, uniting around President Franklin D. Roosevelt and the policies of the New Deal, helped ensure that the state assisted the needy and emerged from the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights.
