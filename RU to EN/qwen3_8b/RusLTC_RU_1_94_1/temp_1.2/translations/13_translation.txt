British property was evaluated to an even lesser extent: debts to British creditors were annulled without much deliberation; some states, including Virginia, closed cases designated for hearing in suits claimed by British subjects (see Eli 1998, p. 35). The widespread confiscation of property from opponents of independence from England was not a good omen for the security of property in the new republic. The main rebels fought to curb what they considered "excess democracy" (see Tomlinson 1993, p. 60). Ensuring property security, "defender of any right" troubled the creators of the first constitutions of all states (see Eli 1998, p. /think).
