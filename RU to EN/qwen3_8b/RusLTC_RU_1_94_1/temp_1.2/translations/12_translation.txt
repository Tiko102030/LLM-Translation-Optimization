Some Americans accumulated property based on John Locke's principles, combining their labor with land ownership and materials. However, most people became wealthy through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to seize the land of indigenous peoples. Even the 1776 War of Independence, a war in the name of liberal ideals, became an example of mass looting, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs and most of the land parcels in Queens, Richmond, Kings, and Suffolk counties (see Flik 1901, p. 153).
