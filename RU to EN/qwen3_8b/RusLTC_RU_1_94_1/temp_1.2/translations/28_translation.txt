The new approach was neither a general criticism of property rights nor a general affirmation of these rights. Instead, through the redistribution of rights, it merely asserted some property in comparison with another property. According to one legal historian, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the legal order of the United States of America – "the freedom of the creative energy of mankind." Despite the fact that the burden of compensating neighbors for indirect damages remains on entrepreneurs, they still have the opportunity to proceed with profitable projects. Theoretically, even if a project's cost were higher than the sum of the damage caused, its profitability would still increase, even if the entrepreneur had to pay the sum of the damage caused. However, in practice, the entire burden is transferred to the victim party, which only to a greater extent contributes to the increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, while all risks and operational costs fall on the neighbors. If property rights were enforced to a greater legal extent, high operational costs would hinder the realization of entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by reducing the costs of risk and operational costs for entrepreneurs, the new principle has further contributed to the development of entrepreneurship.
