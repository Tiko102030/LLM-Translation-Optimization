Instrumental, competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, and they should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must take precedence, new conditions are necessary to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that a return to discussions about natural rights will occur, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed U.S. legislation in any substantial way. Any change would require courts to completely revise the legal justification history according to which property is regarded as a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
