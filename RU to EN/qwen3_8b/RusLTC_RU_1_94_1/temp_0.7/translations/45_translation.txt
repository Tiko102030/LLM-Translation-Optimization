Gradual withdrawal turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the Great Depression of the 1930s. A new generation of liberals, uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the policies of the New Deal, supported the state's role in helping the needy and exited the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's 1937 decisions, even referring to the "constitutional revolution of 1937" after two conservative judges resigned when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened to "pack" the Supreme Court.
