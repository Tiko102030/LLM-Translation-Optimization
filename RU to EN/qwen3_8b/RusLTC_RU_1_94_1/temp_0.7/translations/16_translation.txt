Local authorities used the right of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "right of eminent domain," first coined by the Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, was incorporated into English law by John Locke, who placed it at the foundation of "natural law" as the consent of the entire population that granted all powers to the state's legislative bodies. (see Iglesias 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters fully expanded the concept, merely based on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the 1791 Vermont Constitution stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
