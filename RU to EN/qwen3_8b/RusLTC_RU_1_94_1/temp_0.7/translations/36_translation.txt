The U.S. Supreme Court did not rush to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field questioned the concept of police power as early as 1872 in the case "New Orleans Meat Association v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Company." This case arose after the legislative body of the state of Louisiana had limited the slaughter of cattle in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances previously imposed in many other cities.
