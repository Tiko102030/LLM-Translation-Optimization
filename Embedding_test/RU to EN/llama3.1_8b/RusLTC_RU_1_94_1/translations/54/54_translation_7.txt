Instrumental and competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, nor should they ever become an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are necessary for considering and competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to discussing natural rights, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed US legislation in any significant way. It would require courts to completely change the legal history of justification, according to which property is considered a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
