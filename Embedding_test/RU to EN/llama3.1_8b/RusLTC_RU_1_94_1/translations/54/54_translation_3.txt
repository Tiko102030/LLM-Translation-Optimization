Instrumental competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, they should never become an end in themselves. Since some rights must take precedence, new conditions are necessary to consider and competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to discussing natural rights, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed US legislation in any significant way. It would require courts to completely change the legal history, according to which property is considered a social institution, not an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
