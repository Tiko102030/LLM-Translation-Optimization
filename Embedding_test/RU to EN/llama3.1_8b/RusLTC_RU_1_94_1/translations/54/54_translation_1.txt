Instrumental and competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, nor should they ever become an end in themselves. Since some rights must take precedence, new conditions are necessary to consider and compete with claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed US legislation in any significant way. It would require courts to completely change the history of legal justification, according to which property is considered a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
