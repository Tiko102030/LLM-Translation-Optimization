Instrumental competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, they should never become an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are necessary to consider and competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but modern judicial conservatives have not changed US legislation in any significant way. A change would require courts to completely alter the legal justification history, according to which property is considered a social institution, not an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
