In 1787, the Constitutional Convention limited the authority of regional governments to interfere in transactions (Foner 1976, p. 133; Eli 1998, p. 26). The fears of conservatives led to the addition of the Fifth Amendment to stop property confiscation. The process of confiscating property did not follow due process and did not provide any compensation (see Raykov 1996, pp. 314-5; Eli 1998, pp. 43-54; Jacobs 1954, pp. 4-5).
