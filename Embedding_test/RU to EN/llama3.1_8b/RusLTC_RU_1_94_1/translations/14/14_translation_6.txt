In 1787, the Constitutional Convention limited the authority of regional governments to intervene in transactions (Foner 1976, p. 133; Elie 1998, p. 26). Later, they added the Fifth Amendment to prevent confiscation of property. The process of confiscating property did not follow due process and offered no compensation (see Raykov 1996, pp. 314-5; Elie 1998, pp. 43-54; Jacobs 1954, pp. 4-5).
