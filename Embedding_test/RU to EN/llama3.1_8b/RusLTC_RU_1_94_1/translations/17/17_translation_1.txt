The Supreme Court of the United States also indicated that the right to compulsory expropriation of private property is based on public benefit and instrumental considerations, and in the case of 1875 "Kohl v. United States" ruled that the right to compulsory expropriation of private property - "is a result of political necessity, and it cannot be separated from sovereign power". The right to compulsory expropriation of private property was one example of the use of government authority to promote the development of a "well-organized society", to increase the quality of "public benefit" or "welfare" through ordinances dealing with interference in the rights of property owners. These ordinances reflected common law principles such as: "the good of the people shall be the highest law" (lat. salus populi suprema lex est) and "use your own so as not to harm another" (lat. sic utere tuo ut alienum non laedas). Frequently cited in court decisions, these main principles serve as "common law programs for managing a well-regulated society", providing a legal basis for comprehensive state regulation of property in order to reflect the mutual interests and rights of all citizens (see Novak 1996, p. 42).
