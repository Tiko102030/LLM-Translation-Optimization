The concepts of property, instrumentalism, and the New Deal Lorchner could have been logically determined from other court decisions that upheld property rights. In the case of Adkins v. Children's Hospital (1923), the Court struck down a minimum wage law for the District of Columbia, ruling that this law unconstitutionally confiscated workers' and firms' property, violating their "freedom of contract," which must be "a common rule" and cannot be taken away.
