Local authorities have used the right of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "right of eminent domain" was first coined by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625 and recorded in English law with J. Locke, who placed it on the basis of "natural rights," as a coincidence of the will of all the population that granted all powers to state legislative bodies (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters have fully expanded the concept based solely on instrumental considerations. Denying any sanctity of private property rights, Vermont's Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
