Local authorities used the right of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "right of eminent domain" was first coined by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625 and recorded in English law with J. Locke, who placed it as a basis for "natural rights," as an alignment of the will of all citizens that granted all legislative powers to state legislatures (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters expanded the concept solely based on instrumental considerations, denying any sanctity of private property rights. The Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
