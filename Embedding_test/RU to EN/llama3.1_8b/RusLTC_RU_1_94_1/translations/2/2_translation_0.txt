The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, resulting in many economists abandoning the idea of income distribution and focusing on the values of different legal regimes' efficiency. However, there are several questions related to these assertions. Separate consideration of price and income distribution only takes place when income distribution affects both the level of prices and the structure of production. This will occur when economic subjects have various preferences and when returns to scale increase or decrease. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses related to negotiations and enforcing contracts. This can be extremely important in concluding a trade agreement involving external effects, especially when it is often difficult to encourage a large number of people to bribe the one polluting the environment. The definition of property rights is often considered quite controversial in liberal democracies, similar to how in the United States people have both personal freedom and the right of the majority to govern the country (see Horowitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
