The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, resulting in many economists abandoning the idea of income distribution and focusing on the values of different legal regimes' efficiency. However, there are several questions related to these claims. Separate consideration of price and income distribution only occurs when income distribution affects both prices and production structure. This will happen in cases where economic subjects have various preferences and when returns to scale increase or decrease. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses associated with negotiations and enforcing contracts. This can be extremely important when concluding a trade agreement involving external effects, especially when it is often difficult to persuade a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The definition of property rights is often considered quite controversial in liberal democracies, similar to how in the United States people have both personal freedom and the right of the majority to govern the country. (see Horowitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
