Also at each level of governance there is a clear division between the executive, legislative and judicial powers, each of which is intended to respect and ensure compliance with the norms of the Constitution in which they find their interpretation. The reconciliation of these differences in interpretation was the cause of the subsequent division between written constitutions, codes of law and sometimes legally effective "Common Law", which arose in England and was described by several English legal authors. Different sources offer a rich material for judicial interpretation. Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr. was not the first to recognize that judges in American legislation are independent players, and this is all due to various methods of interpreting one case or another. As interpreters of norms of law, they use their experience to use the law as an instrument for promoting their ideas about an ideal society (see Fisher, Horwitz and Reed 1993). American property rights were established by judges and politicians through civil proceedings caused by specific institutions of the American government, legal tradition and political conflict.
