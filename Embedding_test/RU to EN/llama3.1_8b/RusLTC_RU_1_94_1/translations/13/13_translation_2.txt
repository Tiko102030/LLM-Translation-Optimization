American property was valued even less: English debts to creditors were, without much deliberation, annulled; some states, including Virginia, closed cases brought by British subjects (see Eli 1998, p. 35). The widespread confiscation of the property of those opposed to independence from England did not bode well for the security of property in the new republic. The main rebels fought against what they considered "excess democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60). Ensuring property security, the "protector of any right" became a concern for the creators of the first constitutions of all states.
