Citing the Bible, Locke asserts that the same law of nature which gives us property also limits its extent. "God gave us all in abundance" - this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But for what purpose did He give it to us? For enjoyment. Man has a right to turn his labor into property as much as he can use it for any needs of his life before the object deteriorates. And that which exceeds these limits, surpasses his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or ruin. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a reserve of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal ownership of land," possess power over others. Therefore, restrictions on property accumulation are necessary to ensure that all have access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, which were later taken up by other authors, including the American radical Thomas Paine.
