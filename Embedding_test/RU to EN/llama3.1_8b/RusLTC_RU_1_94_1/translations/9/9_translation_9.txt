Citing the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature which gives us property also limits its extent. "God gave us all in abundance" - this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But for what purpose did he give it to us? For enjoyment. Man has a right to turn his labor into property as much as he can use for any needs of his life before the object deteriorates. What goes beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or ruin. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal ownership of land," possess power over others. Therefore, restrictions on property accumulation are necessary to ensure that everyone has access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, which were later taken up by other authors, including Thomas Paine, a well-known American radical.
