The exceptionalism of American law, the sanctity of property rights and police powers are myths in research on American history that suggest the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent thanks to the merits of Louis Harth and his famous work "Traditions of Liberal America" (1955), American society is considered typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, nurturing a special passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining efforts to encourage workers and other people towards achieving socialist and collectivist goals (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
