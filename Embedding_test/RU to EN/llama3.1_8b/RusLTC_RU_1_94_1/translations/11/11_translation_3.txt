Conditionality or rather a myth in research on American history suggests that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent thanks to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1955), American society is considered typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, nurturing a special passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining efforts to "induce workers and other people to achieve socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
