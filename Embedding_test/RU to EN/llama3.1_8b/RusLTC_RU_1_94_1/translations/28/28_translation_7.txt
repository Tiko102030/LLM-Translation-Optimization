A new approach was neither a general criticism of property rights nor a general affirmation of these rights. Instead, through the redistribution of rights it only asserted some property in comparison to other property. According to one of the historians of law, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the US legal order - "the liberation of all human creative energy". Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have the opportunity to start projects that bring profit. In theory, if a project's cost was higher than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice all the burden is shifted to the injured party, which only contributes to an increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, and all the risk and operational costs fall on neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were observed to a greater legal extent, high operational costs would have hindered entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by saving on the cost of risk and operational expenses, entrepreneurs' new principle only contributed more to the development of entrepreneurship. How then to deal with an excess of democracy?
