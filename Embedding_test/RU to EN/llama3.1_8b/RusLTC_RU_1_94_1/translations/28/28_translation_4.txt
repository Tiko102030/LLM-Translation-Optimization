A new approach was not a general criticism of property rights or a general affirmation of these rights. Instead, through the redistribution of rights it only asserted some property in comparison to other property. According to one of the historians of law, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the rule of law in the United States - "the liberation of all human creative energy". Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on the shoulders of entrepreneurs, they still have the opportunity to start projects that bring profit. In theory, if a project was worth more than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice all the burden is shifted to the injured party, which only contributes to an even greater increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, and all the risk and operational costs fall on the shoulders of neighbors. If property rights were observed to a greater legal extent, high operational costs would have hindered the implementation of entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by saving on the cost of risk and operational expenses, entrepreneurs' new principle has only contributed even more to the development of entrepreneurship.
