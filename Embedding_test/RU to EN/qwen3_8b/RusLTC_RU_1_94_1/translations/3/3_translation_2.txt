On the other hand, the right of the majority to control economic activities, determine public goals, and protect property owners from the consequences of the actions of polluters is considered. However, such a general comparison of property rights can be misunderstood, since these rights, to a greater extent, limit the rights of other people. Property rights grant individuals privileges sanctioned by the state, allowing them to monopolize resources and to appeal to authorized police authorities to limit the activities of other people. Thus, property rights significantly restrict certain advantages because governmental authorities, to a greater extent, support property owners rather than other citizens. Therefore, it is not a debate about the principles of natural freedom and justice, but rather discussions on property regulation that force a choice about whose property should be protected and whose property rights have the advantage in the struggle for them. The discussion of democratic rights and their regulation has become so unclear because legal issues have repeatedly been discussed at various levels of government within the structure of the legal system of the United States. Thus, according to the system of federalism, a single Federal Government is formed, while each state has its own legal system. Also, at each level of government, there is a clear separation of executive, legislative, and judicial powers, each of which is intended to respect and ensure the observance of constitutional norms, in which they find their interpretation.
