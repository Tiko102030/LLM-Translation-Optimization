The conditional or even mythic nature of the study of American history assumes that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent, thanks to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "Traditions of American Liberalism" (1955), American society is viewed as typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, fostering a particular passion for individualism and private property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to motivate workers and others toward the achievement of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
