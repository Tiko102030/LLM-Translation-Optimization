The notion or rather even a myth in the study of American history assumes that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent, this view is due to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "Traditions of American Liberalism" (1955), which portrays American society as typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, characterized by a particular passion for individualism and the rights of private property. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to motivate workers and others towards the achievement of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, pp. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
