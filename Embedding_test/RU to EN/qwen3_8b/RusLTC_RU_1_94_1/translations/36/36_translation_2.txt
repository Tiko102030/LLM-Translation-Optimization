The U.S. Supreme Court did not rush to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field cast doubt on the idea of police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Charitable Association of New Orleans Meat Merchants v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Company." This case arose after the legislative body of the state of Louisiana limited the slaughter of livestock in New Orleans. Since there was no threat to public health, this law was similar to ordinances previously enacted in many other cities.
