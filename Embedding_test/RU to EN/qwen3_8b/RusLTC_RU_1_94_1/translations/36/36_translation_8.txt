The U.S. Supreme Court did not rush to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field cast doubt on the idea of police powers as early as 1872 in the case "New Orleans Meat Association v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slotter-House." This case arose after the legislature of the state of Louisiana limited cattle slaughter in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances previously enacted in many other cities.
