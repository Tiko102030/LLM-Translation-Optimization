Later, the Fifth Amendment was added to stop the confiscation of property. The process of confiscating property did not proceed in due course and did not involve any compensation (see Reikov 1996, pp. 314–5; Elie 1998, pp. 43–54; Jacobs 1954, pp. 4–5).
