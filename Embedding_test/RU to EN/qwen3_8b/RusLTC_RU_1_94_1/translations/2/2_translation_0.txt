The free trade market will lead to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and irrespective of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article exerted a significant influence on economic theory, leading many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and focus instead on the efficiency values of different legal regimes. However, there are several issues related to these statements. Separate consideration of price and income distribution occurs only when income distribution affects both price levels and production structure. This will happen when economic agents have various preferences and when there is an increase or decrease in returns to scale. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or the costs associated with negotiations and enforcing contracts. This may be extremely important in concluding a trade deal involving external effects, especially when it is often problematic to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The definition of property rights is often regarded in liberal democracies as quite contradictory, similar to how in the United States people possess both personal freedom and the right of majority rule in governing the country. (See Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895.)
