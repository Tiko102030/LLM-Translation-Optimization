A free trade market will lead to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a significant impact on economic theory, leading many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and focus instead on the efficiency values of different legal regimes. However, there are several issues related to these statements. Separate consideration of price and income distribution occurs only when income distribution affects both price levels and production structure. This will happen when economic agents have various preferences and when returns to scale increase or decrease. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or the costs associated with negotiations and enforcing contracts. This may be extremely important in concluding a trade deal involving external effects, particularly when it is often problematic to incentivize a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. Defining property rights is often viewed in liberal democracies as quite contradictory, similar to how in the United States people possess both personal freedom and the right of majority rule over the country. (See Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
