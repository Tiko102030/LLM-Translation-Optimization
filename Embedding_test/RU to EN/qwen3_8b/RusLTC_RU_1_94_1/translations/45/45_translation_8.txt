New Benefits of the New Deal The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the Great Depression of the 1930s. Uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the New Deal policies, a new generation of liberals facilitated the state's assistance to the needy and exited the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's 1937 decisions, even referring to the "1937 constitutional revolution" after two conservative judges retired when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened to "pack" the Court.
