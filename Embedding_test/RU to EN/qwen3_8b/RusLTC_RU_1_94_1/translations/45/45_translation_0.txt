The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the 1930s Great Depression. A new generation of liberals, uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the New Deal policies, helped ensure that the state assisted the needy and emerged from the crisis through measures of reformulated capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's 1937 decisions, even referring to the "constitutional revolution of 1937" after two conservative justices retired when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened to "pack" the Supreme Court.
