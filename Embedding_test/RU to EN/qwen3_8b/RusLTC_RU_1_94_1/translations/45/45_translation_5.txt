New Advantages of the New Deal A gradual withdrawal turned into a general retreat when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the Great Depression of the 1930s. Uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the policies of the New Deal, a new generation of liberals facilitated the government's assistance to the needy and exited the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's decisions from 1937, even referring to the "Constitutional Revolution of 1937" after two conservative justices retired when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened to "pack" the Supreme Court.
