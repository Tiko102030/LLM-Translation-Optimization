British property was assessed to an even lesser extent: debts to English creditors were, without much deliberation, annulled; some states, including Virginia, closed cases designated for hearing regarding claims asserted by British subjects (see Eli 1998, p. 35). The widespread confiscation of property from opponents of independence from England did not serve as a good omen for property security in the new republic. The main rebels fought to curb what they considered as "excess democracy" (see Tomlinson 1993, p. 60). Ensuring property security, "defender of any right" became a concern for the creators of the first state constitutions (see Eli 1998, p. /think).
