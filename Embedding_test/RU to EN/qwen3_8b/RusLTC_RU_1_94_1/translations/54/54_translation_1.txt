Instrumental, competing "rights" to property can never be absolute and should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that a return to the discussion of natural rights will occur, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed US legislation in any substantial way. Change would require courts to completely alter the legal rationale that views property as a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require that they elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
