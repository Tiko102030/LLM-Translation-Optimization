 Referring to the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature that gives us property also limits the extent of this property. "God has given us everything abundantly" — this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But why did he give it to us? For enjoyment. A person has the right to turn into their own property as much as they can use for any needs of their life before that item spoils. And what goes beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for spoilage or destruction. However, to Locke's огорчение, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal ownership of land," to have power over others. Therefore, restrictions on the accumulation of property are necessary in order to guarantee everyone access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's property theory contributed to the emergence of discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, later other authors joined these рассуждений, including the famous American radical Thomas Paine.
