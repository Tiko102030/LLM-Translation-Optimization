In fact, the Thirteenth Amendment закрепляет in the Constitution of the United States the principle of имущественной confiscation. According to this amendment, the безвозмездная confiscation of slaves' property redistributed almost 3 billion US dollars, half of all the wealth of the southern states. Therefore, the emancipation of slaves in America became one of the most significant examples in history of property confiscation. Government intervention in private property did not end with these confiscations. During the colonial and pre-war periods, regional governments and local authorities regularly interfered in matters of private property, thereby once again confirming the widespread belief that the government has a moral duty to promote a prosperous economy and support poor citizens. For example, most local authorities set transportation fares and bread prices, restricted food trade, regulated wages and employment opportunities, and also limited the activities of sellers of alcoholic beverages, bars, and prostitutes (see Handlin and Handlin 1947; Burdin 1989; Novak 1996; Lori 1999; Levi 2000). And local authorities used the right to forcibly отчуждать private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners.
