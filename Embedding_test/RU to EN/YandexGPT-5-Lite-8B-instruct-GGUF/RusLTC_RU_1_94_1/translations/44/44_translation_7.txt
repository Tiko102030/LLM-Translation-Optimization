 When can one intervene in trade or already existing transactions? The field of activity must be delimited, and quantitative differences must be defined. In this regard, in the Pennsylvania Coal case, Holmes rather acted as a Trojan Horse rather than a winner-protector of sacred property rights. Instead of considering the right to property as a natural right, Holmes обосновал the "principle of изъятия" of property based on establishing an equilibrium between competing interests. Compensation for damages will depend on the circumstances and will be an optional procedure. According to Holmes, property must be used "reasonably" all the time, but the idea of what is "reasonable" remains vague. If actions go "too far," then compensation for damage will be necessary in this case; however, it will be up to the judges, taking into account public needs and special circumstances, to determine what "too far" means. Neither private property owners nor the public have absolute rights; in each case, judges must carefully assess and balance the competing interests of both parties. Owners should not, in turn, alienate everyone. Holmes argued that "the government can hardly continue its activities if the issue of property is not resolved to some extent."
