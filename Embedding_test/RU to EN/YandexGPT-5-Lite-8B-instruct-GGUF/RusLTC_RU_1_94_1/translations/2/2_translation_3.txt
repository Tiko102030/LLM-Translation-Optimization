 A free trade market will lead to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, as a result of which many economists abandoned the idea of income distribution and focused on the values of efficiency of various legal regimes. However, there are a number of questions related to these statements. Separate consideration of price and income distribution takes place only when income distribution will affect both the price level and the production structure. This will happen when economic agents have different preferences and when returns to scale increase or decrease. In addition, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses associated with negotiating and enforcing contracts. This can be extremely important when concluding a trade deal involving externalities, especially when it is often problematic to persuade a large number of people to bribe someone who pollutes the environment. The definition of property rights is often regarded in liberal democracy as quite contradictory, just as in the United States people have both personal freedom and the right of the majority to govern the country. (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
