 Instrumental, competing "property rights" can never be absolute, they should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some people still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed US legislation in any significant way. Change would require courts to completely change the history of legal justification, according to which property is seen as a social institution, not an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
