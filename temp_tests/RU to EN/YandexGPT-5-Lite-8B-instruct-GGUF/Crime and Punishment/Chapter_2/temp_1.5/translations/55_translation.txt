 He would just spit on someone, bite, it seems, if anyone spoke to him…

He suddenly stopped when he stepped onto the embankment of Malaya Neva, on Vasilievsky Island, near the bridge. “That’s where he lives, in this house,” he thought. “What is this? Surely I have come to Razumikhin myself! Again the same story as then… But it’s very curious, though: did I really come here myself or just walked and dropped by here? It doesn't matter; I said... the other day... that I would go to him the next day after that, well, what of it, I will go! As if I couldn’t drop in now…”

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was home, in his closet-sized room, writing and opened the door for him himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting there in a tattered robe, with slippers on his bare feet, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was expressed on his face.
