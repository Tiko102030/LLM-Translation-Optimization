 “— Is this really how one hides things? My mind is truly abandoning me!” He sat down on the sofa in exhaustion, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him again. Automatically he dragged over to him what lay nearby on a chair — his old student's winter coat, warm but almost tattered — threw it over himself, and sleep and delirium enveloped him once more. He fell asleep. After no more than five minutes he jumped up again and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed to his clothes again. “How could I fall asleep again when nothing is done! That's right, that's right: I still haven't taken off the noose under my arm! I forgot, I really forgot about such a thing! Such evidence!” He pulled down the loop and quickly began to tear it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in his laundry. “Pieces of torn canvas will not arouse suspicion in any case; that seems so, that seems so!” he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painfully intense attention he started looking again around, on the floor and everywhere, to see if he had forgotten anything else?”
