 The stout lady jumped up from her seat when she saw him and began to curtsy with some special delight; but the officer paid no attention to her, and she no longer dared to sit down in front of him. It was a lieutenant, an assistant district supervisor, with reddish moustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and extremely fine facial features, which did not express anything particularly striking except for some insolence. He looked askance at Raskolnikov, partly with indignation: his costume was too shabby, and despite all the lowering, his posture still did not match the costume; Raskolnikov, through неосторожности, looked at him too прямо and long, so much so that he even took offence. “What do you want?” he shouted, probably wondering why such a ragamuffin does not disappear from his lightning-fast glance. “They required me… by summons...” Raskolnikov answered somehow. “It’s about the case of collecting money from the student,” the clerk hurried to say, tearing himself away from the paper. — Here! — and he threw a notebook to Raskolnikov, pointing out the place in it — read it! “Money?”
