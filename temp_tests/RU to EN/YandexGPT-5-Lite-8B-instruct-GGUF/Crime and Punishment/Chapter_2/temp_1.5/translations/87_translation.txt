 — Shall we have some tea?  
— Tea would be nice.  
— Pour it. Wait, I'll pour for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately ordered, poured, then poured another cup, abandoned his breakfast and moved back to the sofa. As before, he embraced the sick man's head with his left hand, lifted him up and began to feed him tea with a teaspoon, blowing on the spoon again continuously and particularly diligently, as if in this process of blowing was the most important and salutary point of recovery. Raskolnikov remained silent and did not resist, despite the fact that he felt quite strong enough to lift himself and sit on the sofa without any посторонней help, and not only to control his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but perhaps even to walk. But for some strange, almost animal cunning, it suddenly occurred to him to hide his strength until the time had come, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, not to understand things fully yet, and at the same time to listen and find out what was going on here? However, he could not control his disgust: after gulping down about ten spoons of tea, he suddenly freed his head, capriciously pushed the spoon away and rolled back onto the pillow. There really were now real pillows under his heads — feather pillows with clean pillowcases; he noticed that too and took it into consideration.
