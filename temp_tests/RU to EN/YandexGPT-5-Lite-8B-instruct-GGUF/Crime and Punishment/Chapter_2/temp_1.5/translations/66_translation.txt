 The pain from the whip subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one беспокойная and not quite clear thought occupied him now exclusively. He stood and looked into the distance for a long time and fixedly; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to university, it usually happened to him—most often when returning home—perhaps a hundred times that he would stop right here, gaze intently at this truly magnificent panorama and each time almost be surprised by one obscure and insoluble impression of his. An inexplicable cold always seemed to emanate from this magnificent panorama; the mute and deaf spirit was full for him in this splendid picture… He was surprised every time by his gloomy and mysterious impression and put off solving it, distrusting himself, to the future. Now suddenly he sharply remembered his previous questions and perplexities, and it seemed to him that he had not forgotten them by chance now. It already seemed strange and funny to him that he had stopped at the same place as before, as if he really imagined that he could think about the same thing now as before, and be interested in the same topics and pictures as... so recently. Even it almost made him laugh and at the same time his chest сжало painfully. In some depth, down there, somewhere barely visible under his feet, everything seemed to him now all this former past, and former thoughts, and former tasks, and former themes, and former impressions, and this whole panorama, and he himself, and everything, everything… It seemed to him that he was flying up somewhere, and everything disappeared from his eyes... Making an involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a two-ruble coin clenched in his fist. He opened his hand, looked fixedly at the coin, swung it and threw it into the water; then turned around and went home. It seemed to him that he had cut himself off from everyone and everything in that moment as if with scissors.
