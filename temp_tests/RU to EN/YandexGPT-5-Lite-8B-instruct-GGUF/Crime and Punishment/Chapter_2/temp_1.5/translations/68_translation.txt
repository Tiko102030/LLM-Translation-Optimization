She howled, squealed and wailed, hurrying, in a rush, letting out words in such a way that one couldn't make out what she was begging for — of course, for them to stop beating her, because they were mercilessly beating her on the stairs. The voice of the one who was hitting her became so terrible with anger and fury that it could only hiss, but still the one who was hitting her also said something, and just as quickly, indistinctly, in a hurry and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice. Ilya Petrovich is here beating the landlady! He beats her with his feet, knocks her head against the steps — it's clear, it can be heard in the sounds, in the howls, in the blows! What is this, has the light turned upside down, or what? It could be heard how a crowd was gathering on all floors, throughout the stairs, voices and exclamations were heard, they went up, knocked, slammed doors, ran together.
