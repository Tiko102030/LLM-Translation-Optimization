 But at that moment the door swung open again, and, bending down a little because he was tall, Razumikhin entered. “What a sea cabin,” he shouted as he came in, “I always bang my forehead; this is also called an apartment! And you, brother, have come to your senses? I just heard it from Pashenka.” “Just now came to his senses,” said Nastasya. “He has just recovered,” chimed in the foreman again with a smile. “And who might you be, sir?” Razumikhin asked, suddenly turning to him. “You see, I am Vrazumikhin; not Razumikhin, as everyone calls me, but Vrazumikhin, a student, a nobleman’s son, and he is my friend. Well, then who are you?”
