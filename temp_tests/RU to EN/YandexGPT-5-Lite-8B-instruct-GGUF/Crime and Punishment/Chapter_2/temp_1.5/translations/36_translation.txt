 — For pity's sake, Captain, — he began rather развязно, turning suddenly to Nikodim Fomich, — understand my situation... I am even ready to ask them for an apology if I have neglected anything on my part. I am a poor and sick student, oppressed (he said so: "oppressed") by poverty. I'm an ex-student because now I can't support myself, but I will get the money... My mother and sister are in — th province. They will send it to me, and I... will pay. My landlady is a kind woman, but she got so angry that I lost my lessons and haven’t paid for four months that she doesn’t even send me dinner… And I don’t understand at all what this promissory note is about! Now she demands from me on the basis of this borrowed letter that I pay her, judge for yourself!.. — But this isn't our business... — said the clerk again...
— Let me, let me, I agree with you completely, but let me also explain, — Raskolnikov picked up again, turning not to the clerk, but still to Nikodim Fomich, trying with all his might to turn to Ilya Petrovich as well, although he stubbornly pretended to be rummaging through papers and contemptuously ignored him, — let me clarify that I've lived with her for about three years now, since my arrival from the province, and before... before... in fact, why shouldn’t I admit it, from the very beginning I promised that I would marry her daughter, a verbal promise, completely free... She was a girl... besides, I even liked her... although I wasn’t in love... in short, youth, that is, what I mean is that my landlady gave me a lot of credit back then and I led something like a life... I was very frivolous...
— We don't need such intimacies from you, sir, and there isn't time, — Ilya Petrovich interrupted rudely and triumphantly, but Raskolnikov stopped him with heat, although it suddenly became extremely difficult for him to talk. — But let me tell you in part what happened... how things were and… in turn… though this is superfluous, I agree with you, to tell, — a year ago that girl died of typhus, I remained a tenant as I was, and my landlady, when she moved to the current apartment, told me... and said friendly... that she had every confidence in me and everything... but whether I wouldn’t like to give her this promissory note for one hundred and fifteen rubles, all she considered I owed. Allow me: she specifically said that as soon as I gave this paper, she would lend me credit again as much as she wanted and that never, never, in turn, — these were her own words — she wouldn’t use this paper until I paid myself... And now when I have lost my lessons and have nothing to eat, she is suing for collection... What will I say? — All these sensitive details, sir, do not concern us, — Ilya Petrovich boldly cut in, — you must give feedback and a bond, but that you were infatuated and all those tragic bits, we don’t care about any of it.
