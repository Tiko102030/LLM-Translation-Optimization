 “If asked, I might say,” he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst from him. It had just moved to a new address, into a new building, on the fourth floor. He had once briefly been to their previous apartment, but it was a long time ago. Walking through the gate, he saw a staircase to the right, up which a peasant was going with a book in his hands: “That must be the janitor; so this is where the office is,” and he began to go upstairs at random. He did not want to ask anyone about anything. “I'll go in, kneel down, and tell them everything...” he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and covered in slops. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood like that almost the whole day. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
