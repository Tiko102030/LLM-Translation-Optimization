 — And where, may I ask? — Along the street. — Short and clear. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, all pale as a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes in front of Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He can barely stand on his feet, and you... Nikodim Fomich remarked casually. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said especially emphatically. Nikodim Fomich was about to add something else, but looking at the clerk, who also stared at him very intently, he fell silent. Suddenly everyone fell silent. It was strange.
