 — And you, oh you such and such and this and that, — he shouted suddenly at the top of his lungs (the mourning lady had already left), — what happened to you last night? huh? Again a disgrace, making a ruckus in the whole street. Again a fight and drinking. You're dreaming of being put in a straitjacket! After all, I've told you, I've warned you ten times that I won't tolerate it on the eleventh! And you again, again, you such and such! Even Raskolnikov's paper fell out of his hands, and he was staring wildly at the magnificent lady, who was being so unceremoniously dealt with; but soon, however, he figured out what was going on, and immediately this whole story began to please him very much. He listened with pleasure, so much so that he wanted to laugh, laugh, laugh... All his nerves were jumping. — Ilya Petrovich!
