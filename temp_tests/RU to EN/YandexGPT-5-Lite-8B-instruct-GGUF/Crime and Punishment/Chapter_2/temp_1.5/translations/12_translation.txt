 He did not respond and was holding a piece of paper in his hands without opening it. “Don't even get up,” Nastasia continued, softening with pity as she saw him lowering his legs from the sofa. “If you’re sick, then don’t go: you won’t burn. What do you have there?” He glanced: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he remembered how, even when half-awake from fever, he would squeeze all of it tightly in his hand and fall back asleep again. “Look at him, picking up rags and sleeping with them as if they were treasure…” And Nastasia burst into her painful, nervous laughter. He quickly shoved everything under his overcoat and stared intently at her. Although at that moment he could hardly think clearly, he felt that a person would not be treated the same way when they came to take him.
