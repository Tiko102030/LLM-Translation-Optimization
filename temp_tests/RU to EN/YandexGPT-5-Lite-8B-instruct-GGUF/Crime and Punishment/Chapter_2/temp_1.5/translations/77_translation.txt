 Well, who are you? — I'm a partner in our office, working for the merchant Shelopayev, and I'm here on business. — Please take a seat on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat down on another one, across from the table. — You did well to wake up, brother, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — For the last four days you have barely eaten or drunk anything. Legally, they gave you tea with a spoonful. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense — perhaps something hit your head. Some kind of nervous nonsense, the rations were poor, he says, they didn't give enough beer and horseradish, hence the illness, but it doesn't matter, it will pass and everything will be sorted out.
