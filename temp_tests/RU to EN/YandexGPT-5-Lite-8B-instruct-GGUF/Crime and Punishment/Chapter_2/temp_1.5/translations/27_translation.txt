 I read it once, another time, and did not understand. “What’s this?” he asked the clerk. “This is money being claimed from you under a loan letter, collection. You must either pay with all expenses, penalties and others, or give a written reply when you can pay, and at the same time an obligation not to leave the capital until payment, nor to sell or hide your property. And the lender has the right to sell your property and deal with you according to the laws.” “But I... owe nobody anything!” “This is none of our business. But a past-due and legally protested loan letter for one hundred fifteen rubles, issued by you to the widow, Collegiate Assessor Zarnitsyna, nine months ago, and then transferred for payment to Councilor Chebarov, has been brought to us for collection, so we invite you to reply.” “But she’s my landlady?”
