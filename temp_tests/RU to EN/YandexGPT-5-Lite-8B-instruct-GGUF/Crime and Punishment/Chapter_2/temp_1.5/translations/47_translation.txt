 Then he went out of the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt that he was all broken up, his mind was with him. He was afraid of being chased, afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps instructions to follow him would already be issued; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the traces for the time being. It was necessary to manage until there were still at least some strength and some reasoning left… Where to go? This had long been decided: “Throw everything into the ditch, and концы в воду, and that’s the end of it.” He had resolved this in his sleep still at night, in those moments when he remembered that several times he tried to get up and go: “Quickly, quickly, and throw everything away.” But it turned out to be very difficult to throw things away. He wandered along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour already, or maybe more, and looked at the approaches to the ditch where they met several times. But one could not even think about carrying out the intention: either barges stood right at the landings and laundresses were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides along the embankments, from all directions, it was possible to see, to notice: it is suspicious that a person deliberately came down, stopped and throws something into the water.
