He, however, was not completely out of his mind throughout the entire time of his illness: it was a лихорадочное состояние, with delirium and semi-consciousness. He later recalled many things. Sometimes it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him away and carry him somewhere, arguing about him fiercely and quarreling. At other times he was alone in the room, everyone had left and they were afraid of him, and only occasionally would they slightly open the door to look at him, threatening him, plotting something among themselves, laughing and teasing him. He often remembered Nastasya being near him; he could make out another person as well, someone who seemed very familiar to him, but he could not figure out who it was and yearned for that answer, even crying. At times it appeared to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times — that the same day was going on. But he completely forgot about that; however, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that he couldn't forget — he was tormented, suffering, trying to recall, moaning, becoming enraged or plunging into terrible, unbearable fear. Then he would pull himself together, wanting to run, but someone would always stop him by force, and he would fall back into helplessness and unconsciousness.
