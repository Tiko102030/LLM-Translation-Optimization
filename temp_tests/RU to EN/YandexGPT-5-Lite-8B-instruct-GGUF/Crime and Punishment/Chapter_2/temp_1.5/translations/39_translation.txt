 The clerk began to dictate to him the form of a typical reply in such cases, that is, “I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, etc.” — “But you can't write, your pen keeps falling from your hand,” remarked the clerk, peering curiously at Raskolnikov. "Are you sick?" — “Yes... my head is spinning… go on!” — “That’s all; please sign.” The clerk took back the paper and turned to others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of getting up and leaving, he laid both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It was as if a nail were being hammered into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly occurred to him: stand up now, approach Nikodim Fomich and tell him everything from yesterday, every detail, then go to the apartment together and show them the things in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already risen from his seat to carry it out.
