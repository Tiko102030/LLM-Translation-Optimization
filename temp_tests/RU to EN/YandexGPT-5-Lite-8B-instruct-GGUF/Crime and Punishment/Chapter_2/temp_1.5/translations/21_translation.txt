 He took a breath more freely. "Perhaps it's not right!" Gradually he began to take heart, he tried with all his might to encourage himself and come to his senses. "Some nonsense, some small indiscretion, and I can betray myself completely! Hm... It's a pity there's no air here," he added, "it's stuffy… My head is getting even more dizzy… my mind too…"

He felt terrible disorder in everything. He himself was afraid of not controlling himself. He tried to latch onto something and think about something completely irrelevant, but it didn't work at all. The clerk interested him greatly, though: he wanted to guess something from his face, to figure him out. This was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark complexion and lively face that looked older than his age, dressed fashionably and flamboyantly, with part in the back, neatly combed and groomed, with many rings on his white brush-cleaned fingers and gold chains on his waistcoat. He even exchanged a couple of words in French with one foreigner present, quite satisfactorily. "Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down," he said casually to the smartly dressed, rather red-faced lady who was standing as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
