 Well, what's this? He rushed to take them out and throw them onto the table. Having taken everything out, even turning his pockets inside out to make sure there was nothing left, he carried all that pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one place the wallpaper was torn off from the wall: immediately he began to shove everything into this hole, under the paper: “It went in! Everything is out of sight, including the wallet!” — he thought joyfully, getting up and staring blankly into the corner, at the hole that had become even more protruding. Suddenly he shivered all over with horror: “Oh my God,” he whispered in despair, “what's happening to me? Was this hidden here? Is this how you hide things?”

Indeed, he didn't expect any items; he thought there would be money alone, which is why he hadn't prepared a place beforehand — "but what am I happy about now? — he thought. — Is this how you hide things?"
