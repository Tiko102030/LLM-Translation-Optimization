 — Don't bother shouting!  
— I'm not screaming, I'm talking quite evenly, and it's you who are shouting at me; but I'm a student and I won't allow anyone to shout at me.  
The assistant got so angry that for the first moment he couldn't say anything, and only some spluttering came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat.  
— Be s-s-so quiet! You are in the presence. Not r-u-udely, sir!  
— And you are in the presence, — Raskolnikov shouted, — and besides, you're smoking a cigarette while shouting, so you're insulting all of us.  
Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
