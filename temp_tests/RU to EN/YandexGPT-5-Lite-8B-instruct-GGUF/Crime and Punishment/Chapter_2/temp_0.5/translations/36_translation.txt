 — For mercy's sake, Captain, — he began quite развязно, addressing Nikodim Fomichev suddenly, — consider my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I have neglected anything on my part. I am a poor and sick student, oppressed (he said so: "oppressed") by poverty. I am an ex-student because now I can't support myself, but I will get the money... My mother and sister are in — province. They will send me some, and I will... pay up. My landlady is a kind woman, but she got so angry that I lost my lessons and haven't paid for four months that she doesn't even send me lunch... And I don't understand at all what this bill of exchange is! Now she demands from me on the basis of this loan letter that I will pay her something, judge for yourself!.. — But this isn't our business... — the clerk noted again...
— Let me, let me, I completely agree with you, but allow me to explain too, — Raskolnikov picked up again, addressing not the clerk, but still Nikodim Fomichev, but trying with all his might to address Ilya Petrovich as well, although he stubbornly pretended to be rummaging through papers and disdainfully ignored him, — allow me to clarify on my part that I have been living with her for about three years, since my arrival from the province and before... before... however, why shouldn't I admit it in turn, from the very beginning I promised that I would marry her daughter, a verbal promise, completely free... She was a girl... however, I even liked her... although I wasn't in love... in a word, youth, that is, I mean to say that my landlady used to give me a lot of credit then and I led a somewhat such life... I was very frivolous...
— You are not required to disclose such intimacies at all, sir, and there's no time, — Ilya Petrovich interrupted rudely and triumphantly, but Raskolnikov stopped him with heat, although it suddenly became extremely difficult for him to speak. — But allow me, let me tell you partly... what happened and... in turn... though this is superfluous, I agree with you, to tell... a year ago this girl died of typhus, while I remained a tenant as I was, and the landlady, when she moved to her current apartment, told me... and said friendly... that she completely trusted me and all... but wouldn't I like to give her this loan letter for one hundred and fifteen rubles, everything she considered my debt. Please: she specifically said that as soon as I gave this paper, she would lend me credit again as much as she liked and that never, never, in turn — these were her own words — she wouldn't use this paper until I paid up myself... And now, when I have lost my lessons and I have nothing to eat, she is suing for collection... What can I say now? — All these sensitive details, sir, do not concern us, — Ilya Petrovich said brazenly, — you must give a response and an obligation, but that you happened to be in love and all these tragic parts, we don't care about it at all.
