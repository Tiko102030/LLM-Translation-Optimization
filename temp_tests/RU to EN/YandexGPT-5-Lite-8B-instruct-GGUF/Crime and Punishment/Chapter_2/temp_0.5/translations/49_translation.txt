He went to the Neva along V Prospect; but on the way, another thought suddenly occurred to him: “Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to go somewhere very far away, again, perhaps to the Islands, and there, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush — bury all this and maybe notice the tree?” And although he felt that he was not able to discuss everything clearly and sensibly at that moment, the thought seemed unmistakable to him. But he was not destined to get to the Islands either, something else happened: as he was leaving V Prospect onto the square, he suddenly saw on his left the entrance to a courtyard lined with completely blank walls. On the right, immediately upon entering the gate, a blank unpainted wall of a neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. On the left, parallel to the blank wall and also just from the gates, there was a wooden fence that extended about twenty steps deep into the yard and then made a bend to the left. It was a blank fenced-off place where some materials lay. Further in the recess of the yard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop.
