 He took a breath more freely. "Perhaps it's not right!" Little by little he began to take heart, he tried with all his might to cheer up and come to his senses. "Some stupidity, some carelessness, the slightest one, and I can betray myself completely! Hmm… it's a pity there's no air here," he added, "it's stuffy… My head is even more dizzy… my mind too…"

He felt terrible disorder in himself. He was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to latch onto something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The clerk greatly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, to figure him out. This was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark and lively physiognomy that looked older than his age, dressed fashionably and smartly, with a parting in the back of his head, combed and groomed, with many rings on his white brush-cleaned fingers and gold chains on his waistcoat. He even said a word or two in French to one foreigner who was there, and very satisfactorily. "Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down," he said casually to the smartly dressed crimson lady, who kept standing as if she didn't dare to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
