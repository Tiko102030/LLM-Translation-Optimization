 — Don't bother shouting!  
— I'm not shouting, but speaking quite evenly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant got so angry that for a moment he couldn't say anything, and only some spluttering came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat.  
— Be so kind as to shut up! You are in the presence. Don't be rude, sir!  
— And you are in the presence too, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides, while shouting, you smoke a cigarette, therefore you are showing disrespect to all of us. Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an unexpressed pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
