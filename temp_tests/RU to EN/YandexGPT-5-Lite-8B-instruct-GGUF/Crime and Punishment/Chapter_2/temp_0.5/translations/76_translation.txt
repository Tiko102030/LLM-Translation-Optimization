 But at that moment the door swung open again, and bending down a little because he was tall, Razumikhin entered. “What a sea cabin,” he shouted as he came in, “I always bang my forehead; this is also called an apartment! And have you come to your senses, brother?” “I just came to,” said Nastasya. “He’s just come to,” the foreman chimed in again with a smile. “And who might you be, sir?” Razumikhin asked, suddenly addressing him. “You see, I am Vrazumikhin; not Razumikhin, as everyone calls me, but Vrazumikhin, a student, a nobleman’s son, and he is my friend. Well, who are you then?”
