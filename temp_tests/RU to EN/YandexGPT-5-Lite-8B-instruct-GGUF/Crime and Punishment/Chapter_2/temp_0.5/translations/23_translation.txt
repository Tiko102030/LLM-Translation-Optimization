 The stout lady jumped up from her seat at sight of him and began to curtsy with some special delight; but the officer paid no attention to her, and she no longer dared to sit down in front of him. This was a lieutenant, an assistant to the district constable, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and extremely fine facial features that did not express anything special, except for some impudence. He glanced askance at Raskolnikov and partly indignantly: his costume was too bad, and despite all the humiliation, his posture was still not suited to his clothes; Raskolnikov looked at him too straight and long out of carelessness, so much so that the officer was even offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin did not think to hide from his lightning glance. "Summoned... on a summons..." Raskolnikov answered somehow. "It's about the case of collecting money from the student," the clerk hurriedly said, tearing himself away from the paper. — Here you are! — and he threw the notebook to Raskolnikov, pointing to the place in it — read it!
