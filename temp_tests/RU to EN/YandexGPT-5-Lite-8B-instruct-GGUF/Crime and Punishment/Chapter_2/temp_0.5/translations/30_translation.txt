As for the curvaceous lady, at first she trembled so much from thunder and lightning; but strangely enough: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the lovelier her appearance became, the more charming her smile directed towards the formidable lieutenant grew. She minced on the spot and squatted continuously, impatiently waiting for the chance to finally get a word in edgewise, and she got it. “I didn’t have any noise or fights, Mr. Captain,” she suddenly started chattering away like spilled peas, with a strong German accent, though briskly in Russian, “and no scandals at all, but they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, it’s not my fault… I have a noble house, Mr. Captain, and noble treatment, Mr. Captain, and I always, always didn’t want any scandal. And they came completely drunk and then asked for three drinks again, and then one lifted his feet and started playing the piano with them, and that’s not good at all in a noble house, and he broke the piano entirely, and there’s absolutely no manners here at all, and I said so. And he took a stick and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the дворник and Karl came, he took Karl and beat his eye, and Henriette also got her eye beaten, and they hit my cheek five times. And that’s so indelicate in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I was screaming. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood at the window, like a little pig, squealing; and it's shameful.”
