But here's the beer! — he moved to his chair, pushed the soup and beef toward him and began to eat with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. “I, brother Rodya, have lunch like this every day now,” he muttered, as much as his mouth full of beef would allow, “and it’s all Pashenka, your little hostess, who is in charge, honoring me with all her heart. I certainly don't insist, well, I don't object either. And here comes Nastasia with tea. She's so quick! Nastenka, do you want some beer?” “—And, go to the mischief!” “—How about some tea?”
