 He would just spit on someone, bite it seems if anyone talked to him…

He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the embankment of the Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. “That’s where he lives, in this house,” he thought. “What is this, I just came to Razumikhin myself! Again the same story as then… But it’s very curious, however: did I come myself or was I just walking and came here? It doesn’t matter; I said... the other day... that I would go to him the next day after that, well, what of it, I will go! As if I couldn’t come now…”

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his tiny room, and was studying at that moment, writing, and opened the door for him himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his worn-out robe, tattered to rags, with barefoot slippers, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise expressed itself on his face.
