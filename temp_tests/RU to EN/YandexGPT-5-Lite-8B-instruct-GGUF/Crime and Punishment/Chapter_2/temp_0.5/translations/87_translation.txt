 — Shall we have some tea?  
— I would like some, please.  
— Pour it. Wait, I'll pour it for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately gave orders, poured it out, then poured another cup, abandoned his breakfast and sat back on the sofa. As before, he put his left hand around the sick man's head, lifted him up and began to feed him tea with a teaspoon, again blowing continuously and especially hard on the spoon, as if this process of blowing was the most important and salutary point of recovery. Raskolnikov was silent and did not resist, despite the fact that he felt quite strong enough to lift himself and sit on the sofa without any посторонней help, and not only to control his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but even perhaps to walk. But for some strange, almost animal cunning, it suddenly occurred to him to hide his strength until the time came, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, that he was not yet fully understanding, and at the same time to listen and find out what was going on here? However, he could not cope with his disgust: after gulping down about ten spoonfuls of tea, he suddenly freed his head, капризно pushed away the spoon and fell back onto the pillow.
