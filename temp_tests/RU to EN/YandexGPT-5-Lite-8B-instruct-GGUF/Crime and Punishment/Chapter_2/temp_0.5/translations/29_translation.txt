 — And you, this and that and the other thing, — he shouted all at once with might and main (the mourning lady had already left), — what happened to you last night? Eh? Shame again, making a racket in the street. Fighting and drinking again. You're dreaming of being put in a straitjacket! After all, I told you, I warned you ten times that the eleventh time I wouldn't let it slide! And you again, again, you this and that and the other thing! Even Raskolnikov's paper fell out of his hands, and he was staring wildly at the lush lady, who was being so unceremoniously dealt with; but soon, however, he understood what was going on, and immediately he began to like this whole story very much. He listened with pleasure, so much so that he wanted to laugh, laugh, laugh... All his nerves were jumping. — Ilya Petrovich!
