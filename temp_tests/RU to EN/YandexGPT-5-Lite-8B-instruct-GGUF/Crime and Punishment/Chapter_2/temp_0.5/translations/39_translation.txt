 The clerk began to dictate to him the form of a reply usual in such cases, that is, "I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, etc." — "But you can't write, your pen is falling out of your hand," observed the clerk, peering curiously at Raskolnikov. "Are you ill?" — "Yes... my head is spinning... go on!" — "That's all; sign your name." The clerk took back the paper and turned to others. Raskolnikov gave up the pen, but instead of getting up and leaving, he rested both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It was as if a nail were being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly occurred to him: to stand up right now, go to Nikodim Fomich and tell him everything from yesterday, every detail, then go to the apartment together and show them the things in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already got up to carry it out.
