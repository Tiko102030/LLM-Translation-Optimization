 A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it. The wallet ended up at the very top, and still there was space left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one turn to its original side, and it just fit back into its original place, perhaps a little, slightly higher. But he raked up some earth and pressed it down at the edges with his foot. Nothing could be noticed. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again, intense, almost unbearable joy, as before in the office, seized him for a moment. “The ends are hidden! And who, who would think of looking under this stone? It has been here, perhaps since the house was built, and will lie there for just as long.”
