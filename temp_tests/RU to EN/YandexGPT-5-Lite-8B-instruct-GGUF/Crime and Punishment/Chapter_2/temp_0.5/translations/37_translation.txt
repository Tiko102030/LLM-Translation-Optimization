 — All these sensitive details, my lord, do not concern us, — Ilya Petrovich bluntly cut in, — you must give a feedback and an undertaking, and what you happened to be in love with and all these tragic places, we don't care about that at all.  
— Well, you... are cruel... — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also starting to sign. He felt somehow ashamed.  
— Write then, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov.  
— What to write? — he asked somewhat грубо (roughly).  
— And I will dictate to you. It seemed to Raskolnikov that the clerk had become more negligent and contemptuous towards him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly became completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened somehow in one moment, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could have spoken to them a minute ago, and even imposed himself with his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
