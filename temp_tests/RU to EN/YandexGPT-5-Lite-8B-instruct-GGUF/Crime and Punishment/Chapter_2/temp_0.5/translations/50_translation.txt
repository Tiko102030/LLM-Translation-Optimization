 Further, in a recess of the yard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, carriage or locksmith, or something like that; almost from the very gate, a lot of coal dust was visible everywhere. “That’s where I would throw it all and leave!” — he suddenly thought. Not noticing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gate and immediately saw, right near the gate, a trough attached to the fence (as is often done in such houses where there are many factory workers, artel members, cab drivers, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, was written in chalk the always-present witticism in such cases: “Stopping of carts is prohibited here.” Therefore, it’s already good that there’s no suspicion that he came in and stopped. “I could throw everything all at once into a heap somewhere and leave!”

After looking around again, he had already stuck his hand in his pocket when suddenly he noticed a large unhewn stone near the outer wall, between the gate and the trough, where the entire distance was about an arshin wide (about half a meter), adjacent directly to the stone street wall. Beyond this wall was the street, the sidewalk, and you could hear passers-by снующих around, of which there were always many; but no one could see him behind the gate, unless someone came from the street, which, however, could very well happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top tightly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. Under the stone there was a small recess; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it.
