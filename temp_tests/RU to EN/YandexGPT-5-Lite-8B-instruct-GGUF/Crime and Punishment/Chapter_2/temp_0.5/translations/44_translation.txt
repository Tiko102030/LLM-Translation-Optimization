 — And where, if I may ask?  
— Down the street.  
— Short and clear. Raskolnikov replied sharply, abruptly, all pale as a handkerchief and without lowering his black inflamed eyes in front of Ilya Petrovich's gaze.  
— He can barely stand on his feet, but you... Nikodim Fomich noted.  
— Nothing! — said Ilya Petrovich somehow especially. Nikodim Fomich was about to add something else, but after glancing at the clerk, who was also looking at him very intently, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly became quiet. It was strange.
