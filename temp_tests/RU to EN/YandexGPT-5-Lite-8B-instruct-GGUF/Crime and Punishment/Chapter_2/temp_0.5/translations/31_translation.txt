 And he opened the window onto the canal and stuck his head out like a little pig, squealing; and that's shameful. And how can one stick one's head out of the window onto the street, like a little pig, squealing; and that's shameful. Fie-fie-fie! And Karl was pulling him by his tailcoat from the window, and here, it's true, Mr. Captain, they tore his clothes. And then he shouted that he had to pay a fine of fifteen silver coins. And I myself, Mr. Captain, paid five silver coins for his clothes. And this is an ungracious guest, Mr. Captain, who makes all the scandals! I will gather great satire about you, I say, because I can write anything about you in all the newspapers. — From writers, then? — Yes, Mr. Captain, and what kind of graceless guest is this, Mr. Captain, when he comes to a noble house…
