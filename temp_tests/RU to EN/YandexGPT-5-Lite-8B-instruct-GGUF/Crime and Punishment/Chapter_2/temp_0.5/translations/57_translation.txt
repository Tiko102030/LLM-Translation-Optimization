 “— Don't надо,” he said, “I came… here’s what: I have no lessons... I was about to… however, I don’t need any lessons at all…”
“— But you know what? You're delirious!” — Razumikhin, who was observing him closely, remarked. 
“No, I’m not delirious…” Raskolnikov got up from the sofa. Rising towards Razumikhin, he had not thought that he would have to face him, face to face. Now, in an instant, he guessed, already by experience, that at this moment he was least disposed to come face to face with anyone in the whole world. All his bile rose in him. He nearly choked with anger at himself as soon as he crossed Razumikhin's threshold. 
“— Goodbye!” — he said suddenly and went to the door.”
