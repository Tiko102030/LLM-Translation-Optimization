 He did not answer and was holding a piece of paper in his hands without opening it. “Don’t even get up,” Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. “If you’re sick, then don’t go: you won’t burn. What do you have there in your hands?” He looked: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he recalled that when half-awake in the heat, he would tightly clench all this in his hand and fall asleep again. “Look at him, collected some rags and sleeps with them as if they were treasure…” And Nastasya burst into her painfully nervous laughter. He instantly shoved everything under his overcoat and stared intently at her. Although at that moment he could very little think clearly, he felt that people would not treat a person the same way when they came to take him.
