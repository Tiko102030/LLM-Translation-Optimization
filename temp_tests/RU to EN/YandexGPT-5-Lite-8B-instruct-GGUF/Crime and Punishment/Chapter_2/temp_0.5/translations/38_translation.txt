If they even sentenced him to be burned at that moment, he would not have moved, he probably wouldn't even listen to the sentence carefully. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden and never experienced before was happening to him. Not that he understood, but he clearly felt, with all the power of sensation, that not only with sensitive expansivenesses, as before, but even with anything else it was no longer possible for him to turn to these people, in the police station, and if they were all his own brothers and sisters, and not police lieutenants, then he would have absolutely no reason to address them and even under no circumstances of life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible sensation before. And what was most tormenting is that it was more of a sensation than consciousness, than concept; an immediate sensation, the most painful sensation of all those he had experienced in his life so far.
