 "That's not it at all!.."

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question knocked him off balance and surprised him bitterly:

"If indeed all this was done consciously and not stupidly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how is it that you have not even glanced into the wallet so far and do not know what you got, why you suffered so much and deliberately went to such a vile, nasty, low deed? You wanted to throw it into the water right now, the wallet, along with all the things you also haven't seen yet... How is that?"

Yes, this is so; this is all so. He knew this, however, even before, and this is not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided to throw it into the water at night, it was done without any hesitation or objection, as if that's how it should be, as if it couldn't be otherwise... Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very minute when he was sitting over the chest and dragging out the cases from it... And yet!.. "This is because I am very ill," he decided sullenly in the end, "I have tormented and tortured myself, and do not know what I am doing... Both yesterday and the day before yesterday, and all this time I have been torturing myself... I will get well and... will not torture myself anymore... But what if I don't recover at all? Lord! How sick I am of all this!.." He walked without stopping. He terribly wanted to somehow distract himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to undertake. A new, irresistible sensation took hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical disgust for everything that met and surrounded him, stubborn, spiteful, hateful. Everyone who met him was disgusting to him — their faces, gait, movements were disgusting to him. He would have spat on someone, he would have bitten them, it seems, if anyone had spoken to him...
