 “If asked, perhaps I will say,” he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst from him. It had just moved to a new address, to a new building, to the fourth floor. He had been to the old location once long ago and only briefly. Entering through the gate, he saw to the right a staircase down which a peasant was going with a book in his hands: “A caretaker, then; this must be the office,” and he began to go upstairs at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. “I will enter, kneel, and tell them everything...” he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and covered in slops. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and remained there almost all day. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
