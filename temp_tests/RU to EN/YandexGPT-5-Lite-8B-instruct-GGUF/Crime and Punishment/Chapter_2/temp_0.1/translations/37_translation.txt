 — All these sensitive details, my lord, do not concern us, — Ilya Petrovich rudely cut in, — you must give a feedback and an undertaking, and what you happened to be in love with and all these tragic parts are none of our business. — Well, that’s harsh... — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also signing. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write then, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What to write? — he asked rather грубо. — And I will dictate to you. It seemed to Raskolnikov that the clerk began to be more negligent and contemptuous with him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly became completely indifferent to anyone’s opinion, and this change happened somehow in one moment, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would have been surprised at how he could talk to them like that a minute ago, and even impose himself with his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
