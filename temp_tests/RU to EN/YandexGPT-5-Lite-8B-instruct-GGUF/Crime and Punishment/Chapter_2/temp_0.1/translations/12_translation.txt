 He did not answer and was holding paper in his hands without opening it. “Well, don’t get up,” Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. “If you're sick, then don't go: you won't burn. What do you have there in your hands?” He looked: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he recalled that when half-awake from the fever, he would clench all this tightly in his hand and fall asleep again. “Look at him, he’s collected some rags and sleeps with them, just like with treasure...” And Nastasya burst into her painfully nervous laughter. He instantly shoved everything under his overcoat and stared intently at her. Although he could not fully comprehend it that minute, he felt that they would treat a person differently when they came to take him.
