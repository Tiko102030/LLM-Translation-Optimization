 And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with police officers but with his very best friends, then even then, it seems, he would not have a single human word for them, their hearts had grown so empty all of a sudden. A gloomy feeling of painful, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously revealed itself to his soul. It was not the meanness of his heartfelt outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, it was not the meanness and Pouchikov's triumph over him that had suddenly turned his heart like that. Oh, what does he care now about his own meanness, about all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, reprimands, offices, etc., etc.! If they even sentenced him to be burned at that moment, he would not have moved, he probably wouldn't even listen to the sentence carefully. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden and never experienced before was happening to him. Not that he understood, but he clearly felt, with all the power of sensation, that not only with sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else it was no longer possible for him to turn to these people in the police station, and if they were all his own brothers and sisters, and not police lieutenants, then there would be absolutely no need for him to turn to them under any circumstances; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible sensation before this moment. And what was most painful — it was more of a feeling than consciousness, than an idea; a direct feeling, the most painful feeling of all those he had experienced in his life so far. The clerk began to dictate to him the form of a response ordinary in such cases, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, etc.
