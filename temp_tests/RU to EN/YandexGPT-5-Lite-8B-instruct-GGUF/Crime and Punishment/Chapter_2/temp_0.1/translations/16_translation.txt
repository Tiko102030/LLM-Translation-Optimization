 His head was dizzy and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with a trick and then knock me down at full speed," he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might blurt out some stupidity..."

On the stairs, he remembered that he was leaving all his things like that, in the wallpaper hole — "and they probably arranged a search without him here," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if I can say, cynicism of doom suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand and went on. "I just want it to be over поскорее!.."

Outside, the heat was unbearable again; there hadn't been a drop of rain in all those days. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, so much so that it hurt to look and his head became completely dizzy — an ordinary sensation for a feverish person stepping out onto the street on a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn to yesterday's street, he looked at it with excruciating anxiety, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
