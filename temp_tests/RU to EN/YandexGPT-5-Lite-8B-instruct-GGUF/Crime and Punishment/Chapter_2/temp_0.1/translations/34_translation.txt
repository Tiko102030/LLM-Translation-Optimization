 Louise Ivanovna began curtseying in all directions with hasty courtesy and, curtsying, backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into a prominent officer from behind, with an open fresh face and excellent thick blond sideburns. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the quarter master. Louise Ivanovna hurried to curtsy almost to the floor and flew out of the office in quick small steps, bouncing.  
“Thunder again, thunder and lightning again, tornado, hurricane!” — Nikodim Fomich addressed Ilya Petrovich affably and friendly, — “again they stirred up my heart, again I boiled over! I heard it from the stairs.”  
“Yes what!” — said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and even not “what”, but somehow: “Yeah, whaaat!”), moving to another table with some papers and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step; — here you see, please look: the gentleman is a writer, that is, a student, former, that is, does not pay money, has given bills, does not clear out the apartment, complaints are constantly received about them, and you took offense when I lit a cigarette in front of them! They themselves commit fraud, but here, please look at them: this is their most attractive appearance now!”  
“Poverty is not a vice, my friend, well, what can we do!”
