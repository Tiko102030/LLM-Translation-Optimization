 He would just spit on someone, bite it seems if anyone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he stepped onto the embankment of the Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. “This is where he lives, in this house,” he thought. “What’s this, I came to Razumikhin by myself! Again the same story as then… But it’s very curious, however: did I come here myself or was I just walking and came here? It doesn't matter; I said... the other day... that I would go to him the next day after that, well, what of it, I will go! As if I couldn’t come now…”

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was home, in his tiny room, and at that moment he was busy writing, and opened the door for him himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his worn-out robe, tattered to rags, with bare feet in slippers, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was expressed on his face.
