 He would just spit on someone, bite it seems, if anyone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he stepped onto the embankment of the Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. “This is where he lives, in this house,” he thought. “What’s this, I’ve come to Razumikhin by myself! Again the same story as then… But it’s very curious, however: have I really come by myself or was I just walking and came here? It doesn’t matter; I said... the other day... that I would go to him the next day after that, well, what of it, I will! As if I couldn’t come now…”

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his tiny room, and was busy writing at that moment, and opened the door for him himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his shabby robe, tattered to rags, with barefoot slippers on his feet, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was expressed on his face.
