 — Don't bother shouting!  
— I'm not shouting, but speaking quite evenly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I'm a student and I won't allow myself to be shouted at.  
The assistant got so angry that for a moment he couldn't say anything, only some spluttering came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat.  
— Be s-s-silent! You are in the presence. Don't b-b-be rude, sir!  
— And you are in the presence too, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides, while shouting, you smoke a cigarette, therefore you are showing disrespect to all of us.  
Having said that, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
