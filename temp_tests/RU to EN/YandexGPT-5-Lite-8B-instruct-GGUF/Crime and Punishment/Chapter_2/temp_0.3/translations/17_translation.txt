 “If asked, I might say,” he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away from him. It had just moved to a new address, a new building, on the fourth floor. He had been there briefly once before, but it was a long time ago. Having entered through the gate, he saw a staircase to his right, down which a peasant was going with a book in his hands: “A janitor, then; this must be the office,” and he began to go upstairs at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. “I will enter, kneel down and tell them everything...” he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and covered in slops. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood there almost the whole day. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
