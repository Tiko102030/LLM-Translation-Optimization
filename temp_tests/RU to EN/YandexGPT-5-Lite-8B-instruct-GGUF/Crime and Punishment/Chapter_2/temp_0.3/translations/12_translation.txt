 He did not answer and was holding paper in his hands without opening it. “Well, don’t get up,” Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. “If you are sick, then stay home: you won’t burn down. What do you have there in your hands?” He glanced: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on it, he recalled that when half-awake from the fever, he would clench all this tightly in his hand and fall asleep again. “Look at him, gathering such rags and sleeping with them, as if they were treasure...” And Nastasya burst into her painfully nervous laughter. He instantly shoved everything under his overcoat and stared intently at her. Although he could not fully think clearly at that moment, he felt that people would treat a person differently when they came to take him.
