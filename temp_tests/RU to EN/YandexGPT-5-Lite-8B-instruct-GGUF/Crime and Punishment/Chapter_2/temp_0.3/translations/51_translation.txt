 A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it. The wallet ended up at the very top, and yet there was still room in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, перевернул it with one turn to its former side, and it just fell into its previous place, perhaps a little higher, barely noticeable. But he raked up some earth and pressed it down at the edges with his foot. Nothing could be seen. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again a strong, hardly bearable joy, as before in the office, seized him for a moment. “The ends are hidden! And who, who would think of looking under this stone? It has been here perhaps since the house was built and will lie there for just as long.”
