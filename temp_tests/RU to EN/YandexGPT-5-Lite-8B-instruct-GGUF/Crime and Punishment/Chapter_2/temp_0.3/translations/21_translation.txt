 He took a breath more freely. "Perhaps it's not right!" Little by little he began to take heart; he tried with all his might to cheer up and come to his senses. "Some stupidity, some minor carelessness, and I can betray myself completely! Hmm... it's a pity there's no air here," he added, "it's stuffy... My head is even more dizzy... my mind too..."

He felt terrible disorder all over him. He himself was afraid of not controlling himself. He tried to latch onto something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The clerk greatly interested him, though: he always wanted to guess something from his face, to figure him out. This was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark complexion and an animated physiognomy that looked older than his age, dressed fashionably and foppishly, with a parting in the back, combed and groomed, with many rings on his white brush-cleaned fingers and gold chains on his waistcoat. He even said a word or two in French with one foreigner present, and very satisfactorily. "Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down," he said casually to the smartly dressed crimson lady who was standing there, as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
