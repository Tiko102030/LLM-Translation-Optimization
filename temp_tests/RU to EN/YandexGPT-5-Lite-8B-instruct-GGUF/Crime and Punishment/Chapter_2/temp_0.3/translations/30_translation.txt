 — Ilya Petrovich! — the clerk began solicitously, but stopped to wait for a while, because an enraged lieutenant could not be restrained any other way than by the arms, as he knew from his own experience. As for the curvaceous lady, at first she trembled so much from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more любезнее her appearance became, the more charming her smile turned towards the formidable lieutenant. She minced on the spot and squatted continuously, impatiently waiting for them to finally let her have her say, and she waited. “I didn’t make any noise or fighting, Mr. Captain,” she suddenly chattered away like spilled peas, with a strong German accent, albeit smartly in Russian, “and there was no scandal at all, but they came drunk, and I’ll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, it’s not my fault… I have a noble house, Mr. Captain, and noble treatment, Mr. Captain, and I always, always didn’t want any scandal. And they came completely drunk and then asked for three drinks again, and then one lifted his feet and started playing the piano with them, and that’s not good at all in a noble house, and he broke the piano completely, and there’s no manners here at all, and I said so. And he took a stick and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the дворник and Karl came, he took Karl and gave him a black eye, and Henriette also got a black eye, and they hit me five times on the cheek. And that’s so indelicate in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I screamed. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood at the window, like a little pig, squealing; and that’s shameful.”
