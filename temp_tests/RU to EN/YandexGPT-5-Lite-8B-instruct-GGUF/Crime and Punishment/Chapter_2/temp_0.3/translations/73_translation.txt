 He remembered only taking a sip of cold water and spilling it from the mug onto his chest. Then unconsciousness set in. III

He, however, was not completely unconscious throughout the entire time of his illness: it was a feverish state, with delirium and semi-consciousness. He recalled many things later. Sometimes it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him somewhere and carry him out, they argued and quarreled a lot about him. Then suddenly he was alone in the room, everyone had left and were afraid of him, and only occasionally would they slightly open the door to look at him, threaten him, conspire among themselves, laugh and tease him. He often remembered Nastasya beside him; he also distinguished another person who seemed very familiar to him, but he could not guess who it was and yearned for this, even cried. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times — that the same day was going on. But about that — he completely forgot about that; however, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that one could not forget — he was tormented, suffered, trying to remember, moaned, fell into бешенство or into terrible, unbearable fear. Then he would lunge from his place, wanted to run, but always someone would stop him by force, and he would again fall into powerlessness and unconsciousness.
