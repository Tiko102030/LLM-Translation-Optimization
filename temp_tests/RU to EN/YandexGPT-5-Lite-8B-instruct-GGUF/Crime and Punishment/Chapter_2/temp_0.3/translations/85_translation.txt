Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved over to him on the sofa, awkwardly like a bear, put his left arm around his head, even though he could have lifted himself up, and with his right hand he brought a spoonful of soup to his mouth, having blown on it several times first so that he wouldn't burn himself. But the soup was only lukewarm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoonful, then another, then a third. But after serving up a few more spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that they should consult Zosimov about further action. Nastasya entered carrying two bottles of beer. “Do you want some tea?” “I do.” “Go and get some tea quickly, Nastasya, because I guess we can manage without consulting the faculty regarding tea.”
