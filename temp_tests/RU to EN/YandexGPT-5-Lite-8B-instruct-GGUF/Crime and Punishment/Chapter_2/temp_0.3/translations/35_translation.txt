 — Poverty is not a vice, my friend, well, what of it! After all, gunpowder couldn't stand the insult. You must have been offended by him in some way and lost your temper yourself, — Nikodim Fomich continued, addressing Raskolnikov любезно, — but you were wrong to do that: I tell you, he's a most noble person, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! And everything passed! And in the end there's only the gold of his heart! They even called him "Lieutenant Gunpowder" in the regiment...
— And what a fine regiment it was! — exclaimed Ilya Petrovich, quite pleased to be tickled so pleasantly, but still playful. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to them all. — But please, captain, — he began quite развязно, turning suddenly to Nikodim Fomich, — understand my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I neglected anything on my part.
