 Louise Ivanovna began curtseying in all directions with hasty courtesy and, while curtsying, backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into a prominent officer from behind, with an open fresh face and excellent thick blond sideburns. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the quarter master. Louise Ivanovna hurried to curtsy almost to the floor and flew out of the office in quick small steps, bouncing. "Thunder again, thunder and lightning again, tornado, hurricane!" — Nikodim Fomich addressed Ilya Petrovich любезно and friendly, — "again they've stirred up my heart, I'm boiling again! Heard it from the stairs." "Yes what!" — said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and even not "shto", but somehow: "Da-a shtaa!"), moving to another table with some papers and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he steps, there goes his shoulder; — here you see, please look: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, former, that is, doesn't pay money, gave bills, doesn't clear out the apartment, complaints come in on them continuously, and you took offense when I lit a cigarette in their presence! They themselves are cheating, but here, please take a look at them: this is their most attractive appearance now!" "Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, what of it!"
