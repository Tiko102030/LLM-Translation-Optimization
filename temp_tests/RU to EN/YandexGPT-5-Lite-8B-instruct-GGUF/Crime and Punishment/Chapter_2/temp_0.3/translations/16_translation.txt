 My head was dizzy and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me down at full speed," he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might blurt out some stupidity..."

On the stairs, he remembered that he was leaving all his things like that, in the wallpaper hole — "and they probably arranged a search without him here," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say, cynicism of doom suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand and went on. "I just want it to be over поскорее!.."

Outside, the heat was unbearable again; there hadn't been a drop of rain in all these days. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, so much so that it hurt to look and his head became completely dizzy — the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly going outside on a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked at it with excruciating anxiety, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, perhaps I will say," he thought, approaching the office.
