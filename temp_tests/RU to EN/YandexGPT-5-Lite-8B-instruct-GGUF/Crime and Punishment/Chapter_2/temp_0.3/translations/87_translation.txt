 — Shall we have some tea?  
— Some tea would be nice.  
— Pour it. Wait, I'll pour for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately ordered, poured, then poured another cup, abandoned his breakfast and moved back to the sofa. As before, he put his left hand on the sick man's head, lifted him up and began to feed him with tea from a teaspoon, again blowing continuously and especially hard on the spoon, as if in this process of blowing was the most important and salutary point of recovery. Raskolnikov remained silent and did not resist, despite the fact that he felt quite strong enough to lift himself and sit on the sofa without any outside help, and not only to control his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but even perhaps to walk. But for some strange, almost animal cunning, it suddenly occurred to him to hide his strength until the time came, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, that he was not yet fully understanding, and at the same time to listen and find out what was going on here? However, he could not cope with his disgust: after gulping down about ten spoonfuls of tea, he suddenly freed his head, капризно pushed away the spoon and rolled back onto the pillow. There were now real pillows under his heads — feather ones with clean pillowcases; he also noticed that and took it into consideration.
