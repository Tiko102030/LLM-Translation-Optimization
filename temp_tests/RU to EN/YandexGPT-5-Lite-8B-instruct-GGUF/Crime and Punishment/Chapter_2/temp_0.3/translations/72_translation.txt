 He was looking at her, hardly breathing. “I heard it myself… I wasn’t sleeping… I was sitting,” he said even more timidly. “I listened for a long time… The warden's assistant came... Everyone rushed to the stairs, from all the apartments…”

“No one came. And that blood is crying out in you. It starts to seem when there’s no way out and your insides start to bake, that’s when it begins. Are you going to eat something?” He didn’t answer. Nastasya stood over him, stared at him intently and did not leave. “Give me some water to drink… Nastasya.” She went downstairs and returned in a couple of minutes with water in a white earthenware mug; but he no longer remembered what happened next. He only remembered taking a sip of cold water and spilling it from the mug onto his chest.
