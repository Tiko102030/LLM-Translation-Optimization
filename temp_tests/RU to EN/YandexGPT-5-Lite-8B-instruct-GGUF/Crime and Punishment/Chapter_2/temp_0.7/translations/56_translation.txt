 Surprise was expressed on his face. "What's up with you?" he shouted, examining his comrade from head to toe; then he paused and whistled. "Is it really that bad? You, brother, have outdone our brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's rags. "Well, sit down, you must be tired!" — and when the latter collapsed onto the oilcloth Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was sick. "You're seriously ill, you know that?" He began to feel for his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. "No need," he said, "I came… here's what: I have no lessons… I was going to… however, I don't really need lessons…"
