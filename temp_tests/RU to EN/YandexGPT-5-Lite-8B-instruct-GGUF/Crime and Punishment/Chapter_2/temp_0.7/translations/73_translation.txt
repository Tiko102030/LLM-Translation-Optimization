 He remembered only how he took a sip of cold water and spilled it from the mug onto his chest. Then unconsciousness set in. III

He, however, was not completely unconscious throughout the whole time of his illness: it was a feverish state, with delirium and semi-consciousness. He later remembered many things. Sometimes it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him somewhere and carry him out, they argued and quarreled a lot about him. Suddenly he was alone in the room, everyone had left and were afraid of him, and only occasionally would they slightly open the door to look at him, threaten him, conspire among themselves, laugh and tease him. He often remembered Nastasya beside him; he also distinguished another person who seemed very familiar to him, but who it was — he could not guess and missed it, even cried. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times — that the same day was going on. But about that — he completely forgot about that; however, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that one should not forget — he was tormented, suffered, trying to remember, moaned, fell into rage or into terrible, unbearable fear. Then he would lunge from his place, wanted to run, but someone always stopped him by force, and he would fall back into helplessness and unconsciousness.
