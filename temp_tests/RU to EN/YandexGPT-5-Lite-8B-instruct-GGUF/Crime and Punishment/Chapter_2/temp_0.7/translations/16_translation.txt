 My head was dizzy and aching from the heat. “This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me off everything,” he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. “The bad thing is that I’m almost delirious... I might tell some stupid lie…”

On the stairs, he remembered that he was leaving all his things like that, in the wallpaper hole — “and they probably planned a search without him,” he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say, cynicism of doom suddenly overwhelmed him that he gave up and went further. “If only it would be quick!..”

On the street, the unbearable heat was there again; not a drop of rain in all these days. Dust, brick and lime again, stench from shops and taverns again, drunken people every now and then, Chukhontsa peddlers and half-collapsed cab drivers. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, so much so that it hurt to look and his head became completely dizzy — an ordinary sensation for someone with a fever, suddenly going out onto the street on a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn into yesterday’s street, he looked at it with painful anxiety, at that house… and immediately looked away. “If they ask, maybe I will say,” he thought, approaching the office.
