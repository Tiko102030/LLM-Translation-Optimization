 — Shall we have some tea?  
— Some tea would be nice.  
— Pour it. Wait, I'll pour it for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately ordered, poured, then poured another cup, abandoned his breakfast and moved back to the sofa. As before, he put his left hand on the patient's head, lifted him up and began to feed him tea with a teaspoon, again continuously and especially diligently blowing on the spoon, as if this process of blowing was the most important and salutary point of recovery. Raskolnikov remained silent and did not resist, despite the fact that he felt quite strong enough to rise and sit on the sofa without any outside help, and not only to control his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but even perhaps to walk. But for some strange, almost animal cunning, it suddenly occurred to him to hide his strength until the time came, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, that he did not even fully understand, and at the same time to listen and find out what was going on here? However, he could not overcome his aversion: after gulping down about ten spoons of tea, he suddenly freed his head, капризно pushed away the spoon and fell back onto the pillow. There were now real pillows under his heads — feather ones with clean pillowcases; he noticed that too and took it into consideration.
