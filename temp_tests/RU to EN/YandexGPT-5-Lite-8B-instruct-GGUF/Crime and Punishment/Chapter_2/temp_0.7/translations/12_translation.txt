 He did not respond and was holding paper in his hands without opening it. — “Oh, don’t get up,” Nastasya went on, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. — “If you’re sick, then stay put: nothing will burn down.” What do you have there in your hands? He glanced: in his right hand he had cut-off pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on it, he remembered that when half awake from the fever, he would tightly clench all this in his hand and then fall asleep again. — “Look at him collecting rags and sleeping with them as if they were treasure...” And Nastasya burst into her painfully neurotic laughter. He immediately shoved everything under his overcoat and stared intently at her. Although he could barely think clearly at that moment, he felt that they would not treat a person the same way when they came to take him.
