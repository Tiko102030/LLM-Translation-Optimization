 The clerk began to dictate the form of a usual reply in such cases to him, that is, “I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, etc.” — “But you can’t write, your pen is falling out of your hand,” observed the clerk, peering curiously at Raskolnikov. “Are you ill?” — “Yes…my head is spinning…go on!” — “That’s all; sign here.” The clerk took back the paper and turned to others. Raskolnikov gave up the pen, but instead of getting up and leaving, he rested both elbows on the table and gripped his head with his hands. As if a nail were being driven into his crown. A strange thought suddenly occurred to him: to get up now, approach Nikodim Fomich and tell him everything from yesterday, every detail, then go with them to the apartment and show them the things in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already gotten up to execute it.
