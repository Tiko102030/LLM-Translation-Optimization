 He breathed more freely. "Perhaps it's not right!" Little by little he began to take heart, he tried with all his might to cheer up and come to his senses. "Some stupidity, some slight неосторожности, and I can give myself away! Hmm... it's a pity there's no air here," he added, "it's stuffy… My head is even more dizzy… my mind too…"

He felt terrible disorder in himself. He was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to latch onto something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The clerk greatly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, to figure him out. He was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark and lively physiognomy that looked older than his age, dressed according to fashion and style, with a parting in the back of his head, combed and well-groomed, with many rings on his white brush-cleaned fingers and gold chains on his vest. He even said a couple of words in French with one foreigner present, and quite satisfactorily. "Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down," he said casually to the smartly dressed crimson-faced lady who was standing as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
