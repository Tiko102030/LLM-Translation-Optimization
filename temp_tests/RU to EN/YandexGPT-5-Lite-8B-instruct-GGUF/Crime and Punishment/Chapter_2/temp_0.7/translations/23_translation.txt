 The stout lady jumped up from her seat at sight of him and began to curtsy with some special delight; but the officer did not pay any attention to her, and she no longer dared to sit down in front of him. It was a lieutenant, an assistant district constable, with reddish-colored mustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and extremely fine facial features that expressed nothing remarkable, except for some impudence. He looked askance at Raskolnikov, somewhat indignantly: his costume was too ugly, and despite all the humiliation, his posture still did not match his clothes; Raskolnikov, through неосторожности, looked at him too directly and for a long time, so much so that the latter was even offended. 
“What do you want?” he shouted, probably wondering why such a ragamuffin does not shy away from his lightning-fast glance.
“Summoned… on the summons…” Raskolnikov answered somehow.
“This is about the claim for money from the student,” the clerk hurried to say, tearing himself away from the paper. “Here!” and he tossed a notebook to Raskolnikov, pointing to a place in it — read! “Money?”
