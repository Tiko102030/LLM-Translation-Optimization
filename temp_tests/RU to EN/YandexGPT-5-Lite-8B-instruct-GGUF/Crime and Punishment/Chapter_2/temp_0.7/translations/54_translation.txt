 “That’s not it at all!..”

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question knocked him off balance and surprised him bitterly:

“If indeed all this was done consciously and not stupidly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how is it that you haven’t even looked into the wallet yet and don’t know what you got, why you suffered so much and deliberately went to such a vile, гадкое, low deed? You wanted to throw it into the water right now, the wallet, along with all the things you also haven’t seen yet… How is that?”

Yes, it's like that; it's all like that. He, however, knew this before, and this is not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided to throw it into the water at night, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that’s how it should be, as if otherwise it’s impossible... Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; yes, perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at that very moment when he was sitting over the chest and dragging drawers out of it… But so!.. “This is because I am very ill,” he finally decided gloomily, “I have tormented and tortured myself, and I don’t know what I’m doing... Both yesterday and the day before yesterday, and all this time I’ve been tormenting myself… I will recover and… won’t torture myself… But what if I never recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of it all!..” He walked without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow dissipate himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to undertake. A new, irresistible sensation took hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical disgust for everything that met and surrounded him, stubborn, spiteful, hateful. He disliked all the people he met — their faces, gait, movements were disgusting to him. He would have spat on someone, bitten them, it seems, if anyone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he came to the embankment of the Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
