 Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt all broken up, his mind was with him. He feared being chased, he was afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps instructions to follow him would already be issued; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the traces for the time being. It was necessary to manage before there was still some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This had long been decided: “Throw everything into the ditch, and cover their tracks, and that's the end of it.” He had resolved this even in the night, in delirium, in those moments when, he remembered, he tried several times to get up and go: “quickly, quickly, and throw everything away.” But throwing things away proved very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Yekaterininsky Canal for half an hour or more, and several times looked at the accesses to the ditch where they met. But it was impossible to even think about carrying out the intention: either rafts stood right by the accesses and laundresses were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides of the embankments, from all sides, you could see, notice: it's suspicious that a person deliberately went down, stopped and throws something into the water. What if the cases don’t sink but float?
