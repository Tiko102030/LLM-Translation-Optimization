 “If asked, I might say,” he thought as he approached the office. The office was a quarter of a verst from him. It had just moved to a new address, to a new building, to the fourth floor. He had been there briefly once in the past, but it was a long time ago. Having entered through the gate, he saw to his right a staircase down which a peasant was going with a book in hand: “A janitor, then; this must be the office,” and he began to go up at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. “I will go in, kneel down and tell everything...” he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep and covered in slops. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood there almost all day long. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
