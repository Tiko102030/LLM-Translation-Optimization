Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved to sit beside him on the sofa, awkwardly like a bear, put his left arm around his head, even though he could have propped himself up himself, and with his right hand lifted a spoonful of soup to Raskolnikov's mouth, having blown on it several times first so that he wouldn't burn himself. But the soup was only lukewarm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoonful, then another, then a third. But after serving a few spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that further action should be discussed with Zosimov. Nastasya entered carrying two bottles of beer. “Do you want some tea?” “I do.” “Go and get some tea quickly, Nastasya, because regarding tea, it seems one can manage without consulting the faculty.”
