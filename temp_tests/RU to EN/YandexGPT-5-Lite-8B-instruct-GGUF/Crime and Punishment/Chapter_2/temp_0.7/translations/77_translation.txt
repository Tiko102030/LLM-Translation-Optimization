 Well, who are you? — I work as a foreman in our office for merchant Shelopaev and came here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat down on the other one, across the table. — You did well, brother, to come to your senses, — he continued, turning to Raskolnikov. — For four days you've barely eaten or drunk anything. In fact, we fed you tea with a spoonful. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense — some kind of blow to the head, perhaps. Some nervous nonsense, he says, the rations were poor, they gave too little beer and horseradish, that's why you got sick, but nothing, it will pass and grind itself down.
