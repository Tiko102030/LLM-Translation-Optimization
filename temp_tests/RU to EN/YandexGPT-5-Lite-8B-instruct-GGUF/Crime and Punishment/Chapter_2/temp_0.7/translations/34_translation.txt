 Louise Ivanovna began curtsying in all directions with hurried politeness and, curtsying, backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into a distinguished officer with an open fresh face and excellent thick blond sideburns. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the district supervisor. Louise Ivanovna hurried to curtsy almost to the floor and flew out of the office with frequent small steps, bouncing. 
“Thunder again, thunder and lightning again, tornado, hurricane!” — Nikodim Fomich addressed Ilya Petrovich in a friendly and amicable manner — “again they stirred up my heart, I boiled over again! I heard it from the stairs.”
“Yes what!” — said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and even not «shto», but somehow: «Da-a shta-a!»), moving to another table with some papers and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step; “here, please see for yourself: the gentleman is a writer, that is, a former student, doesn’t pay money, issued bills, doesn’t clear out the apartment, complaints are constantly filed against them, and you took it as a grievance that I lit a cigarette in front of them! They themselves are cheating, but here, please take a look at them: this is their most attractive appearance now!”
“Poverty is not a vice, my friend, well, whatever!”
