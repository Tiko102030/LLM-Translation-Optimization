 He would just spit on someone, bite it seems if anyone talked to him…

He suddenly stopped when he stepped onto the embankment of the Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. “This is where he lives, in this house,” he thought. “What is this? Why, I’ve come to Razumikhin myself! Again the same story as then… But it’s very curious, however: did I really come here myself or just walked and came here by chance? It doesn’t matter; I said... the other day... that I would go to him the next day after that, well, what of it, I will! As if I couldn’t come now…”

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was home, in his tiny room, and at that moment he was busy writing, and opened the door for him himself. It had been about four months since they had seen each other. Razumikhin was sitting in his shabby robe tattered to rags, with barefoot slippers, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was expressed on his face.
