 He was looking at her, barely breathing. “I heard it myself... I wasn't sleeping... I was sitting,” he said even more timidly. “I listened for a long time... The warden's assistant came... Everyone rushed to the stairs, from all the apartments...

“No one came. And this is your blood screaming. This happens when there's no way out and your insides start to bake, that's when it starts to seem... Are you going to eat, or what?” He didn’t answer. Nastasya still stood over him, stared intently at him and wouldn’t leave. “Give me something to drink... Nastasya.” She went downstairs and returned in a couple of minutes with water in a white clay mug; but he no longer remembered what happened next. He only remembered taking a sip of cold water and spilling it from the mug onto his chest.
