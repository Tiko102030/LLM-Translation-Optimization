 "If they ask, I might say," he thought as he approached the office. The office was a quarter of a verst from him. It had just moved to a new address, into a new building, to the fourth floor. He had only been to the old address once a long time ago, briefly. After going through the gate, he saw a staircase on his right, up which a peasant was walking with a book in his hands: "That must be the janitor; this must be the office," and he began to go upstairs by guesswork. He did not want to ask anyone about anything. "I'll go in, kneel down and tell them everything..." he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and covered in slop. All the kitchens from all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and remained there almost the whole day. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
