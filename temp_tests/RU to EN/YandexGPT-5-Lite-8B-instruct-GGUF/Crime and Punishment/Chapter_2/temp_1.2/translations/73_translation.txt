 He only remembered sipping one gulp of cold water and spilling it from the mug onto his chest. Then unconsciousness set in. III

He, however, was not completely unconscious throughout the whole time of his illness: it was a feverish state, with delirium and half-consciousness. He remembered a lot later. Sometimes it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him away and carry him somewhere, they argued about him a lot and quarreled. Suddenly he was alone in the room, everyone had left and were afraid of him, and only occasionally would they slightly open the door to look at him, threaten him, conspire among themselves, laugh and tease him. He often remembered Nastasya being beside him; he could make out another person as well, who seemed very familiar to him, but who exactly — he couldn't guess and was anguished about it, even cried. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times, that the same day was going on. But he completely forgot about that; however, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that he could not forget — he tortured himself, suffered, remembering, moaned, fell into rage or an awful, unbearable fear. Then he would try to get up, wanted to run, but someone always stopped him by force, and he would again fall into impotence and unconsciousness.
