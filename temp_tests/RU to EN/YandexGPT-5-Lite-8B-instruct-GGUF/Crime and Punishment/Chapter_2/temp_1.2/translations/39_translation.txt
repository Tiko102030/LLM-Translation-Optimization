 The clerk began to dictate to him the form of a usual reply in such cases, that is, "I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away property, etc." — "But you can't write, your pen is falling out of your hand," the clerk noticed, peering curiously at Raskolnikov. — "Are you sick?" — "Yes... my head is spinning... go on!" — "That's all; sign it." The clerk took back the paper and dealt with others. Raskolnikov gave up the pen, but instead of getting up and leaving, he rested both elbows on the table and clutched his head in his hands. It was as if a nail were being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly occurred to him: to stand up right now, go to Nikodim Fomichev and tell him everything from yesterday, every detail, then go with them to the apartment and point out the things to them, in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already got up to execute it.
