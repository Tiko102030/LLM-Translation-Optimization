 — Shall we have some tea?  
— Tea would be nice.  
— Pour it. Wait, I'll pour for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately gave instructions, poured, then poured another cup, abandoned his breakfast and sat back on the sofa. As before, he put his left hand on the patient's head, lifted him up and began to feed him tea with a teaspoon, constantly fanning over the spoon as if that was the most important and salutary aspect of recovery, particularly diligently. Raskolnikov was silent and did not resist, despite feeling strong enough to rise and sit on the sofa without any outside help, and not only having control over his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but perhaps even to walk. But for some strange, almost bestial cunning, it occurred to him to hide his strength until the time came, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, that he was not yet fully aware, and at the same time listen and find out what was going on here? However, he could not cope with his disgust: after swallowing about ten spoonfuls of tea, he suddenly freed his head, капризно pushed away the spoon and rolled back onto the pillow. Now there really were proper pillows under his heads — feather ones with clean cases; he also noticed that and took it into consideration.
