 Well, what's this? He rushed to take them out and throw them on the table in a flash. Having taken everything out, he even turned his pockets inside out to make sure there was nothing else left, he carried all this pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one place, the wallpaper ripped away from the wall: immediately he began shoving everything into this hole, under the paper: “It fits! Everything is out of sight, and so is my wallet!” — he thought joyfully, getting up and staring blankly into the corner, at the gap that was sticking out even more. Suddenly he trembled all over with horror: “Oh my God,” he whispered in despair, “what's happening to me? Was this hidden? Is this how you hide things?”

True, he didn't expect any items; he thought there would be only money, which is why he hadn't prepared a place in advance — "but what am I happy about now? — he thought. — Is this how you hide things?"
