 He let out a sigh of relief. "Maybe it's not that!" Little by little he began to take heart, he tried with all his might to encourage himself and come to his senses. "Some stupidity, some carelessness no matter how minor, and I can betray myself completely! Hmm… too bad there's no air here," he added, "it's stuffy… My head is even more dizzy… my mind too…"

He felt a terrible mess all over him. He himself was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The clerk interested him greatly, however: he always wanted to guess something by his face, to figure him out. This was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark complexion and an animated appearance that looked older than his age, dressed according to fashion and with panache, with a parting in the back of his head, combed and slicked down, with many rings on his white brush-scrubbed fingers and gold chains on his waistcoat. He even said a couple of words in French with one foreigner present, and very satisfactorily. "Louise Ivanovna, you should sit down," he said casually to the smartly dressed ruddy-faced lady who was still standing as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
