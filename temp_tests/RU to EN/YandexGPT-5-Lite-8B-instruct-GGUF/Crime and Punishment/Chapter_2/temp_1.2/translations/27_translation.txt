 Read it once, read it twice and I didn't understand it. — What is this? — he asked the clerk. — This is a demand for money from you under a loan letter, collection. You must either pay with all expenses, penalties and others, or write back when you can pay, and at the same time a commitment not to leave the capital until you pay, nor to sell or hide your property. And the lender has the right to sell your property and deal with you according to the laws. — But I… owe nobody! — That's none of our business. And here we have received for collection an overdue and legally protested loan letter for one hundred and fifteen rubles, issued by you to a widow, collegiate assessor Zarnitsyna, nine months ago, and then passed on for payment to court councilor Chebarov, so we invite you to respond. — But she's my landlady?
