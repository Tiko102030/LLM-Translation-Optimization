 He would just spit on someone, bite, it seems, if anyone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he came to the embankment of the Malaya Neva on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. “This is where he lives, in this house,” he thought. “What’s this, but I’ve come to Razumikhin myself! Again the same story as then... But it’s very curious, however: have I really come by myself or was I just walking and came here? It doesn't matter; I said... the other day... that I would go to him the day after, well, what of it, I will go! As if I couldn’t come now..."

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his tiny room, and at that moment he was studying, writing, and opened the door for him himself. They had not seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his tattered robe, with slippers on his bare feet, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was expressed on his face.
