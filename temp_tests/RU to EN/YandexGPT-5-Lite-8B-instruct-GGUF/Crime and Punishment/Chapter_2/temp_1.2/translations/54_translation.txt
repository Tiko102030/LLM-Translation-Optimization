"If indeed all this was done consciously, and not stupidly, if you действительно had a definite and firm goal, then how is it that until now you haven't even looked into the wallet and don't know what you got, why you endured all the муки and consciously went to such a vile, гадкое, low deed? You wanted to throw it in the water right now, the wallet, along with all the things you also hadn't seen yet… How is that possible?"

Yes, it is so; it's all like that. He knew this, however, even before, and this is not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided to throw it in the water at night, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that's how it should be, as if there could be no other way... Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; yes, perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and pulling drawers out of it… But so!.. "This is because I am very sick," he finally decided sullenly, "I have tormented and worn myself out, and I don't know what I'm doing... Both yesterday and the day before yesterday, and all this time I tortured myself... I will recover and... won't torture myself... But what if I don't recover at all? Lord! How sick I am of it all!.." He walked without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow dissipate, but he didn't know what to do or what to undertake. One new, overwhelming sensation took possession of him more and more almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical disgust for everything he met and encountered, stubborn, spiteful, hateful. Everyone who met him disgusted him — their faces, gait, movements disgusted him. He would have spat on someone, bitten them, it seems, if anyone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he went out onto the embankment of Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
