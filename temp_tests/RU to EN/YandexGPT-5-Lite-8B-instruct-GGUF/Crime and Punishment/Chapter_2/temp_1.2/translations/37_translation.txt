 — All these sensitive details, my good sir, do not concern us, — Ilja Petrovich bluntly cut in, — you must provide feedback and a commitment, and that you happened to be in love and all those tragic parts, we don't care about it at all. — Well, you... are cruel... — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write then, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. “What to write?” he asked somewhat грубо (roughly). — And I will dictate to you. It seemed to Raskolnikov that the clerk had become more negligent and contemptuous towards him after his confession, but strangely enough, he suddenly himself no longer cared about anyone's opinion, and this change happened somehow all at once, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them like that a minute ago, and even force his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
