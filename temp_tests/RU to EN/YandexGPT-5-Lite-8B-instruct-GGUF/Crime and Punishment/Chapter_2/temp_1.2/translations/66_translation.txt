 The pain from the whip subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one restless and not entirely clear thought now occupied him exclusively. He stood and looked into the distance for a long time and intently; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to university, it often happened to him, most often on his way home, perhaps a hundred times, to stop at exactly the same spot, gaze fixedly at this действительно magnificent panorama, and almost every time be surprised by one obscure and unsolvable impression of his own. An inexplicable cold always seemed to blow on him from this magnificent panorama; it was filled with mute and deaf spirit for him this magnificent picture… He wondered every time at his gloomy and mysterious impression and postponed its solution, not trusting himself, to the future. Now he suddenly vividly remembered his previous questions and perplexities, and it seemed to him that he had not forgotten them by chance now. It already seemed strange and odd to him that he had stopped in the same place as before, as if indeed he imagined that he could think about the same thing now as before, and be interested in the same old topics and pictures as he had… so recently. Even a little it made him laugh, and at the same time his chest tightened painfully. In some depth, below, somewhere barely visible under his feet, everything seemed to appear to him now: his past, previous thoughts, previous tasks, previous themes, previous impressions, and this entire panorama, and he himself, and everything, everything... It seemed to him that he was flying up somewhere, and everything disappeared before his eyes… Making an involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a two-rouble coin in his fist. He opened his hand, looked at the coin intently, swung his arm, and threw it into the water; then he turned around and went home. It seemed to him that he had cut himself off from everyone and everything in that moment as if with scissors.
