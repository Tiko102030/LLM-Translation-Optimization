 And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if all of a sudden the room were filled not with orderlies, but with his first friends, then even then, it seems, he would not have found a single human word for them, so suddenly his heart was empty. A gloomy feeling of painful, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly became consciously apparent to his soul. It was not the meanness of his heartfelt outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, it was not the meanness and triumph of Pouchikov over him that had suddenly turned his heart like this. Oh, what did he care now about his own meanness, about all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, reprimands, offices, etc., etc.! If they even sentenced him to be burned at that moment, then he would not have moved either, it is unlikely that he would even listen to the sentence attentively. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden and never experienced was happening to him. Not that he understood, but he clearly felt, with all the power of sensation, that not only with sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else, it was no longer possible for him to turn to these people in the police station, and if all these were his own brothers and sisters, and not lieutenant orderlies, then even then he would have absolutely no reason to turn to them and even under no circumstances in life; he had never experienced such a strange and awful sensation before this very minute. And what was most excruciating — it was more of a sensation than a consciousness, than an idea; an immediate sensation, the most painful sensation of all those that he had experienced in his life so far. The clerk began to dictate to him the form of the usual response in such cases, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, etc.
