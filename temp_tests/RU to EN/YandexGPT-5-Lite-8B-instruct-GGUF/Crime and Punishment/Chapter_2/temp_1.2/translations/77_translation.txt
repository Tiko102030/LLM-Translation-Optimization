 Well, who are you? — I'm a partner in our office, from the merchant Shelopayev, and I'm here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat down on another one, on the other side of the table. — It was good that you woke up, brother, — he continued, turning to Raskolnikov. — For four days now you have barely been eating and drinking. He examined you carefully and said right away that it was all nonsense — some kind of blow to the head, perhaps. Some nervous nonsense, he says, because the rations were bad, they gave too little beer and horseradish, that's why there is an illness, but nothing, it will pass and everything will work out.
