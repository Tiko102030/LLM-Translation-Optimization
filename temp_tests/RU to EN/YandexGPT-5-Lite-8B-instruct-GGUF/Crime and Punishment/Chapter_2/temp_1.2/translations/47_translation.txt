 Then he went out of the room, this time leaving it wide open completely. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt all broken up, his mind was still with him. He was afraid of being chased, afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps instructions to follow him would already be issued; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the traces for a while. It was necessary to get things done before there was still some strength and at least some reasoning left... Where to go? This had been decided long ago: “to throw everything into the ditch, and out of sight, out of mind.” He had made up his mind like that even in the night, in delirium, in those moments when, he remembered, several times he was stirred to get up and go: “quickly, quickly, and throw it all away”. But it turned out to be very difficult to throw it all away. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour or more, and looked at the slopes into the ditch several times, where he met them. But there was no way to carry out his intention: either barges were parked right near the slopes and laundresses were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides of the embankments, from all directions, it could be seen, noticed: suspiciously that a person deliberately went down, stopped and threw something into the water. What if the cases don't drown but float?
