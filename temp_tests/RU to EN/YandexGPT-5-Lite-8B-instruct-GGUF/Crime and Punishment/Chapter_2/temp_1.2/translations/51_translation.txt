 A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it. The wallet ended up on top, and there was still space left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, flipped it back to its original side with a single turn, and it fit right into its previous place, maybe just a little higher. But he shoveled up some earth and pressed down at the edges with his foot. Nothing could be seen. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again strong, barely endurable joy, as earlier in the office, seized him for an instant. “The ends are hidden! And who, who would think to look under this stone? It has probably been here since the construction of the house and will lie there for just as long.”
