 But Raskolnikov was already going out into the street. On Nikolaevsky Bridge he had to come to his senses again completely due to one incident that was very unpleasant for him. He was vigorously whipped on the back with a whip by the coachman of a carriage because he almost fell under the horses, despite the fact that the coachman shouted at him three or four times. The lash made him so angry that he, jumping to the railings (for some reason unknown to him, he had been walking in the very middle of the bridge where vehicles travel rather than people walk), ground his teeth viciously and clicked them.
