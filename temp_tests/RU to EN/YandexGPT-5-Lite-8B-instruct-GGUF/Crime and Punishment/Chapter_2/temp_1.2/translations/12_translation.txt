 He didn't answer and was holding a piece of paper in his hands without opening it. “Well, don’t get up,” Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was putting his legs down from the sofa. “If you're sick, then don't go out: you won't burn. What do you have there in your hands?” He glanced: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he recalled that when half-awake from fever, he would tightly clench all this in his hand and then fall asleep again. “Look at him, what rags he's collected and sleeps with them, as if it were treasure...” And Nastasya burst out laughing with her painful, nervous laugh. He quickly shoved everything under his overcoat and fixed his gaze on her. Although he could barely put anything together in a clear way at that moment, he felt that they wouldn't treat a person the same way when they came to take him.
