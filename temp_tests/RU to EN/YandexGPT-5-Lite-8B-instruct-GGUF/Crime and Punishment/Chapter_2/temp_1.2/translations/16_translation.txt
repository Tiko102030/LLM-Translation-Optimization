 His head was dizzy and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with a trick and then knock me out all at once," he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might blurt out some stupid thing..."

On the staircase, he remembered that he was leaving all his things like that, in the wallpaper hole — "and they probably planned a search here without him," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, one might say, cynicism of doom suddenly took hold of him that he threw up his hands and went on. "If only it would hurry up!.."

Outside, the unbearable heat was still there; not a drop of rain in all these days. Again dust, brick and lime, again the stench from the shops and taverns, again drunken people every minute, Chuхоnces (Finns) — разносчики (peddlers) and half- развалившиеся (half-collapsed) cabbies. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, so much so that it hurt to look and his head became completely dizzy — the ordinary sensation of a лихорадочного (feverish person) stepping out suddenly onto the street on a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn into yesterday's street, he looked at it with painful anxiety, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, maybe I will say," he thought, approaching the office.
