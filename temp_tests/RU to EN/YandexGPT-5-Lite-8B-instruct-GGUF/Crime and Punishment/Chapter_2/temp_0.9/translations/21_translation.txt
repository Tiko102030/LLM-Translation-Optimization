 He caught his breath more freely. "Perhaps it's not right!" Little by little he began to take heart, he tried with all his might to cheer up and come to his senses. "Some stupidity, some minor carelessness, and I can give myself away! Hmm… It's a pity there's no air here," he added, "it's stuffy… My head is even more dizzy... my mind too..."

He felt terrible disorder all over him. He himself was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The clerk greatly interested him, though: he always wanted to guess something from his face, to figure him out. He was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark complexion and an animated physiognomy that looked older than his years, dressed in style and fashionable, with parted hair at the back, combed and groomed, with many rings on his white brush-cleaned fingers and gold chains on his vest. He even said a word or two in French to one foreigner who was there, and very satisfactorily. "Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down," he said casually to the smartly dressed scarlet-faced lady, who kept standing as if she didn't dare to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
