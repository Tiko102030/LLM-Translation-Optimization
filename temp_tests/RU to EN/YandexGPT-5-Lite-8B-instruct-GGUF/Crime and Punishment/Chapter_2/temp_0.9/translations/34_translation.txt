 Louise Ivanovna began to curtsy urgently and with affable любезностью in all directions, and while curtsying she backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into one prominent officer, with an open fresh face and excellent thick blond sideburns. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the district supervisor. Louise Ivanovna hurried to curtsy almost to the floor and flew out of the office with frequent small steps, bouncing.  
— Again thunder, again lightning and thunderstorm, tornado, hurricane! — said Nikodim Fomich affably and friendly to Ilya Petrovich, — they stirred up my heart again, it boiled over again! I heard it from the stairs.  
— Yeah what! — said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and even not “shto”, but somehow: “Da-a shtaa!”), moving with some papers to another table and картинно shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he steps, there goes his shoulder; — here you see: the gentleman writer, that is, a student, formerly that is, doesn’t pay money, issued bills, does not vacate the apartment, complaints are received about them continuously, and you took it as a grievance that I lit a cigarette in their presence! They commit fraud themselves, but here, please look at them: this is what they now look like in their most attractive way!  
— Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, whatever!
