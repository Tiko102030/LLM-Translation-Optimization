 Further, in a recess of the yard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone barn peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, a carriage works or a слесарная мастерская, or something like that; almost from the very gates, a lot of coal dust could be seen everywhere blackened. “That’s just where I would toss everything and leave!” — it suddenly occurred to him. Not seeing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gate and immediately saw, right near the gate, a trough attached to the fence (as often happens in such houses where there are many factory workers, artel members, taxi drivers, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, was inscribed in chalk the ever-present witticism in such cases: “Stopping of carts is prohibited here.” Therefore, it’s already good that there’s no suspicion that he came and stopped. “Here I can just throw everything into a heap somewhere and leave!”

Looking around once more, he had already put his hand in his pocket when suddenly he noticed a large rough stone near the outer wall, between the gate and the trough, where the entire distance was about an arshin wide (about 70 centimeters), perhaps weighing about a pound and a half, adjacent directly to the stone street wall. Beyond this wall was the street, the sidewalk, and you could hear passers-by scurrying around, of which there were always many here; but no one could see him from beyond the gate, unless someone came from the street, which, however, was very likely to happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grasped its top firmly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. Under the stone there was a small hollow; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it.
