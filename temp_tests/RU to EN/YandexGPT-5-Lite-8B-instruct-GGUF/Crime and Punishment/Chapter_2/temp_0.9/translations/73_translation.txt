 He remembered only taking a sip of cold water and spilling it from the mug onto his chest. Then unconsciousness set in. III

He, however, was not completely unconscious throughout the whole illness: it was a feverish state, with delirium and semi-consciousness. He later recalled many things. Sometimes it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him somewhere and carry him out, they argued and quarreled about him a lot. Suddenly he was alone in the room, everyone had left and were afraid of him, and only occasionally opened the door slightly to look at him, threatened him, conspired among themselves about something, laughed and mocked him. He often remembered Nastasyа by his side; he also distinguished another person who seemed very familiar to him, but he could not guess who it was and yearned for it, even cried. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times — that the same day was going on. But about that — he completely forgot about that; however, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that one could not forget — he tortured himself, suffered, remembering, moaned, fell into rage or into terrible, unbearable fear. Then he pulled himself together, wanted to run, but someone always stopped him by force, and he again fell into helplessness and unconsciousness.
