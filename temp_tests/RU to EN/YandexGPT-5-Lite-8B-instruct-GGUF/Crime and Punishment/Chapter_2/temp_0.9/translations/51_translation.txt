 A small hollow formed under the stone; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it. The wallet came to the very top, and still there was room left in the hollow. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one turn to its previous side, and it just fell back into its original place, perhaps a little higher, barely perceptibly. But he shoveled up some earth and pressed it down at the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again strong, hard-to-bear joy, as before in the office, seized him for a moment. “The ends are hidden! And who, who could even think of looking under this stone? It has probably been lying there since the construction of the house and will lie there for just as long.”
