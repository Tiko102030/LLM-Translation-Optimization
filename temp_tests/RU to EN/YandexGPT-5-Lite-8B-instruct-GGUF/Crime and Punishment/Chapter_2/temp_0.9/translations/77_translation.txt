 Well, who are you? — I work as a partner in our office, for the merchant Shelopaev, and I'm here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat on another one, on the other side of the table. "You did well, brother, to come to your senses," he continued, turning to Raskolnikov. "For the fourth day you have been barely eating and drinking. In fact, they fed you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to you twice. Remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense — something hit your head, perhaps. Some nervous nonsense, he says, the ration was bad, not enough beer and horseradish were issued, that's why there is an illness, but it will pass, everything will be crushed."
