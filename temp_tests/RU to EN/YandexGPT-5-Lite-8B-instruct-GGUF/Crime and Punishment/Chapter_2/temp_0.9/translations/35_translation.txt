 — Poverty is not a vice, my friend, well, that's about it! It's known, powder, couldn't tolerate insults. You must have been offended by him in some way and couldn’t restrain yourself, — Nikodim Fomich continued, addressing Raskolnikov kindly, — but you were wrong to do so: a most benevolent person, I will tell you, but powder, powder! Flared up, boiled over, burned out – and that's it! And everything is gone! And in the end, there's only gold in the heart! They called him "Lieutenant Powder" in the regiment even.
— What a regiment it was! — Ilya Petrovich exclaimed, quite pleased to be so pleasantly tickled, but still playful. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to them all. — But please, captain, — he began rather развязно, turning suddenly to Nikodim Fomich, — consider my position… I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I have neglected anything on my part.
