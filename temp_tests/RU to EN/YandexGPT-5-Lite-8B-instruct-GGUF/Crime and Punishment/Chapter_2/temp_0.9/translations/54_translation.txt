 "That's not it at all!.."

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question knocked him off balance and amazed him bitterly:

"If indeed all this was done consciously and not stupidly, if you really had a specific and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you got, why you went through all that suffering and deliberately went to such a vile, гадкое, low act? You wanted to throw it into the water right now, the wallet, along with all the things you also haven't seen yet… How is that possible?"

Yes, that's so; it's all like that. He, however, knew this before, and this is not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided in the night to throw it into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that's how it should be, as if otherwise it couldn't be… Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; yes, perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and pulling drawers out of it… But so!.. "This is because I'm very sick," he finally decided gloomily, "I have tormented and tortured myself, and I don't know what I'm doing… Both yesterday and the day before, all this time I've been torturing myself… I will get better and… won't torture myself… But what if I don't recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of all this!.." He walked without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow dissipate, but he didn't know what to do or what to undertake. A new, irresistible sensation took hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical disgust for everything that met him and surrounded him, stubborn, spiteful, hateful. He hated all passers-by — he hated their faces, gait, movements. He would have spat on someone, bitten them, it seems, if anyone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he went out onto the embankment of the Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
