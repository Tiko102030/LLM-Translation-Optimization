Mechanically, he pulled over the formerly student winter coat lying beside him on a chair, warm but already almost in rags, covered himself with it, and sleep and delirium again seized him at once. He lost consciousness. In no more than five minutes he jumped up again and immediately, in frenzy, rushed to his clothes again. “How could I fall asleep again when nothing has been done! That’s right, that’s right: I haven’t taken off the петлю under my arm yet! I forgot, I really did forget about such a thing! Such evidence!” He pulled down the петля and quickly began to tear it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the linen. “Pieces of torn canvas will not excite suspicion in any case; that seems to be so, that seems to be so!” he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with attention strained to the point of pain he began to look around again, on the floor and everywhere, to see if he had forgotten anything else?
