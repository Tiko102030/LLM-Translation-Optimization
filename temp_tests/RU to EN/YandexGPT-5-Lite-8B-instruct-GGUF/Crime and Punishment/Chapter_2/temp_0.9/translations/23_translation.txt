 The stout lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and began to приседать with some special enthusiasm; but the officer did not pay any attention to her, and she no longer dared to sit down in front of him. It was a lieutenant, an assistant district constable, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and with extremely fine facial features that expressed nothing special, except for some impudence. He looked askance at Raskolnikov, somewhat indignantly: his costume was too ugly, and despite all the humiliation, his posture still did not match his clothes; Raskolnikov, through carelessness, stared at him too directly and for a long time, so much so that he was even offended. “What do you want?” he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin does not shy away from his lightning glance. “Summoned… by summons...” — Raskolnikov answered somehow. “This is about the case of collecting money from them, from the student,” the clerk hurried to say, tearing himself away from the paper. “Here!” and he threw a notebook to Raskolnikov, pointing to a place in it: “Read it!” “Money?
