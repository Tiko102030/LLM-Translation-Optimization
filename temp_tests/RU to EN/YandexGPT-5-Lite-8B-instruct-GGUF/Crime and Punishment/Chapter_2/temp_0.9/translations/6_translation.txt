 — He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath with his whole chest — "just feverish weakness, delirium for a minute," — and he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a ray of sunlight illuminated his left boot: signs seemed to appear on the toe that peeked out of the boot. He threw off the boot: "really marks! The whole tip of the sock is soaked in blood"; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly then... "But what now to do with this? Where to put this sock, fringe, pocket?"
He grabbed it all in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. "In the stove? But they will start rummaging through the stove first. Burn it? And how to burn it? There are not even any matches."
