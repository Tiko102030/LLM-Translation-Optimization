 “If asked, I might say,” he thought as he approached the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away from him. It had just moved to a new address, into a new building, on the fourth floor. He had been to the previous location once long ago and only briefly. As he entered through the gate, he saw a staircase to his right, down which a man was walking with a book in his hands: “A caretaker, then; this must be the office,” and he began to climb up at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. “I will go in, kneel down and tell them everything…” — he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and covered in slop. All the kitchens from all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and remained that way almost the whole day. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
