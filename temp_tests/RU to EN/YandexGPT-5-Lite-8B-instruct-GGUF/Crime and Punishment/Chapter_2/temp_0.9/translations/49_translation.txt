He went to the Neva along V Prospect; but on the way he suddenly had another thought: “Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to go somewhere very far away, again, perhaps to the Islands, and there somewhere, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush — bury all this and maybe mark the tree?” And although he felt that he was not able to discuss everything clearly and sensibly at that moment, but the thought seemed infallible to him. But he was not destined to get to the Islands either, something else happened: coming out from V Prospect onto the square, he suddenly saw on his left the entrance to a courtyard lined with completely blank walls. On the right, immediately upon entering the gate, a blank unpainted wall of a neighboring four-story building stretched far into the yard. On the left, parallel to the blank wall and also just from the gate, there was a wooden fence, stretching about twenty steps into the depths of the courtyard, and then made a bend to the left. It was a blank fenced-off place where some materials were lying. Further, in the recess of the yard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop.
