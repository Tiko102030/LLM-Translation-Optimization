 And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with orderlies, but with his dearest friends, then even then, it seems, he would not have a single human word for them, so suddenly his heart was empty. A gloomy feeling of painful, infinite loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously revealed itself to his soul. Neither the meanness of his heartfelt outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, nor the meanness and triumph of Pouchikov over him had suddenly turned his heart so much. Oh, what does he care now about his own meanness, about all these ambitions, lieutenants, German women, demands, offices, etc., etc.! If they even sentenced him to be burned at that very moment, then he would not have moved, and hardly would he have listened to the sentence carefully. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden and unprecedented was happening to him. Not that he understood, but he clearly felt, with all the strength of his sensation, that not only with sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else it was no longer possible for him to turn to these people in the quarter office, and if they were all his own brothers and sisters, and not quarter lieutenants, then even then he would have had absolutely no reason to turn to them and even in any case of life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible sensation before this very minute. And what was most painful — it was more of a feeling than consciousness, than concept; an immediate feeling, the most excruciating feeling out of all the sensations he had experienced so far in his life. The clerk began to dictate to him the form of the usual response in such cases, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, etc.
