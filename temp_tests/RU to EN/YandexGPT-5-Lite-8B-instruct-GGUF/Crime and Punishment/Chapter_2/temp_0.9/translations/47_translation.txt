 Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt broken, his consciousness was with him. He was afraid of being chased, afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps instructions to follow him would already be issued; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the traces for the time being. It was necessary to sort things out while there was still at least some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This had been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, end it all." He had decided this at night, in delirium, in those moments when, he remembered, several times he made an effort to get up and go: "quickly, quickly, throw everything away." But throwing things away turned out to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of Yekaterininsky Canal for half an hour or more, and looked at the entrances to the ditch several times, where he met them. But it was impossible to carry out his intention: either rafts stood right at the accesses and laundresses were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides of the embankments, from all directions, it could be seen, noticed: it is suspicious that a person deliberately went down, stopped and threw something into the water. What if the cases don't drown but float?
