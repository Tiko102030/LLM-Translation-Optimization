 — But where, if I may ask?   
— Down the street.   
— Short and clear. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, all pale as a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes in front of Ilya Petrovich's gaze.   
— He can barely stand on his feet, but you... — Nikodim Fomich noted.  
— Nothing! — said Ilya Petrovich especially. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but after looking at the clerk, who was also staring at him very intently, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly became quiet. It was strange.
