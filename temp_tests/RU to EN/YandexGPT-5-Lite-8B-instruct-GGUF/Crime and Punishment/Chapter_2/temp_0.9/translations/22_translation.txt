 — Louise Ivanovna, why don't you sit down, — he said to the smartly dressed crimson lady who was still standing, as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby. "Ich danke," she said and quietly sank onto the chair with a silky noise. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like a balloon, taking up almost half the room. A scent of perfume filled the air. But the lady was obviously робкая (timid) about occupying so much space and smelling so strongly of perfume, although she smiled timidly and defiantly at the same time, but with obvious anxiety. The траурная дама (lady in mourning) finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, quite smartly and somehow especially twirling her shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his hat with a cockade on the table and sat down in the armchair. The fluffy lady jumped up from her seat as soon as she saw him and began to curtsy with some special enthusiasm; but the officer did not pay her any attention, and she no longer dared to sit down in front of him anymore.
