He suddenly stopped when he came to the embankment of Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. "This is where he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this? I came to Razumikhin by myself! Again the same story as then... It’s very curious, however: did I come here by myself or just walked and stopped by here?" Anyway; I said…the other day…that I would go to him the next day after that, well, what of it, I will go! As if I couldn't drop in now…"

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his tiny room, writing and opened the door himself at that moment. It had been about four months since they saw each other. Razumikhin was sitting in his worn-out robe, torn to shreds, with bare feet in slippers, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was written all over his face.
