 The clerk began to dictate to him the form of a response ordinary in such cases, that is, “I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, etc.” — “But you can't write, the pen is falling out of your hands,” the clerk noted, peering curiously at Raskolnikov. — “Are you sick?” — “Yes... My head is spinning... go on!” — “That’s all; sign.” The clerk took back the paper and busied himself with others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of getting up and leaving, he rested both elbows on the table and gripped his head in his hands. It was as if a nail were being hammered into his crown. A strange thought suddenly occurred to him: to get up right now, go to Nikodim Fomich and tell him everything from yesterday, every detail, then go with them to the apartment and show them the things, in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already gotten up to do it.
