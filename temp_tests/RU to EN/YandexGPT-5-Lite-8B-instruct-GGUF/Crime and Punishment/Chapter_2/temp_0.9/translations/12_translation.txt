 He did not reply and was holding a piece of paper in his hands without opening it. “Don’t even get up,” Nastasya went on, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. “If you're sick, don't go out: you won't burn. What do you have there in your hands?” He looked: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on it, he recalled that when he was half-awake from fever, he would grip all this tightly in his hand and fall asleep again. “Look at him, gathering rags and sleeping with them as if they were treasure…” And Nastasya burst into her painfully neurotic laughter. He shoved everything under his overcoat in a flash and stared intently at her. Although he could not fully grasp it properly at that moment, he felt that people would treat a person differently when they came to take him.
