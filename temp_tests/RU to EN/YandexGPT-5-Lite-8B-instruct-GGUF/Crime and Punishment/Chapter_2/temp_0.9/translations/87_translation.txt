 — Shall we have some tea?  
— Tea would be nice.  
— Pour it out. Wait, I'll pour for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately gave orders, poured, then poured another cup, dropped his breakfast and moved back to the sofa. As before, he put his left arm around the sick man's head, lifted him up and began to feed him with tea from a teaspoon, blowing on the spoon again uninterruptedly and especially diligently, as if in this process of blowing was the most important and спасительный point of recovery. Raskolnikov was silent and did not resist, despite the fact that he felt quite strong enough to lift himself up and sit on the sofa without any outside help, and not only to control his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but even perhaps to walk. But for some strange, almost animal cunning, it suddenly occurred to him to hide his strength until the time came, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, that he didn't even fully understand, while at the same time listening and finding out what was going on here? However, he could not cope with his aversion: after gulping down about ten spoonfuls of tea, he suddenly freed his head, pushed the spoon capriciously away and fell back onto the pillow. There were real pillows under his heads now — feather ones with clean cases; he noticed that too and took it into consideration.
