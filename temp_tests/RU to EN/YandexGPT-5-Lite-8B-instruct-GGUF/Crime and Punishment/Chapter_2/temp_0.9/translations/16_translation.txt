 His head was dizzy and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to заманить me with cunning and then knock me off," he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might blurt out some stupidity..."

On the staircase, he remembered that he was leaving all his things like that, in the wallpaper hole — "and they probably arranged a search without him on purpose," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say, cynicism of doom suddenly overwhelmed him that he waved his hand and went further. "I just want it to be over поскорее!"

On the street, the unbearable heat was there again; if only a drop of rain had fallen all these days. Again dust, bricks and lime, again stench from shops and распивочных, again drunken people every minute, Chuхоnces-peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun shone brightly in his eyes, so much that it hurt to look and his head felt completely dizzy — the usual sensation of a feverish person going outside suddenly on a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked at it with painful anxiety, at that house... and immediately glanced away. "If they ask, I might say something," he thought, approaching the office.
