 He did not answer and held the paper in his hands without opening it. — Well, don't get up, — Nastasya went on, pitying him and seeing that he was putting his feet down from the sofa. — If you are sick, then stay put: you won't burn. What have you got there? He looked: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he remembered that when he half-awoke in the heat, he would clench all this tightly in his hand and fall asleep again. — Look at him, picking up rags and sleeping with them, just like with treasure... And Nastasya burst into her painfully nervous laughter. He shoved everything under his overcoat in a flash and fixed his gaze on her. Although he could hardly put much thought together at that moment, he felt that they wouldn't treat a person the same way when they came to take him.
