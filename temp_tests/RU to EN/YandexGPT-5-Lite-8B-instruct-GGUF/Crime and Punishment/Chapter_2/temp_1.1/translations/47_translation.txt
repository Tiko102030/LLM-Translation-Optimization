 Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt that he was all broken up, his consciousness remained with him. He was afraid of being pursued, afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour perhaps, there would already be instructions to keep track of him; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the evidence before then. It was necessary to sort things out while there was still at least some strength and some reasoning left… Where to go? This had been decided long ago: “To throw everything into the ditch, and ends in water, and that's the end of it.” He had resolved this even in the night, in delirium, in those moments when, he remembered, he made several attempts to get up and go: “faster, faster, and throw everything away.” But throwing things away proved to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour or more, and several times glanced at the approaches to the ditch where they met. But it was impossible to even think about carrying out the intention: either rafts stood near the approaches and laundresses were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides of the embankments, from all directions, one could see, notice: it is suspicious that a person deliberately came down, stopped and throws something into the water. And what if the cases don't drown but float?
