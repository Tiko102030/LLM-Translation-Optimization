 He took a breath more freely. “Maybe it's not right!” By degrees he began to take heart; he tried with all his might to encourage himself and come to his senses. “Some stupidity, some carelessness, the slightest thing, and I can give myself away! Hmm… it’s a pity there’s no air here,” he added, “it’s stuffy… My head is getting even more dizzy… my mind too…”

He felt terrible confusion all over. He himself was afraid of not controlling himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything at all, something completely unrelated, but it wasn't working at all. The clerk greatly interested him, though: he kept wanting to guess something by his face, to figure him out. He was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark complexion and an animated physiognomy that looked older than his age, dressed according to fashion, combed and groomed, with many rings on his white brush-scrubbed fingers and gold chains on his waistcoat. He even said a word or two in French with one foreigner present here, quite satisfactorily. “Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down,” he said casually to the smartly dressed crimson lady who was standing as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
