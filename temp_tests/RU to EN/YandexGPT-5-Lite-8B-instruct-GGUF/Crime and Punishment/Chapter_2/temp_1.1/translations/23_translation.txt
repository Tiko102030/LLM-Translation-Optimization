 The stout lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began to curtsy with some peculiar delight; but the officer paid no attention to her, and she dared not sit near him anymore. This was a lieutenant, assistant district supervisor, with ginger-colored mustaches торчавшими sticking out horizontally in both directions and extremely fine facial features that expressed nothing special, except for some impudence. He looked askance at Raskolnikov, somewhat indignant: his attire was too shabby; despite all the debasement, his posture still did not match his clothes; Raskolnikov, through carelessness, stared at him too straight and long, so much that the officer was offended. 
“What do you want?” he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin did not shy away from his lightning-fast glance.
“Summoned... on a summons…” Raskolnikov answered somehow.
“This is about a case involving collection of money from a student,” the clerk hurried to say, tearing himself away from the paper. “Here!” and he tossed the notebook to Raskolnikov, indicating a place in it—read it! “Money?”
