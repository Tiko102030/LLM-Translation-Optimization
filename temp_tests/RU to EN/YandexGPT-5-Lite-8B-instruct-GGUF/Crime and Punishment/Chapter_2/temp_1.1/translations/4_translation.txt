Mechanically he dragged over to himself what was lying beside him on a chair, his former student's winter coat, warm but already almost in tatters, covered himself with it, and sleep and delirium seized him again all at once. He forgot himself. In no more than five minutes he jumped up again and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed to his clothes again. “How could I fall asleep again when nothing has been done! That's right, that's right: I still haven’t taken off the петлю under my armpit! I forgot, I really forgot about such a thing! Such evidence!” He pulled down the петля and quickly began to tear it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the linen. “Pieces of torn canvas will not arouse suspicion in any case; it seems so, it seems so!” — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painfully intense attention he again began to look around, on the floor and everywhere, to see if he had forgotten anything else?
