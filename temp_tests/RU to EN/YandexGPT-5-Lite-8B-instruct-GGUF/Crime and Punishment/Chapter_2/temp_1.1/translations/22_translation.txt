 — Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down, — he said to the gaudily dressed, purplish-red lady who was still standing, as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby. “Ich danke,” she said and quietly, with a silky noise, sank into a chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like a balloon, taking up almost half the room. Perfume wafted through the air. But the lady was obviously робкая about occupying half the room and smelling so strongly of perfume, although she smiled timidly and boldly at the same time, but with явным беспокойство (manifest anxiety). The траурная дама finally finished and began to get up. Suddenly, with some noise, quite smartly and in a rather peculiar way, turning his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat down in an armchair. The stately lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and began to curtsy with some special enthusiasm; but the officer did not pay her the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit near him.
