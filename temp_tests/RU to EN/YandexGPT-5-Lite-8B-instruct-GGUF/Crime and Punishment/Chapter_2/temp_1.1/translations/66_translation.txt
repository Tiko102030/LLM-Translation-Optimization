 The pain from the whip утихла, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one restless and not entirely clear thought occupied him now exclusively. He stood and stared into the distance for a long time and intently; this place was particularly familiar to him. When he went to university, it often happened to him—most frequently when returning home—perhaps a hundred times, to stop at exactly this same spot, gaze intently at this truly magnificent panorama, and almost every time be surprised by one unclear and unsolvable impression of his own. An inexplicable cold always blew on him from this magnificent panorama; the place was filled with a mute and deaf spirit for him… He marveled each time at his gloomy and mysterious impression and postponed its solution, distrusting himself, to the future. Now he suddenly vividly remembered his previous questions and perplexities, and it seemed to him that he had not accidentally recalled them now. It already seemed strange and bizarre to him that he had stopped at the same place as before, as if he had really imagined that he could think about the same things now as before, and be interested in the same old topics and scenes as he had been… so recently. He even found it a bit laughable, and at the same time his chest tightened painfully. In some depth, down below, somewhere barely visible under his feet, all his previous past, thoughts, tasks, themes, impressions, this entire panorama, himself, and everything, everything—it all seemed to appear to him now. It seemed to him that he had cut himself off from everyone and everything in that moment, as if with scissors.
