 — Don't bother shouting!
— I'm not screaming, but speaking quite evenly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so angry that for the first moment he could not utter anything, and only some sparks flew from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat.
— Be so kind as to be s-s-silent! You are in the presence. Not to b-b-bluster, sir!
— And you are also in the presence, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides, while you're shouting, you smoke a cigarette, therefore you are snubbing all of us. Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an inexpressible pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
