 And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with police officers but with his dearest friends, then even then, it seems, he would not have a single human word for them, his heart having become so empty all of a sudden. A gloomy sensation of excruciating, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously declared itself to his soul. It was not the meanness of his heartfelt outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, nor the meanness and triumph of Pouchikov over him that had suddenly turned his heart so much. Oh, what did he care now about his own baseness, all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, reprimands, offices, etc., etc.! If they even sentenced him to be burned at this very moment, then he would not have stirred, probably he wouldn't even listen to the sentence carefully. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden and never experienced before was happening to him. Not that he understood, but he clearly felt, with all the strength of sensation, that not only with sensitive expansivenesses, as before, but even with anything at all, he could no longer turn to these people in the police station, and if it were all his own brothers and sisters and not quartermasters, then there would be absolutely no need for him to turn to them in any case of life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible sensation until that very minute. And what was most tormenting — it was more of a feeling than awareness, than concept; direct sensation, the most excruciating sensation of all those he had experienced in his life so far. The clerk began to dictate to him the form of a response common in such cases, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, etc.
