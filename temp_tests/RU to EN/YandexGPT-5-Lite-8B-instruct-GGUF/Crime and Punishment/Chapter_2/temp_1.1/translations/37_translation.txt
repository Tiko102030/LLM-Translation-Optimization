 — All these sensitive details, my lord, do not concern us, — Ilya Petrovich rudely cut in, — you must give feedback and a commitment, but as for your being in love and all these tragic aspects, we don't care about that at all. “Well, you… that’s cruel…” Nikodim Fomich muttered, sitting down at the table and also signing. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write, then, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. “What to write?” he asked in a particularly rude manner. — And I will dictate to you. It seemed to Raskolnikov that the clerk had become more negligent and contemptuous towards him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly became absolutely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened all at once, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could have talked to them like that a moment ago, and even impose his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
