 Nothing and nobody; no one peeked. Not even Nastasia touched anything. But, good lord! How could he have left all these things in that hole the other day? He dashed to the corner, plunged his hand under the wallpaper, started pulling out items and loading them into his pockets. There were eight of them in total: two small boxes with earrings or something like that — he didn't really look carefully; then four small saffian cases. One chain was simply wrapped in newspaper. Something else in newspaper paper, perhaps an order...
He put everything in different pockets, in the coat and in the remaining right pocket of his trousers, trying to make it less noticeable. He also took the wallet along with the things. Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open.
