 He remembered only how he took a sip of cold water and spilled it from the mug onto his chest. Then unconsciousness set in. III

He, however, was not really completely unconscious throughout the entire time of his illness: it was a feverish state, with delirium and semi-consciousness. He recalled much later. Sometimes it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him somewhere and carry him out, they argued about him and quarreled greatly. Then suddenly he was alone in the room, everyone had left and were afraid of him, and only occasionally would they slightly open the door to look at him, threaten him, conspire among themselves, laugh and tease him. He often remembered Nastasya beside him; he also distinguished another person who seemed very familiar to him, but whom exactly — he could not figure it out and was longing for it, even cried. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times — that the same day was going by. But about that — he completely forgot about it; however, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that could not be forgotten — he tortured himself, suffered, trying to remember, groaned, flew into a rage or an awful, unbearable fear. Then he would lunge from his place, want to run, but someone would always stop him by force, and he would fall back into impotence and unconsciousness.
