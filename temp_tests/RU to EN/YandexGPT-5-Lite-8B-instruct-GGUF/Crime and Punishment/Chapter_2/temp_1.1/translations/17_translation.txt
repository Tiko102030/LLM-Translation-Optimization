 “If they ask, perhaps I will say,” he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst from him. It had just moved to a new address, into a new building, to the fourth floor. He had once been there briefly on his previous visit, but that was a long time ago. Having entered through the gate, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a peasant was going with a book in hand: “That means a janitor; this must be the office,” and he began to go upstairs at random. He didn’t want to ask anyone anything. “I will enter, kneel and tell them everything…” — he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The stairs were narrow, steep and covered in slops. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and remained so for almost the whole day. That’s why it was terribly stuffy.
