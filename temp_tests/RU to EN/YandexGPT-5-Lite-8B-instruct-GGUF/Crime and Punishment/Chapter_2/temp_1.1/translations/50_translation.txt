 Further, in a courtyard recess, the corner of a low, закопченного, stone barn peeped out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, carriage or plumbing, or something like that; everywhere, almost from the gate itself, there was a lot of coal dust darkening the ground. “That’s where I would toss it all and leave!” — he suddenly thought. Not seeing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gates and immediately saw a trough attached to the fence right near the gate (as is often done in such houses where there are many factory workers, artisans, cabbies, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, was written in chalk the usual quip for such cases: “Standing a wagon here is prohibited.” Therefore, it was already good that there was no suspicion that he had come in and stopped. “Here I could throw everything all together into a heap and leave!”

Looking around once more, he stuck his hand in his pocket, when suddenly he noticed a large rough stone near the outer wall, perhaps about a pound and a half in weight, adjoining directly to the stone street wall between the gate and the trough. The distance there was only an arshin wide. Beyond this wall was the street, the sidewalk, you could hear passers-by снующие around, of which there were always many; but no one could see him behind the gates, except if someone came from the street, which, however, was very likely to happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grasped its top tightly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it.
