 My head was dizzy and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with a trick and then knock me off everything," he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might blurt out some stupidity..."

On the stairs, he remembered that he was leaving all his things like that, in the wallpaper hole — "and perhaps there will be a search without him on purpose," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if I can say, cynicism of destruction suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went further. "Just поскорее!.."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; if only there had been a drop of rain all these days. Dust again, brick and lime, stench again from shops and taverns, drunks again every minute, Chuconost sellers and half-collapsed cabs. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, so much that it hurt to look and his head became completely dizzy — the usual sensation of a лихорадочный person suddenly going out onto the street on a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn to yesterday's street, he looked at it with excruciating anxiety, at that house... and immediately отвел his eyes. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
