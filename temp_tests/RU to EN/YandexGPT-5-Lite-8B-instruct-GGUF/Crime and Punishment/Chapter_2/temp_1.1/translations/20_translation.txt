 It was some particularly disheveled person with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't find out anything from this because he doesn't care," Raskolnikov thought. — Go there, to the clerk, said the scribe and poked forward with his finger, pointing to the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), cramped and packed with people — folk somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One in трауре, poorily dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk and wrote something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and purple-red, spotted, a prominent woman, and overly lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest the size of a teacup, stood aside and was waiting for something. Raskolnikov thrust his summons to the clerk. He glanced at it briefly, said "wait" and continued to work with the lady in трауре. He breathed more freely.
