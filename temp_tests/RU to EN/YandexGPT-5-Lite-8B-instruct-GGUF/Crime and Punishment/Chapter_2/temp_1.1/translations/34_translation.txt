 Louise Ivanovna began to curtsy in all directions with hasty courtesy and, as she curtsied, backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into an officer with a prominent open fresh face and excellent thick blond sideburns. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, a police inspector. Louise Ivanovna hurried to curtsy almost to the floor and flew out of the office in quick small steps, bouncing. "Thunder and lightning again, storm, hurricane!" - Nikodim Fomich addressed Ilya Petrovich kindly and amicably - "again they stirred up my heart, again I boiled over! I heard it from the stairs." "Well what!" - said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and even not "what," but somehow: "Yes-s sha-a!"), moving with some papers to another table and theatrically shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he steps, there goes the shoulder; - here you see sir: the gentleman writer, that is, a student, former that is, does not pay money, gave out promissory notes, does not vacate the apartment, complaints against them come in continuously, and you took offense when I lit a cigarette with them! They themselves transgress, but look at them here, please: they are now in their most attractive form-s! "Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, what of it!"
