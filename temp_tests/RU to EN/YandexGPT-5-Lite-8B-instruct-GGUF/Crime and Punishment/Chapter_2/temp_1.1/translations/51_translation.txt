 A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it. The wallet came to the very top, and still there was space left in the depression. Then he again grabbed the stone, turned it over with one rotation to its previous side, and it just fell back into its original place, perhaps a little, slightly higher. But he shoveled up some earth and pressed it down at the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed towards the square. Again, strong, barely tolerable joy, as before in the office, seized him for a moment. “The ends are hidden! And who, who can even think of looking under this stone? It has been here perhaps since the construction of the house and will lie there for just as long.”
