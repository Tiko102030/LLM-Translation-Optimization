 — Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but what of it! It's known that powder couldn't bear the insult. You must have been offended by him in some way and lost your temper yourself, — Nikodim Fomich continued, addressing Raskolnikov любезно, — but you were wrong to do so: I'll tell you, he's a most noble-hearted person, but powder, powder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! And everything passed! And in the end there's only gold of heart! They even called him "Lieutenant Powder" in the regiment…
— And what a wonderful regiment it was! — Ilya Petrovich exclaimed, quite pleased to be tickled so pleasantly, but still playful. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — But please, captain, — he began rather развязно, turning to Nikodim Fomich suddenly, — understand my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I have neglected anything on my part.
