 He would just spit on someone, bite it seems, if anyone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the embankment of Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. “This is where he lives, in this house,” he thought. “What is this, I’ve come to Razumikhin myself! Again the same story as then... And it's very curious, however: did I come here myself or was I just walking and came here? It doesn’t matter; I said... the other day... that I would go to him the next day after that, well, so what, I will go! As if I couldn’t come now…”

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was home, in his tiny room, and at that moment he was busy writing, and opened the door for him himself. It had been about four months since they'd seen each other. Razumikhin was sitting in his worn-out robe, tattered to rags, with barefoot slippers, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was written all over his face.
