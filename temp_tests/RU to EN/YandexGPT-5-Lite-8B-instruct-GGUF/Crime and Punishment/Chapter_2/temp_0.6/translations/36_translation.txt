 — For mercy's sake, captain, — he began quite развязно, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly, — consider my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for an apology if I have neglected anything on my part. I am a poor and sick student, oppressed (he said so: "oppressed") by poverty. I'm an ex-student because now I can't support myself, but I will receive money... I have a mother and sister in the — th province. They will send it to me, and I... will pay. My landlady is a kind woman, but she got so angry that I lost my lessons and haven't paid for four months now that she doesn't even send me lunch... And I don't understand at all what this bill of exchange is! Now she demands from me on this loan letter that I borrowed, well, what am I going to pay her, judge for yourselves!.. — But this isn't our business... the clerk was about to notice again...

— Let me, let me, I completely agree with you, but let me also explain, — Raskolnikov picked up again, addressing not the clerk, but still to Nikodim Fomich, but trying with all his might to address Illya Petrovich as well, although he stubbornly pretended to rummage through papers and contemptuously ignored him, — let me also clarify for my part that I have been living with her for about three years, since the arrival from the province and before... before... however, why shouldn't I admit in turn, from the very beginning I promised that I would marry her daughter, a verbal promise, completely free... She was a girl... however, she even liked me... although I wasn't in love... in a word, youth, that is, I mean to say that my landlady used to give me a lot of credit then and I led something of a life... I was very frivolous...

— You are not at all required to disclose such intimacies, sir, and there's no time for it, — Illya Petrovich interrupted rudely and triumphantly, but Raskolnikov stopped him with heat, although he suddenly found it extremely difficult to speak. — But let me, partly, tell you everything... how things were and... in turn... although it's superfluous to tell you, I agree with you, — but a year ago this girl died of typhus, while I remained a tenant as I was, and the landlady, when she moved to her current apartment, told me... and said friendly... that she completely trusted me and all... but wouldn't I like to give her this loan letter for one hundred and fifteen rubles, everything she considered my debt. Please: she specifically said that as soon as I gave this paper, she would lend me credit again as much as she liked and that never, never, in turn, — these were her own words — she wouldn't use this paper until I paid myself... And now, when I lost my lessons and have nothing to eat, she serves for collection... What am I going to say now? — All these sensitive details, sir, do not concern us, — Illya Petrovich boldly cut in, — you must give a response and a commitment, and what you happened to be in love with and all these tragic parts, we don't care about that at all.
