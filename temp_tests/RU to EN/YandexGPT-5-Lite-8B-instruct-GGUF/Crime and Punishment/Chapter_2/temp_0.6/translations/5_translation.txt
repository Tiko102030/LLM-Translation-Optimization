 "Pieces of torn canvas will not arouse suspicion in any case; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful tension he began to look around again, on the floor and everywhere, to see if he had forgotten anything else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even simple reasoning was abandoning him, began to torment him unbearably. "What, is it really starting, is this torture approaching? There, there, so it is!" Indeed, scraps of fringe, which he had cut from his pants, were lying on the floor, in the middle of the room, for anyone to see! "But what's happening to me!" — he cried out again, lost. Then a strange thought came to him: that maybe all his clothes were bloody, that there might be many stains, but that he just didn't see them, didn't notice them because his reasoning had weakened, was fragmented... his mind was clouded... Suddenly he remembered that there was blood on the purse. "Oh! So there must also be blood in my pocket, because I put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" He quickly turned out his pocket, and sure enough — there were traces, stains on the lining of the pocket! "So it turns out that I haven't completely lost my mind yet, so there is reasoning and memory, since I caught myself and guessed it!" — he thought triumphantly, breathing deeply and joyfully with his whole chest — "it's just a febrile weakness, a momentary delirium" — and he pulled the entire lining out of the left pocket of his pants.
