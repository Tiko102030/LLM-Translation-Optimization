As for the curvaceous lady, at first she trembled so much from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more любезнее her appearance became, the more charming her smile directed towards the грозный lieutenant grew. She shuffled in place and squatted continuously, impatiently waiting for them to finally let her have her say, and she waited. “I didn’t make any noise or fight, Captain sir,” she suddenly started chattering away like spilled peas, with a strong German accent, though lively in Russian, “and there was no scandal whatsoever, but they came drunk, and I’ll tell you everything, Captain sir, it’s not my fault… I have a noble house, Captain sir, and noble treatment, Captain sir, and I always, always didn’t want any scandal. And they just came drunk, and then asked for drinks again three times, and then one lifted his feet and started playing the piano with them, and that’s not good at all in a noble house, and he broke the piano completely, and there’s no manners at all here, and I said so. And he took a stick and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the janitor and Karl came, he grabbed Karl and hurt his eye, and Henriette got an eye hurt too, and they hit me in the cheek five times. And that’s so indelicate in a noble house, Captain sir, and I screamed. And he opened the window to the canal and stood at the window, squealing like a little pig; and that’s shameful.”
