 — Shall we have some tea? — Yes, please bring me some. — Pour it. Wait, I'll pour it for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately ordered, poured, then poured another cup, abandoned his breakfast and moved back to the sofa. As before, he put his left hand on the patient's head, lifted him up and began feeding him tea with a teaspoon, again blowing continuously and particularly hard on the spoon as if this process of blowing was the most important and salutary aspect of recovery. Raskolnikov remained silent and did not resist, despite feeling quite strong enough to lift himself up and sit on the sofa without any outside help, and not only to control his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but even perhaps to walk. But for some strange, almost animal cunning, it suddenly occurred to him to hide his strength until the time came, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, that he was not yet fully understanding, while at the same time listening and finding out what was going on here? However, he could not cope with his disgust: after drinking about ten spoonfuls of tea, he suddenly freed his head, капризно pushed away the spoon and fell back onto the pillow. There were now real pillows under his heads — feather ones with clean pillowcases; he also noticed this and took it into consideration.
