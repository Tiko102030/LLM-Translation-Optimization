 Louise Ivanovna began curtsying hastily in all directions and, while curtsying, backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into an officer with a prominent open fresh face and excellent thick blond sideburns from behind. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the police inspector. Louise Ivanovna hurried to curtsy almost to the floor and, jumping in quick small steps, flew out of the office. “Thunder again, thunder and lightning again, tornado, hurricane!” — said Nikodim Fomich to Ilya Petrovich with любезно and friendlyly; — they stirred up my heart again, got all worked up! Heard it from the stairs. “Well, what!” — said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and not even “shto”, but somehow: “Da-a shta-a!”), while moving to another table with some papers and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step; — here, you see: a gentleman writer, that is, a student, former, I mean, doesn’t pay money, gave promissory notes, doesn’t clear out the apartment, there are continuous complaints against them, but you took offense when I lit a cigarette in front of them! They cheat themselves, and here, please look at them: they are now in their most attractive form! “Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, whatever!”
