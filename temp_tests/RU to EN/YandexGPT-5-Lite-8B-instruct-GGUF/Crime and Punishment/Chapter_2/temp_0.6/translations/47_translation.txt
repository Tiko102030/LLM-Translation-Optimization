 Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt that he was all broken up, his consciousness remained with him. He was afraid of being chased, afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps instructions to follow him would already be issued; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the traces for the time being. It was necessary to sort things out while there was still at least some strength and at least some reasoning left... Where to go? This had long been decided: “Throw everything into the ditch, and make an end of it.” He had resolved this even in the night, in his delirium, in those moments when, he remembered, several times he tried to get up and go: “quickly, quickly, and throw it all away.” But throwing it away proved to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour or more, and several times looked at the accesses to the ditch where they met him. But it was unthinkable to carry out his intention: either rafts were standing near the accesses and laundresses were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides of the embankments, from all directions, one could see, notice: it is suspicious that a person deliberately went down, stopped, and throws something into the water. What if the cases don't sink but float?
