 "Not that at all!.."

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question knocked him off balance and surprised him bitterly:

"If indeed all this was done consciously and not foolishly, if you really had a specific and firm goal, then how is it that you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you got, why you went through all the муки and deliberately went to such a mean, vile, low deed? Why, you wanted to throw it into the water right now, the wallet, along with all the things you also haven't seen yet... How is that?"

Yes, this is so; this is all so. He knew this anyway, and it's not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided to throw it into the water at night, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that's how it should be, as if it couldn't be otherwise... Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; yes, maybe it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and dragging drawers out of it... But so!.. "This is because I am very sick," he finally decided sullenly, "I have tormented and tortured myself, and I don't know what I'm doing... Both yesterday and the day before yesterday, and all this time I have been tormenting myself... I will recover and... won't torture myself anymore... But what if I never recover at all? Lord! How sick of it all I am!.." He walked without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow distract himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to undertake. An overwhelming new sensation took hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical disgust for everything that met him and surrounded him, stubborn, vicious, hateful. Everyone who met him was disgusting to him — their faces, gait, movements were disgusting to him. He would have spat on someone, he would have bitten, it seems, if anyone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he walked out onto the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilievsky Island, near the bridge.
