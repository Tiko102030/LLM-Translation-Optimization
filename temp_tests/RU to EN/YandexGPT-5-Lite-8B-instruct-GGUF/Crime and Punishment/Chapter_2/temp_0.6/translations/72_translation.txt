 He was looking at her, barely breathing. “I heard it myself… I wasn’t sleeping… I was sitting,” he said even more timidly. “I listened for a long time… The warder's assistant came... Everyone rushed to the stairs, from all the apartments…”

“No one came. And this is your blood screaming. This happens when there’s no outlet for it and your insides start to bake, that’s when things start to seem real... Are you going to eat something?” He didn't answer. Nastasya still stood over him, gazed at him intently and wouldn't leave. “Give me something to drink… Nastasya.” She went downstairs and returned in a couple of minutes with water in a white earthenware mug; but he no longer remembered what happened next. He only remembered how he took a sip of cold water and spilled some from the mug onto his chest.
