 The clerk began to dictate the form of a common reply in such cases to him, that is, “I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, and so on.” — “But you can't write, the pen is falling out of your hand,” observed the clerk, peering curiously at Raskolnikov. — “Are you sick?” — “Yes… my head is spinning… go on!” — “That’s all; sign it.” The clerk took back the paper and turned to others. Raskolnikov gave up the pen, but instead of getting up and leaving, he rested both elbows on the table and clutched his head in his hands. It was as if a nail were being driven into his темечко. A strange thought suddenly occurred to him: to stand up right now, go to Nikodim Fomich and tell him everything from yesterday, every last detail, then go with them to the apartment and show them the things in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already gotten up to fulfill it.
