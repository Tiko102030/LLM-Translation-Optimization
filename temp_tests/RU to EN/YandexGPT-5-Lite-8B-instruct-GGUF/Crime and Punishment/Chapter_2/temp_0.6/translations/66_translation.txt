 The pain from the whip subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one беспокойная and not совсем clear thought occupied him now exclusively. He stood and looked into the distance for a long time and intently; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to university, it often happened to him — most often when returning home — perhaps a hundred times, to stop at exactly the same spot, gaze intently at this truly magnificent panorama and almost every time be surprised by one unclear and unsolvable impression of his own. An inexplicable cold always blew on him from this magnificent panorama; it was filled with mute and deaf spirit for him, this magnificent picture… He marveled each time at his gloomy and mysterious impression and put off solving it, not trusting himself, to the future. Now he suddenly sharply remembered his previous questions and perplexities, and it seemed to him that he had not forgotten them by chance now. It even seemed strange and bizarre to him that he had stopped in the same place as before, as if he really imagined that he could think about the same things now as before, and be interested in the same old themes and pictures as he used to be… so recently. Even for a moment it almost made him laugh, and at the same time his chest tightened painfully. In some depth, down below, somewhere barely visible under his feet, all his previous past, previous thoughts, tasks, topics, impressions, and this entire panorama, and he himself, and everything, everything… seemed to appear to him now. It seemed to him that he was flying away somewhere up high and everything disappeared before his eyes… Having made an involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a two-ruble coin clutched in his fist. He opened his hand, looked intently at the coin, swung his arm and threw it into the water; then he turned and went home. It seemed to him that he had somehow cut himself off from everyone and everything at that moment with scissors.
