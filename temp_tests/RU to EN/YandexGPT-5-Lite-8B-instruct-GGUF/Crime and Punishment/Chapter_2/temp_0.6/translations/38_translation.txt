 And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with police constables but with his very best friends, then even then, it seems, he would not have a single human word for them, their hearts having been so suddenly emptied. A gloomy feeling of painful, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly made itself consciously known to his soul. Neither the meanness of his heartfelt outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, nor the meanness of Pouchik's triumph over him had suddenly turned his heart like this. Oh, what did he care now about his own meanness, about all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, reprimands, offices, and so on and so forth! If they were even to sentence him to be burned at that very moment, then he would not have moved, he probably would not even have listened to the sentence carefully. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and never experienced before was happening to him. He did not understand it, but he clearly felt, with all the power of sensation, that not only with sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else, he could no longer turn to these people in the police station, and if they were all his own brothers and sisters rather than police lieutenants, then there would be absolutely no need for him to turn to them in any case of life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible sensation before this very minute. And what was most painful — it was more a sensation than consciousness, than an idea; an immediate sensation, the most excruciating sensation of all those he had experienced in his life so far. The clerk began to dictate to him the form of the usual response in such cases, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, and so on.
