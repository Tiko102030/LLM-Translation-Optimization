 He breathed easier. “Perhaps it’s not right!” Gradually he began to take heart, he tried with all his might to cheer up and come to his senses. “Some stupidity, some carelessness, and I could betray myself! Hmm... it's a pity there's no air here,” he added, “it’s stuffy… My head is even more dizzy… my mind too…”

He felt a terrible disorder in himself. He was afraid of not controlling himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it didn’t work at all. The clerk interested him greatly, however: he always wanted to guess something from his face, to figure him out. This was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark complexion and an animated physiognomy that looked older than his age, dressed fashionably and smartly, with a parting at the back of his head, combed and groomed, with many rings on his white fingers cleaned with brushes and gold chains on his waistcoat. He even exchanged a couple of words in French with one foreigner present, and very satisfactorily. “Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down,” he said casually to the smartly dressed crimson-faced lady who was standing as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby.
