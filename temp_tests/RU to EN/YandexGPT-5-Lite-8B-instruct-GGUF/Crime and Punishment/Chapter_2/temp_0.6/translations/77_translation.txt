 Well, who are you? — I work as a foreman in our office for merchant Shelopaev, and I'm here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat on another one, on the other side of the table. — It was good of you to wake up, brother, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely been eating and drinking for four days. In fact, they were feeding you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense — some sort of blow to the head, perhaps. Some nervous rubbish, he says, because the rations were bad, not enough beer and horseradish were issued, hence the illness, but it's nothing, it will pass and be ground down.
