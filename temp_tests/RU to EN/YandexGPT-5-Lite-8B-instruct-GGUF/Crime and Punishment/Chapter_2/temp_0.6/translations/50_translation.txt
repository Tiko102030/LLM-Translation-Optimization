 Further, in a recess of the yard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, coach or locksmith, or something like that; coal dust was blackened almost from the very gates everywhere. “That’s where I would throw it and leave!” — he suddenly thought. Not noticing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gate and immediately saw a trough attached to the fence right by the gate (as often happens in such houses where there are many factory workers, artels, cab drivers, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, an eternal joke for such cases was written in chalk: “It is forbidden to park a cart here.” Therefore, it’s already good that there is no suspicion that he зашёл and stopped. “Here I can throw everything all at once into a heap and leave!”

Looking around once more, he had already stuck his hand in his pocket when suddenly, near the outer wall, between the gate and the trough, where the distance was only an arshin wide, he noticed a large rough stone, perhaps about a pound and a half in weight, adjacent directly to the stone street wall. Beyond this wall was the street, the sidewalk, you could hear passers-by scurrying around, of whom there were always many; but no one could see him beyond the gate, unless someone came from the street, which, however, was very likely to happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top firmly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. Under the stone a small depression formed; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it.
