 He didn't answer and was holding the paper in his hands without opening it. “Well, don’t get up,” Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. “If you’re sick, then don’t go out: you won’t burn. What do you have there in your hands?” He glanced: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he recalled that when half-awake from the fever, he would grip all this tightly in his hand and then fall asleep again. “Look at him, he’s collected such rags and sleeps with them, as if they were treasure...” And Nastasya burst into her painful, nervous laughter. He instantly shoved everything under his overcoat and stared intently at her. Although he could barely think clearly at that moment, he felt that people wouldn’t treat a person the same way when they came to take him.
