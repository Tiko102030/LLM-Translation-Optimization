 Louise Ivanovna began curtseying hastily in all directions and, curtsying, backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into an imposing officer with a fresh open face and excellent thick blond sideburns. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the police inspector. Louise Ivanovna hurried to curtsy almost to the floor and flew out of the office in small quick steps, bouncing.  
“Thunder again, thunder and lightning, tornado, hurricane!” — Nikodim Fomich addressed Ilya Petrovich любезно and amicably, — “again they’ve upset my heart, started to boil again! Heard it from the stairs.”  
“Well, what of it!” – said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and even not “what,” but something like: “Oh, that”), moving with some papers to another table and shrugging his shoulders in a picturesque way with each step, — here you see sir: the gentleman is a writer, that is, a former student, does not pay money, issued bills, doesn’t clear out the apartment, continuous complaints come about them, and you took offence when I lit a cigarette in front of them! They themselves commit fraud, but here they are, look at them: now they’re in their most attractive form!  
“Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, never mind!”
