 But at that moment the door opened wide again, and Raskolnikov entered, bending slightly because he was tall. “What a naval cabin,” he shouted as he came in, “I always bang my forehead; they call this a flat too! And have you come to your senses, brother?” “He just regained consciousness,” said Nastasya. “Just regained consciousness,” chimed in the foreman again with a smile. “And who might you be, sir?” Razumikhin asked, suddenly addressing him. “You see, I’m Vrazumihin; not Razumikhin, as everyone calls me, but Vrazumihin, a student, son of a nobleman, and he is my friend.
