She minced on the spot and kept curtsying continuously, eagerly waiting for them to finally let her have her say, and she waited. “There was no noise or fighting у меня, Captain,” she suddenly began chattering like spilled peas, with a strong German accent, albeit in fluent Russian, “and there wasn’t any, any scandal, but they came пьяные, and that’s what I’ll tell you, Captain, it’s not my fault… I have a noble house, Captain, and noble treatment, Captain, and I always, always did not want any scandal. And they came completely пьяные, and then again asked for three drinks, and then one lifted his legs and started playing the piano with his feet, and that's not good at all in a noble house, and he broke the piano entirely, and there’s no manners at all here, I said. And he took a drink and began to push everyone from behind with a stick. And then how quickly I called for a janitor and Carl came, he took Karl and gave him a black eye, and Henriette also got a black eye, and they hit me in the cheek five times. And that’s so undignified in a noble house, Captain, and I cried out. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood at the window like a little pig, squealing; and it's shameful.”
