 Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt all broken up, his consciousness was with him. He feared being pursued, afraid that in half an hour, a quarter of an hour perhaps, there would already be instructions to follow him; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the evidence until then. It was necessary to deal with everything before even some strength and reasoning remained... Where to go? This had long been decided: "Throw everything into the ditch, make it sink, and that's the end of it." He had resolved this in his sleep еще ночью, in delirium, in those moments when, he remembered, several times he tried to get up and go: "faster, faster, and throw everything away." But throwing it all away turned out to be very difficult. He wandered along the embankment of Catherine Canal for half an hour already, or maybe more, and looked at the approaches to the ditch several times, where they met him. But there was no way to carry out his intention: either barges stood right by the entrances and washerwomen were washing laundry on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides of the embankments it could be seen, noticed: it is suspicious that a person has deliberately come down, stopped, and is throwing something into the water. What if the cases don't drown, but float?
