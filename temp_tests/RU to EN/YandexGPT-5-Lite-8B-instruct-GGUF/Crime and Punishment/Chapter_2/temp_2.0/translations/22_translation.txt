 — Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down, — he said to the gaudily dressed purple-red lady, who was still standing as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair рядом. “Ich danke,” she said and quietly sank onto the chair with silk noise. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like a balloon and took up almost half the room. There was a scent of perfume. But the lady obviously felt timid about occupying half the room and having such a strong perfume, although she smiled both meekly and brazenly at the same time, but with obvious anxiety. The траурная дама finally finished and began to get up. Suddenly, with some noise, quite smartly and turning his shoulders in a peculiar way with each step, an officer entered, threw his hat with a cockade on the table and sat down in an armchair. The lush lady jumped up from her seat as soon as she saw him, and began curtsying with some special kind of enthusiasm; but the officer did not pay the least attention to her, and she no longer dared to sit near him.
