 "Pieces of torn canvas will in no case arouse suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with an attention strained to the point of pain he began to look around again, on the floor and everywhere, lest he had forgotten something else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even simple reasoning was abandoning him, began to torment him unbearably. "What, is it already beginning, is this already torture coming? There, there, sure enough!" Indeed, scraps of fringe, which he had cut from his pants, were lying on the floor, in the middle of the room, for anyone to see! "But what's happening to me!" — he cried again like a lost soul. A strange thought occurred to him: that perhaps all his clothes were covered in blood, that perhaps there were many stains, but that he simply couldn’t see them, didn’t notice them because his reasoning had weakened, been fragmented... his mind was clouded… Suddenly he remembered that there was blood on the purse. "Bah! So, there must be blood in my pocket too, because I put the wet wallet in my pocket then!" He quickly turned out his pocket, and sure enough — there were traces, stains on the lining of the pocket! "Therefore, reason has not completely abandoned me yet, therefore, there is still reasoning and memory, since I caught myself and guessed it!" — he thought triumphantly, breathing deeply and joyfully with his whole chest — "it's just feverish weakness, delirium for a minute"— and he pulled out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
