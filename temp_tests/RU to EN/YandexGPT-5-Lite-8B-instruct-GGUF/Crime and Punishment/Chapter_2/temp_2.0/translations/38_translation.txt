 And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with constables but with his first friends, then even then, it seems, he would not have a single human word for them, his heart had become so empty all of a sudden. A gloomy feeling of painful, endless isolation and alienation was suddenly consciously expressed in his soul. It wasn't the baseness of his emotional outpourings to Ilya Petrovich, nor the meanness of Poruchik's triumph over him that suddenly turned his heart like that. Oh, what did he care now about his own meanness, about all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, reprimands, offices, and so on and so forth! If they even sentenced him to be burned at this moment, he wouldn't have moved even then, it is unlikely that he would have listened to the sentence attentively. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden and unprecedented was happening to him. It wasn't that he understood, but he clearly felt, with all the strength of his feeling, that not only with sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else he could no longer address himself to these people in the district office, and if it were all his own brothers and sisters rather than district poruchiks, then there would have been absolutely no need for him to address them in any case; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible sensation before this minute. And what was most tormenting — it was more of a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; an immediate feeling, the most painful feeling out of all the feelings he had experienced in his life so far. The clerk began to dictate to him the form of a response ordinary in such cases, that is, "I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property, and so on."
