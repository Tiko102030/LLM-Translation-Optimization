 He would just spit on someone, bite them, it seems, if anyone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he walked onto the embankment of Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. "This is where he lives, in this building," he thought. "What is this, I've come to Razumikhin by myself! Again the same story as then... And it’s very curious, however: have I really come by myself or was I just walking and dropped in here? It doesn’t matter; I said… the other day… that I would go to him the next day after that, well, what of it, I will go! As if I couldn’t come now..."

He went up to Razumikhin's apartment on the fifth floor. He was home in his room and was busy writing at that moment, he opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his threadbare robe, slippers on bare feet, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. Surprise was expressed on his face.
