 The pain from the whip утихла, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one restless and not quite clear thought occupied him now exclusively. He stood and stared into the distance for a long time and intently; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to the university, it often happened to him, perhaps a hundred times, to stop именно at this very same place on his way home, gaze fixedly at this truly magnificent panorama, and each time be almost surprised by one obscure and unresolvable impression of his own. An inexplicable chill always blew upon him from this magnificent panorama; it was filled with a mute and deaf spirit for him this gorgeous picture… He would marvel every time at his morose and mysterious impression and put off its explanation, not trusting himself, to the future. Now suddenly he abruptly recalled his previous questions and perplexities, and it seemed to him that he had remembered them now intentionally. It already struck him as дико и чудно that he had stopped in the same spot as before, as if indeed he had imagined that he could think about the same thing now as before, and be interested in the same old topics and pictures that he had been interested in… so recently. Even a little funny it made him feel and at the same time his chest tightened painfully. In some depth, down below, somewhere barely visible under his feet, everything from his past seemed to show itself to him now — his previous thoughts, tasks, themes, impressions, this whole panorama, himself, and all of it… It seemed to him that he was flying up somewhere and everything disappeared before his eyes… Having made an involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a two-rouble coin in his fist. He opened his hand, gazed intently at the coin, swung his arm and threw it into the water; then he turned and went home. It seemed to him that he had as if cut himself off from everyone and everything with scissors in that moment.
