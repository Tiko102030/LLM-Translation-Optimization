 “Not that at all!..”

Suddenly he stopped; a new, совершенно unexpected and extremely simple question knocked him off balance and bitterly amazed him:

“If indeed all this was done consciously, not stupidly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you got, why you went through all the suffering and deliberately went to such an ignoble, vile, low deed? You were going to throw it into the water just now, the wallet, along with all the things that you also haven't seen yet… How can this be?”

Yes, this is so; this is all so. Anyway, he knew this before, and this is not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided to throw it into the water at night, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that's how it should be, as if it could not be otherwise… Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; yes, perhaps it was already decided yesterday, in the same minute when he sat over the chest and dragged the cases out of it… But this is so!.. “This is because I am very ill,” he finally decided gloomily, “I have tortured and worn myself out, and don't know what I am doing… Both yesterday and the day before, all this time I tormented myself… I will recover and… won’t torment myself anymore… But what if I don't recover at all? Lord! How fed up I am with all this!..” He walked without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow distract himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to undertake. An irresistible new feeling took possession of him more and more almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical aversion to everything he met and encountered, stubborn, spiteful, hateful. Everyone who came across him was disgusting to him — their faces, gait, movements were disgusting to him. He would have spat on someone, probably bit someone if anyone talked to him…

He suddenly stopped when he went out onto the embankment of Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
