 He did not respond and was holding the paper in his hands without opening it. “Well, don’t get up,” Nastasya went on, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was putting his legs down from the sofa. “If you're sick, then don't go out: you won't burn. What do you have there?” He looked: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he recalled that when half-awakening in the heat, he would clutch all this tightly in his hand and fall asleep again. “Look at him, what rags he's collected and sleeps with them as if they were treasure...” And Nastasya burst out into her painfully neurotic laughter. He shoved everything under his overcoat in a moment and stared fixedly at her. Although he could hardly think clearly at that moment, he felt that they wouldn’t treat a person the same way when they came to take him.
