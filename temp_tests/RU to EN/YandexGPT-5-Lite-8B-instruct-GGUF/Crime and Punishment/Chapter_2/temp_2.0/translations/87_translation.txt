 — Shall we have some tea?   
— Yes, please some tea.  
— Pour it. Wait, I'll pour it for you myself; sit down at the table. He immediately ordered, poured, then poured another cup, abandoned his breakfast and sat down on the sofa again. As before, he embraced the sick man's head with his left hand, lifted him up and began to feed him tea with a teaspoon, continuously blowing on the spoon again, as if this process of blowing was the most important and salutary point of recovery. Raskolnikov remained silent and did not resist, despite the fact that he felt quite strong enough to lift himself and sit on the sofa without any посторонней help, and not only control his hands enough to hold a spoon or cup, but perhaps even walk. But for some strange, almost bestial cunning, it suddenly occurred to him to hide his strength until the time came, to lie low, to pretend, if necessary, not to understand everything yet, and at the same time to listen and find out what was going on here? However, he could not overcome his disgust: having drunk about ten spoonfuls of tea, he suddenly высвободил his head, pushed the spoon away capriciously and fell back onto the pillow. Under his heads there really were now real pillows — feather ones with clean pillowcases; he also noticed that and took it into consideration.
