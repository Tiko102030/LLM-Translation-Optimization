 — Don't shout at me! 
— I'm not shouting, I'm speaking quite evenly, but you are the one shouting at me; and I am a student and won't allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant got so angry that for a moment he couldn't even say anything, only some splutters came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. 
— Be so good as to keep quiet! You are in the presence. Don't b-r-bble, sir! 
— And you are in the presence, too, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides, while you are shouting, you smoke a cigarette, thus showing disrespect to all of us. Having said that, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable delight. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
