 But no, he hears too clearly! But, therefore, they will come to him now, if so, "because... right, all this is the same thing... because of yesterday... Oh my God!" He would have liked to lock himself in with a hook, but his hand did not rise up... and it was useless! Fear surrounded his soul like ice, tortured him, stiffened him... But finally, after ten minutes or so, all that noise gradually began to subside. The landlady moaned and groaned, Ilya Petrovich still threatened and swore... But finally, it seems, he too fell silent; now you can't hear him either; "could he have left! Oh my God!" Yes, the landlady is also leaving, still moaning and crying... and her door slammed shut... And the crowd dispersed downstairs to their apartments — they gasp, argue, call out to each other, now raising their speech to shouting, then lowering it to a whisper. There must have been a lot of them; almost the whole house had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!"
Raskolnikov fell powerlessly on the sofa, but could no longer close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feelings of boundless horror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a plate of soup. After looking at him carefully and seeing that he was awake, she put the candle on the table and began to arrange what she brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
