He felt somehow ashamed. “Write then,” said the clerk to Raskolnikov. “What to write?” he asked rather rudely. “And I will dictate to you.” It seemed to Raskolnikov that the clerk had become more careless and contemptuous with him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly found himself completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened somehow in one moment, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, of course, he would have been surprised at how he could speak to them like that a moment ago, and even impose himself with his feelings?
