 He only remembered how he took a sip of cold water and spilled it from the mug onto his chest. Then unconsciousness set in. III
He, however, was not really completely unconscious throughout the entire time of his illness: it was a febrile state, with delirium and semi-consciousness. He later remembered many things. At times it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him away and carry him off somewhere, they argued and quarreled a lot about him. Suddenly he was alone in the room, everyone had left and were afraid of him, and only occasionally slightly opened the door to look at him, threatened him, conspired among themselves about something, laughed and teased him. He often remembered Nastasya by his side; he could distinguish one more person, very familiar to him as it seemed, but who именно — he could not guess and was longing for that, even cried. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times - that the same day was going on. But about that - he completely forgot about that; however, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that one should not forget — he was tormented, suffered, trying to remember, moaned, fell into a rage or into terrible, unbearable fear. Then he would pull himself together, wanted to run, but someone always stopped him by force, and he would fall back into helplessness and unconsciousness.
