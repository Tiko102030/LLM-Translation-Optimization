 He breathed more freely. "Perhaps it's not right!" Gradually he began to take heart, he tried with all his might to encourage himself and come to his senses. "Some stupidity, some slight carelessness, and I can give myself away! Hm… it’s a pity there’s no air here," he added, "it's stuffy... My head is even more dizzy... my mind too..."

He felt a terrible mess in everything. He himself was afraid of not controlling himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The clerk was very interesting to him: he always wanted to guess something from his face, to figure him out. This was a very young man, about twenty-two years old, with a dark complexion and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his age, dressed fashionably and smartly, with parting at the back of his head, combed and slicked down, with many rings on his white fingers cleaned with brushes and gold chains around his waist. He even said a word or two in French with one foreigner who was there, and very satisfactorily. “Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down,” he said to the elegantly dressed crimson lady who was standing as if she didn't dare sit herself, although there was a chair nearby.
