 “If they ask, I might tell,” he thought as he approached the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away from him. It had just moved to a new address, into a new building, on the fourth floor. He had visited the old location once briefly, but it was very long ago. Entering through the gate, he saw a staircase to his right, up which a peasant was walking with a book in hand: “That must be the caretaker; this must be the office,” and he began to climb upstairs at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. “I’ll go in, kneel down and tell them everything…” — he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The stairs were narrow, steep, and covered in slop. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and remained there for almost the whole day. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
