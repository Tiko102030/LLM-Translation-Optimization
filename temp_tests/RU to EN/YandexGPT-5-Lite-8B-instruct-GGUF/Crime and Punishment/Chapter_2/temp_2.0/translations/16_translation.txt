 My head was dizzy and hurt from the heat. "This is trickery! They want to lure me in with cunning and then bring me down at full speed," he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something stupid..."

On the stairs, he remembered that he was leaving all his things just like that, in the wallpaper hole — "and they probably searched without him on purpose," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if I can say, cynicism of doom suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went further. "If only it would be over поскорее!.."

Outside, the heat was unbearable again; there hadn't been a single drop of rain in all these days. Again dust, brick and lime, again stench from shops and taverns, again drunken people every minute, Finnish peddlers and half-ruined carriage drivers. The sun flashed brightly into his eyes so that it hurt to look and his head felt completely dizzy — the usual sensation of a feverish person going outside suddenly on a bright sunny day. Reaching the turn onto yesterday's street, he glanced at it with excruciating anxiety, towards that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, perhaps I will say," he thought, approaching the office.
