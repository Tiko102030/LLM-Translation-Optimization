 After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center was damaged by hurricanes, including Hurricanes Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place — the missions "Endeavor" and "Atlantis". After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launching Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as manned Dragon spacecraft. Pad LC-39B is being upgraded for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The center's visitor complex is a museum and entertainment area with guided tours, IMAX cinemas and bus tours around the territory. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on the territory there is the Astronaut Memorial "Space Mirror", where the names of погибших astronauts are immortalized.
