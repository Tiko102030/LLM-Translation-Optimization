 A launch complex No. 39 was built for her with a large-scale infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transport systems. From 1967 to 1973, 13 launches of the Saturn V were carried out. The Apollo 1 crew tragedy occurred during the program. Subsequently, manned flights were conducted, including Apollo 11, during which humans first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used to launch the Skylab station and missions of the Apollo-Soyuz program. Titan and Atlas-Centaur rockets were also used to launch satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first launch of the Columbia shuttle took place in 1981. A landing strip 4.6 km long is located here. After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
