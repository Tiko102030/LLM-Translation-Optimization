 And the local authorities used the right to forcibly отчуждать private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. First coined by the Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, the term "the right to forcibly отчуждать private property" was recorded in English law with the help of J. Locke, who based it on "natural law" as a coincidence of the will of the entire population, which granted all powers to the legislative bodies of the state. (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and interpreters of the law fully expanded the concept, simply basing themselves on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
