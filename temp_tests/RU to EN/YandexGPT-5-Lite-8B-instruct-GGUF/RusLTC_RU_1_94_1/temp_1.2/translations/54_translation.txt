 Instrumental, competing "property rights" can never be absolute, must never be an end in themselves. Since some rights should have priority, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some people still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but modern judicial conservatism has not changed US legislation in any significant way. Change would require courts to completely change the history of legal reasoning according to which property is seen as a social institution, not an end in itself. It would also require them to place abstract property above the needs of living people.
