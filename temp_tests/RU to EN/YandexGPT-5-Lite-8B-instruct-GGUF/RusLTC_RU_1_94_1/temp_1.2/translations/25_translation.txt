 The New York court used the instrumental approach employed in the case "Callender v. Marsh" of Massachusetts in order to reject Queckenbush's claims. The court argued: "Any significant public improvement must necessarily affect more or less the comfort and property of people; and in a case where the damage sustained has a remote causal connection and is predictable, no compensation is recognized for the damage, and the amount of damage itself should be considered as part of the price paid for the future benefits of this public improvement." In the end, the court concluded: "this case is based on the principle that the general good must prevail over private interests." State support for private entrepreneurship.
