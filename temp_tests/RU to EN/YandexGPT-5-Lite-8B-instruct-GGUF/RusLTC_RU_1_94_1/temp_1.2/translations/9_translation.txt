 Referring to the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature that grants us property also limits the extent of this property. "God has given us everything abundantly" — this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But why did he give it to us? For enjoyment. A person has the right to turn into his own property as much as he can use for any needs of his life, before this item deteriorates. And what goes beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for spoilage or destruction. However, to Locke's огорчение, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal ownership of land", to possess power over others. Therefore, restrictions on the accumulation of property are needed in order to guarantee everyone access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's property theory contributed to the emergence of discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, subsequently other authors joined these considerations, including the well-known American radical thinker, Thomas Paine.
