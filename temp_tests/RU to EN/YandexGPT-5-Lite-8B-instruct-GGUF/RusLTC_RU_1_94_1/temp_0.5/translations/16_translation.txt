 And the local authorities used the right to forcibly отчуждать private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "the right to forcibly отчуждать private property," invented by the Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, was incorporated into English law with the help of J. Locke, who based it on "natural law" as a coincidence of the will of the entire population that granted all powers to the legislative bodies of the state (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters have fully expanded the concept, simply based on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property shall be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
