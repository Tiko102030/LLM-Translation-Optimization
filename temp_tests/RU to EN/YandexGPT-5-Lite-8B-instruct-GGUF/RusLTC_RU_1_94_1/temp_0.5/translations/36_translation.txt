The US Supreme Court was slow to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in Butchers' Benevolent Association of New Orleans v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Co. This case came after the Louisiana legislature limited the slaughter of livestock in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances previously prescribed in many other cities. Citing the decision of Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court Chief Justice Shaw in Commonwealth v. Algar (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court upheld the law, finding that "the loss of private right in this case is of no consequence in view of the general profit and trade turnover of this commercial organization."
