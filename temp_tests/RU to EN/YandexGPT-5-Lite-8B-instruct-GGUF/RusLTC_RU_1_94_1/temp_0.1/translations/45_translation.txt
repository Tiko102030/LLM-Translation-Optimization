The New Benefits of the New Deal
Gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the Great Depression of the 1930s. Uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and New Deal policies, a new generation of liberals contributed to the state helping those in need and overcoming the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the conclusions of the Supreme Court in 1937, even mentioning the "constitutional revolution of 1937" after two conservative judges resigned when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened to "remove" the Supreme Court.
