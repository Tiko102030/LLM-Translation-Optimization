 And the local authorities used the right to compulsory отчуждение of private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of owners of private property. First invented by the Dutch scientist, Hugo Grotius, in 1625, the term "the right to compulsory отчуждение of private property" was recorded in English law with the help of J. Locke, who put it at the basis of "natural law", as a coincidence of the will of the entire population, which granted all powers to the legislative bodies of the state. (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and interpreters of the law fully expanded the concept, based simply on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property shall be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Elie 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
