The US Supreme Court was slow to criticize government regulation, but Justice Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Butchers' Benevolent Association of New Orleans v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Co." This case came about after the Louisiana state legislature restricted the slaughter of livestock in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances prescribed before in many other cities. Citing the decision of Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court Chief Justice Shaw in "Commonwealth v. Algar" (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court upheld the law, finding that "the loss of private rights in this case is of no importance in view of the general profit and trade turnover of this commercial organization."
