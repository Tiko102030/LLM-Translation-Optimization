In the case of 1875, Kohl v. United States, it was ruled that the right to forcibly отчуждать private property is “the result of political necessity, and it cannot be separated from sovereign power”. The right to forcibly alienate private property was one example of using governmental power to promote the development of a "well-organized society", to increase the quality of "public good" or "welfare" through regulations concerning interference with the rights of property owners. These regulations reflected principles of common law, such as: "the welfare of the people shall be the supreme law" (Latin: salus populi suprema lex est) and "use your own so as not to harm another" (Latin: sic utere tuo ut alienum non laedas). Frequently quoted in court rulings, these fundamental principles serve as "programs of common law management in a well-regulated society", providing a legal basis for comprehensive government regulation of property in order to reflect the mutual interests and rights of all citizens (see Novak 1996, p. 42).
