However, most people became rich through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to take the land of indigenous peoples. Even the War for Independence in 1776, a war fought in the name of liberal ideals, was an example of mass robberies, including the forced redistribution of two-thirds of the property in New York and its suburbs and most of the land plots in Queens, Richmond, Kings and Suffolk counties (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
