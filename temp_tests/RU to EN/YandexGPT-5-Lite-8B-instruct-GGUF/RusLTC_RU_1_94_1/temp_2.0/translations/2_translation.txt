 The free trade market will lead to the same effective result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, as a result of which many economists abandoned the idea of income distribution and focused on the values ​​of efficiency of various legal regimes. However, there are a number of issues related to these statements. A separate consideration of price and income distribution takes place only when income distribution will affect both price levels and production structure. This will happen when economic agents have various preferences and when returns to scale increase or decrease. In addition, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or the expenses associated with negotiating and enforcing contracts. This can be extremely important when concluding a trade deal involving externalities, especially when it often turns out to be problematic to persuade a large number of people to bribe someone who pollutes the environment. The definition of property rights is often regarded in liberal democracy quite controversially, just as in the United States people have both personal freedom and the right of the majority to govern the country. (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
