In this case, the final decision in the Mann case was also mentioned, and it was delivered by Chief Justice Waite. However, attention must be paid to the last statement considered earlier: "this is only a question of severity." This limitation, written by Judge Oliver Wendell Holmes, consciously reintroduced an instrumental vision of property rights, which declared the previous fifty years of judicial practice unconstitutional. In the Lochner case, Holmes has already expressed his opinion. Beginning his speech with the fact that "the decision was made on the basis of economic theory, which, however, does not suit most of the country's population," he added: 
Everyone's right to act according to their wishes for as long as possible, provided, of course, that they do not violate others' right to do the same, does not correspond to the basic tenets of law schools, post offices, laws of any of the states or other municipal institution that takes his money to achieve goals that seem necessary, whether he wants it or not. The Fourteenth Amendment does not take into account the Social Statics by Mr. Herbert Spencer. Unlike Lochner's despotic protection of intensive property rights, Holmes believed that competing interests require achieving balance. Having lost the Lochner case, he carefully chose his words to launch a new case for an alternative law.
