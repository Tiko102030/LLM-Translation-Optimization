 Referring to the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature which in this way gives us property also limits the size of that property. "God has given us everything abundantly" — this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But why did He give it to us? For enjoyment. Man has the right to convert into his own so much through his labour as he can use for any needs of his life before that object decays. And what goes beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for damage or destruction. However, to Locke's огорчению, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal possession of land", to possess power over others. Therefore, restrictions on the accumulation of property are necessary in order to guarantee everyone access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to the emergence of discussions about natural rights as a limitation on individual accumulation, subsequently other authors joined these рассуждений, including the famous American radical thinker, Thomas Paine.
