However, Locke defends the right to property not as решительно as one might understand from the famous passage quoted above (see Horn 1990; Ketzelson 1998, p. 77). In this passage, he addresses the issue of the right of the Stuart monarchs of England to levy taxes without the consent of Parliament. But besides this issue, Locke recognizes a social definition of property, according to which property rights must be limited by other natural rights. Locke said that everyone should have as much as they can use. Accumulation must be limited in order to ensure that everyone can share the bounty of nature, leaving "enough and as good" for others. Referring to the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature that gives us property in this way also limits the size of that property.
