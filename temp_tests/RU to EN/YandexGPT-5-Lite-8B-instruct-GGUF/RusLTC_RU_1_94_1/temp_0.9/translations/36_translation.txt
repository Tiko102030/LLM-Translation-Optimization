The US Supreme Court was not quick to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Charity Butchers' Association of New Orleans v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter House Firm". This case arose after the Louisiana State Legislature restricted livestock slaughter in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to regulations prescribed before in many other cities. Citing the decision of Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court Chief Justice Shaw in "Commonwealth v. Algar" (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court upheld the law, establishing that "losses of private right in this case are of no importance in view of the general profit and trade turnover of this commercial organization".
