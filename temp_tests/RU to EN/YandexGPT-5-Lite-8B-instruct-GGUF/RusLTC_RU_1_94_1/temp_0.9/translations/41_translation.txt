The final decision in the Mann case was also mentioned here, and it was made by Chief Justice Waite. However, attention should be paid to the last statement considered earlier: "it is only a question of severity." This limitation, written by Judge Oliver Wendell Holmes, consciously reintroduced an instrumental vision of property rights, which declared previous fifty years of judicial practice unconstitutional. In the Lochner case, Holmes had already expressed his opinion. Beginning his speech with the fact that "the decision in the case was made according to the basics of economic theory, which, however, does not suit most of the country's population," he added:

Everyone's right to act as they please for as long as possible, provided, of course, that one's rights are not violated by others doing the same thing, does not correspond to the basic postulates of law schools, the postal service, the laws of any of the states or other municipal institution that take his money to achieve goals that seem necessary, regardless of whether he wants it or not. The Fourteenth Amendment does not take into account the Social Statics by Mr. Herbert Spencer. Unlike Lochner's despotic protection of intensive property rights, Holmes believed that competing interests require achieving a balance. Having lost the Lochner case, he carefully chose his words to initiate a new case for alternative law.
