 Instrumental, competing "property rights" can never be absolute, should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but modern judicial conservatives have not changed US legislation in any significant way. Change would require courts to completely rewrite the history of legal reasoning according to which property is viewed as a social institution, not an end in itself. It would also require them to place abstract property above the needs of living people.
