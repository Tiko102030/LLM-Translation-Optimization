 The property of the British was valued even less: debts to English creditors were, without much ado, annulled; some states, including Virginia, closed the cases scheduled for hearing on claims filed by British subjects (see Alee 1998, p. 35). Widespread confiscation of the property of opponents of independence from England did not bode well for the security of property in the new republic. The leading rebels fought to curb what they saw as "an excess of democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60).
