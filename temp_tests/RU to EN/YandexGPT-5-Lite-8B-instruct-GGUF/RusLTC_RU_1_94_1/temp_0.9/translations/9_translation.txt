 Referring to the Bible, Locke argues that the same law of nature which in this way gives us property also limits the extent of this property. "God has given us everything abundantly" — this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But why did he give it to us? For enjoyment. A person has the right to convert by his labour into his own so much as he can use for some needs of his life, before this item deteriorates. And what goes beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for spoilage or destruction. However, to Locke's огорчение, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolise "disproportionate and unequal possession of land", to have power over others. Therefore, restrictions on the accumulation of property are necessary in order to guarantee everyone access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's property theory contributed to the emergence of discussions about natural rights as restrictions on individual accumulation, later other authors joined these arguments, including the famous American radical thinker Thomas Paine.
