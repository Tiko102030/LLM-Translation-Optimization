 A free trade market will lead to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, as a result many economists abandoned the idea of income distribution and focused on the implications of different legal regimes' efficiency. However, there are a number of questions related to these statements. The separate consideration of price and income distribution only takes place when income distribution will influence both the price level and the structure of production. This will happen when economic agents have various preferences and when returns to scale increase or decrease. In addition, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses associated with negotiation and enforcement of contracts. This can be crucial in concluding a trade deal involving externalities, especially when it is often problematic to encourage a large number of people to bribe someone who pollutes the environment. The definition of property rights is often regarded quite contradictory in liberal democracy, just as in the United States people have both personal freedom and the majority's right to govern the country (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
