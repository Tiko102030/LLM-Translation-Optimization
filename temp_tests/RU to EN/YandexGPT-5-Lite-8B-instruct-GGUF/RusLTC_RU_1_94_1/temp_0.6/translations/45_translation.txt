The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the Great Depression of the 1930s. Uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and New Deal policies, a new generation of liberals contributed to the government helping those in need and getting out of the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights.
