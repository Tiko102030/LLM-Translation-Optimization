In this case, the final decision in the Mann case was also mentioned, and it was delivered by Chief Justice Waite. However, attention should be paid to the last statement considered earlier: "it is only a matter of degree." This restriction, written by Judge Oliver Wendell Holmes, consciously reintroduced an instrumental vision of property rights, which declared previous fifty years of judicial practice unconstitutional. In the Lochner case, Holmes had already expressed his opinion. Beginning his speech with the fact that "the decision in the case was made according to the fundamentals of economic theory, which, however, do not suit most of the country's population," he added:
Everyone's right to act as they please for as long as possible, provided, of course, that one does not violate the rights of others to do the same, does not correspond to the basic tenets of law schools, postal services, laws of any of the states or other municipal institutions that take his money to achieve goals that seem necessary, regardless of whether he wants it or not. The Fourteenth Amendment does not take into account Mr. Herbert Spencer's Social Statics. Unlike Lochner's despotic defense of intense property rights, Holmes believed that competing interests required a balance. Having lost the Lochner case, he carefully chose his words to initiate a new case for alternative law.
