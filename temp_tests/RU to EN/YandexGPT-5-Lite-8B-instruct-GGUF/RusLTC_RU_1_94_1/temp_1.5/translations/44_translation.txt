In this regard, in the Pennsylvania Coal case, Holmes скорее acted as a Trojan Horse rather than a winner-protector of sacred property rights. Instead of considering the right to property as a natural right, Holmes обосновал the "principle of expropriation" of property based on establishing an equilibrium between competing interests. Compensation for losses will depend on the circumstances and will be an optional procedure. According to Holmes, property must be constantly "reasonably" used, but the idea of what is "reasonable" remains blurred. If actions go "too far," then compensation for damage will be necessary in this case; however, it will be up to the judges, taking into account the presence of public needs and special circumstances, to determine what "too far" means. Neither private property owners nor the public have absolute rights; in each case, judges must carefully assess and balance the competing interests of both parties. Owners should not, in turn, alienate everyone. Holmes утверждали that "the government can hardly continue its activities any further if the issue of property is not resolved to some extent".
