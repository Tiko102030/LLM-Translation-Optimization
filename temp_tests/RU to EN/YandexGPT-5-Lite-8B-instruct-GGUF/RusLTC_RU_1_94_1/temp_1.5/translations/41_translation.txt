In this case, the final decision in the Mann case was also mentioned, and it was made by Chief Justice Waite. However, it is necessary to pay attention to the last statement considered earlier: "it is only a question of severity". This limitation, written by Judge Oliver Wendell Holmes, deliberately reinstated an instrumental vision of property rights once again, which declared previous fifty years of judicial practice unconstitutional. In the Lochner case, Holmes had already expressed his opinion. Starting his speech with the fact that "the decision was made according to the fundamentals of economic theory, which, however, does not suit most of the population", he added:
The right of every person to act as they please for as long as possible, provided, of course, that one's own actions do not violate the rights of others to do the same, does not correspond to the basic postulates of law schools, postal authorities, laws of any state or other municipal institution that takes their money to achieve goals that seem necessary, regardless of whether they want it or not. The Fourteenth Amendment does not take into account Mr. Herbert Spencer's Social Statics. Unlike Lochner's despotic defense of intense property rights, Holmes believed that competing interests require achieving balance. Having lost the Lochner case, he carefully chose his words to initiate a new case for alternative law.
