 The property of the British was even less respected: debts to English creditors were summarily canceled; some states, including Virginia, closed cases designated for hearing on claims filed by British subjects (see Eli 1998, p. 35). The widespread confiscation of the property of opponents of independence from England did not bode well for the security of property in the new republic. Major rebels fought to curb what they saw as "an excess of democracy" (see Tomlins 1993, p. 60).
