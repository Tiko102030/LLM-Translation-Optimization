 The new approach was neither a general criticism of property rights nor a general affirmation of these rights. Instead, through the redistribution of rights, it merely established some ownership in comparison to other property. According to one of the legal historians, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the rule of law in the United States of America — «the release of all human creative energy». Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation to neighbours for indirect losses rests on the shoulders of entrepreneurs, they still have the opportunity to embark on profitable projects. In theory, if a project were more expensive than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase, even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice, the entire burden is shifted onto the affected party, which only further contributes to an increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, while all the risk and operating costs fall on the shoulders of neighbours. If property rights were observed to a greater legal extent, then high operating costs would prevent the implementation of an entrepreneur's plans. Thus, by saving on the cost of risk and operating expenses for entrepreneurs, the new principle has only further contributed to the development of entrepreneurship.
