 Instrumental, competing "property rights" can never be absolute, they should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that a return to the discussion of natural rights will happen, but modern society's judicial conservatives have not changed US legislation in any significant way. Change would require courts to completely reverse the history of legal rationale under which property is seen as a social institution and not an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
