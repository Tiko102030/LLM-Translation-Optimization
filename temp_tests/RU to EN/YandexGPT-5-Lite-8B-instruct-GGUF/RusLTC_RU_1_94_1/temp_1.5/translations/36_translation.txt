The U.S. Supreme Court was in no hurry to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Charitable Butchers' Association of New Orleans v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House." This case came after the Louisiana legislature restricted livestock slaughter in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to regulations prescribed before in many other cities. Citing the decision of Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court Chief Justice Shaw in the case "Commonwealth v. Algar" (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court upheld the law, establishing that "the loss of private law in this case is of no importance in view of the common profit and trade turnover of this commercial organization."
