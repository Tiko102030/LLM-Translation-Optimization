The US Supreme Court was slow to criticize state regulation, but Justice Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in the case "Butchers' Benevolent Association of New Orleans v. Crescent City Live-Stock Landing & Slaughter-House Co." This case came about after the Louisiana legislature restricted slaughterhouses in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances previously enacted in many other cities. Citing the decision of Massachusetts Supreme Court Chief Justice Shaw in "Commonwealth v. Alger" (1851), the Louisiana Supreme Court upheld the law, finding that "the loss of private right in this case is unimportant in view of the overall profit and trade turnover of this commercial organization."
