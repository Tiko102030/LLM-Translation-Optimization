 And local authorities have exercised the right to the compulsory отчуждение of private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. First coined by the Dutch scientist Hugo Grotius in 1625, the term "the right to the compulsory alienation of private property" was incorporated into English law with the help of J. Locke, who based it on "natural law" as a coincidence of the will of the entire population, which granted all powers to the legislative bodies of the state. (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and interpreters of the law have fully expanded the concept simply based on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property shall be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Alee 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
