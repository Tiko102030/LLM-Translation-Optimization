In fact, the Thirteenth Amendment закрепляет in the Constitution of the United States the principle of property confiscation. According to this amendment, the безвозмездная confiscation of the property of slaves redistributed almost 3 billion US dollars, half of all the wealth of the southern states. In this regard, the emancipation of slaves in America became one of the most significant examples in history of property confiscation. Government intervention in private property did not end with these confiscations. During the colonial and pre-war periods, regional governments and local authorities regularly interfered in matters of private property, thereby only reaffirming the widespread belief that the government has a moral duty to promote a prosperous economy and support poor citizens. For example, most local governments set transportation tariffs and bread prices, limited food trade, regulated wages and employment opportunities, and restricted the activities of sellers of alcoholic beverages, bars, and prostitutes (see Handlin and Handlin 1947; Burdin 1989; Novak 1996; Lory 1999; Levi 2000). And local authorities used the right to forcibly отчуждать private property in order to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners.
