 INTRODUCTION: the concept of property in economic theory and social practice
Society owns property. Economic systems differ in terms of the definition they give to the concept of property, and often the boundaries are difficult to define. Some believe that property rights extend to objects such as land and trees, including the most perishable objects such as wildlife and running water. Others supplement this notion with the утверждение that property rights also apply to objects such as ideas (patents), some also include humans themselves as a slave. Societies also differ depending on the rights that property owners have; a set of privileges relating to responsibility for taking any actions towards other people. These rights prevent us from considering the law as providing "greater" or "lesser" protection of property, because each use affects others' property. Property has dimensions because it affects human relationships; I have the right to dispose of my property, which reduces your right to be completely free. For example, when polluting the environment, neighbors' rights to clean air and water are significantly reduced; rights that prohibit polluting the environment limit the rights of this property but protect the property of the neighbors. Society, on the one hand, does not prohibit the owner from using his land and water resources, even though it may cause damage to the neighbors' property.
