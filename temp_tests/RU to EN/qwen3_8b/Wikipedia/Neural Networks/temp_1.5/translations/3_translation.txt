On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of learning parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the learning parameters of the neural network. This stage is particularly important for networks trained with a teacher. The correct choice of parameters not only affects how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers. For example, choosing a low learning rate increases the convergence time, although it sometimes allows avoiding network paralysis. Increasing the momentum may lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Based on such conflicting influence of parameters, one can conclude that their values should be chosen experimentally, guided by the criterion of training completion (for example, minimizing error or limiting training time). Neural Network Training

During training, the network reviews the training sample in a certain order. The viewing order can be sequential, random, etc. Some networks trained without a teacher (for example, Hopfield networks) review the sample only once. Others (for example, Kohonen networks), as well as networks trained with a teacher, review the sample multiple times, with one full pass through the sample being called an epoch of training.
