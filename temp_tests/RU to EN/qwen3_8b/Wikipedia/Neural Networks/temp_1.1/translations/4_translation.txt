Other networks (e.g., Kohonen networks), as well as networks trained with a teacher, iterate through the dataset multiple times, with one full pass through the dataset being called an epoch of training. During supervised learning, the dataset is split into two parts — the training data and the validation data; the principle of division can be arbitrary. Training data are fed to the network for training, while validation data are used to calculate the network's error (validation data are never used for training the network). Thus, if the error on the validation data decreases, the network is indeed generalizing. If the error on the training data continues to decrease while the error on the validation data increases, it means the network has stopped generalizing and is simply "memorizing" the training data. This phenomenon is called overfitting. In such cases, training is usually stopped. Other problems may arise during training, such as paralysis or the network getting stuck in a local minimum of the error surface. It is impossible to predict in advance which problem will occur or to give definitive recommendations for resolving them. All the above applies only to iterative algorithms for searching neural network solutions.
