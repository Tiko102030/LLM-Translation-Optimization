There are several ways to improve the "perception" of a network. Additionally, the representation of both input and output data plays a significant role. Suppose a network is trained to recognize letters on images and has a single numerical output—representing the letter's position in the alphabet. In this case, the network may form an incorrect understanding that letters with numbers 1 and 2 are more similar than letters with numbers 1 and 3, which is generally incorrect. To avoid such situations, networks with a larger number of outputs are used, where each output has its own meaning. The more outputs a network has, the greater the distance between classes, and the harder it is to confuse them. Experimental tuning of network characteristics

After selecting the overall structure, it is necessary to experimentally tune the network parameters. For networks similar to perceptrons, this includes the number of layers, the number of blocks in hidden layers (for Word networks), the presence or absence of bypass connections, and neuron activation functions. When choosing the number of layers and neurons within them, one should consider that a network's ability to generalize increases with a higher total number of connections between neurons. On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the size of the training data set.
