On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of training parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the training parameters of the neural network. This stage is particularly important for networks trained with a teacher. The correct choice of parameters depends not only on how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers. For example, choosing a low learning rate increases the convergence time, but sometimes allows avoiding network paralysis. Increasing the momentum of learning can lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Given this contradictory influence of parameters, it can be concluded that their values should be chosen experimentally, guided by the criterion of training completion (e.g., minimizing error or limiting training time). Network training

During training, the network examines the training sample in a certain order. The order of viewing can be sequential, random, and so on. Some networks trained without a teacher (e.g., Hopfield networks) examine the sample only once. Others (e.g., Kohonen networks), as well as networks trained with a teacher, examine the sample multiple times, with one complete pass through the sample called an epoch of training.
