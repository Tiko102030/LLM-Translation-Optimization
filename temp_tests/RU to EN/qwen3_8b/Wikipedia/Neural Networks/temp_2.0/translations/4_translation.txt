Other networks (such as Kohonen networks), as well as supervised networks, repeatedly review the dataset; one complete pass through the data is called an epoch. During supervised learning, the dataset is split into two parts—a training dataset and test data; the method of division can be arbitrary. The training data is fed to the network for learning, while the test data is used to calculate the network's error (test data is never used for training the network). Thus, if the error on the test data decreases, the network genuinely performs generalization. If the error on the training data continues to decrease while the error on the test data increases, this indicates the network has stopped generalizing and is simply "memorizing" the training data. This phenomenon is called overfitting. In such cases, training is typically stopped. During the training process, other problems may arise, such as paralysis or the network getting stuck in a local minimum of the error surface. It is impossible to predict in advance which problem will occur or to provide definitive recommendations for resolving them. All the above relates solely to iterative algorithms for searching neural network solutions.
