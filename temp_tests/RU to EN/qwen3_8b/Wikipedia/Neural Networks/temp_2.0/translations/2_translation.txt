There are several ways to improve the "perception" of the network. Additionally, the representation of both input and output data plays a significant role. Suppose the network is trained to recognize letters on images and has a single numerical output representing the letter's position in the alphabet. In this case, the network may form an incorrect assumption that letters with numbers 1 and 2 are more similar than those with numbers 1 and 3, which is generally incorrect. To avoid such situations, a network with a larger number of output nodes is used, where each output has its own meaning. The more output nodes in the network, the greater the distance between classes, and the more difficult it becomes to confuse them. Experimental adjustment of network characteristics  

After selecting the overall structure, it is necessary to experimentally adjust the network's parameters. For networks similar to the perceptron, this includes the number of layers, the number of blocks in hidden layers (for Word networks), the presence or absence of bypass connections, and the activation functions of neurons. When choosing the number of layers and neurons within them, one should base the decision on the fact that the network's ability to generalize increases with a higher total number of connections between neurons. On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the amount of data in the training set.
