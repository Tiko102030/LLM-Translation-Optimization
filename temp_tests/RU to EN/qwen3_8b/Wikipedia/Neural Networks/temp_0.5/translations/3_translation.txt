On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of training parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the parameters of the neural network. This stage is particularly important for networks trained with a teacher. The correct choice of parameters not only affects how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers but also determines the overall efficiency of the learning process. For example, choosing a low learning rate increases the convergence time, although it may sometimes prevent the network from getting stuck in a local minimum. Increasing the momentum can lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Given this contradictory influence of parameters, it can be concluded that their values should be chosen experimentally, guided by a completion criterion (such as minimizing error or limiting training time). Neural network training

During the training process, the network reviews the training data in a certain order. The order of review can be sequential, random, etc. Some networks trained without a teacher (e.g., Hopfield networks) review the data only once. Others (e.g., Kohonen networks) as well as networks trained with a teacher review the data multiple times, with one complete pass through the data being called a training epoch.
