On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of training parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the training parameters of the neural network. This stage is particularly important for networks trained with a teacher. The correct choice of parameters not only affects how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers but also determines the overall efficiency of the training process. For example, choosing a low learning rate increases the convergence time, although it may sometimes prevent the network from getting stuck in a deadlock. Increasing the momentum can lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Based on such contradictory effects of the parameters, it can be concluded that their values should be chosen experimentally, guided by the training termination criterion (for example, minimizing the error or limiting the training time). Neural network training

During the training process, the network reviews the training data in a certain order. The order of review can be sequential, random, etc. Some networks trained without a teacher (for example, Hopfield networks) review the data only once. Others (for example, Kohonen networks), as well as networks trained with a teacher, review the data multiple times, with one full pass through the data being called an epoch.
