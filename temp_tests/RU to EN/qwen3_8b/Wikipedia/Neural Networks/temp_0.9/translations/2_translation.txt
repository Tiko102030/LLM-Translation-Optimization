There are several ways to improve the "perception" of a network. Additionally, the way input and output data are represented plays a significant role. Suppose a network is trained to recognize letters on images and has a single numerical output — the letter's position in the alphabet. In this case, the network may develop a false assumption that letters with numbers 1 and 2 are more similar than letters with numbers 1 and 3, which is generally incorrect. To avoid such situations, networks with a larger number of outputs are used, where each output has its own meaning. The more outputs a network has, the greater the distance between classes, and the more difficult it is to confuse them. Experimental tuning of network characteristics

After selecting the general structure, it is necessary to experimentally tune the network parameters. For networks similar to perceptrons, this involves the number of layers, the number of blocks in hidden layers (for Word networks), the presence or absence of skip connections, and neuron transfer functions. When choosing the number of layers and neurons within them, one should consider that a network's generalization ability is higher the greater the total number of connections between neurons. On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data.
