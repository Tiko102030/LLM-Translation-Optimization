On the other hand, the number of connections is limited by the number of records in the training data. Experimental selection of training parameters

After selecting a specific topology, it is necessary to choose the training parameters of the neural network. This stage is particularly important for networks trained with a teacher. The choice of parameters not only affects how quickly the network's responses will converge to the correct answers. For example, choosing a low learning rate will increase the convergence time, but sometimes allows avoiding network paralysis. Increasing the momentum of learning may lead to both an increase and a decrease in convergence time, depending on the shape of the error surface. Based on such conflicting influence of parameters, it can be concluded that their values should be chosen experimentally, guided by the completion criteria of training (e.g., minimizing error or time constraints). Neural network training

During training, the network examines the training data in a certain order. The order of viewing can be sequential, random, etc. Some networks trained without a teacher (e.g., Hopfield networks) examine the data only once. Others (e.g., Kohonen networks) as well as networks trained with a teacher examine the data multiple times, with one full pass through the data called an epoch of training.
