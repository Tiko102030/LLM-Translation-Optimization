Following the 1986 Challenger shuttle disaster, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricane Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place — the Endeavour and Atlantis missions. After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the LC-39A launchpad was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as piloted Dragon spacecraft. The LC-39B pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex at the center is a museum and entertainment area featuring tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours around the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Additionally, there is the astronaut memorial "Cosmic Mirror" on the site, where the names of deceased cosmonauts are commemorated.
