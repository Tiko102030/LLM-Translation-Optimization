The center is operated by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962 when NASA started acquiring land for expanding launch capabilities. In the same year, the area was renamed the Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John Kennedy, it was renamed the Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program included three phases: Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo. The Mercury program aimed to send a human into orbit, study their work in space, and test the return procedures. The first flights were conducted using rockets Redstone and Atlas. The Gemini program used two-person spacecraft and allowed for the first spacewalks. Within the framework of this program, 12 missions were carried out. The Apollo program used the Saturn V rocket. For this program, Launch Complex 39 was built with a comprehensive infrastructure, including the vertical assembly building and transportation systems.
