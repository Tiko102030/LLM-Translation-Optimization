After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricanes Francis and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place — the missions 'Endeavour' and 'Atlantis'. After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the LC-39A launchpad was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as piloted Dragon spacecraft. The LC-39B pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex of the center is a museum-entertainment zone with tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours around the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Additionally, there is the astronaut memorial 'Cosmic Mirror' on the site, where the names of deceased cosmonauts are commemorated.
