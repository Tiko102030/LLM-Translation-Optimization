The center is operated by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962 when NASA started acquiring land for expanding launch capabilities. In the same year, the area was named the Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John Kennedy, it was renamed the Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program included three phases: Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo. The Mercury program aimed to send a human into orbit, study his work in space, and develop the return. The first flights were conducted using Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini program used two-person spacecraft and enabled the first extravehicular activities in open space. Twelve missions were completed within this program. The Apollo program used the Saturn V rocket. For this program, Launch Complex 39 was built with extensive infrastructure, including the vertical assembly building and transportation systems.
