Following the 1986 Challenger shuttle disaster, flights were temporarily suspended. The complex suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricane Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the final shuttle missions—Discovery and Atlantis—were launched. After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the LC-39A launchpad was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as piloted Dragon capsules. The LC-39B launchpad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion capsule. The Visitor Complex consists of a museum and entertainment area with guided tours, IMAX cinemas, and bus tours around the facility. The main attraction is the Saturn V rocket. Additionally, the Astronaut Memorial "Space Mirror" is located on the site, commemorating the names of deceased astronauts.
