Launch Complex 39 was built with a scaled infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transport systems. From 1967 to 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. A tragedy occurred during the Apollo 1 crew mission. Later, crewed flights were conducted, including Apollo 11, during which a human first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Rockets such as Titan and Atlas-Centaur were also used to launch satellites and interplanetary spacecraft. Until July 2011, the center served as the primary launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first Shuttle Columbia launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km runway is located here. Following the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
