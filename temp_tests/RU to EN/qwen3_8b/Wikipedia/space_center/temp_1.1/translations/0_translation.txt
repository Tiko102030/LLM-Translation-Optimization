John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and flight control, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is situated near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 kilometers in length and 10 kilometers in width, with a total area of 567 square kilometers. About 13,500 people work at the center. Only approximately 9% of the area is used for its intended purpose, while the rest is a wildlife refuge, including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and Cape Canaveral National Seashore. This area experiences one of the highest lightning strike rates in the United States, so NASA invests significant resources in protecting launches. Main operations are conducted from Launch Complex 39, which houses the Vertical Assembly Building. Two launch pads are located 5 kilometers to the east, and an industrial area with equipment and administrative buildings is situated 8 kilometers to the south. Space shuttles were launched from Complex 39, while uncrewed vehicles were also launched from the U.S. Space Force's base at Cape Canaveral. The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
