Launch Complex 39 with a comprehensive infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems, was constructed for it. Between 1967 and 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. During the program, a tragedy occurred with the Apollo 1 crew. Subsequently, piloted flights were carried out, including Apollo 11, during which a person first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Rockets Titan and Atlas-Centaur were also used to launch satellites and interplanetary spacecraft. Until July 2011, the center served as the primary launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first Space Shuttle Columbia launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km runway is located here. Following the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
