After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center was damaged by hurricanes, including Hurricanes Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches—missions "Endeavour" and "Atlantis"—took place. Following the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, Launch Complex 39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as crewed Dragon capsules. Launch Complex 39B is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The visitor center complex is a museum and entertainment area featuring tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours around the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on the site is the "Space Mirror" astronaut memorial, where the names of deceased cosmonauts are enshrined.
