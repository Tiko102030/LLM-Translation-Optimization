The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962, when NASA started acquiring land for expanding launch capabilities. In the same year, the area was named the Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John Kennedy, it was renamed the Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program consisted of three phases: Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo. The Mercury program aimed to place humans in orbit, study their work in space, and test the return procedures. The first flights were conducted using Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini program used two-man spacecraft and enabled the first spacewalks. Within this program, 12 missions were carried out. The Apollo program used the Saturn V rocket. For this program, Launch Complex 39 was constructed, featuring a large-scale infrastructure, including the vertical assembly building and transportation systems.
