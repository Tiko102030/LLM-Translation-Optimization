Following the 1986 Challenger shuttle disaster, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricanes Francis and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches — missions "Indevour" and "Atlantis" — took place. After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the LC-39A launchpad was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as piloted Dragon capsules. The LC-39B pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The center's visitor complex is a museum and entertainment area with guided tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours around the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on the premises is the "Space Mirror" astronaut memorial, where the names of deceased astronauts are commemorated.
