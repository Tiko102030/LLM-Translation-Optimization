After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including Hurricanes Francis and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place—the Endeavour and Atlantis missions. After the completion of the shuttle program, the transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the LC-39A launch pad was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, as well as for Dragon spacecraft. The LC-39B launch pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex is a museum-entertainment area with guided tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours of the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on-site is the Astronaut Memorial "Space Mirror," where the names of deceased astronauts are enshrined.
