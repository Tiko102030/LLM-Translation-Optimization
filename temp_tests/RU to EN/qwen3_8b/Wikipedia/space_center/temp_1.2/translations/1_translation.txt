The center is operated by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962 when NASA started acquiring land for expanding launch capabilities. In the same year, the area was named the Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John Kennedy, it was renamed the Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program included three phases: Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo. The Mercury program aimed to put a human in orbit, study his work in space, and develop the return. The first flights were conducted using rockets Redstone and Atlas. The Gemini program used two-person spacecraft and allowed the first spacewalks. Twelve missions were carried out under this program. The Apollo program used the Saturn V rocket. For it, Launch Complex 39 was built with a large-scale infrastructure, including the vertical assembly building and transportation systems.
