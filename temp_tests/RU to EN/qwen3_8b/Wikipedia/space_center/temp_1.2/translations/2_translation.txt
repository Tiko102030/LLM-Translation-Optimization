Launch Complex 39 was built with a comprehensive infrastructure, including the Vertical Assembly Building and transportation systems. Between 1967 and 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. A tragedy occurred involving the Apollo 1 crew. Later, piloted flights were carried out, including Apollo 11, during which a human first landed on the Moon in July 1969. The center was later used for launching the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz program missions. Titan and Atlas-Centaur rockets were also used for launching satellites and interplanetary spacecraft. Until July 2011, the center served as the primary launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first launch of the Columbia shuttle occurred in 1981. A 4.6 km runway is also located here. After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
