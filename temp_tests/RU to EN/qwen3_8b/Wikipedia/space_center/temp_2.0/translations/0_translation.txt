John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and flight operations, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is located near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km in length and 10 km in width, with a total area of 567 km². Approximately 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the area is used for its intended purpose, the rest is a wildlife refuge including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and Cape Canaveral National Seashore. Due to the presence of one of the highest number of lightning strikes in the United States, NASA spends significant funds on launch protection. Main operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the Vehicle Assembly Building is located. Two launch pads are located 5 km to the east, while an industrial zone with equipment and administrative buildings is located 8 km to the south. Space shuttles were launched from Complex 39, and unmanned spacecraft were also launched from the US Space Force's base on Cape Canaveral. The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and subcontractors.
