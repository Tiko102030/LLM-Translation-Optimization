All the above applies only to iterative algorithms for searching neural network solutions. For them, nothing can be guaranteed and the learning of neural networks cannot be fully automated. However, along with iterative learning algorithms, there exist non-iterative algorithms that have very high stability and permit fully automating the learning process. Checking the adequacy of the learning

Even in the case of seemingly successful learning, the network does not always learn exactly what the creator intended. There is a known case where a network was trained to recognize tank images from photographs, but later it was discovered that all the tanks were photographed on the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of "learning" to recognize tanks. Therefore, the network "understands" not what was required of it, but what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training must be conducted using examples that were not part of its training. The number of test examples should be greater the higher the quality of training. If the probability of errors in the neural network is close to one in a billion, then a billion test examples are required to confirm this probability.
