A launch complex No. 39 with a scaled infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems, was built for it. From 1967 to 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were completed. A tragedy occurred with the crew of Apollo 1. Subsequently, piloted flights were conducted, including Apollo 11, during which a person first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used to launch the Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions. Titan and Atlas-Centaur rockets were also used to launch satellites and interplanetary probes. Until July 2011, the center served as the primary launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first shuttle Columbia launch took place in 1981. A 4.6 km landing strip is located here. Following the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
