A launch complex No. 39 was built for her with scaled infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973, 13 Saturn V launches were conducted. During the program, a tragedy occurred with the Apollo 1 crew. Later, manned flights were conducted, including Apollo 11, during which a person first landed on the Moon in July 1969. Later, the center was used for launching the Skylab station and missions of the Apollo-Soyuz program. Also used were rockets Titan and Atlas-Centaur for launching satellites and interplanetary spacecraft. Until July 2011, the center served as the main launch site for the Space Shuttle program. The first launch of the Columbia shuttle took place in 1981. A landing strip 4.6 km long is located here. After the Challenger shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
