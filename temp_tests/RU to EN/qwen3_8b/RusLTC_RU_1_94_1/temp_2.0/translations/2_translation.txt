The free trade market leads to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and irrespective of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a significant impact on economic theory, causing many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and to focus instead on the efficiency of different legal regimes. However, there are several issues related to these claims. Separate consideration of prices and income distribution occurs only if income distribution affects both price levels and production structure. This will happen when economic agents have various preferences and when there are changes in returns to scale. Furthermore, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or the expenses associated with negotiation and enforcement of contracts. This may be extremely important in concluding a trade deal involving external effects, especially when it is often problematic to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The determination of property rights is frequently viewed in liberal democracies as quite controversial, similar to how in the United States people possess both personal freedom and the right of majority rule over the country's management. (See Horwitz 1992; Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895.)
