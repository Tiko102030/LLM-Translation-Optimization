The gradual withdrawal turned into a general exodus when political support for the sacred rights of property ceased during the 1930s Great Depression. A new generation of liberals, uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the New Deal policies, helped the state assist the needy and emerge from the crisis through measures of reformulated capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's 1937 rulings, even referring to the "Constitutional Revolution of 1937" after two conservative justices retired when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened to "remove" the Supreme Court.
