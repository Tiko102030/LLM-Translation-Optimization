Instrumental, competing "rights" to property can never be absolute and should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must take precedence, new conditions are required in order to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that a return to the discussion of natural rights will occur, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed U.S. legislation in any substantial way. Such a change would require courts to completely revise the historical legal justification under which property is regarded as a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
