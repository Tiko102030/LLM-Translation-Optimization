Shortly before considering the Adkins case, the Supreme Court issued another historic ruling seemingly entirely devoted to protecting property rights. In the case Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon (1922), the Court declared Pennsylvania’s law unconstitutional, as it limited the operations of coal mining companies in order to preserve subsurface resources. The Court held that the general rule would be that regulation of property may be carried out only to a certain extent; however, if it went too far, then it would constitute expropriation.
