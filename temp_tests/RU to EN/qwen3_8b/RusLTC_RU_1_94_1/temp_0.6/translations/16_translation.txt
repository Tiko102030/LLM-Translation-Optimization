Local authorities used the right of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "right of eminent domain" was first coined by the Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625 and was later incorporated into English law by John Locke, who based it on "natural law," as the coincidence of the will of the entire population, which transferred all powers to the state's legislative bodies. (See Iggle 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters expanded the concept purely based on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
