Instrumental, competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, and they should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that a return to the discussion of natural rights will occur, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed US legislation in any substantial way. Change would require courts to completely change the history of legal justification, according to which property is viewed as a social institution rather than an end. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
