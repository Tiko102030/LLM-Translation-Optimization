Shortly before the Adkins case was considered, the Supreme Court made another historic ruling that seemingly entirely concerned the protection of property rights. In the case of Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon (1922), the Court declared a Pennsylvania law unconstitutional because it limited the operations of coal companies in order to preserve the underlying coal bed. The Court held that the general rule would be that regulation of property can be exercised only to a certain extent; if it goes too far, then it would constitute a taking of property.
