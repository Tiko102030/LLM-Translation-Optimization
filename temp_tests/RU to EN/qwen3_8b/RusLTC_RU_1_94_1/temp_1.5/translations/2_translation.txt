The free trade market will lead to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and irrespective of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article exerted a significant influence on economic theory, leading many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and instead focus on the efficiency values of different legal regimes. However, there are several issues related to these statements. Separate consideration of price and income distribution occurs only when income distribution affects both price levels and production structure. This will happen when economic agents have diverse preferences and when there is increasing or decreasing returns to scale. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses associated with negotiation and enforcement of agreements. This may be extremely important when concluding a trade deal involving external effects, especially when it is often problematic to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The determination of property rights is often regarded in liberal democracies as somewhat contradictory, similar to how in the United States, people possess both individual freedom and the right of majority rule over the country. (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895)
