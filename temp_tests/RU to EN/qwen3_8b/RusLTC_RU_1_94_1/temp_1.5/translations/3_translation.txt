On the other hand, the right of the majority to control economic activities, determine public goals, and protect property owners from the effects of the wrongdoing of polluters is considered. However, such a general comparison of property rights can be misunderstood, since these rights, to a large extent, limit the rights of other people. Property rights grant individuals government-sanctioned privileges of monopolistic control over resources, the ability to appeal to authorized police authorities to restrict the activities of other people. Thus, property significantly limits certain advantages because government authorities support property owners to a greater extent than other citizens. Therefore, it is not a debate about the principles of natural liberty and justice, but rather discussions on property regulation that force a choice about whose property needs to be protected and whose property rights have the advantage in the struggle for them. The discussion of democratic rights and their regulation has become so unclear because legal issues in the structure of the legal system of the United States have been repeatedly discussed at various levels of government. Thus, according to the system of federalism, one Federal Government is formed, while each state establishes its own legal system. Additionally, at each level of governance, there is a clear division of executive, legislative, and judicial powers, each of which is meant to respect and ensure compliance with the Constitution, in which they find their interpretation.
