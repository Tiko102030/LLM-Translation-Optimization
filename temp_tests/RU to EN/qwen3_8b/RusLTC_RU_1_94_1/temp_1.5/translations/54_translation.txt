Instrumental, competing "property rights" can never be absolute, and they should never be the ultimate goal. Since certain rights must take precedence, new conditions are needed to take into account competing claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not significantly altered U.S. legislation. The change would require courts to completely revise the historical legal rationale according to which property is viewed as a social institution rather than an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
