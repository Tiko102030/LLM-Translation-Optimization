On the other hand, the right of the majority to control economic activity, determine public goals, and protect property owners from the consequences of the actions of polluters is considered. However, such a general comparison of property rights can be misunderstood, since these rights, to a greater extent, limit the rights of other people. Property rights grant individuals government-sanctioned privileges for monopolistic control over resources, the ability to appeal to authorized police authorities to limit the activities of other people. Thus, property significantly limits some advantages because government authorities more strongly support property owners than other citizens. Therefore, it is not a debate about the principles of natural freedom and justice, but rather discussions on property regulation that force a choice about whose property needs to be protected and whose property rights have the advantage in the struggle for them. The discussion of democratic rights and their regulation has become so unclear because legal issues have repeatedly been discussed at various levels of government within the structure of the legal system of the United States. Thus, according to the system of federalism, a single Federal Government is formed, while each state has its own legal system. Also, at each level of administration, there is a clear separation of executive, legislative, and judicial powers, each of which is intended to respect and ensure compliance with the Constitution, in which they find their interpretation.
