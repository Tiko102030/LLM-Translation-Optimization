The gradual retreat turned into a general exodus when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the 1930s Great Depression. A new generation of liberals, uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the policies of the New Deal, facilitated the role of the state in helping the needy and exited the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's 1937 decisions, even referring to the "constitutional revolution of 1937" after two conservative justices retired when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened that he would "oust" the Supreme Court.
