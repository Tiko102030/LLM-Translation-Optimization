Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon
Shortly before considering the Adkins case, the Supreme Court issued another historically significant decision that seemingly fully concerned the protection of property rights. In the case of Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon (1922), the Court declared Pennsylvania's law unconstitutional because it limited the activities of coal companies in order to preserve the subsurface. The Court held that the general rule would be that regulation of property may be carried out only to a certain extent, but if it goes too far, it would constitute a taking of property.
