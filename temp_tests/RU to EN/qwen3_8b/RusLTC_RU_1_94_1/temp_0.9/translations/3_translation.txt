On the other hand, the right of the majority to control economic activity, determine public goals, and protect property owners from the consequences of the actions of polluters is considered. However, such a general comparison of property rights concepts can be misunderstood, as these rights, to a greater extent, limit the rights of others. Property rights give people state-sanctioned privileges to monopolize resources and the ability to appeal to authorized police authorities to limit the activities of others. Thus, property significantly limits some advantages because governmental authorities support property owners to a greater extent than other citizens. Therefore, it is not a dispute about the principles of natural freedom and justice, but rather the discussion of property regulation that leads to the choice of whose property needs to be protected and whose property rights have the advantage in the struggle for them. The discussion of democratic rights and their regulation has become so confusing because legal issues have been repeatedly discussed at various levels of government within the structure of the United States legal system. Thus, according to the federal system, a single Federal Government is formed, and each state has its own legal system. Additionally, at each level of administration, there is a clear separation of executive, legislative, and judicial powers, each of which is intended to respect and ensure the observance of constitutional norms, in which they find their interpretation.
