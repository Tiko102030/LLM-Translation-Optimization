Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon  
Shortly before considering the Adkins case, the Supreme Court issued another historically significant decision that seemingly entirely concerned the protection of property rights. In the case of "Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon" (1922), the Court declared Pennsylvania's law unconstitutional because it limited the activities of coal companies in order to preserve the coal bed. The Court held that a general rule applicable to all would be that regulation of property may be carried out only to a certain extent; however, if it goes too far, it would constitute a taking of property.
