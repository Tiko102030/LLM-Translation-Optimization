A free trade market will lead to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a significant influence on economic theory, leading many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and focus instead on the efficiency of different legal regimes. However, there are several issues related to these claims. Separate consideration of price and income distribution occurs only when income distribution affects both price levels and the structure of production. This will happen when economic agents have various preferences and when there is an increase or decrease in returns to scale. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses related to negotiation and enforcement of agreements. This may be extremely important in concluding a trade deal involving external effects, particularly when it is often problematic to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The definition of property rights is often viewed quite controversially in liberal democracies, similar to how in the United States people possess both personal freedom and the right of majority rule in governing the country. (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895)
