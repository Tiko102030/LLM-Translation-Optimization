Local authorities used the right of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The term "right of eminent domain" was first coined by the Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625 and was later incorporated into English law by John Locke, who placed it as the basis of "natural law," as the coincidence of the will of the entire population, which conferred all powers to the legislative bodies of the state. (See Igl 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters expanded the concept purely based on instrumental considerations. Denying any inviolability of private property rights, the 1791 Vermont Constitution stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Elie 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
