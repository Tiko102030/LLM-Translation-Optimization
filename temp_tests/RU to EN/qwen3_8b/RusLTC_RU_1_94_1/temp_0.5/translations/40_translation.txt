Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon
Shortly before considering the Adkins case, the Supreme Court issued another historically significant decision that seemingly fully concerned the protection of property rights. In the case of "Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon" (1922), the Court declared Pennsylvania's law unconstitutional because the law, in order to preserve the subsurface, restricted the operations of coal companies. The Court held that the general rule applicable to all would be that regulation of property may be carried out only to a certain extent; however, if it goes too far, then it would constitute taking of property.
