The market of free trade leads to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and irrespective of who owns the rights to air and water. The Coase Theorem has had a significant impact on economic theory, leading many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and instead focus on the efficiency values of different legal regimes. However, there are several questions related to these statements. Separate consideration of price and income distribution occurs only when income distribution influences both the price level and the structure of production. This happens when economic agents have various preferences and when there is an increase or decrease in returns to scale. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or the costs associated with negotiation and enforcement of agreements. This may be extremely important in concluding a trade deal involving external effects, particularly when it is often problematic to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The definition of property rights is often perceived in liberal democracies as rather contradictory, similar to how in the United States people possess both personal freedom and the right of majority rule in governing the country. (see Horvitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895)
