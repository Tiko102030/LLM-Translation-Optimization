However, most people became wealthy through military victories and theft, following the example of the first Norman lords who accompanied William to England. White Americans became wealthy by subjugating millions of Africans and using military force to take land from the indigenous peoples. Even the 1776 Revolution, a war for liberal ideals, became an example of mass plunder, including forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its suburbs, and most of the land in Queens, Richmond, Kings, and Suffolk counties (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
