The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient outcome regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article exerted a great influence on economic theory, leading many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and instead focus on the efficiency values of different legal regimes. However, there are a number of issues related to these statements. Separate consideration of price and income distribution occurs only when the distribution of income affects both the level of prices and the structure of production. This will happen when economic agents have diverse preferences and when there is increasing or decreasing returns to scale. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or the costs associated with negotiation and enforcement of agreements. This could be extremely important in the conclusion of a trade deal involving externalities, especially when it is often difficult to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. The definition of property rights is frequently regarded in liberal democracy as quite contradictory, much like how in the United States people possess both personal freedom and the right of majority rule over the country. (see Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895)
