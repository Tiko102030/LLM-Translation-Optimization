Shortly before the consideration of the Adkins case, the Supreme Court issued another historic decision that seemingly fully concerned the protection of property rights. In the case of "Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon" (1922), the Court declared Pennsylvania's law unconstitutional, as it limited the activities of coal companies in order to preserve the subsurface. The Court ruled that the general rule would be that regulation of property may be carried out only to a certain extent; however, if it goes too far, it would constitute a taking of property....
