The notion or even more so a myth in the study of American history assumes that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent, thanks to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "Traditions of American Liberalism" (1955), American society is regarded as typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, fostering a particular passion for individualism and rights to private property. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to spur workers and others toward the achievement of socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lipset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
