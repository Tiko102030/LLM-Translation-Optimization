Surprise appeared on his face. — What's going on? — he shouted, inspecting the entering comrade from head to toe; then fell silent and whistled. — Can it really be that bad? You, brother, have surpassed our brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's ragged clothes. — Sit down, you must be tired! — and when he collapsed onto the linen Turkish divan, which was worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. — You're really ill, do you know that? — He started feeling his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. — No need, — he said, — I came… here's the thing: I have no lessons… I had wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — Do you know something?
