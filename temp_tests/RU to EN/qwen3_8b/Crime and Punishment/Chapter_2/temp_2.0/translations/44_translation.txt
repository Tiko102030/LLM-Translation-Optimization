— Where to, please allow me to ask? — Down the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply and abruptly, altogether pale as a handkerchief, not lowering his black, inflamed eyes from Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fomich began to remark. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said in a particularly emphatic tone. Nikodim Fomich had intended to add something else, but upon glancing at the scribe, who was also intently staring at him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
