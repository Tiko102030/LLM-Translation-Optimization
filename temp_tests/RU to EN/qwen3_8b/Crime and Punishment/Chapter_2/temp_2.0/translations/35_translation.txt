—"Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, that's that! It's known, gunpowder cannot bear offenses. You, probably, have offended him and couldn't help but retaliate," continued Nikolai Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov courteously, "but you're wasting your time: he is the noblest, I can tell you, person, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He lost his temper, boiled over, and burned out—and then there was nothing! Everything passed away! And in the end—nothing but gold in the heart! So in the regiment they called him: 'Ensign-Powder'…

—"And what regiment was that! — cried Ilyia Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so amusingly tickled, yet still grumbling. Suddenly, Raskolnikov felt the urge to say something unusually pleasant to them all. — Oh, forgive me, captain," he began, abruptly turning to Nikolai Fomich in a very relaxed manner, "take some time to think about my position… I would even ask them for forgiveness if I have been at fault on my part."
