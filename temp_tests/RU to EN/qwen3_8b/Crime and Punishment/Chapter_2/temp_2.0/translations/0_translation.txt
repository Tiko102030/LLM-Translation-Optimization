Thus he lay for a long time. It used to happen that he seemed to wake up, and in those moments he would notice that it was already night, yet getting up didn't cross his mind. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He was lying on the couch on his back, still stunned from his recent oblivion. To him, the sharp, terrifying screams from the street rang out, which, in fact, he had been listening to every night under his window at three o'clock. These were the ones that woke him up now. "Ah! Here come the drunkards from the taverns," he thought, "it's three o'clock—he suddenly sat up as if someone had yanked him off the couch. 'What! It's already three o'clock!' " He sat on the couch—and then everything came back to him!"
