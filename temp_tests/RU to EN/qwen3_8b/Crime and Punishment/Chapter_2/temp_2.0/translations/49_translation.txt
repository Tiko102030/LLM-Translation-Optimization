He was becoming exceedingly absent-minded and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to hurry! He went to the Neva via the B-Street; but on the way, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Is it not better to go somewhere very far, perhaps again to the Islands, and there somewhere, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—to bury all of this and the tree, which, perhaps, would be noticed?" Although he felt that he was incapable of clearly and soundly discussing everything at this moment, the thought seemed infallibly correct to him. But it was not destined for him to reach the Islands; something else happened: as he was exiting the B-Street onto the square, he suddenly saw on the left a gate leading into a courtyard, completely surrounded by solid walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a solid, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also immediately from the gates, ran a wooden fence twenty steps into the courtyard, and then it turned abruptly to the left. This was a soundproof, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed protruded from the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
