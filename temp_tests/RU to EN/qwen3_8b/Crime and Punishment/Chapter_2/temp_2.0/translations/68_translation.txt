And here, to the greatest astonishment, he suddenly heard his mistress's voice. She was howling, shrieking, and weeping, rushing about in a frenzy, bursting out words so fast that nothing could be made out—that was surely her begging, certainly for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the one beating her had become so terrible with hatred and madness that it could barely be heard, but still, the one beating her was uttering something too, and also in a jumble, in a rush, gasping for breath. All of a sudden, Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized that voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! Ilya Petrovich was here, right here, hitting the mistress! He was beating her with his legs, striking her head against the stairs—that's clear, one can hear it from the sounds, the shrieks, the blows! What is this—has the world turned upside down, or what? One could hear people gathering in all the floors, along the entire stairway, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, feet stomping, people rushing down. "But why, why, and how could this happen!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had gone completely mad. But no, he was too clearly hearing it...
