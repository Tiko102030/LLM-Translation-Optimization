"Please don't shout!" I am not shouting, I am speaking quite calmly, but you are shouting at me; I am a student and will not allow myself to shout. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not even utter a word, and only some spittle flew from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. "Please be quiet! You are in the presence. Don't act like a rogue, sir!" "And you are in the presence too," cried Raskolnikov, "and besides shouting, you're smoking a cigarette, so you are acting like a rogue to all of us." Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
