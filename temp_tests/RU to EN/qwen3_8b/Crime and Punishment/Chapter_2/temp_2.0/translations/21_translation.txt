He breathed more freely. "Probably not!" Slowly he began to comfort himself, advising himself with all his strength to take heart and come to his senses. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness—and I might lose everything! Hmm… Alas, there's no air here—-the stifling heat... My head spins even more... and so does my mind..."

He felt a terrible chaos within himself. He himself feared that he could not control himself. He tried to fasten his mind onto something, to think about something entirely unrelated, but this proved futile. The clerk, however, intrigued him quite a lot: he wanted to read something into his face, to decipher his intentions. The young man was not more than twenty-two, with a swarthy, animated face seeming older than his years; dressed in the latest fashion, wearing a fete, with a parting on the back, meticulously combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and chains on his fair hands, cleansed by brushes, and gold chains on his vest. He even addressed a former foreigner with two words in French, very successfully—"Madam Louise Ivanovna, please sit down," he said, almost passing by the scarlet-ruddy lady, who stood still, as though afraid to sit down herself, although a chair was nearby.
