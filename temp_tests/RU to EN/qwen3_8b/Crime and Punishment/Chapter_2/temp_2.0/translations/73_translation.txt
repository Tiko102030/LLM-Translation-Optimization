He remembered only that he had taken a gulp of cold water and spilled it onto his chest from the cup. Then came amnesia. III

He, however, was not entirely in a state of amnesia throughout his illness: it was a delirious condition, with delirium and half-consciousness. Much later he recalled many things. It seemed to him that a crowd had gathered around him and wanted to take him somewhere, with much argument and conflict. At times he was alone in the room, everyone had gone away, and they feared him; they occasionally opened the door slightly to look at him, threatened him, talked among themselves, laughed and mocked him. Nastasya he often remembered beside him; he distinguished also another man, whom he seemed to know well, but he could not figure out who exactly it was and he mourned over it, even weeping. At times he felt as if he had been lying there for a month, while at others it seemed to be the same day. But about all that he completely forgot; instead, he was continually reminded that he had forgotten something important, which could not be forgotten — he was tortured and tormented, trying to remember, groaning, lapsing into rage or a dreadful, unbearable terror. At such times, he would rush up and try to run away, but someone always stopped him by force, and he would again fall into a state of weakness and insensibility.
