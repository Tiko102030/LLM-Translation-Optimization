The call was so strong that he already rose to act. "Shall I at least think for a minute?"— flew through his mind.—"No, better not think at all, and get rid of it immediately!" But suddenly he stopped as if nailed to the spot: Nicholay Fomichev was speaking passionately to Ilya Petrovich, and the words reached him:—"It's impossible to free both! First, it all contradicts; judge: why call a steward if this is their own affair? To report on themselves? Or for trickery? No, that would be too cunning. And finally, Pestryakov the student was seen at the very gates by both the steward and the merchant's wife at the exact moment he entered: he walked in with three friends and parted from them at the gates and was inquiring about living with the servants even in the presence of his friends. Well, would he start asking about living with them if he came with such an intent?"
