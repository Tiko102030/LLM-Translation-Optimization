Louise Ivanovna began sitting down with an obliging politeness in all directions, and while sitting down, she bent over until she reached the door; but she bumped into a notable officer with an open, fresh face and thick, beautiful fair mustaches. It was Nikodim Fomin himself, the quarterly supervisor. Louise Ivanovna hastened to sit down, almost to the floor, and flew out of the office with frequent small steps, hopping. "Again thunder, again lightning, storm, hurricane!" Nikodim Fomin politely and amicably addressed Ilia Petrovich, "Again you've disturbed their hearts, again it's boiling! Even from the stairs I could hear it." "What a shock!" said Ilia Petrovich with noble indiffence (and even not just "what a shock," but "Da-а shcha-а!" with a drawn-out tone), transferring from some papers to another table and playfully shrugging his shoulders with every step, wherever he stepped, there his shoulder went. "Behold, you see here," he said, "my friend, the gentleman, that is, the student, who was, in fact, not paying the money, issuing bounced checks, refusing to clean his apartment, continuously receiving complains about it, and then, they went and insisted that I had smoked a cigarette in their presence! They themselves are guilty, but here, behold, you see them: here is the beauty of them in their very most attractive present appearance!" "Poverty is not a sin, my friend, but what else can you do!"
