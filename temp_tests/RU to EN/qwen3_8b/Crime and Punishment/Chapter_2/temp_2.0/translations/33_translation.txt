There they are, the writers!—he contemptuously glanced at Raskolnikov.—Three days ago, there was an incident at the inn: he had a meal but wouldn't pay; "I, you know, will describe you in satire for that." On the steamer, too, there was another incident last week: a reputable family of a State Councillor, his wife and daughter, were insulted in the worst possible words. Yesterday, from the confectionery, they kicked me out for making a fuss. That's what they are like, writers, literary people, students, commentators… fudge! Get out of here! I'll pay you a visit myself—then watch out! Did you hear? Louise Ivanovna, with excessively kind politeness, started bowing in all directions and, as she bowed, eventually reached the door; but she bumped into an officer at the door, a notable figure with a fresh, open face and with excellent white-whiskered beard.
