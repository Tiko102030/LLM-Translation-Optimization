He did not reply and held the paper in his hands, without reading it. "Enough of getting up," Nastasia continued, showing her sympathy and noticing that he was sliding his legs down from the couch. "You're ill, so you may as well not go out—you won't catch fire. What's in your hands?" He looked; in his right hand, there were scraps of cut braid, a sock, and shreds from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, reflecting on it, he remembered that, half-awake in a feverish state, he would tightly clutch all of it in his hand and fall asleep again. "He's gathered so many rags and sleeps with them, as if it were a treasure..." Nastasia burst into her painfully nervous laughter. Immediately, he shoved everything under his trench coat and intently stared at it. Even though he could understand little in that moment, he sensed that people would treat him differently when his brothers came to take him.
