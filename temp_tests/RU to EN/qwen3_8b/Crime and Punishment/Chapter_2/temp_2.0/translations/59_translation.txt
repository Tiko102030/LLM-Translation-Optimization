Leave me alone! — Wait a moment, blabbermouth! You're quite mad! As for me, I don't care. You see, I have no lessons, and I don't care—there's the bookseller Keruvimov on Tolkushy, that in itself is a lesson. I won't trade him for five merchants' lessons. He's publishing editions and natural science books—how popular they are! The titles alone are worth a fortune! You always used to say I was stupid; by God, brother, there are people more stupid than me! Now he's also jumped into the direction; he feels nothing, but I, of course, encourage.
