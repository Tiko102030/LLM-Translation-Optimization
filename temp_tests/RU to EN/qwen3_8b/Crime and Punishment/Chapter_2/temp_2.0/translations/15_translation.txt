"Put on the sock!" — it suddenly occurred to him, "it would get even dirtier in the dust, and the traces would disappear." But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with repulsion and horror. He pulled it off, but upon realizing there was no other option, he took it again and put it on— and laughed again. "Everything is conditional, everything is relative, all of this is just forms," — he thought briefly, barely with a corner of his mind, trembling all over, "for here he put it on! He ended up by putting it on!" The laughter immediately turned into despair. "No, it's beyond my strength..." — he thought. His legs were trembling. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head was spinning and aching from the heat.
