—"You, so flouncy and flashy," he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the widow had already left), "what happened during that past night? Come on, tell me! Another disgrace, another rowdy noise that'll be heard throughout the street. Another fight and drinking. You're dreaming of calm! I've already told you, I've warned you ten times that I won't let you down on the eleventh! And you're doing it again, again, you, so flouncy and flashy! Even Raskolnikov's paper fell from his hands, and he looked wildly at the imposing lady who was being so unceremoniously handled; but soon, however, he realized what was going on, and right away this whole episode began to please him very much. He listened with delight, so much so that he felt like laughing, laughing, laughing... All his nerves were vibrating. — Ilya Petrovich!"
