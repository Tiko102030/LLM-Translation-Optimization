But no, he hears too clearly!… But then, if so, they will come to him now too, because… it's all due to the same thing… due to yesterday's… Lord! He wanted to shut himself in with a chain, but his hand would not rise… and it was useless! Fear, like ice, clad his soul, tormented him, made him lifeless… But at last, this whole commotion, lasting exactly ten minutes, gradually subsided. The mistress groaned and whimpered, Il'yich was still threatening and abusing… But at last, it seemed, he also grew quiet; now there was no more sound from him; "could he have gone away? Lord!"; yes, the mistress was going away too, still groaning and crying… and now her door slammed… And now the crowd is dispersing down the stairs into the apartments—shouting, arguing, calling out to each other, raising their voices to a cry, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been quite a number of them; perhaps the whole building had come together. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!?"

Raskolnikov collapsed into a state of helplessness on the sofa, but already could not close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such anguish, in such an insufferable feeling of boundless terror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light filled his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a bowl of soup. Looking at him attentively and seeing that he was not sleeping, she placed the candle on the table and began to arrange the food: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
