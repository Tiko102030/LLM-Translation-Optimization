It seemed to him as though he had cut himself off with scissors from everything and everyone at that moment. He arrived at himself by evening, which meant he had been walking for only six hours. He did not remember where or how he had returned. Undressing, he trembled all over, like a chased horse, and lay on the sofa, pulled on his greatcoat, and immediately fell asleep...

He awoke in deep twilight to a horrifying scream. Goodness, what was this scream! Such unearthly sounds, howling, wailing, screeching, tearing, chaos, insults—such noises he had never before heard or seen. He could not even imagine such cruelty, such frenzy. Terrified, he lifted himself up and sat on his bed, quivering every moment and agonizing. But the fight, the cries, and the insults grew louder and louder. And lo, to his utmost amazement, he suddenly heard his housekeeper's voice.
