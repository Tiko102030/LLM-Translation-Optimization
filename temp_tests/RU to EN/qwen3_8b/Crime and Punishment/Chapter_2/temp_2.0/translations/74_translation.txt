Then he would try to get up, wanting to run away, but someone would always stop him by force, and he would fall back into helplessness and confusion. Finally, he completely regained consciousness. This happened in the morning, at ten o'clock. At this hour of the morning, on clear days, the sun would always sweep across his right wall in a long stripe, lighting the corner by the door. By his bedside stood Nastasia and another person, who was studying him very attentively and completely unfamiliar to him. This was a young man in a kaftan, with a beard, and from appearance, he resembled a master craftsman. The landlady peeked through the half-open door. Raskolnikov raised himself. — Who is that, Nastasia? — he asked, pointing at the young man.
