The clerk began dictating to him the form of a regular apology in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, then I promise to do so (some time, someday), I won't leave the city, I'll neither sell nor give away my property, etc.—You cannot write at all, your pen slips from your hand," remarked the clerk, looking curiously at Raskolnikov. "Are you ill?"—"Yes... my head's going round... go on, speak!"—"Yes, everything; sign, please. The clerk took the paper and proceeded with the others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of rising and leaving, he put both elbows on the table and pressed his hands over his head. It felt as though a nail was being driven into his temple. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to rise now, go up to Nikodim Fomitch, and tell him everything from yesterday, every single detail, then go with them to the apartment and point out the items in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat, ready to carry it out.
