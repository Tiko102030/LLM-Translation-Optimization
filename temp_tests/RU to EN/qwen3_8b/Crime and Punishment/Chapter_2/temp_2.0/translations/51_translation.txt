Under the stone, a small indentation formed; he immediately started throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet ended up on the very top, and still there was room left in the indentation. Then he grabbed the stone again, overturned it with one twist to the previous side, and it came back to its original place, only slightly, barely seeming a little higher. But he gathered soil and pressed down the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely endurable joy, as before in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It's been here since the house was built and will probably remain here for just as long."
