The light was enough, and he quickly began examining himself from head to toe, his entire dress: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering with a chill, he began removing everything from his body and once again inspecting the surroundings. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and piece of cloth, and, not trusting himself, repeated the examination three times. But there was nothing, apparently, no traces; only at the spot where his pants had cut off and hung as a fringe, there remained dense traces of burnt blood on the fringe. He grabbed a foldable small knife and cut the fringe off. It seems that was all. Suddenly he remembered that his wallet and the things he had pulled out from the old woman's chest were still lying in his pockets all along! He hadn't even thought of taking them out and hiding them yet! He hadn't even thought of them now, while examining his dress! What is this?
