He was lying here, perhaps on the construction site, and would lie there for just as long. And even if they did find him, who would think about me? Everything is over! No evidence!"—and he laughed. Yes, he remembered afterwards that he had laughed with a nervous, petty, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and he kept laughing, all the while, as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto the Kth boulevard, where he had met that girl three days earlier, his laughter suddenly vanished. Other thoughts entered his mind. Suddenly it seemed terribly repulsive to pass by that bench where he had sat after the girl left, pondering, and it would also be terribly hard to meet again that bearded man to whom he had given a kopeck: "Damn him!" 

He walked, looking around listlessly and spitefully. All his thoughts now revolved around a certain main point—and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point, and that now, precisely now, he was alone with this main point, and that this was even the first time since these two months. "A damned thing this is all!"
