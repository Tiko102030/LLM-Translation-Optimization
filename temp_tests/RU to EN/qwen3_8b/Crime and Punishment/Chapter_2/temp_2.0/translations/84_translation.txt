—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with profound wonder and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really..."  
Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would soon arrive. With the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire service: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other items that had not been placed in such order for a long time. The tablecloth was clean. —Not bad, Nastasya, if Praskovya Pavlovna could send two bottles of beer. We'll have a drink, shall we. —Well, you, with the Eastern legs!— Nastasya muttered and went to execute the command.
