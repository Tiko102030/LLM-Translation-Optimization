Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt entirely shattered, his mind was with him. He feared the pursuit, feared that within half an hour, within a quarter of an hour already, someone would come out, maybe the instructions to monitor him; therefore, regardless of what happened, he had to bury the ends by the time. He had to take care of it while there was still some strength left and some sort of reasoning… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Toss everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this even during the night, in his delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he had several times tried to get up and go: "As soon as possible, as soon as possible, and throw everything away." But to throw it away proved very difficult. He had already been wandering along the Embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe even more, and several times looked at the wharf into the ditch, where they had met him. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either rafts were standing right at the wharves and launderers were washing clothes on them, or boats were anchored, and people were everywhere, and from all sides, from the embankments, from all around, one could see and notice: suspiciously, that someone intentionally came down, stopped, and threw something into the water. And what if the cases don't sink, but float?
