Not at all!...

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question at once confused him and deeply surprised him:

"If all of this was really done on purpose, not clumsily, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you've got, for which you've suffered all these troubles and consciously went on to such a degrading, ugly, low thing? You wanted to throw the wallet into the water now, along with all the things you've yet to see... What sense is there in it?"

Yes, it is so; everything is exactly as it is. He knew this beforehand and it wasn't a new question for him; when it was decided at night to throw everything into the water, there was no hesitation or argument—just as if it was only natural, as if there was no other way... Yes, he knew and remembered everything; almost as if it was decided yesterday, in that exact moment when he sat by the chest and took out the cases from it... That's exactly what happened! "It's because I am very sick," he decided gloomily at last, "I've tortured and mutilated myself, and I don't even know what I'm doing... Yesterday, and three days ago, and all this time, I've been torturing myself... I will get better and... stop torturing myself... But what if I never get better? God! How sick of this I am!..." He walked on without stopping. He was desperate to get his mind off somehow, but he didn't know what to do or take action. One new, insurmountable feeling seized upon him, increasingly more with every passing minute: it was a kind of endless, almost physical repulsion for everything he encountered and surrounding, stubborn, spiteful, and hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive—his faces, their gait, their movements. He wanted simply to spit on someone, maybe even bite someone if they talked to him...

He stopped abruptly when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vyislyevsky Island, by the bridge.
