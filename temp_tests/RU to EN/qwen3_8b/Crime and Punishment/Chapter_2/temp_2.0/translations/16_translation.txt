His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in by deception and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued muttering to himself as he descended the stairs. "The unfortunate thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something stupid..."  

As he was going down the stairs, he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the partition hole, "— and here, perhaps deliberately, there would be a search," he thought, pausing. But suddenly a sense of despair and a sort of cynicism about death took hold of him, and he shrugged his shoulders and went on. "Just as soon as possible...!"  

Again, unbearable heat lingered on the streets; one would wish for a single drop of rain throughout these days. Again there was dust, brick, and lime, again the stench from benches and drinking dens, again drunken petty-traders who shouted at everyone, and half-ruined coachmen. The sun shone intensely into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head completely spun— an ordinary sensation of fever when someone suddenly steps out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner leading to the street of yesterday, he gazed with terrible dread into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might perhaps say something," he thought, approaching the office.
