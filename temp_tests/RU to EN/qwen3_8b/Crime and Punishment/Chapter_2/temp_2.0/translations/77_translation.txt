Well, well, and who are you? — I am an artel'shik in our firm, from the merchant Shelopaeve, and I've come here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — said Razumikhin himself, taking the other chair on the opposite side of the table. — Well, brother, you've done well to recover, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — Four days barely ate and drank. To be honest, tea was given with a spoon. I brought Zosimov here twice. Do you recall Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said everything was trivial — as if something hit you on the head. It's some sort of nervous nonsense, they said, the ration was poor, they gave little beer and pork, that's why you got ill. But don't worry, it'll pass and go away.
