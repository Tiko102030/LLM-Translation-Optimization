Just spit on someone, bite them, seems like, if anyone talked to him...  
He stopped abruptly when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, nothing but I came myself to Razumikhin! It's the same story again, as before... But it's quite interesting: did I come myself or just walked by and happened to end up here? Anyway; I said three days ago that I'd go to him the day after, well, I'll go! As if I can't even visit now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. He was at home, in his room, and at that moment was occupied, writing, and let him in himself. It had been about four months since they had last met. Razumikhin was seated in his room in a worn-out coat threadbare to rags, in socks, his hair all unkempt, unshaven and unclean. Surprise appeared on his face.
