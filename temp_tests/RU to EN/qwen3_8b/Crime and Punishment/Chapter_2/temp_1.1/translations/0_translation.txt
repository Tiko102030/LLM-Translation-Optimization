Thus he lay very long. It sometimes happened that he seemed to wake up, and during these moments he noticed that already it was night, and the thought of getting up never crossed his mind. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the sofa on his back, still stunned from recent unconsciousness. To him came sharply the sound of terrible, desperate cries from the street, which, in fact, he had every night listened to under his window at three o'clock. It was these cries that woke him now. "Ah! Here comes the drunkards from the taverns," he thought, "it's three o'clock— and suddenly he jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the sofa. 'What! It's already three o'clock!' He sat down on the sofa—and then everything came back to him!"
