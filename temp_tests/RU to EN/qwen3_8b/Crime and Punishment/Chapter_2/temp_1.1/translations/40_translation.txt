The impulse was so strong that he was already standing up from his place to carry it out. "Should I think about it for a moment?"—whispered through his mind. "No, better not to think at all and just throw it to the shoulders!" But suddenly he stopped as if nailed down: Nikodim Fomich was speaking with fervor to Ilyi Petrovich, and the words reached him: "It's impossible, both of them will be set free! First of all, everything contradicts; judge: why call a servant if it's their matter? To denounce oneself? Or for cunning? No, that would be too cunning! And finally, Pestryakov, the student, was seen by both the servants and the townsman at the very moment he entered: he was walking with three friends and parted from them at the very gates, asking about residence with the servants even in the presence of his friends. Well, he would ask about residence if he came with such an intention?"
