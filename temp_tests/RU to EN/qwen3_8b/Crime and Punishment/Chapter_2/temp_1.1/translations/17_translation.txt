"If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a versta away. She had just moved to a new apartment, a new building, to the fourth floor. He had been there briefly before, but a long time ago. Entering the gate, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a man was descending with a book in his hand: "a janitor, that means; that means the office is here," and he began climbing up randomly. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. "I'll go in, kneel down, and confess everything..." he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and full of filth. All the kitchens from all the apartments in the four floors opened onto this staircase and stood like that for almost the entire day. That's why there was a suffocating heat.
