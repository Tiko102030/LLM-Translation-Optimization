Not at all!...  

Suddenly, he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question at once threw him off balance and bitterly surprised him:  

"If indeed all this was done deliberately, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into your wallet so far and don't know what you've gotten yourself into, for all the suffering you've endured, and for consciously going on such a despicable, nasty, low act? You even wanted to throw the wallet into the water now, along with all the things you hadn't seen yet... How is that possible?"  

Yes, that's true; everything is exactly like that. He, in fact, knew this all along, and it wasn't a new question for him at all; and when it was decided during the night to throw everything into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that was the way it should be, as if it was impossible to be any other way... Yes, he knew everything and remembered everything; and maybe it was decided just yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting by the chest and pulling out the boxes from it... But that's how it was!... 'It's because I am very ill,' he darkly decided at last, 'I have tortured and torn myself apart, and I don't even know what I'm doing... And yesterday, and the day before, and all this time, I've been torturing myself... I will recover and... stop torturing myself... What if I never recover at all? God! How tired I am of all this!...' He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve his mind, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. A single new, insurmountable feeling was taking hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical revulsion toward everything he encountered and surrounding him, persistent, spiteful, hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive—repulsive were their faces, their gait, their movements. He simply wanted to spit on someone, to bite someone, perhaps, if someone spoke to him...  

He suddenly stopped when he emerged onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilievsky Island, near the bridge.
