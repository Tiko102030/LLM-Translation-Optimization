A small hollow formed under the stone; he immediately began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet landed at the very top, yet there was still space left in the hollow. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it back to its original side with one twist, and it once again fell into its former place, slightly, barely seeming a bit higher. But he pushed the dirt down and pressed it with his foot along the edges. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed toward the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the previous day at the office, briefly overwhelmed him. "The ends are hidden! And who, who would ever think to look under this stone? It has been lying here since the house was built and will remain there for just as long."
