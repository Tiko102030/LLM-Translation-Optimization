Louise Ivanovna began to bow in all directions with servile politeness, and as she bowed, she managed to reach the doors; but at the doors she ran into a prominent officer from behind, who had an open, fresh face and splendid thick golden mustaches. It was Nekodim Fomich himself, the quartermaster. Louise Ivanovna hurried to bow almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, sprang out of the office, hopping as she went. "Again thunder, again lightning and tornado, hurricane!" Nekodim Fomich kindly and warmly addressed Ilyia Petrovich, "again disturbed the heart, again boiled over! Even heard it from the stairs." "What on earth!" Ilyia Petrovich said with noble indifference (and even not "what on earth," but rather: "Da-aa shcha-a!") while moving from one table to another with some papers, dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he stepped, there his shoulder went; "Here you are, my dear, you see: the gentleman, that is, the student, who used to be, doesn't pay money, gave promissory notes, doesn't clean the apartment, and they keep coming in with endless complaints, and they even went so far as to file a complaint that I smoked a cigarette in front of them! They themselves are the ones who are rude, but here you are, my dear, see them: here they are in their most attractive appearance!" "Poverty is not a sin, my friend, but what can you do!"
