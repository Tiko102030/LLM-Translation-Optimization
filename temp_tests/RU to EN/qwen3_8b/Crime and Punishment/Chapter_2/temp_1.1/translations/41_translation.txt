Well, why would they start asking about residency if they came with such an intention? As for Koch, he sat for half an hour at the silverware keeper's before going up to the old woman at precisely seven-fifty-eight. Now, think it over...

—"But allow me, how could there be such a contradiction? They themselves claim they knocked and that the door was locked, yet three minutes later, when they came with the doorkeeper, it turned out the door was unlocked?" — That's the trick: the murderer was definitely there and locked himself in; he would surely have been caught there if it weren't for Koch's carelessness, who went off on his own to fetch the doorkeeper. And he managed to descend the stairs during that very interval and slip past them somehow. Koch crosses himself with both hands: "If I had stayed there, he would have jumped out and killed me with an axe." A Russian prayer service wants to serve, he-he... — Have they seen the murderer at all? — Where could they have seen him? The house is like Noah's Ark, — remarked the scribe, who was listening from his place.
