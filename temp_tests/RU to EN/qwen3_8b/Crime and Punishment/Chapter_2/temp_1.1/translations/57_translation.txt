— No, thank you, — he said, — I came… here's the thing: I have no lessons… I wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — "Do you know what? You're talking nonsense!" remarked Razumikhin, observing him closely. — No, I'm not! — Raskolnikov stood up from the sofa. As he rose to face Razumikhin, he didn't consider that he was about to come face to face with him. Now, in an instant, he realized, through experience, that he was least inclined to face anyone in the world at this moment. All his bitterness surged within him. He was almost overcome by wrath at himself for having crossed the threshold of Razumikhin's room. — Goodbye! — he suddenly said and walked toward the door.
