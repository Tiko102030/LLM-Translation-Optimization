But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, if that is the case, people will come to him now, too, "because... surely, all of this is from the same... because of yesterday's... Lord!" He had wanted to lock himself in, but his hand wouldn't raise... and it was useless anyway! Fear, like ice, clamped his heart, tormented him, numbed him... But finally, all this clamor, which had lasted for sure ten minutes, began to subside gradually. The housekeeper was groaning and moaning, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and shouting... But finally, it seemed, he had fallen silent; now he was no longer heard; "Could he have left? Lord!" Yes, the housekeeper was leaving, still groaning and weeping... and now her door had slammed shut... And now the crowd was dispersing down the stairs to the apartments—laughing, arguing, shouting, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering to a whisper. There must have been quite a number of them; perhaps the whole house had come. "But God, is all of this possible! And why, why did he come here!"

Raskolnikov, in despair, fell onto the sofa, but he could no longer close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such an unbearable feeling of boundless horror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a plate of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he was awake, she placed the candle on the table and began to arrange the things she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
