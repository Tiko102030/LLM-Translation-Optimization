He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath from his entire chest, "It's just a feverish weakness, a momentary delirium," and he tore out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a ray of sunlight illuminated his left boot: on the toe, which was peeking out of the boot, as if signs appeared. He kicked off the boot: "Indeed, signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood"; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly... "But what now? What to do with this sock, the fringe, the pocket?" He gathered all of this in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. "Into the stove? But first, they'll start digging in the stove. Burn it? But what to burn it with? There's not even a match."
