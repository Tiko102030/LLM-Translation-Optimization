He asked, pointing at the boy. "Look at that, he's awake!" she said. "Woke up," replied the artel'nik. Realizing he had woken up, the landlady, who had been peeking through the door, immediately closed them and hid herself. She had always been shy and bore conversations and explanations with reluctance; she was forty years old, fat and plump, with black eyebrows and black eyes, kind from her fatness and laziness; and even very slick in her own way. She was shamefully shy beyond necessity. "Who are you?" he continued interrogating, addressing the artel'nik directly. But at this moment the door opened wide again, and, leaning slightly because he was tall, Razumikhin entered.
