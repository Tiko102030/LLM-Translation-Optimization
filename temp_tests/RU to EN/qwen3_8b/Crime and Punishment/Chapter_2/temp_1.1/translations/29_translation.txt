—"You, so charming and all that," he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the widow had already left), "what happened last night? Ah? Another disgrace, making a ruckus all over the street again. Another fight and drunkenness. You're dreaming of calm! I've already told you, I've already warned you ten times that I won't let you in the eleventh! And you're doing it again, again, you, so charming and all that! Even the paper fell out of Raskolnikov's hand, and he wildly looked at the stately lady who was being so impolitely treated; but soon, however, he realized what was going on, and immediately this whole story began to please him very much. He listened with pleasure, so much that he wanted to laugh, laugh, laugh... All his nerves were jumping.—Ilya Petrovich!"
