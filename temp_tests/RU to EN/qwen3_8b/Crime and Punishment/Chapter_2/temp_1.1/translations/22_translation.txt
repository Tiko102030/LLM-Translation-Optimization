—"Louise Ivanovna, please sit," he said to the hastily dressed crimson lady, who stood motionless as if too shy to sit herself, though a chair was right there. "Ich danke,"[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] she said softly, with a silken whisper, and sank onto the chair. Her light-blue dress, adorned with white lace, spread around the chair like a balloon, taking up almost half the room. It carried the scent of perfume. Yet the lady, obviously shy about occupying almost half the room and the scent emanating from her, though she smiled timidly and brazenly at the same time, clearly distressed. The widow finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow particularly swaying her shoulders with each step, the officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table, and sat down in the chair. The plump lady jumped up upon seeing him and, with some special admiration, began to sit down, but the officer paid her no attention at all, and she no longer dared to sit in his presence.
