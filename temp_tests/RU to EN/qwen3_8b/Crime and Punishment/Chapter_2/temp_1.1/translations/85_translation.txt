Meanwhile, Razumikhin sat down on the couch awkwardly, like a bear, encircling his head with his left hand, despite the fact that he himself could have lifted it up, and brought a soup spoon to his mouth with his right hand, having previously blown on it several times to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoonful, then another, the third. But while bringing several spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that regarding the further course of action, they needed to consult with Zosimov. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want some tea? — Yes, I want. — Hurry up and make some tea, Nastasya, because, regarding tea, it seems we can do without the faculty.
