But here's the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and meat closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. — I, brother Rodia, have lunch here every day now, — he murmured, as much as his meat-filled mouth allowed, — and it's all Pashenka, your housekeeper, who's hosting me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. Here comes Nastasya with tea. How quick she is! Nastenka, do you want some beer? — And there he goes, to the devil! — A little tea?
