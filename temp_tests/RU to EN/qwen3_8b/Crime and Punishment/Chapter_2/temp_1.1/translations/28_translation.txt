The clerk looked at him with a compassionate smile tinged with some triumph, as if at a new recruit just beginning to be fired upon: "So, what do you feel now, eh?" But what concern was there now for borrowed writing, for punishment! Did it even deserve a flicker of worry, let alone any attention! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions, but all mechanically. Triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the crushing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without foresight, without analysis, without future speculations or guesses, without doubts or questions. It was a moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But at that very moment, something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The ensign, still shaken by the disrespect, seething with anger and obviously wishing to support the wounded ambition, launched himself at the unfortunate "puffy lady," who had been looking at him with the most foolish smile since he had entered. — You, such and such, what happened last night? (The mourner lady had already left.)
