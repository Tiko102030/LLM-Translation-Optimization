He was becoming extremely scattered and forgetful and knew it. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along B Street; but on the way, a sudden thought came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Would it not be better to go somewhere very far, perhaps again to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush, bury everything and the tree, maybe it would be noticed?" Although he felt that he could not clearly and soberly discuss all of this at that moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, and instead, while exiting from B Street to the square, he suddenly saw a gate leading to a courtyard with completely soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a soundproof, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched deep into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also right next to the gates, a wooden fence stretched twenty steps into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the recess of the courtyard, the corner of a low, soot-stained, stone shed peeked out behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
