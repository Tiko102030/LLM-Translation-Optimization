— All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us, — brusquely cut off Ilya Petrovich, — you must give your opinion and commitment, and as for all these tragic places where you happened to be in love, it's entirely unrelated to us. — Well, you… mercilessly… — muttered Nikolay Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He somehow felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the scribe to Raskolnikov. — What to write? — asked the latter particularly bluntly. — Well, I'll dictate. Raskolnikov noticed that the scribe had become more careless and disrespectful toward him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly felt entirely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened somehow in an instant, in a single moment. If he had wanted to think about it a bit, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a moment ago, even imposing his feelings on them, and where these feelings came from?
