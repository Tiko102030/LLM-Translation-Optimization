It was enough light, and he began to examine himself hastily, from head to toe, his entire attire: were there any traces? But that was impossible: shivering from a chill, he started removing everything and inspecting again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and, not trusting himself, repeated the examination three times. But there was nothing, apparently, no traces; only on the spot where his pants had frayed and hung as fringe, there remained thick crusted blood on the fringe. He seized a small folding knife and cut off the fringe. It seemed there was nothing else. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had pulled from the old woman's chest were still lying in his pockets! He had not even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even remembered them now, while inspecting his dress! What is this?
