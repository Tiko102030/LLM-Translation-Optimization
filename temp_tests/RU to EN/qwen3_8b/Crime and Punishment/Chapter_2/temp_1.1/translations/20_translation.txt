This was some particularly disheveled person with an immovable idea in his gaze. "You'll learn nothing from him, since he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there, to the scribe," said the clerk, jabbing forward with his finger to indicate the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), narrow and packed with people—crowds somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning attire, poorly dressed, sat at the table facing the scribe, writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and ruddy-red with freckles, a noticeable woman, dressed in excessively elaborate fashion, with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed his summons to the scribe. The scribe glanced at it briefly, said, "Wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He caught his breath more freely.
