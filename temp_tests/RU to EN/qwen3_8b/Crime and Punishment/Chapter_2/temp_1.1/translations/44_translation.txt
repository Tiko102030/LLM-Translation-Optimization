— Allow me to ask, where to? — Along the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply and abruptly, entirely pale like a cloth, without lowering his black, inflamed eyes from Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, but you... — Nikodim Fomich was about to remark. — Nothing at all! — Ilya Petrovich said in a particularly emphatic manner. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also very intently looking at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
