And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled not with his quarterly friends, but with his closest friends, then, it seemed, he would still have no human word left for them, his heart having suddenly grown so empty. A gloomy sensation of agonizing, endless solitude and estrangement suddenly consciously struck his soul. It was not the baseness of his emotional outbursts before Ilyin Petrovich, nor the baseness and the lieutenant's triumph over him that suddenly turned his heart upside down. Oh, what business does he have now with his own pettiness, with all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, demands, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were condemned to be burned at this very moment, he would not have stirred, and probably would not have even listened attentively to the verdict. Something completely unfamiliar to him, new, sudden, and unprecedented was taking place within him. It was not that he understood it, but he clearly felt, with all the force of his feeling, that he could no longer address these people, whether with his sensitive expansivenesses as before, or with anything else. Even if they were all his own brothers and sisters, and not just quarterly lieutenants, he would have had absolutely no need to address them, and even in no case in life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible sensation up to this very moment. And what was most painful of all — it was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; a direct, agonizing feeling, the most painful feeling of all he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the standard form of a complaint in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, etc.
