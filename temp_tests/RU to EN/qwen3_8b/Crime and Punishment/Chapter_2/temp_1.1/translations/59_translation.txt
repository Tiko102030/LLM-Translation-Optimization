Leave me alone! — Wait a minute, you chimney sweep! You're completely mad! As for me, whatever you want. You see, I have no lessons, and I don't care, but there's a bookseller named Heruvimov on Tolkuchy, which is in its own way a lesson. I won't exchange him for five merchant lessons. He produces such publications and natural science booklets—how they spread! The titles alone are worth something! You've always claimed I'm foolish; by God, brother, there are people even dumber than me! Now he's also going in the direction; he feels nothing at all, but I, of course, encourage him.
