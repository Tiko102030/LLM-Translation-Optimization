Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt torn apart, yet his mind remained with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, perhaps within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, probably with instructions to watch him; therefore, no matter what, he had to hide the ends by the appointed time. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left... Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Toss everything into the ditch, throw the ends into the water, and that's the end of it." He had decided this during the night, in his delirium, during those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to rise and go: "Quickly, quickly, and throw everything away." But it turned out to be very difficult to throw away. He had been walking along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe even more, and several times looked at the wharves where the ditch meets, where he had met them. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were standing right at the wharves, and the washerwomen were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and people were everywhere, and from everywhere along the embankment, from all sides, one could see and notice: it looked suspicious that a person intentionally stepped off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases did not sink but floated away?
