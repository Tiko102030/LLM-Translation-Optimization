Just spat on someone, bit them, I suppose, if someone had spoken to him...

He stopped suddenly as he came out onto the embankment of the Small Neva, on the Vyborg Island, near the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, indeed, I came here myself to Razumikhin! It's the same story again, as before... Yet it's very curious: did I come on my own or just walked and happened to end up here? Anyway; I said three days ago that I would go to him the day after that, well, and I'll go! As if I couldn't even stop by now..."

He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. He was at home, in his room, and at that moment was occupied, writing, and opened the door for him himself. Four months had passed since they last saw each other. Razumikhin sat in his torn-to-rags robe, in socks on his bare feet, tousled, unshaven, and unclean. Surprise appeared on his face.
