There's no evidence!"—and he laughed. Yes, he remembered afterwards that he laughed with a nervous, small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and kept laughing all the time, as he passed through the square. But when he stepped onto the K-th boulevard, where he had met that girl three days before, his laughter suddenly ceased. Other thoughts came into his head. Suddenly it seemed to him that it was extremely repulsive to pass by that bench where he had sat, after the girl had left, pondering, and that it would also be very hard to meet again that bearded man to whom he had given two kopecks: "By God, let him take it!" 

He walked, glancing around vaguely and maliciously. All his thoughts now revolved around one particular main point—and he himself felt that this was indeed such a main point and that now, precisely now, he was alone with this main point—and that this was even the first time since those two months. "By God, that's all!"
