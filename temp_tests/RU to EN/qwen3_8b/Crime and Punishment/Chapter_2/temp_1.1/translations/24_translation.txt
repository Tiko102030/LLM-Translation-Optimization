"Money? What money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but… surely, it's not that!" And he shuddered with joy. Suddenly, it became incredibly easy for him, immeasurably light. Everything fell off his shoulders. — What time are you supposed to come, sir? — shouted the ensign, growing increasingly offended, — You're supposed to come at nine, but it's already twelve o'clock now! — They brought me only a quarter of an hour ago, — answered Raskolnikov loudly, from behind, suddenly and unexpectedly to himself getting angry and even finding some pleasure in it. — That's enough, I came here feverish and ill. — Don't shout!
