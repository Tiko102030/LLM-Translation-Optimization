—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep astonishment and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to stay silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really happening..."  

Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would come soon. To the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire set: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other items, which had long since not been in such order. The tablecloth was clean. — It's not bad, Nastasya, that Praskovya Pavlovna sends two beer bottles. We'll drink, you know. — Well, you, bigfoot! — muttered Nastasya and went to carry out the order.
