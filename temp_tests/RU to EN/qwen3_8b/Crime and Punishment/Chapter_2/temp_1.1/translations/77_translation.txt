Well, then, who are you? — I'm a partner in our firm, from the merchant Shelopayev, and I've come here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — said Razumikhin, sitting on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to recover, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten or drunk for four days. Honestly, they gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to you twice. Do you remember Zos, the doctor? He examined you carefully and immediately said it was all nonsense — as if something had hit you on the head. Some kind of nervous nonsense, he said, you had poor rations, they gave you little beer and onions, that's why you got sick, but don't worry, it will pass and blow over.
