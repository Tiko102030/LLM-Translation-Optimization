His head spun and throbbed from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued thinking to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "The bad part is that I'm almost in a delirium... I might say something foolish..."  

On the staircase he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the wallpaper crevice— "and perhaps here, intentionally, they'll search without it,"— and then he stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say so, cynical acceptance of death suddenly seized him, and he waved his hand and continued on. "Just hurry up...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not even a drop of rain for all these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and taverns, again the ever-present drunken peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun flashed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely— the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with a torturous sense of anxiety into it, at that house... and immediately turned away. "If they ask, perhaps I'll say," he thought, approaching the office.
