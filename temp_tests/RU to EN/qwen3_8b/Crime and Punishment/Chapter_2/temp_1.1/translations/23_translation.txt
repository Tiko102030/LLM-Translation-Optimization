The plump lady sprang up from her seat upon seeing him and began sitting down with some peculiar enthusiasm; but the officer paid her no attention at all, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a sublieutenant, assistant quartermaster, with horizontally protruding reddish mustaches on both sides and extremely small facial features, nothing in particular except for some cheekiness. He glanced at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with annoyance: his clothes were too shabby, and despite all his humility, his posture still betrayed his attire. Raskolnikov, by his inconsiderate manner, looked at him too directly and for too long, so that the officer even felt offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged fellow didn't flinch from his lightning glare. "I was summoned… by order…" Raskolnikov managed to answer. "It's about the case of collecting money from them, from the student," hastened the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Here you are!" and he tossed Raskolnikov a notebook, pointing out the place in it—read! "Money? /think"
