—Ilya Petrovich!— the scribe began, with concern, but stopped to wait, since the boiling corporal could not be restrained except by holding his hands, something he knew from experience. As for the distinguished lady, at first she trembled from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more pleasant her demeanor grew, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the furious corporal. She swayed on the spot, continually bowing, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak, and finally did. —There will be no noise and fights here, sir captain— she suddenly chattered, as if spilling peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently in Russian— and no scandal at all, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, sir captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble house, sir captain, and noble manners, sir captain, and I have always, always refused to involve myself in any scandal. They came completely drunk, and then they asked for three more paths, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his feet, and that's very unbecoming in a noble house, and he broke the piano entirely, and there's absolutely no manner here, and I said so. He took the path and started pushing everyone from behind with the path. And then I quickly called the butler and Karl came, he took Karl and punched him in the eye, and Henriette also got punched in the eye, and me five times on the cheek. And that's very indecorous in a noble house, sir captain, and I screamed. And he opened the window and started howling like a little pig from the window; and that's a disgrace.
