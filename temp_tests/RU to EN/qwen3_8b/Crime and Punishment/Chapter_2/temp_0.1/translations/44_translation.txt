— Where to, may I ask? — To the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, entirely pale as a cloth, without lowering his black inflamed eyes before the gaze of Ilya Petrovich. — He's barely standing on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fomich began to remark. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said, as if particularly emphasizing it. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also intently staring at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
