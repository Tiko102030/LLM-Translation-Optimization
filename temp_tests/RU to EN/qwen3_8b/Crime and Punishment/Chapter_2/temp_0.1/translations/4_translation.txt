"—Is that how they hide it? My mind is leaving me!" Collapsing in exhaustion, he sat on the sofa, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him again. Mechanically, he pulled up the student's winter coat, lying nearby on the chair, warm but already in tatters, covered himself with it, and sleep and delirium once more seized him. He fell into oblivion. No more than five minutes later, he suddenly jumped up again and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed back to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, when nothing has been done! Exactly so, exactly so: the necktie under the armpit is still not removed! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the necktie and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the bedding. "Pieces of torn linen will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with intense, almost painful attention, he began once again to scrutinize everything around him, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?"
