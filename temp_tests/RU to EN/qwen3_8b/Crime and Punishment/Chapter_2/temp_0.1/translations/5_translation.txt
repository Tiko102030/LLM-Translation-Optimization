"The torn pieces of cloth will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he kept repeating, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful intensity he began to scrutinize everything around him, the floor, everywhere, making sure he hadn't forgotten anything else. The certainty that everything, even memory, even simple reasoning, was leaving him began to torment him unbearably. "What, is it really beginning? Is it really the execution that's about to start? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the frayed edges of the fringe he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at a glance! "What is happening to me!" he cried out again, as if lost. At that moment, a strange thought came to him: perhaps his entire outfit was covered in blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his reasoning had weakened, fragmented... his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So that means there should also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" In an instant, he turned the pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains! "So, it means I haven't completely lost my wits, there is still reasoning and memory, since I managed to realize it myself and figure it out!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest, "it's just the weakness of fever, a momentary delirium!" And he ripped out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
