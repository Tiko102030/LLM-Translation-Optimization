— Louise Ivanovna, please sit, — he said to the partially dressed blood-red lady, who stood still as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke, — she said softly, with a silk-like sound, and sat down. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread around the chair and took up almost half the room. It carried a scent of perfume. But the lady, obviously shy about occupying almost half the room and the fragrance emanating from her, though she smiled nervously and arrogantly, was clearly troubled. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very manly and somehow particularly twisting her shoulders with every step, a young officer entered, threw his cockade hat on the table, and sat in the chair. The wealthy lady jumped up upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her no attention at all, and she no longer dared sit in his presence.
