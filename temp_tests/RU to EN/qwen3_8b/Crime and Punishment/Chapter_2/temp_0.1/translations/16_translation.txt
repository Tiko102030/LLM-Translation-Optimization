His head was spinning and throbbing from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with a trick and suddenly knock me down altogether," he continued thinking to himself as he went down the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he left all his belongings there, in the wall hole—"and here, perhaps deliberately, without it, a search"—and he remembered and stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say, cynicism of death suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's soon...!"  

Again, the heat on the street was unbearable; just a drop of rain would have been welcome these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from benches and drinking places, again the ever-present drunkards, the beggars, and the half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun gleamed brightly in his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely— the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with agonizing anxiety into it, at that house… and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
