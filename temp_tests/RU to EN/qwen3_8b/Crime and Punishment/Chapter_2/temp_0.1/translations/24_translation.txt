"Money? Which money?" thought Raskolnikov, "but... surely it's not that!" And he shuddered with joy. Suddenly, it became terribly, immeasurably easy for him. Everything fell off his shoulders. "At what hour are you to come, sir?" shouted the ensign, growing more and more offended without knowing why, "you're told to come at nine, and now it's already twelve o'clock!" "They brought me the note only a quarter of an hour ago," answered Raskolnikov loudly, turning around, also suddenly and unexpectedly to himself enraged, even finding some pleasure in it. "It's enough that I've come here, feverish and ill." "Don't shout!"
