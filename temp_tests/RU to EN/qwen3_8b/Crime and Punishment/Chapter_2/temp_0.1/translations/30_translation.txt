—Ilya Petrovich!— the scribe began, with care, but paused to wait, since the boiling corporal couldn't be restrained except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the plump lady, at first she trembled from the thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more amiable her appearance became, and the more charming her smile directed at the furious corporal. She shuffled in place and kept bowing continuously, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak, and finally did. —No noise or brawl here, sir captain, — she suddenly chattered, as if popping peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently in Russian, — and no, no scandal at all, and they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, sir captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble house, sir captain, and noble manners, sir captain, and I always, always wanted no scandal. And they came completely drunk, and then asked for three drinks, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, and that's very bad in a noble house, and he broke the piano, and there's absolutely, absolutely no manners here, and I said. And he took a drink and started pushing everyone from behind with the drink. And then I quickly called the janitor and Carl came, he took Carl and hit him in the eye, and Henrietta also got hit in the eye, and me five times on the cheek. And this is so indecent in a noble house, sir captain, and I shouted. And he opened the window on the canal and started screaming like a little pig from the window; and this is a disgrace.
