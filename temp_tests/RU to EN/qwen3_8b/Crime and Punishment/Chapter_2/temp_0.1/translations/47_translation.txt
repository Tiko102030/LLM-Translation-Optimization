Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt thoroughly shattered, yet his mind was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, perhaps within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, probably giving instructions to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to get rid of the ends before the time. He had to manage it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left... Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had made this decision the previous night, in a delirium, during those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "As soon as possible, as soon as possible, and throw everything away." But throwing it away proved very difficult. He had been walking along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe more, and several times he looked at the access points to the ditch where they had met him. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were right at the access points with laundry workers washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were crowding, and from all sides, from the embankments, from all around, one could see, notice: it would look suspicious that a person deliberately went off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated away?
