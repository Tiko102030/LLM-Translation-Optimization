A small depression formed under the stone; immediately, he began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet ended up at the top, yet there was still room left. Then he grabbed the stone again, flipped it back to its original side with a single twist, and it landed exactly in its previous place, barely, slightly seeming higher. He scooped up some soil and pressed it down along the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the one before in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It has been lying here since the house was built and will remain there for just as long."
