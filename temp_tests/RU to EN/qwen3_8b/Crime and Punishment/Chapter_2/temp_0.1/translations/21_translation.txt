He breathed more freely. "Probably not that!" Gradually, he began to cheer himself up, urging himself with all his strength to cheer up and regain his senses. "A certain foolishness, a certain minor carelessness, and I might give myself away entirely!" Hmm… it's a pity there's no air here—stifling… My head spins even more… and my mind too…" 

He felt a terrible disorder throughout himself. He himself feared not being able to control himself. He tried to latch onto something and think about something completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The secretary, however, greatly interested him; he was always eager to guess something from his face, to figure him out. He was a very young man, about twenty-two, with a dark and lively face that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and with a fathom, with hair parted at the back, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings on his white, comb-cleaned fingers and gold chains on his vest. He even spoke two words in French to one of the former foreigners here, and very satisfactorily. "Madam Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down," he said to the briefly dressed crimson lady, who stood all the time as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby.
