— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It's known that gunpowder can't bear an insult. You must have offended him somehow, and you couldn't help it, — continued Nickodim Fomich, politely addressing Rascolnikov — but you're wasting your time: he's the most wonderful person, I'll tell you, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He got angry, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! Everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! They even called him in the regiment: "Captain Gunpowder"...

— What a fine regiment! — exclaimed Ilya Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely teased, but still somewhat irritable. Suddenly, Rascolnikov felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Well, goodness, captain, — he began, very casually, suddenly addressing Nickodim Fomich — take my situation into account... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
