Quite the opposite!...

All of a sudden he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately threw him off and bitterly surprised him:

"If indeed all this was done deliberately, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all these sufferings and deliberately went along with such a base, nasty, lowly deed? You wanted to throw it all into the water now, the wallet together with all the things you haven't even seen yet... How is that possible?"

Yes, that's exactly it; that's all true. He had known this all along, and it wasn't a new question for him at all; and when it was decided during the night to throw everything into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as though it was only natural, as though there was no other way... Yes, he knew all this and remembered it all; almost as if it had been decided yesterday, right at the moment when he was sitting by the chest and taking out the cases from it... And that's exactly what happened!... 'It's because I'm very ill,' he bitterly decided at last, 'I've tortured and torn myself apart myself, and I don't even know what I'm doing... And yesterday, and the day before, and all this time I've been tormenting myself... I'll recover and... stop tormenting myself... What if I never recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of all this!...' He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking hold of him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical repulsion towards everything he encountered and surrounded him, persistent, spiteful, hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive— their faces, their gait, their movements. He simply wanted to spit on someone, to bite someone, perhaps, if someone spoke to him...
