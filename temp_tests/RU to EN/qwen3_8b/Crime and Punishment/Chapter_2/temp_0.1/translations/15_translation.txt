"Put on the sock!" — it occurred to him suddenly, "it will get even dirtier in the dust, and the traces will disappear." But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with disgust and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other option, he put it on again — and laughed once more. "All of this is conditional, everything is relative, all of this is merely forms," — he thought briefly, with only a fleeting thought, while trembling all over, "after all, he did put it on! After all, he ended up by putting it on!" However, the laughter was immediately replaced by despair. "No, it's beyond my strength…" — he thought. His legs trembled. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head spun and ached from the heat.
