And lo and behold, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly recognized the voice of his mistress. She was screaming, wailing, and crying out, rushing and hurrying, spewing words so fast that they were unintelligible, pleading—obviously, to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the attacker had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was barely more than a rasp, yet the attacker was still saying something, speaking incoherently, rushing, gasping. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized this voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich. Ilyi Petrovich was here, beating the mistress! He was kicking her, hitting her head against the steps—clearly, one could hear it by the sounds, the screams, the blows! What was this—had the world turned upside down? One could hear people gathering on all floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, people converging. "But why, why, and how can this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he was hearing it all too clearly...
