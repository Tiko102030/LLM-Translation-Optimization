—"He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath from his entire chest,"— "It's just feverish weakness, a momentary delirium,"— and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a ray of sunlight illuminated his left boot: on the sock peeking out of the boot, as if signs appeared. He took off the boot: "Indeed, signs! The entire end of the sock is soaked in blood"; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly... "But what now to do with this? Where to put this sock, the frayed part, the pocket?" He gathered all of this in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. "Into the stove? But first they'll start digging in the stove. Burn it? And with what? There's not even a match."
