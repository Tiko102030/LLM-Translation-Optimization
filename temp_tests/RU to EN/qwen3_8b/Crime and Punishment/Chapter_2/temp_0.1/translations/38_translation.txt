And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled not with his quartermates, but with his closest friends, then, it seems, he would still have no human word to address them, his heart having suddenly grown empty. A dark feeling of tormenting, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. Not the baseness of his heartfelt confessions to Ilyá Petrovich, not the baseness and the corporal's triumph over him suddenly turned his heart upside down. Oh, what business does he have now with his own baseness, with all these ambitions, corporals, Germans, grievances, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were sentenced to be burned at this very moment, he would not have stirred, and perhaps would not even have listened attentively to the verdict. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt, with all the force of his feeling, that he could no longer address these people, not even with anything, not even with his sensitive expansivenesses, as before, but with anything at all. And what was most tormenting—this was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; a direct, most tormenting feeling among all the feelings he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form of such a statement, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (some day), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.
