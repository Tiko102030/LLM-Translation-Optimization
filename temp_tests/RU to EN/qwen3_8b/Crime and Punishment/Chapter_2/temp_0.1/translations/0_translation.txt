He lay like that for a very long time. Sometimes it seemed as if he were waking up, and during those moments he would notice that it was already night, yet the thought of getting up never occurred to him. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the couch, still stunned from his recent sleep. The terrible, desperate cries from the street reached him sharply, which he had heard every night under his window at three o'clock. It was these cries that woke him up now. "Ah! Now the drunkards from the taverns are coming out," he thought, "it's three o'clock— and suddenly he jumped up as if someone had yanked him off the couch. 'What! It's already three o'clock!' He sat on the couch—and then everything came back to him!"
