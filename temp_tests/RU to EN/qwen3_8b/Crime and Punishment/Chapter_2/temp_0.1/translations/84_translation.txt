—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with profound amazement and with dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really..."
In two minutes, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would come now as well. Two spoons, two plates, and the entire set of utensils appeared: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other things that had long since not been in such order. The tablecloth was clean. — Not bad, Nastasya, you'd better have Praskovya Pavlovna send off two beer bottles. We'll have a drink. — Well, you, bigfoot! — Nastasya murmured and went to carry out the command.
