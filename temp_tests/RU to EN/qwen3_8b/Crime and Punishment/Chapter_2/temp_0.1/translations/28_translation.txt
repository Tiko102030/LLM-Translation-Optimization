— Well, isn't she my mistress? — Well, so what if she's the mistress? The clerk looked at him with a compassionate smile tinged with some triumph, as if looking at a new recruit just beginning to be fired upon: "What, you feel now?" But what concern did he now have for borrowed writing, for punishment! Was it worth even the slightest worry, let alone any attention! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions himself, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the pressing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without foresight, without analysis, without future mysteries or riddles, without doubts or questions. It was a moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But at that very moment, in the office, something like thunder and lightning occurred. The ensign, still shaken by the disrespect, burning with anger, obviously wanting to support the wounded ambition, launched himself at the unfortunate "plump lady," who had been looking at him with the most foolish smile since he had entered. — And you, such a silly and peculiar one, — he shouted at the top of his lungs (the widow had already left), — what happened during the past night?
