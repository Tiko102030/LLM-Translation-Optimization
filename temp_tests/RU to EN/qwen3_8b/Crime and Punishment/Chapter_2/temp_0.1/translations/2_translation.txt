The light was sufficient, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, his entire outfit: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering from a chill, he began to remove everything from himself and once again examine the surroundings. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the spot where the pants had been cut off at the bottom and hung as frayed tassels, there remained thick traces of congealed blood on the frayed tassels. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the frayed tassels. More, it seemed, nothing else was there. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even remembered them now, as he examined the dress! What was this?
