—"All these sensitive details, my good sir, have nothing to do with us," scoffed Ilya Petrovich, "you must give your statement and commitment; as for all these tragic places and your being in love, it's entirely irrelevant to us." —"Well, you're cruel," muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He suddenly felt ashamed. —"Write down," said the scribe to Raskolnikov. —"What to write?" —asked Raskolnikov rather rudely. —"I'll dictate to you," said the scribe. Raskolnikov felt that the scribe had become more careless and contemptuous toward him since his confession, yet strangely enough, he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred in an instant, in one minute. If he had only thought a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, even imposing his feelings on them. And where did these feelings come from?
