Just spit on someone, bite them, I suppose, if anyone talked to him...  
He stopped suddenly as he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this? I came to Razumikhin on my own! It's the same story again, as before... It's very interesting, though: did I come on my own or just happened to walk here? Anyway; I said three days ago that I would go to him the next day, well, I'll go now! As if I couldn't visit him now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth-floor apartment. Razumikhin was home, in his own room, and at that moment he was occupied, writing, and opened the door for him himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his tattered robe, barefoot, disheveled, unshaven, and unclean. Surprise appeared on his face.
