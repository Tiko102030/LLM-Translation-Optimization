He remembered only that he had taken a sip of cold water and spilled it from the cup onto his chest. Then came amnesia. III

He, however, was not entirely in a state of amnesia throughout the illness; it was a feverish condition, with delirium and semi-consciousness. Much later he recalled many things. At times it seemed to him that a crowd had gathered around him and wanted to take him away somewhere, with much argument and quarreling about him. At other times he suddenly found himself alone in the room, everyone having left and fearing him, opening the door only occasionally to look at him, threatening him, whispering among themselves about something, laughing and mocking him. He often remembered Nastasya by his side; he distinguished also another person, very much as if he were familiar, but who exactly he could not figure out, and he longed for this, even weeping. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had already been lying there for a month; at other times — that it was still the same day. But about that — about that he had completely forgotten; instead, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something that could not be forgotten — tormented, suffering, recalling, groaning, falling into a frenzy or into a terrible, unbearable fear. Then he would try to get up, wanting to run away, but always someone would stop him by force, and he would again fall into helplessness and amnesia.
