Then he would try to get up, wanting to run away, but always someone would stop him by force, and he would again fall into helplessness and confusion. Finally, he fully came to himself. This happened in the morning, at ten o'clock. At this hour in the morning, on clear days, the sun always streamed across his right wall as a long strip, illuminating the corner by the door. Standing by his bed was Nastasia and another person, who was looking at him curiously and completely unknown to him. This was a young man in a kaftan, with a beard, and from appearance, he resembled an artelshchik. The landlady was peeking through the half-open door. Raskolnikov raised himself. — Who is this, Nastasia? — he asked, pointing at the young man.
