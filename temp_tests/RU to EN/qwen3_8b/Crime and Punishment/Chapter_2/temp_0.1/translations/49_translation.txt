He became extremely distracted and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to hurry! He went to the Neva along the B-Street; but on the way, another thought came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Is it not better to go somewhere very far, again even to the Islands, and there somewhere, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush, — to bury all this and the tree, perhaps, to notice?" And although he felt that he was not able to clearly and soundly discuss everything at this moment, but the thought seemed to him infallible. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, but another thing happened: as he was leaving the B-Street to the square, he suddenly saw to the left an entrance to the courtyard, surrounded by completely soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a soundproof, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also immediately from the gates, a wooden fence ran twenty steps into the courtyard, and then already made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
