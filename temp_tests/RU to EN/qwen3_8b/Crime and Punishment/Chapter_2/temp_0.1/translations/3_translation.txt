What's this? He immediately rushed to take them out and throw them on the table. Having selected everything, even turning his pockets inside out to make sure there wasn't anything left, he moved all this pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one place, the wallpaper that had peeled off the wall was torn: at once he began stuffing everything into this hole under the paper: "It's in! Everything out of sight and the wallet too!" — he happily thought, standing up and staring blankly at the corner, at the hole that had become even more pronounced. Suddenly he shuddered all over with fear: "My God," — he whispered in despair, — "what's happening to me? Is this hidden? Do they hide things like this?" Indeed, he hadn't expected anything but money; he thought there would be only money, and therefore hadn't prepared a place in advance — "but now, now what am I happy about?" — he thought. — "Do they hide things like this?"
