His face showed surprise. "What are you doing?" he shouted, examining his incoming companion from head to toe; then he fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad? You, brother, have outdone our brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's rags. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when Raskolnikov collapsed on the Turkish crepe paper divan, which was worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. "You're seriously ill, you know that?" he said, starting to feel his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. "No need," he said, "I came... that is, I had no lessons... I wanted to... in any case, I don't need lessons at all..."
