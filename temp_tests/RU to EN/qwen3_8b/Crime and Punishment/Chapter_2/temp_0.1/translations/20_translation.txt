It was some particularly disheveled person with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because he doesn't care at all," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there to the clerk," said the scribe, and with a finger he pointed forward, indicating the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), cramped and packed with people—crowds, somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the desk facing the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and ruddy-red with spots, an obvious woman, and dressed extremely elegantly, with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea plate, stood in the corner waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed his summons to the clerk. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said, "Wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He breathed more freely.
