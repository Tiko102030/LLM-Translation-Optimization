The plump lady sprang up from her seat upon seeing him and began sitting down with some peculiar enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a sublieutenant, assistant quartermaster, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally on both sides and extremely small facial features, nothing in particular except for some impudence. He glanced at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with disdain: his costume was too shabby, and despite all his humility, his posture was still not appropriate. Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too directly and for too long, so that the officer even took offense. "What's your business?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged fellow didn't even flinch from his lightning glance. "I was summoned… by order…" Raskolnikov replied rather awkwardly. "It's about the case concerning the recovery of money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers.
