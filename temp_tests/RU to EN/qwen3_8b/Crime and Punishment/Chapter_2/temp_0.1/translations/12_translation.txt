He did not answer and held the paper in his hands without unfolding it. — "Don't get up," Nastasia continued, softening and seeing that he was putting his legs down from the sofa. "He's ill, so just stay in bed; it won't burn. What's in your hands?" He looked: in his right hand were pieces of torn tassels, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, reflecting on it, he remembered how, half-awake in the heat, he tightly clutched all of it in his hand and then fell asleep again. — "What a bunch of rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, as if with a treasure..." — And Nastasia laughed in her painful, nervous way. At once, he tucked everything under his overcoat and stared intently at it. Though he could hardly think clearly at that moment, he felt that people would not treat him properly when they came to take him.
