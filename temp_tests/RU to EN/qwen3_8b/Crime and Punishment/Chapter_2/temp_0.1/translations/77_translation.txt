Well, then, and who are you? — I am a cooperativist in our office, from the merchant Shelopayev, and I came here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, that you came to your senses, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten and drunk for four days. Indeed, they gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it's all nonsense—something hit your head, perhaps. Some kind of nervous nonsense, the ration was bad, they gave little beer and garlic, that's why you got sick, but nothing, it will pass and settle down.
