Louise Ivanovna began to sit down in all directions with affected politeness, and as she sat down, she managed to reach the doors; but she bumped into a notable officer at the door, with an open, fresh face and superbly thick white mustaches. It was Nickodim Fomich himself, the quartermaster. Louise Ivanovna hastened to sit down almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, leaping about, flew out of the office. "Again thunder, again lightning and tornado, hurricane!" Nickodim Fomich politely and warmly addressed Ilya Petrovich, "Again they have disturbed the heart, again it is boiling! I even heard it from the staircase." "What's the matter!" Ilya Petrovich said with noble indifference (and even not "what's the matter," but rather "Da-aa shcha-aa!") while moving from some papers to another desk, dramatically jerking his shoulders with each step, where he stepped, there his shoulder went; "Here you are, my dear, behold: the gentleman author, that is, the student, once being, doesn't pay money, gave promissory notes, doesn't clean the apartment, they constantly complain about him, and they have entered a claim that I smoked a cigarette in their presence! They themselves are behaving badly, but here you are, my dear, behold them: here they are in their most attractive present form!" "Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what's to be done!"
