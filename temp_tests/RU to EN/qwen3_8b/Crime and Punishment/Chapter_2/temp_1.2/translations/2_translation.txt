The light was sufficient, and he hurriedly began examining himself from head to toe, his entire dress: were there any traces? But that was impossible: shivering with chills, he began removing everything from his body and once again inspecting the surroundings. He turned everything over, down to the last thread and shred, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. Yet there was nothing, apparently, no traces; only on the place where the pants had been cut off and hung with tassels, on these tassels remained dense traces of coagulated blood. He grabbed a small folding knife and cut off the tassels. It seemed there was nothing else. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and the belongings he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He had not even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even thought of them now, while inspecting the dress! What on earth is this?
