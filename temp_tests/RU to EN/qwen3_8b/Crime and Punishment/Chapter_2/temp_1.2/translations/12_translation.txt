He did not answer and held the paper in his hands without unfolding it. "Don't get up," Nastasia continued, piteously lamenting and seeing that he was putting his legs down from the couch. "He's ill, so don't go out—don't let him burn up. What's in your hands, then? He looked: in his right hand he held pieces of torn tassels, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, reflecting on it, he remembered that, half-awake in the heat, he tightly clutched all of it in his hand and fell asleep again. "Look at all the rags he's gathered up and sleeps with them, just like with a treasure…" And Nastasia burst out in her painfully nervous laugh. He quickly tucked everything under his coat and fixedly stared at it. Though he could hardly make much sense of it at that moment, he felt that people would not treat him properly when his brother came to take him.
