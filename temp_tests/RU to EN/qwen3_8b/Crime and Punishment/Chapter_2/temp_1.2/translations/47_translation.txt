Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though feeling thoroughly battered, yet his mind remained clear. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, perhaps even within a quarter of an hour, someone would come out, probably with instructions to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to get rid of the matter before the time came. He had to act swiftly while he still had some strength and some ability to think… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: “Throw everything into the ditch, dump the ends into the water, and that's that.” He had decided this during the night, in a fit of delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he had several times tried to get up and go: “Quickly, quickly, and get rid of everything.” But it proved very difficult to get rid of things. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, perhaps even more, and several times he looked at the wharf in the ditch where they had met him. But it was impossible to carry out the plan: either the rafts were right at the wharf and the laundrywomen were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were densely gathered, and from all sides, from all directions, one could see and notice that it looked suspicious for a person to deliberately come out, stop, and throw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated?
