There are no clues!" — and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed with a nervous, small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and kept laughing all the time as he passed through the square. But when he stepped onto Kth Boulevard, where he had met that girl three days ago, his laughter suddenly ceased. Other thoughts came into his head. Suddenly it seemed to him that it was extremely repulsive to pass by that bench where he had sat and pondered after the girl had left, and it would also be extremely difficult to meet again that barber to whom he had given two kopecks: "By hell!" He walked, looking around carelessly and bitterly. All his thoughts now revolved around some main point, and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and that now, precisely now, he was alone with this main point — and that this was even the first time since these two months. "By hell, that's all!"
