The scribe began dictating to him the form of a regular withdrawal in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, but I promise then—when-ever, I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.—You cannot write at all, your quill is falling from your hand—remarked the scribe, keenly observing Raskolnikov. —Are you ill? —Yes… my head is spinning… go on, speak! —Yes, everything; sign it. The scribe took the paper and began dealing with others. Raskolnikov handed over the quill, but instead of getting up and leaving, he put both elbows on the table and clasped his head with his hands. It felt as if a nail was being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to stand up now, go to Nikodim Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, everything down to the last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and show them the items in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat, ready to carry it out.
