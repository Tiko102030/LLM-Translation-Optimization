Where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled not with his quarter friends, but with his very closest friends, then, it seems, he would still have no human word to say to them, his heart having suddenly grown so empty. A dark feeling of agonizing, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. Not the baseness of his heartfelt confessions to Ilyy Petrovich, nor the baseness and the ensign's triumph over him suddenly overturned his heart. Oh, what does it matter to him now about his own baseness, about all these ambitions, ensigns, Germans, grievances, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were condemned to be burned at this very moment, he would still not move, and perhaps would not even listen attentively to the verdict. Something entirely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. He did not understand it, but he clearly felt it with all the strength of his feeling—that not only with his sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else, he could no longer address these people in the quarter office. Even if they were all his own brothers and sisters, and not the quarter ensigns, he would still have no need to address them at all, and certainly never in any case of his life; he had never before felt such a strange and terrifying sensation. And what was most tormenting was that it was more of a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; a direct, most tormenting feeling out of all the sensations he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the standard form of such a statement, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.
