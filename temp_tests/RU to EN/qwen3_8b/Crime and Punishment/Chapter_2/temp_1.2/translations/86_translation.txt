— Hurry up and have some tea, Nastasya, because, I suppose, you don't need a faculty for that. But here's the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and meat close, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. — I, brother Rodia, have lunch here every day now, — he murmured, as much as his full mouth of meat allowed, — and all thanks to Pashenka, your housekeeper, who's treating me with such heartfelt hospitality. I, of course, don't insist, but neither do I protest. And here comes Nastasya with the tea. How quick she is! Nastenka, do you want some beer? — Oh, you mischievous fellow! — A cup of tea?
