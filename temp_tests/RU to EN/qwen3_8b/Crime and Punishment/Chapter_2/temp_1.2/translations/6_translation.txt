At that moment, a sunbeam lit up his left boot: on the sock protruding from the boot, as if signs appeared. He kicked off the boot: “Indeed, signs! The entire end of the sock is soaked in blood”; he must have carelessly stepped into that puddle… “But what am I to do with this now? Where to put this sock, the bandage, the pocket lining?” He scooped all of it up in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. “Into the fireplace? But first they will start digging through the fireplace. Burn it? And with what? There's not even a match.”
