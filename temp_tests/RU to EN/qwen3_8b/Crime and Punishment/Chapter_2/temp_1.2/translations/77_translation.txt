Well then, who are you? — I am a cooperative member in our office, from the merchant Shelopayev, and I came here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — said Razumikhin, sitting down on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to come to your senses. — He continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten or drunk for four days. Honestly, they even gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense — as if something hit you on the head. Some kind of nervous nonsense, he said, you had a bad ration, they gave you little beer and garlic, that's why you got ill, but don't worry, it will pass and get better.
