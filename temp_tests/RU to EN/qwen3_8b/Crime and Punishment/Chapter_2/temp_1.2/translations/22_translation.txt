— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — he said to the heavily made-up, crimson-blonde lady, who stood still as if daunted to sit herself, though the chair was right there. — Ich danke,[17 – Thank you (German) – Ed.] — she said quietly, with a silken rustle, and sat down. Her light sky-blue dress, adorned with white lace, spread out around the chair like an air balloon, taking up almost half the room. It filled the air with perfume. Yet the lady, evidently timid, seemed flustered by taking up half the room and the strong perfume, though she smiled foolishly and boldly, yet clearly uneasy. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, in a particularly swaggering manner, twisting her shoulders with each step, entered an officer, flung his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat down in the chair. The voluminous lady leaped up from her seat upon seeing him, and began to curtsey with a special, almost fanatical enthusiasm; but the officer paid her no attention at all, and she no longer dared sit in his presence.
