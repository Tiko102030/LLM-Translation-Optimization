What's this? He rushed to take them out and throw them on the table. Having selected everything, even turning out his pockets to make sure there wasn't anything left, he carried all this pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one spot, the wallpaper that had come loose from the wall was torn: at once he began to shove everything into this hole, under the paper: "It's all gone! Everything out of sight and the wallet too!" — he happily thought, standing up and stupidly gazing at the corner, at the gaping hole even more pronounced. Suddenly he shuddered with fear: "My God," he muttered in despair, "what has happened to me? Is this how it's done? Do they hide things like this?" 

Indeed, he had not expected to find things; he thought there would be only money, so he hadn't prepared a hiding place in advance — "But now, now, what am I happy about? — he thought. — Is this how one hides things?"
