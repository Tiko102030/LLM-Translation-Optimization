"Pieces of torn linen will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he kept repeating, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful intensity he began to scan the surroundings, the floor, everywhere, making sure he hadn't forgotten anything. The certainty that everything, even memory, even simple reasoning, left him started to torment him intolerably. "What, is it really beginning, is it already the execution? There, there, that's exactly it!" Indeed, the frayed fringe he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, visible to the first observer. "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. A strange thought came to him: perhaps his entire clothing was stained with blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his reasoning had weakened, been shattered… his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there had been blood on his wallet. "Bah! So, there must be blood in the pocket too, since I had shoved the wet wallet into the pocket!" He immediately turned the pocket inside out, and indeed—there were traces, stains on the lining! "Therefore, I haven't entirely lost my wits, therefore there is reasoning and memory, since I realized and guessed on my own!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest, "it's merely feverish weakness, a momentary delirium!" And he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
