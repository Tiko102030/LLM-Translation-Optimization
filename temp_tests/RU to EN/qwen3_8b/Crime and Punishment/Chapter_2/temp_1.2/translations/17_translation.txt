"If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought as he approached the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away. She had just moved to a new apartment, a new building, to the fourth floor. He had been there once before, but a long time ago. Entering the gates, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a man was descending with a book in his hand: "A janitor, then; so the office must be here," and he started going up at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. "I'll go in, kneel down, and tell everything…" he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and entirely covered in filth. All the kitchens from all the apartments in all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood there almost all day. That's why the air was stifling.
