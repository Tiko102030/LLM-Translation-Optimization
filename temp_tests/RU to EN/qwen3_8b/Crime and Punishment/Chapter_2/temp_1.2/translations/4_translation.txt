"— Is that how they hide? My mind is truly leaving me!" He collapsed into the armchair, and at once an unbearable chill shook him. Mechanically, he pulled up the old winter coat, which had once belonged to him, warm but already almost in rags, covered himself with it, and sleep, and delirium once more overwhelmed him. He fell into oblivion. No more than five minutes later he jumped up again, and immediately, in a frenzy, he rushed back to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, when nothing has been done! So it is, so it is: the loop under my armpit is still not removed! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He tore off the loop and quickly began to tear it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow along with the underwear. "Pieces of torn flannel will certainly not raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he once again began to scrutinize all around, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?
