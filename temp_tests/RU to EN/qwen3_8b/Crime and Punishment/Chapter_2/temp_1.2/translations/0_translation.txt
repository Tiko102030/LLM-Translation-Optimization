Thus he lay there for a very long time. It happened that he seemed to wake up, and during these moments he noticed that it was already night, and the idea of getting up never crossed his mind. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the sofa on his back, still stunned from recent forgetfulness. Sharp and terrifying cries from the street reached him, which, in fact, he had listened to every night under his window at the third hour. It was these cries that woke him up now. "Ah! Here they come, the drunkards from the taverns," he thought, "third hour," – and suddenly he jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the sofa. "What! It's already the third hour!" He sat on the sofa – and then everything came back to him!
