He drew a deeper breath. "Probably not!" Gradually, he began to cheer himself up, urging himself with all his strength to regain composure and shake off his stupor. "Some trivial nonsense, some minor carelessness, and I might give myself away entirely!" He added, "Hmm... I wish there were air here—there's a stuffiness... My head spins even more... and my mind too..." He felt a terrible disorder within himself. He himself feared not being able to control himself. He tried to fix his attention on something and think about something entirely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The scribe, however, greatly interested him; he wanted to read something into his face, to decipher him. This was a very young man, about twenty-two, with a dark and lively face that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and with a fatigued look, with a parting on the back of his head, combed and powdered, with numerous rings and finger rings on his white, meticulously cleaned fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. He even spoke a couple of words in French to one of the former foreigners here, and quite satisfactorily. "— Madame Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down," he said to the brightly dressed crimson lady, who stood still, as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby.
