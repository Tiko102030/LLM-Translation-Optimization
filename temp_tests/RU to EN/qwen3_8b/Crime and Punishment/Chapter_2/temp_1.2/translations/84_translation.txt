—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep amazement and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is actually..." 

Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with the soup and announced that tea would come soon. Along with the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire set of utensils: saltcellar, pepper mill, mustard for beef, and other items that had long since ceased to be in such order. The tablecloth was clean. — It would not be bad, Nastasya, if Praskovya Pavlovna sent two beer bottles. We'll drink, shall we. — Well, you big-nosed one! — Nastasya muttered and went to carry out the order.
