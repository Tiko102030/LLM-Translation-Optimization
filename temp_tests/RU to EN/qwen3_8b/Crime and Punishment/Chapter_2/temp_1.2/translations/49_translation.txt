He became extraordinarily scattered and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along the Vosstaniya Prospekt; but as he walked, a new thought suddenly came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far away, perhaps back to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush, bury everything and the tree, perhaps? Maybe it would be noticed?" Although he felt he was not capable of clearly and rationally considering all this at the moment, the thought seemed unquestionably correct to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, and something else happened: as he was leaving the Vosstaniya Prospekt onto the square, he suddenly saw to the left an entrance to a courtyard enclosed by completely soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a soundproof, unwhitewashed wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also right next to the gates, ran a wooden fence extending twenty steps into the courtyard, and then it made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, enclosed place where certain materials lay. Further, in the recess of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
