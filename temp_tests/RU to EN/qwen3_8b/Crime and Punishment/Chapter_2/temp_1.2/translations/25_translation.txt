— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some droplets were spewing from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Please be quiet! You are in the presence. Don't be a brute, sir! — "And you are also in the presence," cried Razumov, "and besides shouting, you are smoking a cigarette, which means you are insulting us all." After saying this, Razumov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk watched them with a smile.
