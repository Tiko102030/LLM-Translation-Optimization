There was terrible stuffiness. Cleaners with books under their arms ascended and descended, while a motley crowd of both sexes—visitors—moved about. The door to the main office was also ajar. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Here, everyone stood waiting for some men. There was also an extraordinary feeling of stuffiness, and in addition, the fresh, still-wet paint on old linseed oil caused a nausea-inducing smell. After waiting a while, he decided to move further ahead into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. A dreadful impatience pulled him further and further. No one noticed him.
