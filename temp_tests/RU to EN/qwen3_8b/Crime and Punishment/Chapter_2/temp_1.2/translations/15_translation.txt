Put on the sock! — it suddenly occurred to him, — it will get even dirtier, and the traces will disappear. But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with repulsion and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other choice, he put it on again—and laughed again. "All this is conditional, all relative, all just forms," — he thought briefly, with only a fragment of a thought, while trembling all over, — for here he had put it on! For he had ended up by putting it on!" Laughter, however, immediately turned into despair. "No, it's beyond my strength..." — he thought. His legs trembled. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head spun and ached from the heat.
