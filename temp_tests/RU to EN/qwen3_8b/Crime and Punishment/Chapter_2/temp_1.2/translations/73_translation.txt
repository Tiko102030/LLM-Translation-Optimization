He remembered only that he had taken a sip of cold water and spilled it from the cup onto his chest. Then came the state of amnesia. III

He, however, was not entirely in a state of amnesia throughout his illness; it was a feverish condition, with delirium and semi-consciousness. Later, he recalled many things. Sometimes it seemed to him that a crowd of people gathered around him, wanting to take him away somewhere, arguing fiercely and quarreling over him. At other times, he suddenly found himself alone in the room, with everyone having gone away, and people feared him; they opened the door occasionally, just a little, to look at him, threatening him, whispering among themselves, laughing, and taunting him. He often remembered Nastasya by his side; he distinguished also another man, who seemed very familiar to him, but he could not figure out who exactly it was, and he longed for this, even weeping. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had already been lying there for a month; at other times, that it was still the same day. But of that—he had completely forgotten; instead, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something he could not forget—tormented, suffering, recalling, groaning, plunging into a rage or into a terrible, unbearable fear. Then he would try to get up, wanting to run away, but someone always stopped him by force, and he would again fall into helplessness and amnesia.
