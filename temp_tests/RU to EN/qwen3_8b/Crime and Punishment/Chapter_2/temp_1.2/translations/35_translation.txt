— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can I say! It's well known that powder can't bear insult. You, I suppose, have taken offense against him and couldn't help it—Nikodim Fomich continued, politely addressing Raskolnikov—But you're wasting your time: he's the most honorable man, I'll tell you, but powder, powder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out—and there was none left! Everything passed away! And in the end, only a heart of gold! They called him in the regiment: "the Powder Ensign"…

— What a regiment! — exclaimed Ilya Petrovich, very pleased that they had so nicely tickled him, but still rubbing his cheek. Suddenly Raskolnikov felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Oh, pardon me, Captain, — he began, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly in a very free manner — take my position into account… I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I have been at fault on my side.
