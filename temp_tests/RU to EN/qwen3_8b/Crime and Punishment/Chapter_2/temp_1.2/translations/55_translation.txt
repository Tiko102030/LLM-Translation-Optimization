I'd just spit on someone and bite them, I suppose, if someone talked to me... He suddenly stopped when he emerged onto the embankment of the Little Neva on Vasilievsky Island, near the bridge. "This is where he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I actually came to Razumikhin by myself! It's the same story again, like back then... But it's very interesting, indeed: did I come myself or just walked here by accident? Anyway; I said... three days ago... that I would go to him the next day after that, well, I'll go! As if I can't visit him now..." He went up to Razumikhin's apartment on the fifth floor. He was home, in his small room, and at that moment he was occupied, writing, and opened the door himself. It had been four months since they had last seen each other. Razumikhin was sitting in his room in a tattered coat worn to rags, in socks, barefoot, tousled, unshaven, and unclean. Surprise appeared on his face.
