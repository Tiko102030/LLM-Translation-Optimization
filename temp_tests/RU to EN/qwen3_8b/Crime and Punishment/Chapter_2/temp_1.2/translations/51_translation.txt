A small hollow formed under the stone; immediately, he began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet ended up at the very top, yet there was still room left. Then he grabbed the stone again, flipped it back to its original side with a single twist, and it landed in its former place, though slightly higher, just a little bit. He tamped down the soil and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like before in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It's been here since the house was built and will remain here for just as long."
