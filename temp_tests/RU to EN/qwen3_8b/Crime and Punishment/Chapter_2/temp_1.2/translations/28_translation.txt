— Well, isn't she my mistress? — So what if she is your mistress? The clerk looked at him with a compassionate smirk tinged with some triumph, as if he were a novice just beginning to be fired upon: "So, tell me, how do you feel now?" But what did it matter now to him about borrowed writing, about reprimands! Was it worth the slightest worry, let alone any attention! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions himself, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the crushing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without forethought, without analysis, without future speculations or guesses, without doubts or questions. It was a moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But at that very moment, something like thunder and lightning happened in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by the disrespect, full of fury and obviously wanting to support the wounded ambition, pounced on the unfortunate "plump lady," who had been looking at him since he entered with the most foolish grin. — So you, such a one and such another, — he suddenly shouted at the top of his voice (the widow had already left), — what happened to you last night?
