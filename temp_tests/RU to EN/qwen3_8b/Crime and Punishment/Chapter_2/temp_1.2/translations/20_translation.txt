He was some particularly wild-haired person with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there to the clerk," said the clerk, and with a finger he pointed forward, indicating the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), which was cramped and crowded with people—people somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Between the visitors were two women. One in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk, writing something under his dictation. The other woman, very plump and ruddy-red, with freckles, a noticeable woman, and something excessively gaudily dressed, with a brooch on her chest, the size of a tea plate, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov slipped his summons to the clerk. He glanced at it briefly, said "Wait," and continued working with the mourning woman. He drew a breath more freely.
