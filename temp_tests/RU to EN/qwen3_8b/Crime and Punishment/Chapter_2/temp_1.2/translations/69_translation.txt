But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, if that's the case, they'll come to him now, too, because... it's surely all from the same thing... from yesterday's... Lord! He had intended to shut himself in, but his hand wouldn't raise... and it was useless anyway! Fear, like ice, had encased his soul, tormented him, paralyzed him... But at last, after this whole commotion, which had lasted for about ten minutes, began to gradually subside. The householder was groaning and sobbing, and Ilyá Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But at last, apparently, he also fell silent; now there was no more sound of him; "could he have left already? Lord!" Yes, the householder is already leaving, still groaning and weeping... and now her door has closed... And now the crowd is dispersing along the stairs to their apartments, shouting, quarreling, calling out to one another, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been quite a number of them; perhaps the whole building had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!" 

Raskólnikov, in utter helplessness, fell onto the sofa, but he could no longer close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feelings of infinite terror, as he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a soup dish. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he was not asleep, she placed the candle on the table and began to set out the things she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
