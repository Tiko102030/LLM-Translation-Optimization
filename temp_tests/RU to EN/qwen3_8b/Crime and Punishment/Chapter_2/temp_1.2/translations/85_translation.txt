— Nastasya murmured and went to carry out the command. Raskolnikov continued to stare at him wildly and with tension. Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved to sit on the divan, awkwardly like a bear, encircled his head with his left hand, despite the fact that he himself could have raised up, and with his right hand brought a spoon of soup to his mouth, having previously blown on it several times to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoonful, then another, the third. But after bringing several spoons, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that regarding further proceedings, they needed to consult with Zosimov. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want some tea? — I want. — Hurry up and make tea, Nastasya, because, regarding tea, it seems one can do without the faculty.
