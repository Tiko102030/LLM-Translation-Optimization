—"All these sensitive details, your grace, have nothing to do with us," Ilya Petrovich rudely cut in, "you must give your statement and commitment; as for your being in love and all these tragic places, it's entirely irrelevant to us." —"Well, you... you're cruel," muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also starting to sign. He felt somewhat ashamed. —"Write down," said the scribe to Rasputin. —"What to write?" —asked he rather coarsely. —"I'll dictate to you," said the scribe. Rasputin felt that the scribe had become more indifferent and contemptuous toward him since his confession. But strangely enough, —he suddenly felt himself quite indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been amazed at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
