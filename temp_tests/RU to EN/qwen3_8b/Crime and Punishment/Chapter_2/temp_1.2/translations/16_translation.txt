His head spun and throbbed from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with deceit and suddenly take me down altogether," he continued thinking to himself as he came down the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the hole in the wall, "--and here, perhaps intentionally, they'll search without him," he remembered and stopped. But such despair and, if one can say so, a cynicism about his death suddenly took hold of him, so he waved his hand and went on. "Just hurry up..."  

On the street the heat was unbearable again; only a drop of rain would have been welcome these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and drinking dens, again constantly drunk huckster peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun blazed into his eyes, making it painful to look and his head spun completely--a common sensation for a feverish person suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Reaching the turn on yesterday's street, he looked with agonizing dread at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
