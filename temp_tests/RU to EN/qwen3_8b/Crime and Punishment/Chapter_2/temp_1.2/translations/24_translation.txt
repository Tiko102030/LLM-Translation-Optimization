"Money? What money? — thought Raskolnikov — but… surely, it's not that!" And he shivered with joy. Suddenly, it became terribly, immeasurably easy for him. Everything fell from his shoulders. — "In what hour is it written that you should come, Your Grace?" — shouted the ensign, increasingly and more unknown to himself offended — "You are written to come at nine, but now it's already twelve o'clock!" — "I was brought a quarter of an hour ago," — answered Raskolnikov loudly over his shoulder, also suddenly and unexpectedly for himself getting angry and even finding some pleasure in it. — "That's enough that I came sick with a fever." — "Do not shout!"
