His head spun and throbbed from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with deception and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued thinking to himself as he climbed the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything as it was, in the interior wall hole—"and here, perhaps intentionally without it, there will be a search,"—and he stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say so, cynicism about dying suddenly seized him, and he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's quick...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; even a drop of rain would have been welcome during all these days. Again dust, bricks, and plaster, again the stench from the benches and taverns, again the drunken peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun gleamed brightly into his eyes, so that it became painful to look, and his head completely spun— the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner leading to yesterday's street, he looked with a painfully anxious gaze into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office.
