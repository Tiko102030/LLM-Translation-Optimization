Well, then, who are you? — I'm a partner in our firm, with merchant Shelopaev, and I've come here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — said Razumikhin, sitting on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to wake up. — He continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten or drunk for four days. Honestly, they gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it's all nonsense, — as if something had hit you on the head. Some kind of nervous disorder, he said, the food was poor, they gave little beer and garlic, that's why you got sick, but nothing serious, it will pass and disappear.
