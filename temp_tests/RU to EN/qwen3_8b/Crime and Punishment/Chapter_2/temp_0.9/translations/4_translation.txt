"— Is that how they hide? My mind is leaving me!" He collapsed into a chair in exhaustion, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him. Mechanically, he pulled his old student winter coat, warm but already in rags, from the chair beside him, covered himself with it, and once again sleep and delirium seized him. He lost consciousness. No more than five minutes later he sat up again and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed back to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, when nothing has been done! Exactly so, exactly so: the loop under my armpit is still there! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the loop down and began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in his underwear. "Torn linen scraps will certainly not raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a painful intensity of attention, he began once again to scan around, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?
