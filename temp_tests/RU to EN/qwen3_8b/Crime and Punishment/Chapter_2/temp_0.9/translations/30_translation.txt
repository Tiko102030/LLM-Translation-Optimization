— Ilya Petrovich! — the scribe began, with care, but paused to wait for the right moment, because you couldn't restrain the boiling corporal except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the grand lady, at first she trembled at the thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and fierce the curses became, the more pleasant her demeanor grew, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the thunderous corporal. She shuffled in place and continuously bowed, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak and finally did. — There's no noise or fight here, sir captain, — she chattered suddenly, as if spilling peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently in Russian, — and no scandal at all, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, sir captain, and I'm not at fault… I have a noble house, sir captain, and noble manners, sir captain, and I've always, always wanted no scandal. They came completely drunk and then asked three pathetic questions, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, which is very bad in a noble house, and he completely broke the piano, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I said. He took the pathetic and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the butler and Karl came, he took Karl and hit him in the eye, and Henrietta also got hit in the eye, and I got slapped five times on the cheek. And this is very indecent in a noble house, sir captain, and I cried out. And he opened the ditch window and started squealing from the window like a little pig; and this is a disgrace.
