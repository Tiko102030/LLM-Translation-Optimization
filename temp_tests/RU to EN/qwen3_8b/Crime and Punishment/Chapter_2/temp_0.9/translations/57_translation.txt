—"No need," he said, "I came… here's the thing: I have no lessons… I wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all…" 

—"Do you know what? You're hallucinating!" remarked Razumikhin, who had been watching him closely. "No, I'm not raving…" Raskolnikov got up from the sofa. As he rose to face Razumikhin, he didn't realize that he was destined to meet him face to face. Now, in an instant, he suddenly realized, through experience, that at this very moment he was least inclined to face anyone in the entire world. All his bitterness surged within him. He was almost overcome by hatred for himself the moment he crossed Razumikhin's threshold. "Goodbye!" he suddenly said and went toward the door.
