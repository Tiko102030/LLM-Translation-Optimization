But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, if that's the case, they will come to him now too, because... it's all the same... because of yesterday's... Lord! He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand would not rise... and it was useless! Fear, like ice, had covered his soul, tormented him, paralyzed him... But at last, after this turmoil, which had lasted for sure ten minutes, began to gradually subside. The mistress was groaning and moaning, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and shouting... But at last, it seemed, he had finally fallen silent; now there was no sound of him; "perhaps he's gone! Lord!" Yes, the mistress is leaving too, still groaning and weeping... and now her door has closed... And now the crowd is dispersing down the stairs to their apartments, exclaiming, arguing, shouting, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been quite a number of them; perhaps the whole building had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!" Raskolnikov, in helplessness, fell onto the sofa, but could no longer close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such suffering, in such an unbearable feeling of boundless terror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a tray of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he was not asleep, she placed the candle on the table and began to arrange the items: bread, salt, a tray, a spoon.
