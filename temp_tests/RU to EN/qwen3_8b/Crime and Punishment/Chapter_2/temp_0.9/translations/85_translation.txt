Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved awkwardly onto the couch, like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, despite the fact that he could have raised himself, and brought a spoonful of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed a spoonful, then another, the third. But while bringing several spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and announced that they needed to consult with Zosimov about the further course of action. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want some tea? — I want. — Go ahead and make tea, Nastasya, because, I suppose, you can do it without the faculty.
