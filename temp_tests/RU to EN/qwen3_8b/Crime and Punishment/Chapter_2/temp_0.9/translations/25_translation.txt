— Do not shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant had grown so furious that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some spluttering came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Do you please be quiet! You are in the presence. Do not act like a rustic, sir! — Yes, and you are in the presence, — cried Raskolnikov, — and besides, you are not only shouting, but also smoking a cigarette; therefore, you are insulting us all. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The secretary looked at them with a smile.
