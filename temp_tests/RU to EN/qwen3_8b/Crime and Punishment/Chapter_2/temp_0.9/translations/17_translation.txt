"If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away. She had just moved to a new apartment, a new building, on the fourth floor. He had once briefly visited her old apartment, but that was a long time ago. Entering under the gates, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a man was coming with a book in his hand: "A caretaker, then; so the office must be here," and he began climbing up randomly. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. "I'll go in, kneel down, and tell everything..." he thought, entering the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and entirely covered in filth. All the kitchens of the apartments in the four floors opened onto this staircase and stood like that almost all day. That's why there was a suffocating heat.
