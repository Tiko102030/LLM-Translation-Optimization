—"With triumph, he deeply and joyfully drew a breath, thinking, 'It's merely feverish weakness, a momentary delirium,'—and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a ray of sunlight illuminated his left boot: on the sock, which peeked out of the boot as if showing signs. He kicked off the boot: 'Indeed, signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood'; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly... 'But what am I to do now? Where to put this sock, the bandage, the pocket?' He gathered all of it in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. 'Into the stove? But first they'll start digging through the stove. To burn it? And with what? There are even no matches."
