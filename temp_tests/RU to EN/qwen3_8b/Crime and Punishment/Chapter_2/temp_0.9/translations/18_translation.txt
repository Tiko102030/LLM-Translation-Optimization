There was a terrible stuffiness. Up and down, janitors with books under their arms came and went, along with strapping and various people of both sexes—visitors. The door into the very office was also left ajar. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Here, everyone stood and waited for some men. Here, too, the stuffiness was extraordinary, and, in addition, it was hitting the nose to the point of nausea with the fresh, still not dried paint on the rancid linseed oil of the newly painted rooms. After waiting a bit, he decided to move further into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. Terrible impatience was pulling him further and further. No one noticed him.
