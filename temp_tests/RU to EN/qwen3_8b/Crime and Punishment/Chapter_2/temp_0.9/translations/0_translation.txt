He lay there for a very long time. Occasionally, it seemed as if he were waking up, and during those moments he noticed that it was already night, yet the thought of getting up never occurred to him. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the couch, still stunned from his recent slumber. Suddenly, he heard the fearful, desperate cries from the street, which he had heard every night at three o'clock under his window. It was these cries that woke him up now. "Ah! Here come the drunkards from the taverns at last," he thought. "Three o'clock—it's three o'clock!" and suddenly jumped up as if someone had yanked him off the couch. "What! It's already three o'clock!" He sat down on the couch, and then everything came back to him!
