Just spit on someone, bite them, I suppose, if someone talked to him...  
He stopped suddenly when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I came to Razumikhin on my own! It's the same story again, as before... It's very curious, indeed: did I come on my own or just walked here? Anyway; I said... three days ago... that I would go to him the next day, well, I'll go! As if I can't go now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. He was home, in his room, and at that moment he was occupied, writing, and opened the door for him himself. Four months had passed since they last saw each other. Razumikhin was sitting in his own room in a tattered, threadbare robe, in socks on bare feet, tousled, unshaven, and unwashed. Surprise appeared on his face.
