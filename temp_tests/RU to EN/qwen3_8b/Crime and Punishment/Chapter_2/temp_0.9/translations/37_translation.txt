— All these sensitive details, my good sir, have nothing to do with us," snarled Ilyá Petrovich, "you must give your statement and commitment, and as for your being in love there and all these tragic places, it's entirely none of our business." — Well, you... are cruel..." muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He somehow felt ashamed. — Write, then," said the scribe to Raskolnikov. — What to write?" asked he, rather rudely. — I shall dictate. Raskolnikov thought that the scribe had become more careless and contemptuous toward him after his confession, but, strangely enough, he suddenly felt entirely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in an instant, in one minute. If he had thought a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago and even impose his feelings upon them? And where did these feelings come from?
