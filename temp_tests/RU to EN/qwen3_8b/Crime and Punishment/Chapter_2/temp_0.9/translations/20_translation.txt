This was some particularly disheveled person with an immovable idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because he doesn't care at all," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there to the clerk," said the clerk, jabbing his finger forward and pointing to the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), a cramped and crowded space filled with people—somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and reddish-brown with freckles, a noticeable woman, extremely overdressed with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea plate, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed his summons to the clerk. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said, "Wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He exhaled more freely.
