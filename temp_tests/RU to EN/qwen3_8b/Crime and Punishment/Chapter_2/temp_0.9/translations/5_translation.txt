"Pieces of torn fabric will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful intensity once more scanned the area, the floor, everywhere, making sure he hadn't forgotten anything? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, had left him began to torment him intolerably. "What, is it beginning? Is this punishment about to start? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the frayed tassels he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at once! "What is happening to me!" he cried out again, as if lost. Then a strange thought came to him: perhaps all his clothing was covered in blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he simply didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his reasoning had weakened, fragmented… his mind was clouded… Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So, there must also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" Instantly he turned the pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains! "Therefore, I have not completely lost my senses, there is still thought and memory, since I have just realized and guessed it myself!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his chest, "it's merely the weakness of fever, a momentary delirium," and he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
