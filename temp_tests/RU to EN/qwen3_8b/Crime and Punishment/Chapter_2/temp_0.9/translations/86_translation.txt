— Hurry up, Nastasya, and bring the tea, because, I suppose, you don't even need a faculty for that. But here's the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and beef closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten in three days. — I, brother Rodia, have lunch like this every day here, — he muttered, as much as his mouth full of beef allowed, — and it's all Pashenka, your housekeeper, who's taking charge and worshiping me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. Here's Nastasya with the tea. How quick she is! Nastenka, want some beer? — And now to the devil! — And a cup of tea?
