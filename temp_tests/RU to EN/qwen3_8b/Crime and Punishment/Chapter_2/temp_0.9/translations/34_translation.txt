Louise Ivanovna, with a morning politeness, began to sit down on all sides and, while sitting, reached the doors; but she bumped into a prominent officer at the door, with a fresh open face and superb thick white mustaches. It was Nicholas Fomich himself, the quarter supervisor. Louise Ivanovna hastened to sit down almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, leaping about, flew out of the office. "Again a crash, again thunder and lightning, a tornado, a hurricane!" Nicholas Fomich politely and friendlily addressed Ilyia Petrovich, "again disturbed the heart, again it boiled over! I even heard it from the stairs." "What's this!" Ilyia Petrovich said with noble indifference (and even not "what's this," but rather: "Da-aa shcha-aa!") while moving from some papers to another table and dramatically jerking his shoulders with every step, where he stepped, there his shoulder went; "Here you see, sir: the gentleman, that is, the student, who used to be, does not pay money, gave promissory notes, does not clean the apartment, and continuous complaints are coming in about them, and they entered a complaint that I smoked a cigarette in front of them! They themselves are being rude, but here, you see, here they are in their most attractive present appearance!" "Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what's to be done!"
