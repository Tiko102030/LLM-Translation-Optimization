Well, why would they inquire about residence if they had that intention? As for Koch, he sat for half an hour downstairs with the silverware man, and precisely at 7:58 he went up to the old woman. Now figure it out...

— But allow me, how could there be such a contradiction? They themselves claim they knocked and the door was locked, yet three minutes later, when they came with the janitor, it turned out the door was unlocked? — The trick is that the murderer was definitely there, locked himself in, and would have been caught there if not for Koch's carelessness in going himself to fetch the janitor. He managed to descend the stairs during that interval and slipped past them somehow. Koch crosses himself with both hands: "If I had stayed there," he says, "he would have jumped out and killed me with an axe." A Russian prayer service wants to be held, ha-ha! — Nobody saw the murderer? — Where could you see him? The house is like Noah's Ark, — noted the clerk, who had been listening from his place.
