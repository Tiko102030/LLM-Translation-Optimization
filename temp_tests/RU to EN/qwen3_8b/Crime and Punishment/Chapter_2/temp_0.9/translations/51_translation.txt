A small hollow formed under the stone; immediately he began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet landed at the very top, and yet there was still room left in the hollow. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over in one motion to its original side, and it just fell back into its original place, barely, slightly seeming higher. But he scooped up some soil and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely endurable joy, like the other day in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It's been lying here since the house was built and will lie there for just as long."
