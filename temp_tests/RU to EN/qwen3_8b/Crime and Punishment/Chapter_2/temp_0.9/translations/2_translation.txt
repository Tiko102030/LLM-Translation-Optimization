The light was sufficient, and he quickly began to examine himself thoroughly, from head to toe, his entire dress: were there any traces? But that was impossible: trembling from chill, he started removing everything from himself and inspecting again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only at the spot where the pants had been cut off and hung with tassels, the tassels themselves had left thick traces of congealed blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the tassels. It seemed there was nothing else. Suddenly he remembered that his wallet and the belongings he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets all this time! He had not even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even thought of them now, while examining his dress! What is this?
