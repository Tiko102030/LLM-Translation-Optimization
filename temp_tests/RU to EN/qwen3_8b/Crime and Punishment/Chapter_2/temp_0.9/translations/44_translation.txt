— Where to, may I ask? — Along the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, pale as a cloth, without lowering his black inflamed eyes before Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He barely stands on his feet, and you... — Ilya Petrovich remarked. — Nothing! — he said in a particularly manner. Nekodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also intently staring at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
