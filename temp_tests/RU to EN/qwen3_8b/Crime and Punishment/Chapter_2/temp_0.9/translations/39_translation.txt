The scribe began dictating to him the standard form of an ordinary withdrawal, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then—whensoever—that I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on. —You cannot write at all, your pen slips from your hand, —noted the scribe, looking curiously at Raskolnikov. —Are you ill? —Yes... my head is spinning... go on! —Well, everything; sign. The scribe took the paper and turned to others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and gripped his head with his hands. It felt as though a nail was being driven into his skull. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to stand up now, go to Nikolai Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, every last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and point out the items, in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat, ready to carry it out.
