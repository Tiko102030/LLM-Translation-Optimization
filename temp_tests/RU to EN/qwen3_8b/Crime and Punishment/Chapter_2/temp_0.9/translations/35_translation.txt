— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It's well known, gunpowder can't bear insult. You've probably taken offense against him and couldn't help but react—said Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble person, I'll tell you, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flew into a rage, boiled over, burned out—and that's it! Everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! They even nicknamed him in the regiment: "sergeant-major gunpowder"...

— What a regiment indeed! — exclaimed Ilya Petrovich, very pleased that they had tickled him so nicely, though still buzzing. Suddenly, Raskolnikov felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Well, forgive me, captain, — he began quite casually, suddenly turning to Nikodim Fomich — consider my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've been at fault on my part.
