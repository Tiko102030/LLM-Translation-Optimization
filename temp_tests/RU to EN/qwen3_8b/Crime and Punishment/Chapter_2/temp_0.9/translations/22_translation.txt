— Miss Luiza Ivanovna, please sit down, — said he to the woman, dressed in a fleeting crimson-red outfit, who stood still as if unwilling to sit herself, though a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said quietly, with a silken sound, and sat down. Her light-blue dress with white lace trimming, like an air balloon, spread around the chair and took up nearly half the room. It carried the scent of perfume. But the lady, obviously, hesitated because she occupied almost half the room and because of the scent emanating from her, although she smiled timidly and impudently at the same time, but with evident unease. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very manly and somehow particularly turning her shoulders with each step, a young officer entered, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat down in the chair. The voluminous lady jumped up from her place the moment she saw him, and with some special enthusiasm began to bow; but the officer paid her no slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence.
