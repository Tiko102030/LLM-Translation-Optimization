His face showed surprise. — What are you doing? — he shouted, inspecting his incoming companion from head to toe; then he fell silent and whistled. — Is it really that bad? You, brother, have outdone our brother, — he added, looking at Raskolnikov's rags. — Sit down, you must be tired! — and when he collapsed onto the Turkish crepe fabric divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. — You are seriously ill, you know that? — He started feeling his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. — No need, — he said, — I came… that is: I have no lessons… I had wanted… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — Do you know what?
