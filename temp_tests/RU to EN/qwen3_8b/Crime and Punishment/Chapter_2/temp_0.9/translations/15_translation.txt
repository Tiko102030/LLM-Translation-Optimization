"Put on the sock!" — it occurred to him suddenly, "it will get even more dusty, and the traces will disappear." But just as he had put it on, he immediately pulled it off with disgust and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other option, he took it again and put it on — and laughed again. "All of this is conditional, all is relative, all of this are just forms," — he thought briefly, with just a flicker of thought, trembling all over, "indeed, he did put it on! Indeed, he ended up with putting it on!" Laughter, however, immediately turned into despair. "No, it's not within his strength..." — he thought. His legs trembled. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head spun and ached from the heat.
