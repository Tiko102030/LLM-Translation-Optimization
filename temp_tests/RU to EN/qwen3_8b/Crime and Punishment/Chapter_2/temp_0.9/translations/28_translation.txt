— Well, isn't she my mistress? — Well, so what if she is your mistress? The clerk looked at him with a compassionate, pitying smile, mixed with a certain triumph, as if he were a novice just beginning to be bombarded: "So, what do you feel now, huh?" But what did he care now about borrowed writing, about claims! Was it worth even the slightest worry, let alone any attention! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions himself, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the pressing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without forethought, without analysis, without future riddles and guesses, without doubts and without questions. It was the moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But at that very moment, in the office, something like thunder and lightning occurred. The ensign, still shaken by the disrespect, burning with passion and obviously wishing to support the suffering ambition, launched himself at the unfortunate "plump lady," who had been looking at him since he entered with the most idiotic smile. — You, such and such, and so and so, — he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the widow had already left), — what happened to you last night?
