He became extremely absent-minded and forgetful, and he knew this. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along the B-Street; but on the way, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Is it not better to go somewhere very far, again perhaps to the Islands, and there somewhere, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—to bury all this and the tree, perhaps to notice?" And although he felt that he was not able to discuss everything clearly and soundly at this moment, the thought seemed to him infallible. But even reaching the Islands was not in his fate, and another thing happened: as he was exiting from the B-Street to the square, he suddenly saw to the left an entrance to the courtyard, surrounded by completely enclosed walls. On the right, immediately after entering the gates, a solid, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story house stretched far into the courtyard. On the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also immediately from the gates, a wooden fence ran twenty paces into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a completely enclosed, soundproof area where certain materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, soot-stained, stone shed jutted out from the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
