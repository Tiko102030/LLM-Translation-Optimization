—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep wonder and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really happening..."

Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would come soon. With the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire set of tableware: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other items that had not been used in such order for a long time. The tablecloth was clean. —Nastasya, you'd better have Praskovya Pavlovna send two beer bottles. We'll have a drink. —Well, you drunkard!— muttered Nastasya and went to carry out the order.
