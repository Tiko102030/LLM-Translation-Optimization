He did not answer and held the paper in his hands, without unfastening it. — Don't get up, — Nastasya continued, softening and seeing that he was taking his legs off the couch. — Ill, so don't go: won't burn. What's in your hands? He looked: in his right hand, there were cut pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, thinking about it, he remembered that, half-awake in the heat, he tightly gripped all of it in his hand and then fell asleep again. — Look at all those rags he's collected and sleeps with them, as if with a treasure… — And Nastasya laughed in her painful, nervous way. He quickly put everything under his coat and stared at it intently. Though he could barely comprehend much at that moment, he felt that people wouldn't treat him that way when they came to take him.
