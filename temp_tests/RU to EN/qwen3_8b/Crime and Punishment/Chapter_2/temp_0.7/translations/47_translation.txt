Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely open. He walked quickly and firmly, and though he felt completely broken, his mind was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, perhaps an instruction to watch him; therefore, no matter what, he had to hide the ends until the time came. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning... Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this even earlier, during the night, in his delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "As soon as possible, as soon as possible, and throw everything away." But throwing it away proved very difficult. He had been walking along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe more, and several times looked at the wharves into the ditch where they had met. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were standing right at the wharves and the laundresses were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and people were everywhere, and from all sides, from the embankments, from all around, one could see, notice: suspiciously, that a person deliberately went down, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink, but floated?
