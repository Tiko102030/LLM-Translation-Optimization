"Money? Which money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but… surely, it's not that!" And he trembled with joy. Suddenly, it became extremely, immeasurably easy for him. Everything slid off his shoulders. — And when are you supposed to come, sir? — shouted the ensign, growing more and more offended, — you're supposed to come at nine, but it's already twelve o'clock! — I was brought a quarter of an hour ago, — answered Raskolnikov loudly, over his shoulder, also suddenly and unexpectedly for himself angered, even finding some pleasure in it. — That's enough, I came here feverish and ill. — Don't shout!
