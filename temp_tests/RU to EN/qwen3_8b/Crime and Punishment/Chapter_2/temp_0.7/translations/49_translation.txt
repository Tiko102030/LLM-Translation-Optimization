He became extremely distracted and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along the B-Street (Prospect); but on the way, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far, perhaps again to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—to bury all of this and the tree, perhaps notice?" Although he felt that he was unable to clearly and soundly discuss everything at this moment, the thought seemed to him infallible. But he was destined not to reach the Islands, and instead, as he was exiting from the B-Street Prospect onto the square, he suddenly saw to the left an entrance to a courtyard surrounded by completely soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a long, unwhitewashed, soundproof wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also immediately from the gates, a wooden fence ran twenty paces into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, walled-off area where some materials lay. Further, in the recess of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
