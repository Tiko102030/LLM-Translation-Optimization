Not at all!...

He suddenly stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately confused him and bitterly surprised him:

"If indeed all this was done deliberately, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into your wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all these torments, and intentionally went along with such a mean, nasty, lowly deed? You wanted to throw it all into the water now, the wallet along with all the things you haven't even seen yet... How is that possible?"

Yes, that's exactly it; that's all true. He had known this before, and it wasn't a new question for him at all; and when the decision was made at night to throw everything into the water, it was made without any hesitation or objection, as though it was only natural, as though there was no other way... Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; almost as if it had been decided yesterday, right at the moment when he was sitting by the chest and pulling out the cases from it... That's exactly what happened! "It's because I'm very ill," he grumpily decided at last, "I've tortured and torn myself apart myself, and I don't even know what I'm doing... I've tormented myself yesterday, three days ago, and all this time... I'll recover and... stop tormenting myself... What if I never recover at all? Lord! I'm sick and tired of all this!..." He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. A single new, insurmountable feeling was gradually taking hold of him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical revulsion towards everything he encountered and the surrounding world, persistent, hostile, hateful. Everything he met was repulsive to him—his faces, their gait, their movements. He just wanted to spit on someone, perhaps even bite someone if they spoke to him...
