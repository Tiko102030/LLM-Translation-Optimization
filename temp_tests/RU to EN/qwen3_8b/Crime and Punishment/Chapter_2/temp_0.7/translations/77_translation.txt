Well, now, who are you? — I am an artelsman in our office, from the merchant Shelopayev, and I came here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — said Razumikhin, seating himself on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to recover your senses, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten or drunk for four days. Honestly, they gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you thoroughly and immediately said that it was all nonsense — as if something had hit you on the head. Some kind of nervous disorder, poor rations, he said, they gave little beer and herring, that's why you were ill, but nothing serious, it will pass and get better.
