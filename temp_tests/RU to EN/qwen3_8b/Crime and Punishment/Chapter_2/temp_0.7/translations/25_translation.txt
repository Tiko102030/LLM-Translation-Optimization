— Do not shout! — I am not shouting, but rather speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant had grown so angry that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some droplets were spilling from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Please be quiet! You are in the presence. Do not be vulgar, my dear! — And you are in the presence, — Raskolnikov shouted, — besides, you are shouting and smoking a cigarette, so you are being rude to all of us. — After saying this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The stenographer looked at them with a smile.
