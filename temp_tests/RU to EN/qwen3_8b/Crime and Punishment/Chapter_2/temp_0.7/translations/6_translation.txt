—"He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath from his entire chest,"— "It's just feverish weakness, a fleeting delusion,"— and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a ray of sunlight illuminated his left boot: on the sock peeking out of the boot, as if signs appeared. He took off the boot: "Indeed, signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood"; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly... "But what am I to do with this now? Where to put this sock, the fringe, the pocket?" He gathered all of this in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. "Into the stove? But in the stove, they'll start digging first. Burn it? And with what? There's not even a match."
