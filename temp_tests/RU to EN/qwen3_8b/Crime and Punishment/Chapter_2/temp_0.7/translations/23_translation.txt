The plump lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some peculiar enthusiasm; but the officer paid her no attention at all, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a lieutenant, assistant to the quartermaster, with reddish mustaches sprouting horizontally in both directions and with exceptionally small facial features, nothing special, except for a certain impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were too ragged, and despite all his lowered posture, his bearing still didn't match his attire. Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and for too long, so that the officer even felt offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged fellow didn't flinch from his lightning glance. "I was summoned… by order…" Raskolnikov answered in a mumbled fashion. "This is about the case of collecting money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Here you are!" and he tossed Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it, "read it! 'Money?'
