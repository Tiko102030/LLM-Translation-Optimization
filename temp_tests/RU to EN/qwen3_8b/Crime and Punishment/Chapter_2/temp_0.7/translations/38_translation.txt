And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled not with his quarter friends, but with his closest friends, then, it seemed, he would still have no human word to say to them, his heart having suddenly become empty. A dark feeling of tormenting, endless solitude and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. Not the baseness of his heart's confessions to Ilyá Petrovich, not the baseness and the poruchik's triumph over him suddenly overturned his heart. Oh, what business does he have now with his own baseness, with all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, complaints, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were condemned to be burned at this very moment, he would not have stirred, and perhaps even not listened attentively to the sentence. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt, with all the force of his feeling, that not only with his sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else, he could no longer address these people in the quarter office. And even if these were all his own brothers and sisters, and not quarter poruchiks, then still he would have had absolutely no need to address them, and in no case in life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible feeling up to this moment. And what was most tormenting of all — it was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; a direct, most tormenting feeling among all the feelings he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form of such a statement, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (when-ever), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.
