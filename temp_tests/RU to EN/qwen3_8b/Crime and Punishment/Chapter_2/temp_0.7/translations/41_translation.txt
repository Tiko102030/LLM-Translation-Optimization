Well, will they start asking about residence if he came with such an intention? And Koch, that man, sat for half an hour at the silverware below before going upstairs to the old woman at exactly seven minutes to eight. Now figure it out...

— But allow me, how did they end up with such a contradiction: they themselves claim they knocked and the door was locked, but three minutes later, when they came with the doorman, it turned out the door was unlocked? — The thing is: the murderer was definitely there, seated and locked from the inside; and they would have caught him there if not for Koch's mistake, not going himself to the doorman. And he exactly during that interval managed to descend the stairs and slip past them somehow. Koch crosses himself with both hands: "If I had stayed there, he would have jumped out and killed me with an axe." Russian prayer wants to serve, hehe... — No one saw the murderer? — Where would you see him? The house is Noah's ark, — noted the clerk, who was listening from his place.
