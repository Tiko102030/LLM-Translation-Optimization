He was lying there, perhaps since the house construction started and would lie there for just as long. And even if they did find out— who would think of me? Everything is over! No evidence!"—and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed with a nervous, small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and kept laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto the Kth Boulevard, where he had met that girl the day before, his laughter suddenly ceased. Other thoughts came into his head. It suddenly occurred to him too that it was terribly unpleasant for him now to pass by that bench on which he had sat and pondered after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard too to meet again that mustachioed boy to whom he had given a kopeck: "By God!" 

He walked, looking around absentmindedly and spitefully. All his thoughts now revolved around some main point— and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and that now, precisely now, he was left alone with this main point— and that this was even the first time after these two months. "By God, it's all over!"
