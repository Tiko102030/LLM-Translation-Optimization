— Where to, may I ask? — Down the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, entirely pale as a handkerchief, without lowering his black, inflamed eyes before Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fomich was about to remark. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said in a particularly emphatic manner. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also intensely staring at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
