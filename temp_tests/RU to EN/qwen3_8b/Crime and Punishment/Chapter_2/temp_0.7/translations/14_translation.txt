Nastasya, so she couldn't notice anything from afar, praise be! Then, trembling, he unsealed the notice and began to read; he read for a long time and finally understood. It was an ordinary summons from the quarter to appear today, at half past ten, at the office of the quarter's overseer. "When has this ever happened? I have no business with the police on my own! And why exactly today?" he thought in agonizing confusion. "Lord, just hurry up!" He was about to kneel down to pray, but even he laughed at himself—not at the prayer, but at himself. He hurriedly began to get dressed. "I'll be ruined either way! Noseck!"
