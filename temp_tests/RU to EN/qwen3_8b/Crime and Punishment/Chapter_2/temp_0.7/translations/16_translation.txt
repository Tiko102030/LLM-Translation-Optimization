His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with a trick and then take me down all at once— he continued to himself as he went down the stairs. It's bad that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he left all his belongings in a wall crevice— "and here, perhaps deliberately, they searched without him"— he remembered and stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say, cynicism of death suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it were sooner..."  

On the street, the heat was again unbearable; just a drop of rain for all these days. Again dust, bricks, and plaster, again the stench from benches and drinking dens, again the drunken, huckster-like peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely— the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out onto a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner of yesterday's street, he looked with agonizing anxiety into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought as he approached the office.
