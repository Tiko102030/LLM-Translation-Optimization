It was some particularly disheveled person with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there, to the clerk," said the clerk, and with a finger he pointed forward, indicating the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), a crowded and packed space with people—more decently dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the table facing the clerk, writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and flushed red with spots, a noticeable woman, dressed extremely flamboyantly, with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed his summons to the clerk. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said "wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He breathed more freely.
