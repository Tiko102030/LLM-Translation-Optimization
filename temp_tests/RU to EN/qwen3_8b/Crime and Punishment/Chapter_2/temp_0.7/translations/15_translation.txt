Put on the sock! — he suddenly thought, — it would only get dirtier, and the traces would disappear. But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with disgust and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other option, he put it on again — and laughed once more. "All this is conditional, all is relative, all of this is just form," — he thought briefly, only with the edge of his mind, while his whole body trembled, — "after all, he did put it on! After all, he finished by putting it on!" However, his laughter was immediately replaced by despair. "No, it's beyond my strength..." — he thought. His legs trembled. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head was spinning and aching from the heat.
