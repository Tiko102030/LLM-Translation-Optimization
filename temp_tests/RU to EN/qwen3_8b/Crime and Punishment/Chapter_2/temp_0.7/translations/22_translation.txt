— Louisa Ivanovna, please sit down, — he said to the crimson-red, elegantly dressed lady, who stood still as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said quietly, with a silken whisper, and sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread around the chair like an air balloon, taking up almost half the room. It carried the scent of fragrances. But the lady, obviously hesitating because she occupied almost half the room and because she smelled of fragrances, although she smiled timidly and boldly at the same time, but with evident unease. The widow finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very swaggering and somehow especially turning her shoulders with each step, entered an officer, tossed his hat with a cockade onto the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began to bow with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she could no longer sit down in his presence.
