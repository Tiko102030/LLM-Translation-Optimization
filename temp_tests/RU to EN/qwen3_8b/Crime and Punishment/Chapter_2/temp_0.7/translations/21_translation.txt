He drew a breath more freely. "Probably not!" Gradually, he began to encourage himself, striving with all his strength to cheer up and recover his senses. "Some foolishness, some utterly trivial carelessness, and I might entirely lose myself! Hmm... it's a pity there's no air here—added he—stifling... My head spins even more... and my mind too..." 

He felt a terrible disorder within himself. He himself feared not being able to control himself. He tried to fix his thoughts on something, to think about something entirely unrelated, but this entirely failed. The clerk, however, greatly interested him: he longed to guess something from his face, to decipher it. He was a very young man, about twenty-two, with a dark and lively face, appearing older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and with a fathom, with a parting on the back of his head, combed and powdered, with numerous rings and bracelets on his white, brush-cleaned fingers, and gold chains on his vest. Even with one former foreigner here, he had managed to say two words in French, and quite satisfactorily. "Madam Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down," he said to the brightly red, somewhat overdressed lady, who stood still as if not daring to sit herself, though a chair was nearby.
