— Poverty is not a vice, my friend, well, what can you do! It's known, powder, couldn't bear insult. You, perhaps, took offense against him and couldn't help it, — continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov — but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble-hearted man, I'll tell you, but powder, powder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! And everything passed! In the end, only a heart of gold! They called him in the regiment: "Ensign Powder"...

— What a wonderful regiment indeed! — exclaimed Ilya Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so amusingly teased, though still somewhat irritable. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Well, please, captain, — he began, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly and rather casually — take a closer look at my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I overstepped on my part.
