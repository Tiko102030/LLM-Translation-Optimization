Just spit on someone, bite them, I guess, if someone talked to him...  
He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the Small Nevsky Embankment on Vasilievsky Island, near the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I actually came to Razumikhin on my own! The same old story again, like back then... It's very interesting, indeed: did I come on my own or just walked here by accident? Anyway; I said three days ago that I would go to him the next day, well, and I will! As if I can't visit now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's apartment on the fifth floor. He was home, in his room, and at that moment he was occupied, writing, and opened the door himself. Four months had passed since they had last seen each other. Razumikhin was sitting in his room in a tattered-to-rags robe, in socks on his feet, with matted hair, unshaven, and unclean. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
