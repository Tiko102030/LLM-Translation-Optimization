— Well, isn't she my mistress? — Well, so what if she is your mistress? The clerk looked at him with a compassionate smile of pity, yet at the same time a touch of triumph, as if he were a new recruit just beginning to be bullied: "So, what do you feel now, my dear?" But what did it matter now to him about borrowed handwriting, about the reprimand! Was it worth even a hint of worry, let alone any attention at all! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions himself, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the crushing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without forethought, without analysis, without future speculations or guesses, without doubts or questions. It was the moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But at that very moment, in the office, something like thunder and lightning occurred. The lieutenant, still shaken by the disrespect, burning with anger and obviously wishing to support the injured ambition, rushed upon the unfortunate "plump lady," who had been looking at him since he entered with the most foolishly smug smile. — So you, such a one and such another, — he suddenly shouted at the top of his voice (the mourning lady had already left), — what happened to you last night?
