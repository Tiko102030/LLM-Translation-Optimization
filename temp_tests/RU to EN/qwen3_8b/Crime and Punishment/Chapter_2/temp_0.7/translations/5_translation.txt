"Pieces of torn linen will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with attention strained to the point of pain, he began once more to scrutinize the surroundings, the floor, everywhere, wondering if he had perhaps forgotten something else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, left him began to torment him intolerably. "What, is it really beginning, is the execution really about to start? There, there, that's exactly it!" Indeed, the remnants of the lace he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at a glance! "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. At that moment, a strange thought came to him: perhaps all his clothes were bloodstained, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his thoughts had weakened, had been shattered… his mind was clouded… Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So, it follows that there must be blood in the pocket as well, since I had shoved the wet wallet into my pocket then!" In an instant, he turned out his pocket, and—there it was—the lining of the pocket had traces, stains! "So, it follows that I have not entirely lost my wits, that there is thought and memory left, since I have just realized it and guessed it myself!"—he thought to himself triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest—"It's merely the weakness of fever, a fleeting delirium!" And he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
