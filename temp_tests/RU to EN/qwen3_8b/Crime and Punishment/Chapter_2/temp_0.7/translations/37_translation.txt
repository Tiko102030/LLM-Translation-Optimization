— All these sensitive details, Your Grace, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich rudely cut in, — you must give your opinion and commitment, and as for all these tragic places and your being in love, that's none of our business. — Well, you're really cruel… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He somehow felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What should I write? — asked Raskolnikov rather rudely. — I'll dictate to you. Raskolnikov felt that the clerk had become more careless and contemptuous toward him since his confession, but strangely enough, he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened in an instant, in one minute. If he had only taken a moment to think, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, even forcing his feelings upon them. And where did these feelings come from?
