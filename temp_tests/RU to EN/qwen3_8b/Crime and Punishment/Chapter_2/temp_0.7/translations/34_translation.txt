Louise Ivanovna began to crouch on all sides with affected politeness, and while crouching, she managed to reach the door; but at the door she collided with a notable officer, with an open, fresh face and with excellent thick, blond mustaches. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the quartermaster. Louise Ivanovna hurried to sit down almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again lightning, tornado, hurricane! — Nikodim Fomich politely and friendlily addressed Ilya Petrovich, — again disturbed the heart, again boiled over! He even heard it from the stairs. — What's that! — Ilya Petrovich said with noble negligence (and not even "what", but rather: "Da-ah sh-ta-ah!") while moving with some papers to another desk and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he stepped, there his shoulder went; — here, you see, please: Mr. the writer, that is, the former student, does not pay money, gave promissory notes, does not clean the apartment, continuous complaints are received from them, and they entered a complaint that I smoked a cigarette in front of them! They themselves, p-p, are lying, but here, please look at them: here they are in their most attractive form now! — Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what can be done!
