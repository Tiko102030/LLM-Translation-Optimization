But no, he hears too clearly!... But then, if that's the case, people will come to him now too, because... surely, all this is from the same... from yesterday's... Lord! He wanted to lock himself away, but his hand would not raise... and it was useless! Fear, like ice, had covered his soul, tormented him, paralyzed him... But at last, after this commotion, which had lasted for sure ten minutes, began gradually to subside. The mistress was groaning and sobbing, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But at last, it seemed, he had finally fallen silent; now he was no longer heard; "did he really leave? Lord!" Yes, the mistress was leaving too, still groaning and weeping... and now her door had slammed shut... And now the crowd is dispersing down the stairs to the apartments, shouting, arguing, calling out to each other, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been quite a number of them; perhaps the whole building had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!" 

Raskolnikov fell helplessly onto the couch, but could no longer close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such suffering, in such an unbearable feeling of infinite terror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a plate of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he was not asleep, she placed the candle on the table and began to arrange the things she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
