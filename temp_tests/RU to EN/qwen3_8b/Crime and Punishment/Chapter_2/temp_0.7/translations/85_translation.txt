Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved to sit on the couch next to him, awkwardly like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, despite the fact that he himself could have raised up, and with his right hand brought a spoon of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoonful, then another, the third. But after bringing several spoons, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that regarding further steps, it was necessary to consult with Zosimov. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want tea? — I want. — Hurry up and make tea, Nastasya, because regarding tea, apparently, one can do without the faculty.
