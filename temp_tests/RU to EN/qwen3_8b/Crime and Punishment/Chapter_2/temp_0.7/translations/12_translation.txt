He did not respond and held the paper in his hands without unfolding it. — "Don't get up," Nastasia continued, softening and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the couch. — "He's ill, so just stay in bed; it won't burn." What was in his hands? He looked: in his right hand were cut pieces of tassels, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, thinking about it, he recalled how, half-awake in the heat, he would tightly clutch all of it in his hand and then fall asleep again. — "What a mess of rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, as if with treasure…" — And Nastasia laughed her painfully nervous laugh. He quickly shoved everything under his overcoat and stared at it intently. Though he could hardly make much sense of it at that moment, he felt that people would not treat him kindly when his brothers came to take him.
