"— Do they hide like that? My mind is leaving me!" He collapsed onto the sofa in exhaustion, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him again. Mechanically, he pulled his old student winter coat, warm but already in rags, from the chair beside him, covered himself with it, and once more sleep and delirium overwhelmed him. He fell into a trance. No more than five minutes later, he suddenly sat up again, and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed back to his attire. "How could I have fallen asleep again, with nothing done! So it is, so it is: I still haven't taken off the tie under my armpit! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the tie off and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow with his underwear. "Pieces of torn flannel will certainly not raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he began once more to scrutinize everything around him, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?"
