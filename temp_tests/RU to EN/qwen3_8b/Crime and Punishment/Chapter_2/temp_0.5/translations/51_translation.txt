A small depression formed under the stone; he immediately started throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet landed at the very top, and there was still room left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with a single twist to its original side, and it just landed in its former place, albeit slightly higher, just a little bit. But he shoveled some earth and pressed it down along the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the time in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It's been lying here since the house was built and will probably lie there for just as long."
