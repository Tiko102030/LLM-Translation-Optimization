—"You, such and such a person," he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the widow had already left), "what happened last night, huh? Another disgrace, another rowdy scene on the street. Another fight and drunkenness. You're dreaming of peace! I've already told you, I've warned you ten times that I won't let you go on the eleventh! And you're doing it again, again, you such and such a person! Even the paper fell from Raskolnikov's hands, and he stared wildly at the plump lady who was so impudently teasing her. But soon, however, he realized what was going on, and immediately this whole story began to please him very much. He listened with enjoyment, so much so that he wanted to laugh, laugh, laugh... All his nerves were jumping.—Ilya Petrovich!"
