But at that moment the door opened wide again, and, leaning slightly because he was tall, Razumikhin entered. "What a sea cabin!" he shouted as he came in, "I always bang my forehead against it; it's also called an apartment! And you, brother, have you woken up? I just heard from Pashenka." "I've just woken up," said Nastasya. "We've just woken up," the foreman nodded again with a smile. "And who, may I ask, are you yourself?" Razumikhin suddenly asked him. "Well, you see, I am Vrazumikhin; not Razumikhin, as everyone calls me, but Vrazumikhin, a student, son of a nobleman, and he is my friend. Well, and who are you?"
