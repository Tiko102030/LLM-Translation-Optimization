"— Is that how they hide? My mind is truly leaving me!" He collapsed in exhaustion onto the couch, and immediately an unbearable shiver shook him. Mechanically, he pulled the lying nearby, on the chair, his former student winter coat, warm but already almost in rags, covered himself with it, and sleep and delirium once again overwhelmed him. He fell into oblivion. No more than five minutes had passed when he jumped up again and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed again to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, when nothing has been done! That's right, that's right: I still haven't removed the loop under my armpit! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the loop and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the bedding. "Pieces of torn linen will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful intensity, he began to scan around, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?
