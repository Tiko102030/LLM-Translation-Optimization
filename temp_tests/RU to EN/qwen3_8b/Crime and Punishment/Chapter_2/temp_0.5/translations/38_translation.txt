And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled not with his quarter's friends, but with his closest friends, then, it seems, he would still have no human word to address them, his heart having suddenly become empty. A dark sense of tormenting, endless solitude and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. Not the lowliness of his heartfelt confessions to Ilyich Petrovich, not the lowliness and the lieutenant's triumph over him suddenly turned his heart upside down. Oh, what does it matter to him now about his own baseness, about all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, grievances, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were sentenced to be burned at this very moment, he would still not move, and perhaps would not even listen attentively to the verdict. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. He did not understand it, but he clearly felt it, with all the force of his feeling, that not only with his sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything at all, he could no longer address these people in the quarter's office. And even if these were all his own brothers and sisters, and not the quarter's lieutenants, then he would still have no need to address them at all, and in no case in life; he had never before experienced such a strange and terrible feeling. And what was most tormenting of all — it was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; a direct, most tormenting feeling among all the feelings he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form of a statement in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (some day), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.
