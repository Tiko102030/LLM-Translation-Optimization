— Well, isn't she my mistress? — Well, so what if she's the mistress? The clerk looked at him with a compassionate smile tinged with some triumph, as if looking at a new recruit just beginning to be fired upon: "What, you feel now?" But what did it matter now to him about borrowed writing, about punishment! Was it worth even the slightest worry, let alone any attention at all! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions himself, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the pressing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without anticipation, without analysis, without future uncertainties and speculations, without doubts and without questions. It was a moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But at that very moment, something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by the disrespect, burning with anger and obviously wishing to support the wounded ambition, launched himself at the unfortunate "puffy lady," who had been looking at him with the most foolish smile since he had entered. — What happened to you last night, you such-and-such and so-and-so, — he shouted at the top of his voice (the widow had already left), — what happened during the past night?
