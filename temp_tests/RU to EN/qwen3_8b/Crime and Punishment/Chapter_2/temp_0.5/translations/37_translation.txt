— All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich rudely cut in, — you must give your statement and commitment, and as for all these tragic places and your being in love, it's entirely irrelevant to us. — Well, you're being cruel… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He suddenly felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What to write? — asked Raskolnikov rather rudely. — I'll dictate it to you. Raskolnikov thought that the clerk had become more careless and disrespectful toward him after his confession, but, strangely enough, he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened in an instant, in a single moment. If he had only thought a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them like that a minute ago, and even impose his feelings upon them? And where did these feelings come from?
