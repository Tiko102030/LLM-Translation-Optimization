Just blow someone off, take a bite out of someone, I guess, if someone talked to him...  
He stopped suddenly as he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I've come to Razumikhin by myself! It's the same story again, like before... And it's really, I must say, interesting: did I come on my own or just walked and happened to end up here? Anyway; I said... three days ago... that I would go to him the next day, well, I'll go! As if I couldn't visit him now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth-floor room. Razumikhin was at home, in his room, and at that moment was occupied, writing, and opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other in four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his tattered-to-rags robe, barefoot, tousled, unshaven, and unclean. Surprise was expressed on his face.
