So he lay like that for a very long time. Sometimes it seemed as if he were waking up, and during these moments he noticed that it was already night, yet the thought of getting up never occurred to him. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the couch on his back, still dazed from recent unconsciousness. Sudden, frightful, desperate cries from the street reached him, which, however, he had been listening to every night under his window at three o'clock. It was these that woke him up now. "Ah! Now the drunkards from the taverns are coming out," he thought, "three o'clock—it's three o'clock!—and suddenly he jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. 'What! It's already three o'clock!' He sat on the couch—and then he remembered everything!"
