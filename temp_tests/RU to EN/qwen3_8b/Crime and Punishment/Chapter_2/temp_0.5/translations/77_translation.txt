Well, now, who are you? — I'm a partner in Shelopayev's shop, and I came here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — said Razumikhin, sitting himself on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to come to your senses. — He continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — It's the fourth day you've barely eaten or drunk. Honestly, they gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zos, the doctor? He examined you carefully and immediately said everything was trivial — as if something had struck you in the head. Some nervous nonsense, he said, the ration was bad, they gave little beer and garlic, that's why you got sick, but nothing serious, it will pass and be over.
