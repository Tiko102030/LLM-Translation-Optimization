— No need, — he said, — I came… here's the thing: I have no lessons… I wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — "Do you know what? You're going mad!" remarked Razumikhin, who had been watching him closely. — No, I'm not raving… — Raskolnikov stood up from the sofa. As he rose to face Razumikhin, he didn't realize that he was supposed to confront him face to face. Now, in an instant, he realized, through experience, that he was least inclined, at this very moment, to confront anyone face to face in the whole world. All his bitterness rose within him. He was almost suffocating from his own wrath upon stepping over Razumikhin's threshold. — Goodbye! — he suddenly said and went to the door.
