The scribe began dictating to him the standard form of a withdrawal, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on. — "You cannot write at all, your quill slips from your hand," remarked the scribe, looking curiously at Raskolnikov. "Are you ill?" — "Yes... my head is spinning... go on!" — "Yes, everything; sign. The scribe took the paper and began working with the others. Raskolnikov handed over the quill, but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It felt as if a nail was being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to stand up now, go to Nikodim Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, everything down to the last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and show them the things, in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat to carry it out.
