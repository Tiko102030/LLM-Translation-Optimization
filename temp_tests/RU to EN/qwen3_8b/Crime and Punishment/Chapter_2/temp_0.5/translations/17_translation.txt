"If they ask, maybe I will say," he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away. She had just moved to a new apartment, a new building, to the fourth floor. He had once briefly been at her old apartment, but a long time ago. Entering the gates, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a man was descending with a book in his hand: "A janitor, so; so the office must be here," and he began climbing up randomly. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. "I'll go in, kneel down, and tell everything…" he thought, entering the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and full of filth. All the kitchens of all the apartments in all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood there almost all day. That's why there was terrible stuffiness.
