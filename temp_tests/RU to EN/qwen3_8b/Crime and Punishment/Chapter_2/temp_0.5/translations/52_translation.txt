He was there, perhaps lying on the construction site, and would lie there for just as long. And even if they did find him—who would think of me? Everything is finished! No evidence!"—and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he laughed with a nervous, small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and kept laughing all the time, as he passed through the square. But when he stepped onto the K-th boulevard, where he had met that girl the day before, his laughter suddenly ceased. Other thoughts came into his head. Suddenly it seemed to him that it was terribly repulsive to pass by that bench where he had sat after the girl left, pondering, and it would also be terribly hard to meet again that bearded man to whom he had given two kopecks: "Damn it!"

He walked, looking around listlessly and spitefully. All his thoughts now revolved around some main point, and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point, and that now, precisely now, he was left alone with this main point— and that this was even the first time after those two months. "Damn it all!"
