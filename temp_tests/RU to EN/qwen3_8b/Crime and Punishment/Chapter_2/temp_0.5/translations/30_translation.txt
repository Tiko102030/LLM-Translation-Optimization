—Ilya Petrovich!— the clerk began, with care, but paused to wait, since the boiling corporal couldn't be restrained except by the hands, something he knew from his own experience. As for the plump lady, at first she trembled from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more amiable her demeanor grew, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the furious corporal. She shuffled in place and kept bowing continuously, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak, and finally did. —There's no noise or fighting here, sir captain— she chattered suddenly, as if spilling peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently in Russian— and no, no scandal at all, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, sir captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble house, sir captain, and noble manners, sir captain, and I've always, always wanted no scandal. They came completely drunk and then asked for three more glasses, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, and that's very bad in a noble house, and he broke the piano, and there's absolutely no manner here, and I said so. He took a glass and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the stable boy and Karl came, he took Karl and hit him in the eye, and Henrietta also got hit in the eye, and I got my cheek slapped five times. And that's very indecent in a noble house, sir captain, and I shouted. He opened the window and started howling like a little pig from the window; and that's a disgrace.
