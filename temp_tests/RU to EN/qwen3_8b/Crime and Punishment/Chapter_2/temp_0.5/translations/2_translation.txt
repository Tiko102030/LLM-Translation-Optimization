The light was enough, and he began to examine himself thoroughly, from head to toe, his entire outfit: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering from the cold, he started taking off everything and examining the surroundings again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the spot where the pants had frayed at the bottom and hung like fringes, there were thick traces of dried blood on the fringe. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. There was nothing else, it seemed. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even remembered them now, as he examined the dress! What's this?
