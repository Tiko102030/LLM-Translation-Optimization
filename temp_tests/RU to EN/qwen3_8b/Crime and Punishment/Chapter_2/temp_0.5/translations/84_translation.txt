—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep amazement and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really..."  

Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would come soon. To the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire set: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other things, which had not been in such order for a long time. The tablecloth was clean. — Not bad, Nastasya, you should have Praskovya Pavlovna send two beer bottles. We'll drink some. — Well, you, you tall fellow! — Nastasya muttered and went to carry out the command.
