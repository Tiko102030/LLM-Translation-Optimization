He did not answer and held the paper in his hands without unfolding it. — Well, don't get up, — Nastasia continued, feeling compassion and seeing that he was putting his legs down from the sofa. — He's ill, so don't get up: it won't catch fire. What's in your hands? He looked: in his right hand were cut pieces of tassels, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. So he slept with them. Later, when thinking about it, he remembered that, half-awake in the heat, he tightly clutched all of this in his hand and then fell asleep again. — Look at all those rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, just like with a treasure… — And Nastasia laughed with her painfully nervous laugh. He quickly tucked everything under his overcoat and stared at it intently. Though he could hardly think clearly at that moment, he felt that they wouldn't treat him the same way when they came to take him.
