"Pieces of torn flannel will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painfully tense attention once again scrutinized everything around him, the floor, everywhere, making sure he hadn't forgotten anything? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, was leaving him began to torment him intolerably. "What, is it really beginning, is the punishment about to start? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the frayed edges of the fringe he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at once! "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. Then a strange thought came to him: perhaps his entire outfit was bloodstained, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his reasoning had weakened, fragmented… his mind was clouded… Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So there must also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" He quickly turned his pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains, on the lining! "Therefore, I haven't completely lost my senses, there is still reasoning and memory, since I have just realized and guessed it myself!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest, "it's just a feverish weakness, a momentary delirium," and he pulled out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
