The plump lady sprang up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit down in his presence. He was a lieutenant, assistant of the quarter master, with horizontally sticking out reddish mustaches on both sides and with extremely small facial features, nothing, in fact, special except for some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were too shabby, and despite all his lowering himself, his posture was still not fitting his clothes; Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and long, so that he even got offended. — What do you want? — he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged person didn't even think of shrinking away from his lightning glance. — Required… by order… — answered Raskolnikov somewhat awkwardly. — This is about the case of collecting money from them, from the student, — hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. — Here you are! — and he threw Raskolnikov a notebook, indicating the place in it, — read! "Money?"
