"Money? Which money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but… surely, it's not that!" And he shivered with joy. Suddenly, it became incredibly easy for him. Everything fell off his shoulders. — "And when are you supposed to come, sir?" — shouted the ensign, growing increasingly offended, — "You're written to come at nine, but now it's already twelve!" — "They brought me just a quarter of an hour ago," — answered Raskolnikov loudly from behind, also suddenly and unexpectedly to himself enraged, even finding some pleasure in it. — "It's enough that I came here feverish and ill." — "Do not shout!"
