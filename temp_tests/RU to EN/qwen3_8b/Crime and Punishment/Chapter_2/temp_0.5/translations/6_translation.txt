—he thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath—“it's just feverish weakness, a momentary delirium,” and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a sunbeam lit his left boot: on the sock, which peeked out of the boot, seemed to appear signs. He took off the boot: “they are indeed signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood”; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly... “But what now? What to do with this sock, the lining, the pocket?” He gathered all of it in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. “Into the stove? But first they will start digging in the stove. To burn? And with what? There are not even matches.”
