— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — he said to the brilliantly dressed garnet-red lady, who stood motionless as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was right there. — Ich danke, — she said quietly, with a silken whisper, and sat down. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like a balloon, spread around the chair and took up almost half the room, carrying a fragrance. But the lady, obviously shy about occupying almost half the room and the scent emanating from her, though she smiled timidly and boldly, yet clearly with evident anxiety. The widow finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very manfully and in a particularly exaggerated way, turning her shoulders with each step, a young officer entered, threw his cockade hat on the table, and sat down in the chair. The plump lady jumped at the sight of him and began bowing with a special kind of enthusiasm; but the officer paid her absolutely no attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence.
