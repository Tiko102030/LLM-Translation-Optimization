Louise Ivanovna, with somewhat affected politeness, began to sit down on all sides, and while sitting down, she managed to reach the doors; but she bumped into an officer with her backside, a man with an open, fresh face and excellent thick light-brown mustaches. It was none other than Nikodim Fomich, the quarter inspector. Louise Ivanovna hurried to sit down almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. "Again thunder, again lightning and storm, tornado, hurricane!" Nikodim Fomich politely and friendlily addressed Ilya Petrovich, "again disturbed the heart, again boiled over! Even heard it from the stairs." "What's that?" Ilya Petrovich said with noble indifference (and even not "what's that," but rather: "Da-aa shcha-aa!") while moving from one table to another with some papers, dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he stepped, there his shoulder went. "Here you are, you may see: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, does not pay money, gave promissory notes, does not clean the apartment, continuous complaints come in about them, and they even entered a complaint that I smoked a cigarette in front of them! They themselves are being unscrupulous, but here you are, you may look at them: here they are in their most attractive appearance!" "Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what can be done!"
