He became extraordinarily scattered and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to act decisively! He went towards the Neva River via the B-avenue; but on the way, a sudden thought came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Is it not better to go somewhere very far, perhaps again to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush, bury all of this and the tree, perhaps even notice it?" Although he felt he was unable to discuss everything clearly and soundly at that moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, and instead, as he exited the B-avenue onto the square, he suddenly saw a gate to the courtyard, surrounded by completely soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gate, a soundproof, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also immediately from the gate, a wooden fence ran twenty steps into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
