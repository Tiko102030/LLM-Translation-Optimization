Not at all!...

Suddenly, he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately threw him off and bitterly surprised him:

"If indeed all this was done intentionally, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into your wallet up to now and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all these torments, and deliberately went on this low, nasty, despicable business? You wanted to throw it into the water now, the wallet together with all the things you hadn't even seen yet... How is that possible?"

Yes, that's exactly it; everything is just like that. He, in fact, knew this before, and it was not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided at night to throw it into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if it was only natural, as if there was no other way... Yes, he knew everything and remembered everything; almost certainly, this was decided yesterday, right at the moment when he was sitting by the chest and taking out the cases from it... And that's exactly what happened!... 'It's because I'm very ill,' he grumpily decided at last, 'I've tortured and torn myself apart myself, and I don't even know what I'm doing... And yesterday, and three days ago, and all this time I've been tormenting myself... I'll recover and... stop tormenting myself... What if I never recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of all this!...' He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking over him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical revulsion toward everything he encountered and the surrounding environment, persistent, spiteful, hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive—repulsive were their faces, their gait, their movements. He simply wanted to spit on someone, maybe bite someone if they spoke to him...
