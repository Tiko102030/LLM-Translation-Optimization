His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued thinking to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the staircase, he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the wall cavity—"and here, perhaps deliberately, they'll search without me," he thought, and stopped. But such despair and, if one could say so, cynicism regarding death suddenly took hold of him, and he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's quick...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from benches and bars, again drunk peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun gleamed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely—this was the usual feeling of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with agonizing anxiety at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
