— Poverty is not a sin, friend, well, what else! It's known, Porokh, could not bear an insult. You, surely, have offended him somehow and couldn't help it—continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: the most noble, I'll tell you, person, but Porokh, Porokh! He got angry, boiled over, burned out—and that's it! And everything passed! And in the end, only the gold of the heart! And they called him in the regiment: "Ensign-Porokh"…

— What regiment could be like that! — exclaimed Ilyas Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely tickled, but still somewhat flustered. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to them all. — Please, captain, — he began very casually, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fomich, — take into account my situation… I am even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I have, on my part, erred.
