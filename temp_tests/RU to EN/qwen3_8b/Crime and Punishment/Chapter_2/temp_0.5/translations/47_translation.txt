Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt entirely broken, yet his mind was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, perhaps within a quarter of an hour, someone would come out, probably with instructions to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to get rid of the ends before the time came. He had to manage it while there was still some strength left and some capacity for reasoning… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Dump everything into the ditch, toss the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this during the night, in a delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "Hurry, hurry, and throw everything away." But throwing it away proved very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, perhaps even longer, and several times looked at the wharf where the ditch was, where they had met him. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were right at the wharf and the laundresses were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were crowding, and from all sides, from every direction, one could see, notice: it would look suspicious that a person deliberately stepped off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated?
