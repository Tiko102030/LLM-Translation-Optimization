But no, he hears it all too clearly!... But then, if that's the case, they will come to him now too, "because... surely, all of this is from the same... from yesterday's... Lord have mercy!" He had wanted to lock himself in, but his hand would not rise... and it was useless anyway! Fear, like ice, encased his soul, tormented him, froze him... But at last, this entire commotion, which had lasted for sure ten minutes, began to gradually subside. The mistress was groaning and sobbing, Ilyia Petrovich was still threatening and shouting... But at last, it seemed, he too fell silent; now there was no sound of him; "could he have gone? Lord have mercy!" Yes, the mistress is leaving now, still groaning and weeping... and now her door has closed... And now the crowd is dispersing down the stairs to their apartments, shouting, arguing, calling out to each other, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been quite a number of them; perhaps the whole building had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!" 

Raskolnikov fell helplessly onto the couch, but he could no longer close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such suffering, in such an unbearable feeling of boundless terror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a tray of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he was awake, she placed the candle on the table and began to arrange the items: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
