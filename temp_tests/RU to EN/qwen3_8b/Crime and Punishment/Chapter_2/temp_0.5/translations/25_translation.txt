— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but rather speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant had become so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some sort of splashes were flying from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Please be quiet! You are in the presence of the court. Don't behave like a boor, sir! — And you are also in the presence of the court, — Raskolnikov shouted, — besides, you are not only shouting but also smoking a cigarette, so you are showing off to all of us. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The scribe looked at them with a smile.
