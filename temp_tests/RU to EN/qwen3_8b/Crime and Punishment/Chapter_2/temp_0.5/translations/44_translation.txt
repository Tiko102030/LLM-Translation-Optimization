— Where to, may I ask? — On the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, entirely pale as a handkerchief, without lowering his black, inflamed eyes before Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, but you… — remarked Nikodim Fomich. — Nothing at all! — said Ilya Petrovich in a particularly emphatic manner. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, looking at the scribe, who was also very intently looking at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
