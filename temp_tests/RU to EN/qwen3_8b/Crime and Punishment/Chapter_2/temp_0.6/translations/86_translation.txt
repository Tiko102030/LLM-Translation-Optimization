But here comes the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and meat closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. — I, brother Rodia, have lunch like this every day here, — he muttered, as much as his full mouth of meat allowed, — and that's all Pashenka, your housekeeper, is taking care of me, entertaining me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. And here comes Nastasya with the tea. What a quick one! Nastenka, do you want some beer? — And, by the devil! — A cup of tea?
