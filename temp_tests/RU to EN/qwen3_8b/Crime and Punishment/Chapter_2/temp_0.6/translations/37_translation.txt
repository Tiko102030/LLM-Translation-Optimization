—"All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us," brazenly cut in Ilya Petrovich, "you must give a statement and an undertaking, and as for all these tragic places where you happened to be in love, it has nothing to do with us." — "Well, you… cruelly…" muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. Somehow he felt ashamed. — "Write down," said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — "What to write?" — asked the latter rather rudely. — "I'll dictate to you," said the clerk. Raskolnikov felt that the clerk had become more careless and disrespectful toward him since his confession, but strangely enough, he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened in an instant, in one minute. If he had only thought a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even impose his feelings upon them?
