— Poverty is not a sin, friend, well, what can you do! It's known, gunpowder can't bear insult. You've probably taken offense against him and couldn't help it, — continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov — but you're wasting your time: he's the nicest, I'll tell you, person, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He got angry, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! And everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! They even called him in the regiment: "Captain-Gunpowder"...

— What regiment could be any other! — exclaimed Ilya Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely teased, but still a bit tipsy. Suddenly Raskolnikov felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Well, pardon me, captain, — he began rather carelessly, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fomich — take a closer look at my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
