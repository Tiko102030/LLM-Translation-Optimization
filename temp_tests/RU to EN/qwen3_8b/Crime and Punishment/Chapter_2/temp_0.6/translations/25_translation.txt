"Don't shout!" — "I am not shouting, but rather speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some spittle flew out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — 'Do be quiet! You are in the presence. Don't be rude, sir!' — 'And you are in the presence,' cried Raskolnikov, 'and besides shouting, you are smoking a cigarette, so you are being rude to all of us.' — After saying this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile."
