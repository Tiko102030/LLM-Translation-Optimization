He found himself breathing more freely. "Probably not that!" Gradually, he began to cheer himself up, urging himself with all his strength to regain composure and snap out of it. "A certain foolishness, a certain minor carelessness, and I might give everything away!" Hmm… it's a pity there's no air here—stuffy air… My head spins even more… and my mind too…" 

He felt a terrible chaos within himself. He himself feared he couldn't control himself. He tried to cling to something, to think about something entirely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The secretary, however, greatly interested him; he wanted to guess something from his face, to decipher him. He was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and lively face, appearing older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and with a fato, with a parting on the back, combed and powdered, with many rings on white, cleaned fingers and gold chains on his vest. With one of the former foreigners here, he even said two words in French, and very satisfactorily. "— Louise Ivanovna, you would sit," he said to the briefly dressed crimson lady, who stood all the time as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby.
