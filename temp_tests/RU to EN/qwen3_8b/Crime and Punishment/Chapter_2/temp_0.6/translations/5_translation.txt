"Pieces of torn cloth will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painfully intense attention began scanning around, on the floor and everywhere, wondering if he had forgotten something else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought had left him began to torment him intolerably. "What, is it really starting now, is the punishment about to begin? There, there, that's exactly it!" Indeed, the remnants of the fringe he had cut from his pants lay on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen by the first person who entered! "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. A strange thought came to him: perhaps his entire outfit was bloodstained, perhaps there were many stains, but he simply didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his thoughts had weakened, fragmented... his mind was clouded... Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Baa! So, that means there must also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into the pocket then!" He immediately turned the pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains, on the pocket lining! "So, I haven't completely lost my senses, so there is still thought and memory, since I realized it myself and guessed it!—he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest—just a feverish weakness, a temporary delirium,"—and he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
