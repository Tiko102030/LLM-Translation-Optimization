Louise Ivanovna began to sit down with a solicitous politeness in all directions, and while sitting down, she managed to reach the doors; but she ran into a prominent officer from behind, who had an open, fresh face and superb thick golden-brown mustaches. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the quarterly overseer. Louise Ivanovna hurried to sit down almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, hopping, flew out of the office. "Again thunder, again lightning and thunder, tornado, hurricane!" Nikodim Fomich politely and warmly addressed Ilya Petrovich, "again disturbed the heart, again boiled over! I even heard it from the stairs." "What's that?" Ilya Petrovich said with noble negligence (and even not "what," but rather: "Da-a shcha-a!") while moving with some papers to another table and dramatically jerking his shoulders with each step, wherever he stepped, there his shoulder went; "Here you are, my dear sir, behold: the gentleman, that is, the student, who used to be, does not pay money, issued promissory notes, does not clean the apartment, and continuous complaints are received about them, and they entered a claim that I lit a cigarette in their presence! They themselves are the ones who are being unbecoming, but here you are, my dear sir, please look at them: here they are in their most attractive present form!" "Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what can you do!"
