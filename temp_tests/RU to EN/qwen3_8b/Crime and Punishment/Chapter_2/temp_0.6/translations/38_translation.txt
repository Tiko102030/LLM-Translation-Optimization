And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled not with quarterly friends but with his closest friends, then, it seems, he would still not have a single human word for them, his heart having suddenly emptied. A dark feeling of tormenting, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. Not the baseness of his heart's confessions to Ilyá Petrovich, not the baseness and the ensign's triumph over him suddenly overturned his heart. Oh, what business does he have now with his own baseness, with all these ambitions, ensigns, Germans, grievances, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were condemned to be burned at that very moment, he would not have stirred, and perhaps would not have even listened attentively to the verdict. Something completely unfamiliar to him, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt, with all the force of his feeling, that not only with his sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything else, he could no longer address these people in the quarterly office. And even if they were all his own brothers and sisters, and not quarterly ensigns, then he would still have had absolutely no need to address them, and in no case in life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrible feeling up to this very moment. And what was most tormenting of all—this was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; a direct, most tormenting feeling among all the feelings he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form of a statement in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property and so on.
