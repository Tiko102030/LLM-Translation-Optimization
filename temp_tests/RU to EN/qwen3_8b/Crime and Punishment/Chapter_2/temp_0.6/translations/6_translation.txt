He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath with his entire chest, "It's just a feverish weakness, a delirium for a moment," and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a ray of sunlight illuminated his left boot: on the sock, which peeked out of the boot, as if signs appeared. He kicked off the boot: "Indeed, signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood"; he must have carelessly stepped into that puddle... "But what to do now with this? Where to put this sock, the bakhrom, the pocket?" He gathered all this in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. "Into the stove? But in the stove, they will first start digging. Burn it? And with what to burn? There are even no matches."
