The scribe began dictating to him the standard form of an ordinary withdrawal, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then—when ever—never to leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.—You cannot write at all, your pen slips from your hand," remarked the scribe, curiously observing Raskolnikov.—Are you ill?—Yes... my head is spinning... go on!—Well, everything; sign. The scribe took the paper and began dealing with the others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It felt as if a nail were being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to stand up now, go to Nikodim Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, everything down to the last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and point out the things in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat, ready to carry it out.
