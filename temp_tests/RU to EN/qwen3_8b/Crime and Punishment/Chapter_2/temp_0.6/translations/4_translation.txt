"—Is that how they hide it? My mind is really leaving me!" He collapsed into the armchair, and immediately an unbearable shiver shook him again. Mechanically, he pulled his student's winter coat, warm but already in tatters, from the chair beside him, covered himself with it, and once more sleep and delirium seized him. He fell asleep. No more than five minutes later he jumped up again, and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed back to his dress. "How could I fall asleep again, when nothing has been done! Exactly so, exactly so: I still haven't taken off the collar under my armpit! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the collar off and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the bedclothes. "Pieces of torn linen will certainly not raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!"—he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he began once more to scan around, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?"
