And lo and behold, to the greatest surprise, he suddenly recognized the voice of his mistress. She was howling, screaming, and wailing, rushing, hurrying, spilling out words so fast that it was impossible to make sense of what she was saying, pleading—obviously, to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the attacker had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was barely more than a gasp, yet the attacker himself was also speaking, incoherently, in a hurry, gasping and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized this voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich. Ilyi Petrovich was here, beating the mistress! He was beating her with his feet, hitting her head against the steps—this is clear, one can hear it by the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is this? Has the world turned upside down? One could hear how crowds were gathering on all the floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, footsteps approaching. "But why, why, and how could this happen!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he was hearing it all too clearly...
