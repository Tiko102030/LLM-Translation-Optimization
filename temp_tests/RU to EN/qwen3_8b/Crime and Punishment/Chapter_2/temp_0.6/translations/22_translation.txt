— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — said he to the crimson-red lady, who stood motionless as if unable to sit herself, though a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said softly, with a silken whisper, and sat down. Her light blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room, carrying the scent of perfume. But the lady, obviously shy, hesitated at taking up almost half the room and at the scent that emanated from her, though she smiled nervously and boldly, yet clearly with visible unease. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very dashing and somehow particularly twisting her shoulders with every step, entered the officer, threw his cockade hat on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up upon seeing him and began sitting down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence.
