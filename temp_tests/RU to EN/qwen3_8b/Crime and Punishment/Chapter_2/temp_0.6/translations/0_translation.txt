So he lay there for a very long time. It happened that he seemed to wake up, and during these moments he noticed that it was already night, and the thought of getting up did not come to his mind. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the couch on his back, still stunned from recent forgetfulness. Suddenly, terrible and desperate cries from the street reached him, which, in fact, he had heard every night under his window at the third hour. It was these that woke him up now. "Ah! Here come the drunkards from the drinking houses," he thought, "the third hour— and suddenly he jumped up, as if someone had pulled him off the couch. — What! It's already the third hour!" He sat on the couch—and then he remembered everything!
