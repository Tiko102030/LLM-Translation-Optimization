He did not answer and held the paper in his hands, not unsealing it. — Don't get up, already, — Nastasya continued, feeling sorry and seeing that he was putting his legs down from the couch. — He's ill, so don't walk; it won't burn. What's in your hands? He looked: in his right hand were cut pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, reflecting on this, he remembered that, half-awake in the heat, he tightly clutched all of it in his hand and then fell asleep again. — What a collection of rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, as if with a treasure… — And Nastasya laughed with her painful, nervous laugh. He immediately tucked everything under his overcoat and stared at it intently with his eyes. Although he could hardly think clearly at that moment, he felt that people would not treat him the same way when they came to take him.
