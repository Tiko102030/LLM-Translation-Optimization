Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely open. He walked quickly and firmly, and though he felt that he was entirely torn apart, his mind was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, probably orders to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to disappear before the time came. He had to take care of things while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: 'Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's the end of it.' He had decided this even during the night, in his delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: 'As soon as possible, as soon as possible, and throw everything away.' But throwing it away turned out to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe more, and several times looked at the access points into the ditch, where they had met. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were standing right at the access points and the laundresses were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and people were everywhere, and from all sides, from the embankments, one could see, notice: suspiciously, that a person intentionally walked off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases don't sink but float?
