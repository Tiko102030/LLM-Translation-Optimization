A small indentation formed under the stone; he immediately started throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet landed at the top, yet there was still space left in the indentation. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it back to its original side with one twist, and it just landed in its original place, barely, slightly seeming higher. But he scraped the earth and pressed down the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the one he had felt in the office before, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who, who would ever think to look under this stone? It's been here since the house was built and will remain here for just as long."
