—Ilya Petrovich!— the scribe began solicitously, but stopped to wait for the time, because the boiling corporal could not be restrained except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the plump lady, she first trembled from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses, the more amiable her appearance became, the more charming her smile directed at the thunderous corporal. She stamped in place and kept squatting, impatiently waiting for her to be allowed to speak her piece, and she waited.— There's no noise or fight here, sir captain, — she rushed out, as if spilling peas, with a strong German accent, though nimbly in Russian, — and no, no scandal, they came drunk, and I will tell you all, sir captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble house, sir captain, and noble manners, sir captain, and I always, always didn't want any scandal. And they came completely drunk and then asked three times about the piano keys, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, which is completely inappropriate in a noble house, and he broke the entire piano, and there's absolutely no manner here, and I said. And he took the piano key and started pushing everyone from behind with the piano key. And here, as I started soon, the servant called and Karl came, he took Karl and hit his eye, and Henrietta also hit the eye, and I was hit five times on the cheek. And this is so inconsiderate in a noble house, sir captain, and I screamed. And he opened the canal window and started screaming from the window, like a little pig; and this is shameful.
