There are no clues!" — and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he laughed with a nervous, small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and kept laughing all the time, as if passing through the square. But when he stepped onto the Kth Boulevard, where he had met that girl three days before, his laughter suddenly ceased. Other thoughts came into his head. Suddenly it seemed to him that it was extremely repulsive to pass by that bench where he had sat, after the girl left, pondering, and that it would also be terribly hard to meet again that bearded man to whom he had given a kopeck: "By God, take him!" He walked, looking around listlessly and spitefully. All his thoughts now revolved around one main point, and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and that now, precisely now, he was left alone with this main point — and that this was even the first time since those two months. "By God, that's it!"
