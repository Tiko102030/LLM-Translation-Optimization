Just spit on someone, bite them, I suppose, if anyone had spoken to him...  
He stopped suddenly as he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I've come myself to Razumikhin! It's the same story again, as before... Yet it's very curious: did I come on my own, or just happened to pass by here? Anyway; I said three days ago that I would go to him the next day, well, and I'll go! As if I couldn't visit him now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. The latter was at home, in his room, and at that moment was occupied, writing, and opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his torn-to-rags robe, in socks on bare feet, with matted hair, unshaven, and unwashed. Surprise appeared on his face.
