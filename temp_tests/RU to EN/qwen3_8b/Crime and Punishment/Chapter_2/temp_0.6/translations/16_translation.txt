His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued to himself as he came out onto the staircase. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say some foolish thing..."  

On the staircase he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the wall cavity—"and here, perhaps, they deliberately searched without me," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say so, cynical attitude toward death suddenly took hold of him, and he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it would hurry up...!"  

Again unbearable heat stood on the street; just a drop of rain would have been nice for all these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and taverns, again the frequent sight of drunk peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head completely spun—this was the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner of yesterday's street, he looked with agonizing anxiety into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might perhaps say," he thought, approaching the office.
