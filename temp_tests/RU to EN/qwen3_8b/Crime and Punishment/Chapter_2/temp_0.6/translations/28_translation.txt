— Well, isn't she my mistress? — Well, so what if she is your mistress? The clerk looked at him with a condescending smile of pity, yet at the same time a touch of triumph, as if he were a new recruit just beginning to be bombarded: "So, what do you feel now, my dear?" But what did it matter now to him about borrowed writing, about reprimands! Was it worth even the slightest worry, let alone any attention! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, the salvation from the crushing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without foresight, without analysis, without future speculations or guesses, without doubts or questions. It was the moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But at that very moment, in the office, something like thunder and lightning occurred. The lieutenant, still shaken by the disrespect, burning with anger, and obviously wishing to support the wounded ambition, launched himself at the unfortunate "plump lady," who had been looking at him since he entered with the most idiotic smile. — So you, such and such a one, he suddenly shouted at the top of his voice (the widow had already left), — what happened to you last night?
