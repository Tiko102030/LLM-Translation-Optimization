He became extremely scattered and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along the B Prospect; but as he was walking, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far, once again to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a secluded place, in the forest, under a bush, bury everything, perhaps even the tree, which might be noticed?" Although he felt he was unable to discuss everything clearly and rationally at that moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him; instead, as he was exiting the B Prospect onto the square, he suddenly saw an entrance to a courtyard, completely enclosed by solid walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a solid, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the solid wall and also close to the gates, ran a wooden fence extending twenty steps into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a secluded, enclosed area where some materials lay scattered. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, soot-stained, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
