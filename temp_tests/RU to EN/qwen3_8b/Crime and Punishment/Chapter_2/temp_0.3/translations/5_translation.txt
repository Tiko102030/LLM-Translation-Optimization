"Pieces of torn flannel will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with intense, almost painful attention, began to look around again, on the floor and everywhere, not forgetting anything else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even simple reasoning, left him began to torment him unbearably. "What, is it really beginning, is the punishment about to start? There, there, that's exactly it!" Indeed, the frayed edges of the flannel he had cut from his pants lay on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen by the first person who looked! "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. A strange thought came to him: perhaps all his clothes were in blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice, because his reasoning had weakened, shattered… his mind was clouded… Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So, that means there should also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into the pocket then!" He immediately turned the pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains, on the pocket lining! "So, he hasn’t completely lost his mind, so there is still reasoning and memory, since he himself realized and guessed!—he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his chest—just a feverish weakness, a momentary delirium,"—and he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
