— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can I say! It's well known that gunpowder can't bear an insult. You, I suppose, have taken offense against him and couldn't help it—said Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble person, I'll tell you, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flew into a rage, boiled over, burned out—and that's it! And everything passed! In the end, only a heart of gold! And in the regiment they called him: "Ensign Gunpowder"...

— And what army was there ever! — exclaimed Ilyia Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely tickled, though still somewhat flustered. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Do forgive me, captain, — he began, very casually, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fomich — take a closer look at my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness, if I've done anything wrong on my part.
