"No, the matter is very unclear," said Ilya Petrovich. Raskolnikov raised his hat and went toward the doors, but he did not reach them... 

When he awoke, he saw that he was sitting on a chair, that someone was supporting him on the right, that another person was standing on the left with a yellow glass filled with yellow water, and that Nikolai Fomich was standing before him, staring intently at him; he stood up from the chair. "What is this, are you ill?" Nikolai Fomich asked rather sharply. "They signed barely, barely traced with a pen," noted the note-taker, sitting down in his place and resuming his work with the papers. "How long have you been ill?" Ilya Petrovich shouted from his place, also handling the papers.
