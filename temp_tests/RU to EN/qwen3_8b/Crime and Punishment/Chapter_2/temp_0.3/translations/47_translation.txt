Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt entirely broken, yet his mind was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, perhaps within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, probably with instructions to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to hide the ends until the right time. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left... Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had made this decision the previous night, in a delirium, during those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "Hurry, hurry, and throw everything away." But throwing it away proved very difficult. He had been strolling along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, perhaps even longer, and several times looked at the wharves in the ditch where they had met. But it was impossible to carry out the plan: either the rafts were tied up right at the wharves with laundry women washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were bustling, and from all sides, from all directions, one could see, notice: it looked suspicious that a person intentionally stepped off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated away?
