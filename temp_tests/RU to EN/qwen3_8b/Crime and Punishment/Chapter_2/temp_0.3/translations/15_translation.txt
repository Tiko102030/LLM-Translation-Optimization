Put on the sock! — he suddenly thought, — it will get even more dusty, and the traces will disappear. But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with revulsion and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other option, he took it again and put it on — and laughed again. "All of this is conditional, all is relative, all of this are just forms," — he thought briefly, only with the edge of his mind, while his whole body trembled, — "after all, he put it on! After all, he finished by putting it on!" Laughter, however, immediately turned into despair. "No, it's not within my strength…" — he thought. His legs were trembling. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head was spinning and aching from the heat.
