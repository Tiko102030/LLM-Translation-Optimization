What's this? He immediately rushed to take them out and throw them on the table. Having gone through everything, even turning inside out his pockets to make sure nothing was left, he carried all this pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one spot, were torn remnants of the wall paper that had come loose from the wall: at once he began stuffing everything into this hole, under the paper: "It's all in! Everything out of sight and the wallet too!" — he thought happily, standing up and staring blankly at the corner, at the even more gaping hole. Suddenly he shuddered with fear: "My God," he whispered in despair, "what has happened to me? Is this hidden? Is this how one hides things?" 

Indeed, he hadn't expected anything but money; he thought there would be only money, and therefore hadn't prepared a place in advance — "but now, now, what am I happy about? — he thought. — Is this how one hides things?"
