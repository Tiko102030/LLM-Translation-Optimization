"Money? Which money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but... surely, it's not that!" And he shivered with joy. Suddenly, he felt an inexpressible ease. Everything fell from his shoulders. — "At what hour are you to come, sir?" — shouted the ensign, increasingly and more unknownly offended, — they write you at nine, and now it's already twelve! — "I was brought only a quarter of an hour ago," — answered Raskolnikov loudly over his shoulder, also suddenly and unexpectedly for himself getting angry and even finding some pleasure in it. — "And that's enough that I came here as a feverish patient." — "Do not shout!"
