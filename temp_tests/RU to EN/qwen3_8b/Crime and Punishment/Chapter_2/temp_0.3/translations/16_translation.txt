His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with deceit and then knock me down," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "It's bad that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."
On the stairs, he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the wall hole— "and here, perhaps deliberately, they would search without him," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and, if one can say so, the cynicism of death suddenly seized him, so he waved his hand and continued on. "Just hurry up..."
On the street, unbearable heat still lingered; even a drop of rain would have been welcome. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and drinking places, again the frequent sight of drunk, street-vendor Chukhons and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun gleamed brightly in his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun again—the usual sensation of a feverish person stepping suddenly out into a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with tormenting anxiety at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
