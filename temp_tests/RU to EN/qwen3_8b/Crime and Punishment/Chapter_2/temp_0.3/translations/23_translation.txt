The plump lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a lieutenant, assistant to the quarter master, with russet-colored mustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and with exceptionally small facial features, nothing special, except for some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his costume was too wretched, and despite all his humility, his posture still did not match his clothes; Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and for too long, so that the officer even felt offended. "What's the matter with you?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged fellow didn't even flinch from his lightning glance. "They required... by order..." Raskolnikov answered rather awkwardly. "It's about recovering money from them, from the student," hastened the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Here!" and he tossed Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it, "read! 'Money?'"
