There's not even a match. No, better to go somewhere and throw everything away. Yes! Better to throw it all away!—he kept repeating, sitting down again on the sofa—and right now, this very minute, without delay!... But instead of that, his head again sank onto the pillow; again the unbearable chill seized him; again he pulled the overcoat over himself. And for a long time, several hours, he still kept imagining, in fits, that "now, without delay, he should go somewhere and throw everything away, so that it would be out of sight, as soon as possible, as soon as possible!" He several times tried to get up from the sofa, wanted to stand, but already couldn't. Finally, he was awakened by a strong knock on the door. "Open up, alive or not? And all the time he's sleeping!"—Nastasya shouted, pounding her fist on the door— "sleeping all day long, like a dog!"
