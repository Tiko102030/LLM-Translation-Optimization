And lo and behold, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly recognized the voice of his mistress. She was screaming, wailing, and crying out, rushing and hurrying, spilling words so fast that it was impossible to make sense of what she was saying—clearly pleading, of course, to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the attacker had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was now only a rasp, yet the attacker himself was still uttering something, speaking in a mumbled, hurried fashion, gasping and struggling to get words out. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized that voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich. Ilyi Petrovich was here, beating his mistress! He was kicking her with his feet, hitting her head against the steps—clearly, one could hear it by the sounds, the screams, the blows! What was this—has the world turned upside down? One could hear people gathering on all the floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, footsteps, and people gathering. "But why, why, and how can this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he was hearing it all too clearly...
