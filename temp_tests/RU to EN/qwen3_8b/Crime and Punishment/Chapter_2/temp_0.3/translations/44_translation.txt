— Where to, may I ask? — On the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, entirely pale as a sheet and without lowering his black inflamed eyes before Ily Petrovich's gaze. — He barely stands on his feet, but you… — remarked Nikodim Fomich. — Nothing! — said Ily Petrovich in a particularly manner. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, looking at the scribe, who was also intensely staring at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
