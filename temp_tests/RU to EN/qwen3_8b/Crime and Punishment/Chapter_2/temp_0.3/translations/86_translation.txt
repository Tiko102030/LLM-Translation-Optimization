— Hurry up and get the tea, Nastasya, because, I suppose, you can manage the tea without a faculty. But here comes the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and beef closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. — I, brother Rodya, now have lunch like this every day here, — he muttered, as much as his beef-filled mouth allowed. — And this is all Pashenka, your housekeeper, who's entertaining me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. And here comes Nastasya with the tea. How quick she is! Nastenka, do you want some beer? — And by the devil! — And a cup of tea?
