Just spit on someone, bite them, I suppose, if anyone talked to him...  
He stopped suddenly as he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I didn't even realize I came to Razumikhin on my own! It's the same story again, as before... Yet it's very interesting: did I come on my own or just happened to walk here? Anyway; I said three days ago that I would go to him the next day, well, and I will! As if I can't even visit now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth-floor apartment. The latter was home, in his own room, and at that moment was occupied with writing, and opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other for about four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his torn-to-rags robe, socks on his bare feet, with matted hair, unshaven, and unclean. Surprise appeared on his face.
