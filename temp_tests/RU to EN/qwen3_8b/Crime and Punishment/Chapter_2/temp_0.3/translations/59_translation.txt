Leave me alone! Wait a minute, chimney-sweeper! You're completely mad! As for me, it's up to you. You see, I have no lessons, and I don't care at all; there's the bookseller Heruvimov on Tolkuchy, that's in itself a lesson. I won't exchange him for five merchant's lessons. He produces such publications and issues natural science books—how they sell! The titles alone are worth something! You always used to say I was foolish; by God, brother, there are people even more foolish than me! Now he's also gone into the direction; he feels nothing at all, but I, of course, encourage him.
