—"All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us,"—Ilya Petrovich cut in rudely,—"you must give your opinion and commitment, and as for all these tragic places where you happened to be in love, it has nothing to do with us."— "Well, you... you're cruel,"—murmured Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. Suddenly he felt ashamed.—"Write down,"—said the scribe to Rasikolnikov.—"What to write?"—asked the latter rather rudely.—"I'll dictate to you." Rasikolnikov felt that the scribe had become more carelessly and disdainfully towards him after his confession, but strangely enough, suddenly he himself felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened in an instant, in one minute. If he had only taken a moment to think, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on expressing his feelings. And where did these feelings come from?
