— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — he said to the brightly dressed, blood-red lady, who stood still as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said quietly, with a silk-like sound, and sat down. Her light blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread around the chair and took up almost half the room. It carried a scent. But the lady, obviously shy, hesitated because she occupied almost half the room and because she smelled so strongly, although she smiled timidly and boldly at the same time, but with evident unease. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very manly and somehow particularly turning her shoulders with every step, a officer entered, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up from her place upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her no slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence.
