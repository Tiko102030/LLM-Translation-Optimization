— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student and will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some spittle came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Please be silent! You are in the presence. Don't be a rude person, sir! — And you are also in the presence, — Raskolnikov shouted, — besides, while you shout, you are smoking a cigarette, so you are offending everyone. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
