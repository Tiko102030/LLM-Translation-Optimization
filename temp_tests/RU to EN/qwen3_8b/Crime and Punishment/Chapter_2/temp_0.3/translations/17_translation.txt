"If they ask, maybe I will say," he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away. She had just moved to a new apartment, a new building, to the fourth floor. He had once briefly been there, but a long time ago. Upon entering the gates, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a man was coming with a book in his hand: "A janitor, then; that means the office is here," and he began climbing up at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. "I'll go in, kneel down, and tell everything..." he thought, entering the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and entirely dirty. All the kitchens of the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood like that almost all day. That's why the air was stifling.
