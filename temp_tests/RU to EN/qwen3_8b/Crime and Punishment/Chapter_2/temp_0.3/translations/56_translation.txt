His face showed surprise. "What's the matter?" he shouted, inspecting his incoming companion from head to toe; then he fell silent and sighed. "Is it really that bad? You've outdone our brother, brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's rags. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when the latter collapsed onto the Turkish veneer divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. "You're seriously ill, do you know that?" He started feeling his pulse; Raskolnikov wrenched his hand away. "No need," he said, "I came... that is, I had no lessons... I wanted to... in any case, I don't need lessons at all..."
