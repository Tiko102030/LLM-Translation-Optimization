He did not answer and held the paper in his hands, not unsealing it. "Don't get up, really," Nastasya continued, piteously, seeing that he was taking his legs off the couch. "He's ill, so don't go out: it won't burn." What's in your hands, then? He looked: in his right hand, he had cut-off pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, reflecting on it, he remembered that, half-awakening in the heat, he tightly clutched all of this in his hand and fell asleep again. "What a mess of rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, just like with a treasure…" And Nastasya laughed with her painful, nervous laugh. He quickly put everything under the overcoat and stared at it intently with his eyes. Although he could hardly think clearly at that moment, he felt that they would not treat the person so when they came to take him.
