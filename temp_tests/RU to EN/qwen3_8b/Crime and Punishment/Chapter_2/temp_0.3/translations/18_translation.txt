There was a terrible stuffiness because of that. Up and down, janitors with books under their arms came and went, lame and various people of both sexes—visitors. The door to the very office was also left ajar. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Here everyone stood and waited for some men. Here there was also extraordinary stuffiness, and in addition, the fresh, still wet paint on the old, rancid oil of the newly painted rooms was so nauseating that it hit the nose. After waiting a bit, he decided to move further forward into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. Terrible impatience was pulling him further and further. Nobody noticed him.
