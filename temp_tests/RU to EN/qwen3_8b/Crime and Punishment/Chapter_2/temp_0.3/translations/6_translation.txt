He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath with his entire chest, "It's just a feverish weakness, a delirium for a moment," and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a ray of sunlight illuminated his left boot: on the sock, which was peeking out of the boot, as if signs appeared. He kicked off the boot: "Indeed, signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood"; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly... "But what to do with this now? Where to put this sock, the tassels, the pocket?" He gathered all this in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. "Into the stove? But first, they will start digging in the stove. Burn it? And with what? There are even no matches."
