— Corporal Il'yá Petrovich! — began the clerk carelessly, but stopped to wait, since there was no other way to hold back the boiling corporal except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the plump lady, at first she trembled from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more amiable her expression grew, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the furious corporal. She shuffled in place and kept bowing continuously, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak, and finally did. — There's no noise or fight here, Mr. Captain, — she suddenly burst out, as if spilling peas, speaking rapidly in Russian with a strong German accent — and no scandal at all, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble house, Mr. Captain, and noble manners, Mr. Captain, and I always, always wanted no scandal. They came completely drunk, and then asked for three pistols, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, and that's very bad in a noble house, and he broke the piano, and there's absolutely no manner here, and I said so. He took a pistol and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the steward and Karl came, he took Karl and hit him in the eye, and Henrietta also got hit in the eye, and I got my cheek hit five times. And that's very indecent in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I shouted. He opened the window of the ditch and started squealing like a little pig from the window; and that's a disgrace.
