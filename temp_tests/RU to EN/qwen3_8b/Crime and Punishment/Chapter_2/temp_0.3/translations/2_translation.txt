The light was enough, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, all his clothes: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering from cold, he began to remove everything from himself and again inspect the surroundings. He turned everything over, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the spot where the pants had frayed at the bottom and hung like fringes, there were thick traces of congealed blood on the fringes. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringes. More, it seemed, nothing else was there. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He didn't even remember them now, as he was inspecting the dress! What is this?
