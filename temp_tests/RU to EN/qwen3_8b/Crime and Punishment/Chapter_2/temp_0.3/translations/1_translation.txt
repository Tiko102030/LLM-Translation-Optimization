"Three hours already!" He sat on the couch—and suddenly everything came back to him! In an instant, he remembered everything! At first, he thought he was going mad. A terrible cold gripped him; but the cold was also from a fever that had already begun with him in his sleep. Now, however, a sudden chill struck him so strongly that his teeth almost came out, and everything inside him was spinning. He opened the door and began to listen: the house was completely silent. He looked at himself and everything around him in the room, bewildered and unable to understand: how could he, yesterday, having entered, not lock the doors with the hook and throw himself onto the couch, not only without undressing, but even with his hat on? It had slid down and immediately lay on the floor near the pillow. "What would someone think if they came in?" he mused. "That I'm drunk, but…" He rushed to the window. There was enough light, and he began to examine himself thoroughly, from head to toe, all his clothes: were there any traces?
