Yes, he remembered later that he laughed with a nervous, small, inaudible, long laugh, and kept laughing all the time, as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto the Kth boulevard, where he had met the girl three days ago, his laughter suddenly ceased. Other thoughts entered his mind. It suddenly seemed to him that it was extremely repulsive for him to pass by that bench, where he had sat after the girl left, pondering, and it would be extremely hard to meet again that bearded man, to whom he had given a kopeck: "By God!" He walked, looking around distractedly and angrily. All his thoughts now revolved around a certain main point — and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and that now, precisely now, he was left alone with this main point — and that this was even the first time after these two months. "By God, that's it!"
