It was suffocatingly hot. Janitors with booklets under their arms went up and down, cloddy and various people of both sexes—visitors. The door to the office was also left ajar. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Here, everyone stood and waited for some men. The heat was unbearable, and, in addition, a nauseating smell of fresh, still-wet paint on the rancid linseed oil of freshly painted rooms. After waiting a bit, he resolved to move forward into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. A dreadful impatience pulled him further and further. No one noticed him.
