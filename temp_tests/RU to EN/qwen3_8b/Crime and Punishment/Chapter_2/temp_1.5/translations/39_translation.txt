The clerk began dictating to him the form of an ordinary disclaimer in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (whensoever), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on. — "You can't write at all, your quill is slipping from your hand," remarked the clerk, looking with curiosity at Raskolnikov. "Are you ill?" — "Yes... my head is spinning... go on!" — "That's all; sign it." The clerk took the paper and proceeded with the others. Raskolnikov handed over the quill, but instead of rising and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clasped his head with his hands. It felt as if a nail was being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to get up now, go to Nicholas Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, every detail, then go together with them to the apartment and show them the things, in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat to carry it out.
