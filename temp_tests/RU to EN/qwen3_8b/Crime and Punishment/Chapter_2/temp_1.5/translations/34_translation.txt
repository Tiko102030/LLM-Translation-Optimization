Louisa Ivanovna, with ingratiating politeness, began to sit down on all sides and, while doing so, shuffled up to the door; but at the door she bumped into a prominent officer with a fresh, open face and excellent, thick golden mustaches. It was none other than Nikodym Fomich, the quartermaster. Louisa Ivanovna hurriedly sat down almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, leaped out of the office. "Again thunder, again lightning, whirlwind, storm!" Nikodym Fomich politely and amiably addressed Ilyy Petrovich, "Again disturbed the heart, again boiling up! Even heard it from the stairs." "What's this?" Ilyy Petrovich spoke with a noble indifference (and actually not "what" but rather "da-aa shcha-a!") while moving with some papers to another desk, dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he stepped, there his shoulder went; "Here you see, you may observe: the gentleman-author, that is to say, the student, former one, doesn't pay money, gave promissory notes, doesn't clean the apartment, constant complaints come in about them, and they entered a complaint that I lit a cigarette in front of them! They themselves are rude, but here you see, you may look at them: here they are in their most attractive form now!" "Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what can be done!"
