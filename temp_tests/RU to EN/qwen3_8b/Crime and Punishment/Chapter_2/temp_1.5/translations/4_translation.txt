"—Is that how they hide it? My mind is truly leaving me!" He collapsed in exhaustion onto the sofa, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him again. Instinctively, he pulled the lying nearby on the chair, his former student winter coat, warm but already almost in rags, covered himself with it, and sleep and delirium once again seized him. He lost himself in unconsciousness. No more than five minutes later, he sat up again, and immediately, in a frenzy, he rushed once more to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, with nothing done! So it is, so it is: the necktie is still under my armpit! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the necktie off and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow along with his underwear. "Pieces of torn linen will in no way raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he began once again to scan around, on the floor and everywhere, not having forgotten anything else?
