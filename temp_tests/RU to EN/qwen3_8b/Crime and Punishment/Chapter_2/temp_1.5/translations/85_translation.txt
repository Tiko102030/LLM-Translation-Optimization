Meanwhile, Razumikhin sat down on the couch next to him awkwardly, like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, despite the fact that he himself could have raised his head, and with his right hand brought a spoonful of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand to prevent it from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed a spoonful, then another, the third. But after bringing several spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and announced that they needed to consult with Zosimov about the further course of action. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want tea? — I want. — Hurry up and make tea, Nastasya, because, regarding tea, it seems we can do without the faculty.
