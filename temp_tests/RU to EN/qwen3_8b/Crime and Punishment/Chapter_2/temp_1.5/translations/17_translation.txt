"If they ask, perhaps I will say," he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a versta away. She had just moved to a new apartment, a new building, on the fourth floor. He had been there briefly before, but a long time ago. Upon entering the gates, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a man was descending with a book in his hand: "So, a caretaker; therefore, the office must be here," and he started climbing up at random. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. "I'll go in, kneel down, and tell everything..." he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and covered in filth. All the kitchens from all the apartments on the four floors opened onto this staircase and remained in that state almost all day. That's why the air was oppressively stuffy.
