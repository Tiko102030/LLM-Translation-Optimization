"Money? Which money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but... surely it's not that!" And he trembled with joy. Suddenly, it became incredibly easy for him, unbearable lightness. Everything fell off his shoulders. — "In what hour are you supposed to come, dear sir?" shouted the poruchik, growing more and more offended without knowing why — "You were told at nine, but it's already twelve now!" — "I was given it only a quarter of an hour ago," answered Raskolnikov loudly, through his shoulder, also unexpectedly and suddenly for himself angered, even finding some pleasure in it. — "It's enough that I've come as a feverish patient." — "Don't shout!"
