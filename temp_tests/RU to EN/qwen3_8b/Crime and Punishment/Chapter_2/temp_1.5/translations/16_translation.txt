His head spun and throbbed from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with deceit and suddenly knock me down—," he continued thinking to himself as he descended the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say some nonsense..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he left all his belongings in the wall hole—"and here, perhaps, they intentionally searched it,"—and recalled this, stopping. But such despair and such, if one can say so, cynical acceptance of death suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's soon!..."  

The heat on the street was unbearable again; perhaps a drop of rain would have been welcome these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from benches and taverns, again the ever-present drunkards, the beggars, and the half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun flashed brightly into his eyes, causing him pain and his head spun completely—this was the usual feeling of a feverish person emerging suddenly onto a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner leading back to the street from yesterday, he looked anxiously into it, at that house... and immediately averted his eyes. "If they ask, I might perhaps tell the truth,"—he thought, approaching the office.
