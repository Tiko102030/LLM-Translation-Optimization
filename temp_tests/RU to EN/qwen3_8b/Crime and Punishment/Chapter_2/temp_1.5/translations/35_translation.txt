— Poverty is not a fault, my friend, but well, what can you do! It is well known that powder cannot bear insult. You must have offended him somehow, and perhaps you couldn't help it, — Nikodim Fomich continued, politely addressing Raskolnikov — but you're wasting your time: he is, I tell you, the most honorable person, but powder, powder! He got angry, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! And everything passed! In the end, only the gold of the heart remained! They called him in the regiment: "sergeant-powder"…

— What regiment could it be! — exclaimed Ilyá Petrovich, greatly pleased that he had been so nicely tickled, but still somewhat irritable. Suddenly, Raskolnikov felt like saying something remarkably nice to them all. — Please, captain, — he began, addressing Nikodim Fomich abruptly, in a very free and easy manner — take my situation into account… I am even ready to ask their forgiveness if I have, on my part, been at fault.
