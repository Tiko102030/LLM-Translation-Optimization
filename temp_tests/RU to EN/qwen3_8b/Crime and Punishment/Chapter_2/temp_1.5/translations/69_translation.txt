But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, if that's the case, they'll come to him now too, "because... surely, all of this is from the same... from yesterday's... Lord, I'm afraid!" He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand would not raise... and it was useless anyway! Fear, like ice, covered his soul, tormented him, paralyzed him... But at last, after this tumult had been going on for an exact ten minutes, it gradually began to subside. The landlady was groaning and sobbing, and Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and shouting... But now, apparently, he had also fallen silent; now he was no longer heard; "could he have left? Lord!" Yes, now the landlady is leaving too, still groaning and weeping... now her door has slammed shut... Now the crowd is dispersing down the stairs into the apartments, shouting, quarreling, calling out to each other, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been a lot of them; perhaps the whole building had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!" 

Raskolnikov fell into helpless despair onto the sofa, but could no longer close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such torment, in such an unbearable sense of limitless terror, of which he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a plate of soup. Looking at him attentively and realizing he was not asleep, she put the candle on the table and began to lay out the food: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
