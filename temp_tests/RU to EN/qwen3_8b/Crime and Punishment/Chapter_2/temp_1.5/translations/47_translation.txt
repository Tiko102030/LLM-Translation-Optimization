Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt completely battered, yet remained conscious. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, maybe even within a quarter of an hour, someone would come out, perhaps an order to watch him; hence, regardless of what happened, he had to get rid of the ends before the time came. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength left and some ability to think… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this during the night, in his delirium, during those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to stand up and go: "Hurry, hurry, and throw everything away." But it turned out to be very difficult to throw it away. He was already wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe even longer, and several times looked at the gangway into the ditch where they had met him. But it was impossible to carry out the plan: either the barges were tied up right at the gangway and the laundry women were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were densely gathered, and from all sides along the embankments, from all directions, one could see, notice: it would look suspicious that a person deliberately went down, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated?
