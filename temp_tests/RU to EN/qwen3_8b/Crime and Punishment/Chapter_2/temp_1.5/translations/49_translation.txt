He was becoming extremely distracted and forgetful, and he was aware of this. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along the B Prospect; but as he was on his way, a sudden thought occurred to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far, perhaps again to the Islands, and there, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush, bury all of this and the tree, perhaps even notice it?" Although he felt he couldn't fully discuss everything clearly at that moment, the thought seemed infallible. But it was not destined for him to reach the Islands; instead, upon exiting the B Prospect onto the square, he suddenly saw an entrance to a courtyard, surrounded by completely soundproof walls. On the right, immediately after entering the gates, a soundproof, unpainted wall of a neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. On the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also right from the gates, a wooden fence extended twenty steps into the courtyard and then made a left turn. This was a soundproof, secluded area where some materials lay. Further in the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed protruded from the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
