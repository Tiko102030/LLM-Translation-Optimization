"Three hours already!" He sat on the sofa—and then it all came back to him! Suddenly, all at once, he remembered everything! In the first moment, he thought he was going mad. A terrible cold enveloped him; but the cold was also from the fever, which had started with him in his sleep long ago. Now, however, a sudden chills struck him so violently that his teeth almost came out, and everything within him was in a state of convulsion. He opened the door and began to listen: the whole house was completely asleep. He looked at himself with amazement and everything around him in the room, not understanding: how could he, upon entering yesterday, not lock the doors on the hook and run to the sofa without even taking off his clothes, let alone removing his hat? It had fallen down and was already lying on the floor near the pillow. "What would someone think if they came in? That I was drunk, but…" He rushed to the window. There was enough light, and he quickly began to examine himself, from his feet to his head, all his clothes: were there any traces?
