"Pieces of torn cloth will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he repeated, standing in the center of the room, and with a strained, aching attention began to scrutinize everything around him, on the floor and everywhere, not forgetting anything? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, left him began to intolerably torment him. "What, is it really beginning? Is this the moment of death? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the cuts of the fringe he had cut from his pants were lying on the floor, scattered around the room, so that the first glance would see them! "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. At that moment, an odd thought came to him: perhaps all his clothes were covered in blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just couldn't see them, didn't notice them, because his reasoning had weakened, shattered… his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there was blood on his wallet too. "Ba! So there must also be blood in my pocket, because I had still a wet wallet in my pocket then!" He immediately turned his pocket inside out, and indeed—there were traces, spots! "So I haven't lost my mind completely, there is still thought and memory, since I have realized myself and guessed it! — he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest — it's just a delirious weakness, a momentary delusion," — and he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
