He caught his breath more freely. "Probably not!" Gradually, he began to encourage himself, urging himself with all his strength to cheer up and recover his senses. "Some silly thing, some utterly minor carelessness, and I might give myself away entirely! Hmm… Alas, there's no air here—stuffy air… My head spins even more… and my mind as well…" He felt a terrible chaos within himself. He himself feared losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about something completely unrelated, but it completely failed. The stenographer, on the other hand, interested him greatly; he was always wanting to figure out something from his face, to decipher him. He was a very young man, about twenty-two, with a dark and lively face, which seemed older than his years, dressed according to the latest fashion and in a fathom, with a part in his hair, combed and powdered, with numerous rings and finger rings on his white, brush-cleaned fingers, and with golden chains on his vest. He even spoke two words in French to one of the former foreigners here, and very satisfactorily. "— Madame Louise Ivanovna, you'd sit down," he said to the hastily dressed flushed-red lady, who stood all the time as if not daring to sit herself, though a chair was nearby.
