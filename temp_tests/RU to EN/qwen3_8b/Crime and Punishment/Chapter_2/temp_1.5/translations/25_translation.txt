— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it's you who are shouting at me; I am a student and will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant had been so enraged that for the first moment he couldn't say anything, and only some drips were dripping from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Be quiet! You're in the presence. Don't be so rude, fool! — Yes, and you are also in the presence, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides shouting, you are smoking a cigarette, therefore treating us all improperly. Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable joy. The clerk smiled at them.
