A small depression formed under the stone; immediately, he began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet landed at the very top, yet there was still space left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, twisted it once, and flipped it back to its original side, which coincidentally landed exactly where it had been before, slightly, just a little, seeming a bit higher. But he pushed the earth aside and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed toward the square. Again, a strong, almost unbearable joy, like the time in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who, who would ever think of looking under this stone? It might have been lying here since the house was built and will remain there for just as long."
