Not at all!.."

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question at once threw him off guard and bitterly astonished him:

"If indeed all this was done intentionally, not by accident, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even glanced into your wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you endured all these torments, and for which you knowingly went on such a low, nasty, degrading mission? You wanted to throw it all into the water now—the wallet, together with all the other things you hadn't even seen yet… How is that possible?"

Yes, that's exactly it; it's all like that. He, in fact, knew this before, and it was not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided during the night to throw everything into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if it was only natural, as if there was no other way… Yes, he knew everything and remembered it all; perhaps it was decided almost yesterday, at that very moment when he was sitting by the chest and was taking out the boxes from it… And that's exactly how it was!.. "It's because I am very ill," he grumbled at last, "I have tortured and torn myself apart, and I don't even know what I'm doing… Yesterday, three days ago, and all this time I have been tormenting myself… I'll recover and… stop tormenting myself… What if I never recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of this!..” He continued walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. A single new, insurmountable feeling was taking hold of him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical repulsion towards everything he encountered and surrounded him, persistent, spiteful, hateful. All the people he met were revolting to him—revolting were their faces, their walk, their gestures. He simply wanted to spit on someone, perhaps even bite someone if they talked to him…

He suddenly stopped when he reached the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
