The pain from the lash had subsided, and Raskolnikov had forgotten about the blow; one restless and not quite clear thought now entirely occupied him. He stood and looked into the distance for a long and intently; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to the university, he usually, more often than not on returning home, found himself stopping precisely at this same spot, staring intently at this truly magnificent panorama and was almost amazed each time by a single unclear and unsolvable impression of his. An inexplicable coldness always emanated from this magnificent panorama; it was filled for him with a silent and heavy spirit... Each time he marveled at his gloomy and enigmatic impression, postponing its solution, not trusting himself, to the future. Suddenly, he sharply recalled these previous questions and doubts of his, and it seemed to him that he had not recalled them by accident now. It seemed to him strange and strange that he had stopped at the same spot as before, as if he had really imagined that he might be thinking about the same thing now, and being interested in the same themes and pictures as before... just recently. Even slightly amused him, and at the same time, it painfully crushed his chest. In some depth, below, somewhere barely visible under his feet, it seemed to him now all this previous past, and previous thoughts, and previous tasks, and previous themes, and previous impressions, and this entire panorama, and himself, and everything, everything... It seemed as if he was flying off somewhere upwards and everything was disappearing from his eyes... Making one involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a kopeck tightly gripped in his fist. He uncurled his hand, stared intently at the coin, swung it, and threw it into the water; then he turned around and walked home. It seemed to him that he had somehow cut himself off from all and everything at that moment with scissors.
