And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with quarter officers, but with his closest friends, then perhaps even then he would not have had a single human word for them, his heart had suddenly become empty. A gloomy sensation of tormenting, endless solitude and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. It was not the baseness of his heart's confessions before Ilyich Petrovich, nor the baseness and the poruchik's triumph over him that suddenly overturned his heart so. Oh, what business does he have now with his own cowardice, with all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, grievances, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were condemned to be burned at this very moment, he would not have stirred, not even likely listened attentively to the sentence. Something completely unfamiliar to him, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. Not that he understood it, but he clearly sensed, with the full strength of feeling, that he could no longer address these people, whether in the quarter office or with them, even if they were all his dear brothers and sisters, and not mere quarter officers. Even then, he would have had absolutely no need to address them, and never in any case in his life; he had never before experienced such a strange and terrifying sensation. And what was most agonizing of all — it was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; an immediate, agonizing sensation, the most tormenting sensation of all he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form of a report in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise to do so then (some time), I will not leave the town, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.
