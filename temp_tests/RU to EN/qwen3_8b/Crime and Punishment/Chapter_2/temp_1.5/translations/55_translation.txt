Just blow someone off, bite someone, as if someone had talked to him...  
He stopped suddenly when he reached the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilevsky Island, near the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I came to Razumikhin on my own! It's the same story again, as before... It's very interesting: did I come on my own or just walked here by chance? Either way; I said three days ago that I would come the next day, so I'll go! As if I can't visit now..."  
He climbed up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. The latter was at home, in his room, and at that moment was writing, and opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other in four months. Razumikhin was wearing a tattered robe, barefoot, unkempt, unshaven, and unclean. Surprise was expressed on his face.
