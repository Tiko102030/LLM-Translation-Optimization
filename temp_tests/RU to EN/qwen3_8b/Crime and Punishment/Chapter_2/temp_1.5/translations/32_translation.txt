— Yes, Mr. Captain, and what a base guest, Mr. Captain, when in a noble house…

— Nonsense! Enough! I've already told you, I've told you, I've told you…

— Ilya Petrovich! — the clerk spoke again significantly. The ensign quickly glanced at him; the clerk slightly nodded his head. — …Here's my final tale for you, esteemed Laviza Ivanovna, and this is the last time, — continued the ensign. — If there is another scandal in your noble house, I'll settle it myself, on tuzhunger, as it's said in refined style. Heard? So a writer, a scribe, took five kreuzers for a fur in the noble house? There they are, the scribes!
