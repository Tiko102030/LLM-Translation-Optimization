— Where to, may I ask? — Along the street. — Brief and clear. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, all pale as a cloth and not lowering his black, inflamed eyes from the gaze of Ilyi Petrovich. — He is barely standing on his feet, but you... — remarked Nikodim Fomich. — Nothing! — said Ilyi Petrovich, in a particularly emphatic tone. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, looking at the scribe, who was also intently staring at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
