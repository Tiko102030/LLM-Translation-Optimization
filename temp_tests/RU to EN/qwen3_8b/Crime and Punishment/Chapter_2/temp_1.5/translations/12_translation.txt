He did not answer and held the paper in his hands without unfolding it. "Don't get up, after all," Nastasia continued, pitifully, noticing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. "He's ill, so just stay in bed; it won't burn." What did he have in his hands? He looked: in his right hand were pieces of cut fringe, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, reflecting on it, he remembered how, half-awake in the heat, he tightly gripped all of it in his hand and then fell asleep again. "Look at all these tatters he's gathered and sleeps with them, as if with a treasure," Nastasia said, rolling with her painful, nervous laughter. He quickly hid everything under his cloak and stared intently at it. Though he could hardly fully comprehend the situation at that moment, he felt that people would not treat him kindly when his brothers came.
