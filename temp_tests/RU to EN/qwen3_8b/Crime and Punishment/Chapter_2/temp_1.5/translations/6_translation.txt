—"He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath from his whole chest, 'It's just a feverish weakness, a delirium for a moment,'— and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a sunbeam illuminated his left boot: on the sock peeking out of the boot, as if signs appeared. He kicked off the boot: 'Indeed, signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood'; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly... 'But what now? What to do with this sock, the braid, the pocket?' He gathered all of it in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. 'Into the stove? But first they'll start digging in the stove. To burn it? And with what? There's not even a match."
