There was enough light, and he began to examine himself hastily, from head to toe, his entire dress—were there any traces? But that was impossible: shivering with cold, he started removing everything from his body and inspecting it again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and, doubting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the place where his pants had frayed at the bottom, hanging like fringes, there remained thick traces of dried blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. It seemed that was all. Suddenly he remembered that his wallet and the belongings he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even remembered them now, as he was inspecting his dress! What was this?
