The plump lady sprang up from her seat the moment she saw him, and with some special zest began to sit down again; but the officer paid her absolutely no attention, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence. He was a sublieutenant, assistant quartermaster, with russet-colored mustaches sprouting horizontally in both directions and with extremely small facial features, nothing remarkable, except for a certain impudence. He glanced at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were so shabby, and despite all his humbling himself, his posture still hadn't improved. Raskolnikov, in his inconsiderate manner, had looked at him too straight and for too long, so that the officer even got offended. "What's the matter with you?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged person didn't flinch at his lightning glance. "I was summoned… by order…", Raskolnikov managed to answer. "It's about the case of reclaiming money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Here you are!" and he tossed Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it, — read! "Money?"
