Surprise showed on his face. — What are you? — he shouted, inspecting the incoming companion from head to toe; then he fell silent and whistled. — Is it really that bad? You, brother, have outdone our brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's ragged clothes. — Sit down, you must be tired! — and when he collapsed onto the crepe Turkish sofa, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. — You're seriously ill, you know it? — He began to check his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. — No need, — he said, — I came… here's the thing: I have no lessons… I had wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — Do you know something?
