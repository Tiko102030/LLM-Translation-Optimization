— All these sensitive details, my good sir, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich snapped arrogantly, — you must give your statement and an undertaking; as for all these tragic places where you, by your indulgence, chose to be in love, it's entirely none of our business. — Well, you're being awfully harsh… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. A certain shame had come over him. — Write down, — said the scribe to Raskolnikov. — What should I write? — asked Raskolnikov rather rudely. — I shall dictate to you. Raskolnikov noticed that the scribe had become more careless and contemptuous toward him since his confession. Strange, however, — he suddenly felt completely indifferent to what anyone else thought, and this change came over him in an instant, in one minute. If he had merely wanted to think a little more, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist upon expressing his own feelings. And where did these feelings come from?
