So he lay there for a very long time. It sometimes seemed as if he were waking up, and during those moments he would notice that it was already night, yet getting up never occurred to him. Finally, he noticed that it was already light enough for daytime. He lay on the couch on his back, still dazed from his recent slumber. The loud, desperate cries from the street came sharply to his ears, which he had heard every night under his window at three o'clock. It was these cries that woke him up now. "Ah! Here come the drunkards from the taverns," he thought, "three o'clock—it's three!—and suddenly he jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. 'What! It's already three o'clock!' He sat on the couch—and then everything came back to him!"
