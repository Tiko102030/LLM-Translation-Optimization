— Luiza Ivanovna, please sit, — he said to the elaborately dressed crimson lady, who stood still as if unwilling to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she replied, and quietly, with a silk-like whisper, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like a balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room. It carried a scent. But the lady, obviously, felt timid about taking up almost half the room and the scent that came from her, although she smiled shyly and impudently at the same time, yet with evident worry. The widow finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, quite boldly and in a particularly mannered way, twisting her shoulders with each step, a officer entered, tossed his fur cap with a cockade onto the table, and sat in the chair. The opulent lady jumped from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with a peculiar enthusiasm; but the officer paid her absolutely no attention, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence.
