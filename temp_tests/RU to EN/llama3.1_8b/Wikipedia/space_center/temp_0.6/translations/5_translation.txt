Everything mentioned above applies only to iterative algorithms for finding neural network solutions. For them, it is really impossible to guarantee anything and completely automate the training of neural networks. However, alongside with iterative learning algorithms, there exist non-iterative algorithms with very high stability that allow fully automating the training process. Verification of the adequacy of training

Even in a case where training appears successful at first glance, the network is not always trained to learn exactly what its creator wanted it to. There is a known case when a network was trained for recognizing tank images from photographs; however, later it turned out that all tanks were photographed against the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of learning to recognize tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what is required of it, but what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training must be performed on examples that did not participate in its training. In this case, the number of test examples should be as large as possible, and even more so if the quality of training is high. If a neural network's errors have a probability close to one billionth, then it also takes one billion test examples to confirm this probability.
