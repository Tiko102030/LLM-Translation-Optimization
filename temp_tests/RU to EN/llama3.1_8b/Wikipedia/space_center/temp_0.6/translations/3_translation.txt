After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center was damaged by hurricanes, including hurricanes Francis and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle launches took place - missions "Endeavour" and "Atlantis". After completing the shuttle program began transition to commercial use. In 2014, launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launching rockets Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy, as well as crewed Dragon spacecraft. The LC-39B pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft. Visitor complex includes a museum entertainment area with tours, IMAX theaters and bus tours of the territory. Main exhibit - Saturn V rocket. Also on site is an astronaut memorial "Cosmic Mirror", where names of deceased astronauts are immortalized.
