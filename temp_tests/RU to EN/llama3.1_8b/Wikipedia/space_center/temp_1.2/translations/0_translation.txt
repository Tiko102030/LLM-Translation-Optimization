The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a rocket launch and spacecraft testing facility, located on Merritt Island, Florida, along the Atlantic coast of the United States. It is one of two main Centers for Launch Services operated by NASA within the United States. The center is about 35 miles (56 km) long and 7 miles (11 km) wide and occupies approximately 219 sq mi (567 km2). About 15% of it is developed land, including numerous buildings; the rest is natural habitat. It has three major facilities: a Vehicle Assembly Building (VAB), a Launch Complex (LC) which houses LC-39, and a Press Site.
