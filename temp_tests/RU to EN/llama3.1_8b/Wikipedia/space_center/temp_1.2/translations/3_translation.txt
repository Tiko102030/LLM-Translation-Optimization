After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily halted. The center was damaged by hurricanes, including Hurricane Francis and Wilma. In 2011, there were final shuttle launches - the Endeavour and Atlantis missions. After completion of the shuttle program, a transition to commercial use began. In 2014, launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of rockets Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy, as well as crewed spacecraft Dragon. The LC-39B launch pad is being upgraded for the SLS rocket and Orion spaceship. Visitor Complex grounds comprise a museum entertainment area with tours, IMAX theaters and bus excursions across the territory. Main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also located on site is the Astronaut Memorial "Cosmic Mirror," where names of deceased astronauts are immortalized.
