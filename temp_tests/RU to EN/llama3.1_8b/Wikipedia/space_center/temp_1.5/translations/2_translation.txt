The start-up complex No. 39 was built with a large infrastructure including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems. From 1967 to 1973, there were 13 Saturn V launches performed here. During the program, an Apollo-1 tragedy occurred with its crew. Further manned flights took place, including the first landing of man on the Moon in July 1969 with the Apollo-11 mission. Later, Kennedy Space Center was used for Skylab station and Apollo-Soyuz missions as well. Rockets Atlas-Centaur were used here to launch satellites and interplanetary probes too. Until July 2011, it remained the primary launch site of the Space Shuttle program. The first launch of the shuttle Columbia took place in 1981. There is a runway of 4.6 kilometers here where the space shuttles would land safely. After the Challenger disaster in 1986, all flights were temporarily suspended.
