After the Challenger space shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center was damaged by hurricanes, including Hurricane Frances and Wilma. In 2011, the last Space Shuttle missions, Endeavour and Atlantis, were launched. Following the conclusion of the Space Shuttle program, a transition to commercial use began. In 2014, the launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launching the Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets as well as the Dragon spacecraft. The LC-39B site is being upgraded for the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex comprises an exhibition area with guided tours, IMAX theaters, and bus tours of the facility grounds. Its main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. Also on-site is the astronaut memorial "Space Mirror", which lists the names of astronauts who have died in space exploration.
