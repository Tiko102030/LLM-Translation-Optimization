All the above mentioned applies only to iterative algorithms for neural network solution search. For them it's impossible to guarantee anything and fully automate training of neural networks. However, alongside with iterative training algorithms, there are non-iterative algorithms possessing very high robustness that allow full automation of the training process. The adequacy of training verification

Even in case of successful initial training, the network is not always trained for exactly what its creator wanted from it. There is a known case where a network was trained to recognize images of tanks by photographs but later revealed it was because all the tanks were photographed against the same background and so the network 'learned' to identify that type of landscape rather than actually recognizing tanks. Thus, the network understands not what was required from it but the easiest thing to generalize instead. Testing the quality of a neural network training must be conducted on examples that did not participate in its learning process.
