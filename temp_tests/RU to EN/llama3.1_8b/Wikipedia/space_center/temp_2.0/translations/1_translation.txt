The center is serviced by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the centre began in 1962 when NASA began purchasing land for launch expansion. The same year it received the name Launch Center and in 1963, after the assassination of President John Kennedy, was renamed the John F. Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program consisted of three phases: Mercury, Gemini and Apollo. The Mercury program had goals to place a human on an orbit, study his work in space and training for return. Initial flights were carried out using Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini programme used two-seat vessels and allowed the first extravehicular activities. Twelve missions were performed within this framework. The Apollo program used Saturn V rockets.
