After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center suffered damage from hurricanes, including hurricanes Frances and Wilma. In 2011, there were the last shuttle launches — missions "Endeavour" and "Atlantis." After completing the space shuttle program, a transition to commercial use began. In 2014, Launch Complex 39A was transferred to SpaceX on a 20-year lease. It is used for Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rocket launches as well as crewed Dragon spacecraft. The LC-39B complex is being upgraded for the SLS and Orion vehicles. Visitor's Complex has an interactive area with guided tours, IMAX theatres, and bus rides around the territory. The primary exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. A memorial to astronauts "Cosmic Mirror" also resides on site where names of deceased cosmonauts are immortalized.
