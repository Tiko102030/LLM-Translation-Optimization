John F. Kennedy Space Center — a complex of facilities for launching spacecraft and managing flight, owned by NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is located near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with a total area of ​​567 square kilometers. Approximately 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the land is used for its intended purpose, while the rest is a nature reserve, including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island Wildlife Refuge and Canaveral National Seashore. This area experiences one of the highest levels of lightning strikes in the US, so NASA spends significant resources on launch protection. Primary operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building is located. Two additional launch pads are located about 5 kilometers to the east, and an industrial area with equipment and administrative buildings is about 8 kilometers to the south. Shuttles were launched from Pad 39-A, while unmanned spacecraft were also launched from the US Space Force base at Cape Canaveral. The center employs approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
