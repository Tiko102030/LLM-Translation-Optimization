The center is served by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962 when NASA started acquiring land for expansion launches. In the same year, the territory received the name Launch Center, and in 1963, after President John Kennedy's assassination, it was renamed the John F. Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program included three stages: "Mercury," "Gemini," and "Apollo." The Mercury program aimed to put a man into orbit, study his work in space, and rehearse return. The first flights were performed on Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini program used two-seat ships and allowed the first open spacewalks.
