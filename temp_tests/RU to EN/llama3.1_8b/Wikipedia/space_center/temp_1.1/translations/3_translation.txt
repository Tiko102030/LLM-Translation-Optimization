After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The center was damaged by hurricanes including Francis and Wilma. In 2011, the final shuttle launches took place - the Endeavour and Atlantis missions. After the end of the shuttle program, a transition to commercial use began. In 2014, launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rocket launches, as well as crewed Dragon spacecraft. Launch pad LC-39B is being upgraded for the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex area consists of a museum-entertainment zone with guided tours, IMAX theaters and bus tours around the facility. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. There is also a memorial to astronauts called "Space Mirror" on site, where the names of fallen cosmonauts are immortalized.
