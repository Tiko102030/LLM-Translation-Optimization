The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of buildings for launching spacecraft and managing flights, belonging to NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is situated near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with an overall area of 567 km². Approximately 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, while the rest is a nature reserve including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island National Wildlife Refuge, and Cape Canaveral National Seashore. This area records one of the highest numbers of lightning strikes in the United States, so NASA spends significant funds on protecting launches. The main operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building is located. Two launch pads are located about 5 km east, and an industrial zone with equipment and administrative building is situated about 8 km south. Shuttles were launched from complex 39, while unmanned spacecraft were also launched from the US Space Force Base at Cape Canaveral. The center is serviced by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors.
