The center is serviced by approximately 15,000 employees and contractors. The history of the center began in 1962 when NASA started acquiring land for expansion of launches. The same year, the area was named Launch Center, and in 1963, after the assassination of President John F. Kennedy, it was renamed the Kennedy Space Center. The lunar program consisted of three stages: "Mercury", "Gemini", and "Apollo". The Mercury program had goals of putting a man into orbit, studying his work in space, and testing return capabilities. The first flights were made on Redstone and Atlas rockets. The Gemini program used two-seat spacecraft and allowed the first open-spacewalks to be conducted.
