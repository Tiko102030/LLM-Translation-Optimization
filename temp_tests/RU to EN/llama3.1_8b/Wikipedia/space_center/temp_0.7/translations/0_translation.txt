The John F. Kennedy Space Center is a complex of buildings for the launch of spacecraft and flight control, belonging to NASA and located on Merritt Island in Brevard County, Florida, USA. The center is near Cape Canaveral, between Miami and Jacksonville. Its dimensions are approximately 55 km long and 10 km wide, with an area of 567 square kilometers. Approximately 13,500 people work at the center. Only about 9% of the territory is used for its intended purpose, while the rest represents a nature reserve including Mosquito Lagoon, Indian River, Merritt Island Wildlife Refuge, and the Cape Canaveral National Seashore. The area records one of the highest numbers of lightning strikes in the United States, so NASA spends significant funds on launch protection. Main operations are managed from Launch Complex 39, where the Vertical Assembly Building is located. Two launch pads are 5 km to the east, and an industrial zone with equipment and administrative building is 8 km south. Shuttles were launched from Complex 39, while unmanned vehicles were also launched from the US Space Force base on Cape Canaveral. The center employs approximately 15 thousand staff members and contractors.
