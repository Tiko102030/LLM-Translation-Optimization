All the above mentioned refers only to iterative algorithms for searching neural network solutions. For them it is indeed impossible to guarantee anything and completely automate neural network training. However, alongside with iterative learning algorithms there exist non-iterative algorithms that possess a very high stability and allow fully automating the training process. Checking the adequacy of training

Even in the case of successful training at first sight, the network does not always learn exactly what its creator wanted it to. There is a known case when the network was trained for recognizing tank images from photos, but later it turned out that all tanks were photographed on the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of "learning" to recognize tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what is required of it, but what is easiest to generalize. Quality control of neural network training must be conducted using examples that did not participate in its training. At the same time, the number of test examples should be as high as possible for the quality of training. If a neural network's errors have a probability close to one billionth, then a billion test examples are needed to confirm this probability.
