The launch complex No. 39 with a large infrastructure, including a vertical assembly building and transportation systems, was built for her. From 1967 to 1973, there were 13 launches of the "Saturn V". A tragedy occurred during the program when the crew of Apollo-1 perished. Later, manned flights took place, including Apollo-11, which made history in July 1969 as the first time a human landed on the Moon. The center was later used to launch the Skylab station and missions from the Apollo-Soyuz program. Rockets Titan and Atlas-Centaur were also used for launching satellites and interplanetary vehicles. Until July 2011, the center served as the primary site for the Space Shuttle program launches. The first launch of the shuttle Columbia took place in 1981. A landing strip 4.6 km long is located here as well. After the Challenger disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended.
