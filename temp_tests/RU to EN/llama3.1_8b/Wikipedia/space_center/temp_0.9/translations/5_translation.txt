All of the above applies only to iterative algorithms for searching neural network solutions. For them, indeed, nothing can be guaranteed and it is impossible to completely automate the training of neural networks. However, alongside with iterative training algorithms there exist non-iterative algorithms possessing a very high stability and allowing for the complete automation of the learning process. Checking the adequacy of the training 

Even in case of successful-looking training, the network does not always learn exactly what its creator wanted it to. There is a known case where a network was trained to recognize tank images from photos, but later it turned out that all tanks were photographed on the same background. As a result, the network "learned" to recognize this type of landscape instead of "learning" to recognize tanks. Thus, the network "understands" not what it was required to, but what is easiest to generalize. Testing the quality of neural network training should be conducted using examples that did not participate in its training. At the same time, the number of test examples must be as much as possible higher than the quality of the training. If the errors of a neural network have a probability close to one billionth, then for confirmation of this probability it is necessary to have a billion test examples.
