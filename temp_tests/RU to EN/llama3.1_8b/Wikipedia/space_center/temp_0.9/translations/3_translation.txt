After the Challenger space shuttle disaster in 1986, flights were temporarily suspended. The centre suffered hurricane damage, including hurricanes Francis and Wilma. In 2011, the last shuttle missions took place - Endeavour and Atlantis. After the completion of the shuttle program, a transition to commercial use began. In 2014, launch pad LC-39A was leased to SpaceX for 20 years. It is used for launches of Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets as well as Dragon crew vehicles. The LC-39B pad is being modernized for the SLS rocket and Orion spacecraft. The visitor complex features a museum entertainment area with tours, IMAX theatres and bus excursions around the site. The main exhibit is the Saturn V rocket. A memorial to astronauts called "Space Mirror" is also located on the grounds, where the names of deceased cosmonauts are commemorated.
