American property was valued even less: debts to British creditors were, without much deliberation, annulled; some states, including Virginia, closed court proceedings for lawsuits filed by British subjects (see Eli 1998 p. 35). The widespread confiscation of property from opponents of independence from England did not serve as a good omen for property security in the new republic. Main rebels fought to curb what they saw as an "excess of democracy" (see Tomlins 1993 p. 60). In securing property rights, "a defender of any right" became a concern for the creators of the first constitutions of all states.
