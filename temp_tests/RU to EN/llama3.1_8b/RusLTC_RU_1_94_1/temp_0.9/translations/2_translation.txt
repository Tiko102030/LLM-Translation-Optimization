The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the right to air and water. The Coase article has had a tremendous impact on economic theory, as many economists have abandoned the idea of income distribution and focused on the values of various legal regimes' efficiency. However, there are several questions related to these assertions. Separate consideration of price and income distribution occurs only when income distribution will affect both the level of prices and production structure. This will happen in the case when economic entities have different preferences and when scale returns increase or decrease. In addition, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses related to conducting negotiations and enforcing contracts. This can be extremely important when concluding a trade agreement implying external effects especially, when it often becomes challenging to motivate a large number of people to bribe those polluting the environment. The definition of property rights is often considered in liberal democracy to be quite controversial, much like how in the United States people possess both personal freedom and the majority's right to manage the country (see Horwitz 1992; Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
