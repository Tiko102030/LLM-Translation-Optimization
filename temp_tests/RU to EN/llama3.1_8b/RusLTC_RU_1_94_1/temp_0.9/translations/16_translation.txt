Local authorities have used the right of eminent domain to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. The concept "right of eminent domain," first coined by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, was recorded in English law through John Locke, who placed it as a foundation of "natural rights" as the coincidence of the will of all inhabitants that granted all authority to state legislative bodies (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters have fully expanded the concept based on instrumental considerations alone. Denying any sanctity of private property rights, Vermont's State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property shall be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
