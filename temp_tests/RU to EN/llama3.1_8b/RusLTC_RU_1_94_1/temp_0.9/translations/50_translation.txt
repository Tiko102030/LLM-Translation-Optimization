The court notified the Los Angeles County administration that a "decision depriving an owner of using their property" was necessary in order to give them the right to recover damages. The court's ruling on the case of the First Anglican Church, which was supported by liberal judges Brennan and Marshall, was not based on constitutional principles but rather only confirmed the main points of the Pennsylvania Coal case. However, the same-year case "Nollan v California Coastal Commission" (1987) was a completely different instance because it established a new standard regarding old rules. Thus, owning a coastal property plot located between public parks to the north and south of each other, Nollans wanted to build instead of their dilapidated bungalow a three-bedroom house similar to others in this district.
