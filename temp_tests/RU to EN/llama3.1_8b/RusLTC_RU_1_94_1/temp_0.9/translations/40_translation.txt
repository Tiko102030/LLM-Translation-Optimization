Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania Coal Company

Shortly before hearing Adkins' case, the Supreme Court made another historic decision that seemed to fully cover the protection of property rights. In the case of "Pennsylvania Coal Co. v. Mahon" (1922), the Court declared the Pennsylvania law unconstitutional because this law, aimed at preserving subsoil, restricted coal mining companies. The Court ruled that a general principle for all is that regulation of property can be exercised only to some extent, and if it goes too far, then it becomes confiscation of property.... It remains unclear why human failures or needs will justify shifting the responsibility for damage onto someone else's shoulders.... A strong desire of society to improve its position will not suffice to guarantee this goal is achieved by a more short-term, than constitutional way of avoiding blame.... This is only a question of degree. The aforementioned Pennsylvania Coal case has been cited over 100,000 times in the following seventy-five years and remains a valid law.
