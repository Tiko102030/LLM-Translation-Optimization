Instrumental competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, they should never be an end in themselves. Since some rights must have priority, new conditions are necessary to take into account and competing claims. Some still believe that a return to the discussion of natural rights will occur, but contemporary judicial conservatives have not changed any laws in any way. Changing it would require courts to completely change the legal justification history, according to which property is considered a social institution, not an end in itself. It also required them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
