Holmes claimed that "the government is unlikely to be able to continue its activities in any degree, if the question of property rights is not resolved to some extent". Therefore, judges must carefully evaluate competing interests on a case-by-case basis.

The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ceased during the Great Depression of the 1930s. United around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the New Deal policies, a new generation of liberals promoted state aid to those in need and escaped the crisis through measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights.

Scholars drew public attention to the Supreme Court's 1937 conclusions, even referring to the "constitutional revolution" of that year after two conservative justices resigned when Franklin Delano Roosevelt threatened to "pack" the Supreme Court.
