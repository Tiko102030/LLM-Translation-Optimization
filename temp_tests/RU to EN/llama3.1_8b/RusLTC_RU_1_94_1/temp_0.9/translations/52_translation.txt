But after these liberal icon's retirement in 1990 and 1991, the Court introduced additional limitations on government regulation. For instance, in the case of Lucas v. South Carolina Coastal Council (1992), the Court required a property owner in South Carolina to be compensated for damages to their property following the new Beachfront Management Act's moratorium on construction on their land. Again expressing distrust in elected lawmakers, Scalia warned, as he had in Nollan, that judges should carefully scrutinize regulations to protect against "the risk that private property will be used in public purposes under the guise of reducing serious harm to society." The Court refined its new standards in Dolan v. City of Tigard (1994), where courts were tasked with shifting some of the burden of proof from property owners onto the government.
