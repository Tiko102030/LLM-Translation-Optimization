Citing the Bible, Locke asserts that the same natural law which grants us property also limits its scope. "God gave us all abundantly" - this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But what did He give it to us for? For enjoyment. A man has the right to convert his labor into property up to the point where he can use it for any needs of his life before it deteriorates. And whatever exceeds these limits belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or ruin. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some people to monopolize "unequal and disproportionate ownership of land", possess power over others. Therefore, restrictions on the accumulation of property are necessary to ensure that everyone has access to society's wealth. This part of Locke's theory contributed to discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, which were later taken up by other authors, including Thomas Paine, an American with radical views.
