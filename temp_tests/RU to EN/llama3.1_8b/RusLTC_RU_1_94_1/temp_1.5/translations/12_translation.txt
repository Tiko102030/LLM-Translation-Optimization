Some Americans accumulated property in accordance with John Locke's principle, combining their labor with land and materials. However, most people became wealthy through military victories and looting, following the example of the first Norman lords who followed William to England. White Americans became wealthy by enslaving millions of Africans and using military force to take land from Native American peoples. Even the 1776 War for Independence, a war in the name of liberal ideals, became an example of mass plundering, including forced redistribution of two-thirds of property in New York and its surroundings and most of plots in Queens, Richmond, Kings and Suffolk counties (see Flick 1901, p. 153).
