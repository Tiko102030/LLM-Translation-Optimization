Exceptionalism of American law, sanctity of private property and the power of police
The conditionality or even myth in research on American history suggests that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. To a greater extent thanks to the merits of Louis Harzt and his famous work "Traditions of Liberal America" (1955), the American society is considered a typically liberal one in the classical spirit of J. Locke, nurturing a special passion for individualism and property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to incite workers and other people in the name of achieving socialist and collectivist goals" (see Lippset 1996, p. 69, 95; Friedman 1999).
