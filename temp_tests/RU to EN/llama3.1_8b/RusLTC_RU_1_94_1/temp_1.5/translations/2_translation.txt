The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient result regardless of the distribution of property rights and regardless of who owns the rights to air and water. Coase's article had a tremendous impact on economic theory, as many economists have rejected the idea of income redistribution and focused instead on the values of different legal regimes' efficiency. However, there are several questions related to these assertions. Separate consideration of prices and income distribution only occurs when income distribution affects both price levels and production structure. This will happen in the case where economic agents possess heterogeneous preferences and where returns to scale either increase or decrease. Moreover, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses related to negotiating agreements and enforcing contracts. This can be very important when concluding a trade deal that involves externalities, particularly when it is often difficult to motivate a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. Defining property rights is often viewed as quite controversial in liberal democracies much like the way individuals in the United States possess both personal freedom and the majority's right to govern the country. (See Horwitz 1992, Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895)
