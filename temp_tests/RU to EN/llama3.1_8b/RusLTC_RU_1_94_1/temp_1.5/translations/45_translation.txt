The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when the political support for sacred property rights ended during the Great Depression of the 1930s. By uniting around President Franklin Delano Roosevelt and the policy of the New Course, a new generation of liberals promoted state help for the needy and helped to come out of the crisis with measures of reformed capitalism. Under such pressure even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights.
