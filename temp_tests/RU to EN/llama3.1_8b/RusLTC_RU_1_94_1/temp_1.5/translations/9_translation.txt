Citing the Bible, Locke asserts that the same law of nature that grants us property also limits the scope of this ownership. "God hath given us all things richly" - that is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But why did God give it to us? For our enjoyment. Man has the right to turn his labor into his own property as much as he can use for his necessities before it perishes. And what goes beyond this exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or devastation. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a reserve of wealth allowed some to monopolize "the disproportionate and unequal possession of land", having power over others. Therefore, there must be limitations on property accumulation to ensure that all have access to society's wealth. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to discussions about natural rights as limits on individual accumulation, and other authors later added their own arguments to these lines of reasoning, including the notable American radical thinker Thomas Paine.
