Despite constitutional guarantees of property rights, states continued to redistribute wealth. In reality, the Thirteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution enshrines the principle of asset confiscation in it. Under this amendment, unfettered confiscation of slave owners' properties redistributed nearly $3 billion US, half of the total wealth of Southern states. In connection with the emancipation of slaves in America, one of the most significant examples in history of property confiscation was accomplished. Government intervention into private property matters did not end these confiscations.
