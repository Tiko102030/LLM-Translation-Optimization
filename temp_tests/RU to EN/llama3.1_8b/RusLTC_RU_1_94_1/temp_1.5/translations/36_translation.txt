The bias of judges towards the policy of non-interference by the state in state economy may have been best illustrated by the West Virginia Appellate Court in the case "State of West Virginia v Goodwill" (1889). According to the court decision, a law requiring payment was unconstitutional because the government should not "do for its people what they themselves can do better. Natural laws of supply and demand - this is the best law of trade".
