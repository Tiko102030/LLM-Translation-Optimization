Citing the Bible, Locke argues that the same natural law which gives us property also limits its extent. "God gave us all in abundance" - this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But for what purpose did He give it to us? For enjoyment. Man has the right to turn his labor into his own property as much as he can use for any needs of his life before the object deteriorates. And what exceeds these limits, surpasses his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or ruin. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a reserve of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal ownership of land", possess power over others. Therefore, restrictions on accumulation of property are necessary to ensure that everyone has access to the wealth of society. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, which were later adopted by other authors, including the American radical thinker Thomas Paine.
