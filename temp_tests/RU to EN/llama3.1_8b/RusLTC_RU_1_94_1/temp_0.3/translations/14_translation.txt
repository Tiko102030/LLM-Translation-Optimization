The fears of the conservatives led to the U.S. Constitutional Convention in 1787 limiting the authority of regional governments to intervene in transactions. Later, they added the Fifth Amendment to prevent confiscation of property. The process of confiscating property did not follow due process and provided no compensation (see Raykov 1996, p. 314-5; Eli 1998, pp. 43-54; Jacobs 1954, pp. 4-5).
