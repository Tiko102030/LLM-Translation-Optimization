Citing the Bible, Locke claims that it is the same law of nature that gives us property and at the same time limits the size of this property. "God gave us all abundantly" - this is the voice of reason, confirmed by inspiration. But why did he give it to us? For our enjoyment. A person has a right to turn his labor into property up to what he can use for any necessities of his life before the object decays. What exceeds these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or damage. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a storehouse of wealth allowed some to monopolize "in proportionally and unfairly large land ownership", exercise power over others. Therefore, there are restrictions on accumulating property to guarantee everyone access to the community's wealth. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, which were later elaborated upon by other authors, including the well-known radical American, Thomas Paine.
