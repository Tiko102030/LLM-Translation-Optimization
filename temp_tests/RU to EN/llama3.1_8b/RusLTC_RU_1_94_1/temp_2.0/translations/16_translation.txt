Local government used the power of eminent domain to achieve public objectives at the expense of private property owners. Initially formulated by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, the term "right to compulsory private property" was recorded in English law through J Locke, who placed it as the basis for natural rights, coinciding with the will of the entire population that had granted all legislative powers to state bodies. (see Eagle, 1996, p.8). American judges and interpreters of the law expanded the concept even further solely on instrumental considerations. Denying any private property immunity, Vermont's State Constitution from 1791 stated: "Private property must be subordinated to public use when need requires" (see Eli, 1998, p. 33; author’s notes).
