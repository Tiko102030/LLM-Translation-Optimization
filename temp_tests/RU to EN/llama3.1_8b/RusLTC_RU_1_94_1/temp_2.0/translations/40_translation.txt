The concepts of property, instrumentalism and the New Deal Lochner doctrine could logically be identified based on other court decisions that upheld property rights. In Adkins v. Children's Hospital (1923), the Court invalidated a minimum wage law for the District of Columbia, holding that this law in an unconstitutional way confiscated workers' and businesses' property, infringing upon their "freedom of contract", which must be a common rule and which cannot be taken away. Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr. and Pennsylvania coal

Shortly before hearing Adkins case, Supreme Court made another historic ruling that seemed to fully address protection of property rights. In the Pennsylvania Coal Company v. Mahon (1922) case, the court declared a Pennsylvania law unconstitutional because it limited mining companies' activity for preserving subsoil purposes. The Court held that a general rule would be that property regulation could only be exercised up to a certain degree; if it went too far, then there would already be a taking of property.
