However, on the other hand, it is also considered that the majority has the right to control economic activity and determine public purposes, while protecting property owners from the consequences of external factors caused by polluters. However, a general comparison of these concepts can be understood incorrectly, since these rights largely restrict the rights of others. Property rights give people state-approved privileges for monopolistic management of resources, as well as the opportunity to turn to authorized law enforcement bodies with the aim of limiting the activities of other people. In this way, property essentially limits some benefits due to the fact that government authorities primarily support property owners rather than other citizens. Therefore, it is not a dispute over the principles of natural freedom and justice, but a discussion of property regulation that forces one to choose whose property should be protected and whose property rights have an advantage in fighting for them. The consideration of democratic rights and their regulation has become so incomprehensible due to the fact that legal questions are repeatedly discussed at various levels of government in the structure of the legal system of the United States.
