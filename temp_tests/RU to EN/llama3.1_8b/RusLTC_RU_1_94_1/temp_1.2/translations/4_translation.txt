Also at each level of governance there is a clear division of powers between the executive, legislative and judicial authorities, each of which is intended to respect and ensure compliance with the norms of the Constitution, in which they are interpreted. The harmonization of these differences in interpretation was the cause of the subsequent division between written constitutions, codes of laws and the occasional "common law" that existed in England and was described by several English legal authors. Various sources provide a rich material for judicial interpretation. Oliver Wendell Holmes was not the first to recognize that judges in American legislation are independent players, thanks to various methods of interpreting specific cases. Acting as interpreters of the norms of right, they use their experience to employ law as an instrument for promoting their ideas about an ideal society (see Fisher, Horwitz and Reed 1993). American property rights were established by judges and political figures through civil litigation caused by specific institutions of the US government, legal tradition, and political conflict.
