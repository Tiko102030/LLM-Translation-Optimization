A new approach was neither a general critique of property rights nor an unconditional confirmation of these rights. Instead, through the redistribution of rights it only acknowledged some form of property in comparison to other forms of property. According to one of the historians of law, Willard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the American system of governance - "the liberation of all human creative energy." Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have the opportunity to start projects that are profitable. In theory, if a project's cost was higher than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would increase nonetheless, even if the entrepreneur had to pay out the sum of the damages incurred. However, in practice all the burden falls on the part of the person who suffered the loss, thus only increasing the number of entrepreneurs' projects, with all risks and operational expenses falling on their neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were observed to a greater degree legally, high operating costs would hinder the execution of entrepreneurs' plans. Therefore, by economizing on the cost of risk and operational expenditures, entrepreneurs, this new principle has contributed more significantly to the development of entrepreneurship.
