The market of free trade will lead to the same efficient result regardless of property rights distribution and regardless of who owns the right to air and water. Coase's article had a huge impact on economic theory, causing many economists to abandon the idea of income distribution and focus instead on the values of different legal regimes' efficiency. However, there are several questions associated with these assertions. Separate consideration of price and distribution is only relevant when distribution affects both price levels and production structures. This will happen in cases where economic subjects have different preferences and scale returns either increase or decrease. Furthermore, as Coase himself noted, his theory does not take into account transaction costs or expenses related to negotiation and contract enforcement. This may be very important when concluding a trade agreement that implies external effects particularly often, especially it is also challenging to incentivize a large number of people to bribe those who pollute the environment. Property rights are often considered quite controversial in liberal democracy much like the fact that, in the United States, individuals possess both personal freedom and the right of the majority to govern the country (see Horwitz 1992; Demsetz 1964; Dillon 1895).
