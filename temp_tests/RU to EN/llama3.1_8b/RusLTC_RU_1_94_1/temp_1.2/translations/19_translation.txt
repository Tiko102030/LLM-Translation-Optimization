Local authorities frequently confiscated homes in which immoral activities were conducted, filled with waste the sewers of factories that "posed a danger to public health," banned the production of hazardous chemicals and extinguished fires by demolishing private dwellings and warehouses without compensation. 

Officials gained access to dozens of economic sectors, including healthcare-related ones, such as pharmaceuticals manufacturing and slaughtering livestock, and others of significant economic value unrelated to public health issues, like trade and sale rights (see Novak 1996 p. 84-94). In cases when regulation was unable to achieve its public purpose, officials started going on strike.
