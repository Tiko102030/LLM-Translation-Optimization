Holmes claimed that the government would hardly be able to continue its activity if the property issue was not resolved to some extent. Therefore, judges should carefully evaluate competing interests depending on the specific case. The gradual retreat turned into a general flight when political support for sacred property rights ended during the Great Depression of the 1930s. United around President Franklin D. Roosevelt and New Deal policy, a new generation of liberals promoted helping those in need and getting out of the crisis through reformed capitalism measures. Under such pressure, even the Supreme Court retreated from absolute protection of property rights. Scholars drew attention to the Supreme Court's conclusions from 1937, even mentioning a "constitutional revolution of 1937" after two conservative judges resigned when Franklin D. Roosevelt threatened that he would "pack" the Supreme Court.
