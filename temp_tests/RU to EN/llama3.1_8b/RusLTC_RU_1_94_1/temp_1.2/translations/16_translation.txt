Local authorities used the power of eminent domain to achieve public objectives at the expense of private property owners. The term "power of eminent domain" was first coined by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625 and recorded in English law by John Locke, who placed it as a foundation for "natural rights," as coinciding with the will of the entire population that gave all legislative authority to state legislatures (see Egl 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters expanded on this concept solely based on practical considerations. Denying any immunity from private property ownership, Vermont's State Constitution in 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when the need requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author’s notes).
