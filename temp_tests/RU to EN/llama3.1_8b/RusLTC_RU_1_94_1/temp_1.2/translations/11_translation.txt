The peculiarity of American law, the sanctity of property, and police powers Exceptionalism or rather a myth in research into American history suggests that the United States is a country uniquely devoted to private rights and market freedom. In large measure thanks to the merits of Louis Hartz and his famous work "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1955), the American society is considered typically liberal in the classical spirit of J. Locke, fueling a special passion for individualism and property rights. This national ideal sanctifies private property and individual initiative, undermining "efforts to encourage workers and other people towards achieving socialist and collectivist goals."
