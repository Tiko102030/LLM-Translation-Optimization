Iron processing and steel industry, with favorable tariffs on their products, was able to provide the developing network of railroads. At the top of the entire financial pyramid was a recently centralized banking system that had been founded in New York during wartime (see Benzell 1984, 1990). The enraged workers and farmers saw through the true intentions of the bankers and owners of iron processing and railroad companies whose properties were sponsored by government agencies. They wanted new laws that would expand public authority in regulating property rights. They achieved a remarkable success in enacting legislation extending the eight-hour workday law and legislation setting rail tariffs.
