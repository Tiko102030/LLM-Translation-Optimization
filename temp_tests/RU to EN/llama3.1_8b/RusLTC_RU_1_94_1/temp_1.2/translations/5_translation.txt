New paradigm of property recognizes various definitions of property, however, still considers "property rights" as relations between people and things (see Barzel 1997; Leibreich 1989). Thus, property rights exist independently of other social circumstances. As a relation between people and things, property exists without human society; for Robinson Crusoe has also property rights too. Like the relations between people and things they create, property acquires moral legitimacy and economic value because it is a reward for the work that forms the non-human material world.
