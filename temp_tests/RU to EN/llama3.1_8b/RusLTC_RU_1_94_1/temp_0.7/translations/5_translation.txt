New paradigm of property rights recognizes various definitions of ownership, however, still considers "property rights" as relations between people and things (see Barzel 1997; Leibhach 1989). Thus, property rights exist independently of other social circumstances. As a relation between people and things, property exists without human society; indeed, Robinson Crusoe also has property rights. Like the relations between people and things they create, property gains moral legitimacy and economic significance because it is a reward for labor that forms the non-human material world.
