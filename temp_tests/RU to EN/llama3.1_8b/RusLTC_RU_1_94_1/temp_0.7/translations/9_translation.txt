The same natural law that gives us property in this way also limits the extent of our ownership. "God gave us all abundance" - that is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But what did he give it to us for? For enjoyment. A person has the right to turn his labor into property as much as he can use it for any needs of his life, before this thing becomes perished. And what goes beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or devastation. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "the disproportionate and unfair possession of land", possess power over others. Therefore, limitations on property accumulation are necessary in order to ensure everyone access to the society's wealth. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to discussions about natural rights as restrictions on individual accumulation, which were later taken up by other authors, including the well-known American radical Thomas Paine.
