Instrumental competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, they must never become an end in themselves. Because some rights have to take precedence, new conditions are needed to consider and conflicting claims. Some still believe that there will be a return to the discussion of natural rights, but the modern society of judicial conservatives has not changed US legislation in any substantial way. It would require courts to completely change the history of legal justification according to which property is considered a social institution, not an end in itself. It would also require them to elevate abstract property above the needs of living people.
