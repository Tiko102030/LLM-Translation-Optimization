Local authorities used the right to compulsory expropriation of private property to achieve public goals at the expense of private property owners. Initially coined by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, the term "right to compulsory expropriation of private property" was recorded in English law with J. Locke, who put it on the basis of "natural law", as a coincidence of the will of the entire population, which granted all powers to state legislative bodies (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and legal interpreters further expanded the concept solely based on instrumental considerations. Denying any sanctity of private property rights, the Vermont State Constitution of 1791 stated: "Private property must be subject to public use when necessity requires it" (see Eli 1998, p. 33; author's notes).
