The concepts of property, instrumentalism, and the New Deal Lochnor program can be logically inferred from other court decisions that upheld property rights. In the case of "Adkins vs The Children's Hospital" (1923), the Court overturned the Minimum Wage Act for the District of Columbia, ruling that this law unconstitutionally confiscated the property of workers and firms, violating their "freedom of contract," which must be a "common rule" and cannot be taken away. Oliver Wendell Holmes and Pennsylvania Coal

Just before considering Adkins' case, the Supreme Court made another historic decision that seemed to completely cover the protection of property rights. In the case of "Pennsylvania Coal Company vs Mahon" (1922), the Court declared the Pennsylvania statute unconstitutional because this law, aimed at preserving subsoil, limited the activities of coal mining companies. The Court ruled that a general rule for all is that regulation of property can be carried out only to some extent, but if it goes too far, then it will pass into confiscation.... It remains unclear why human failures or needs would justify shifting responsibility for damage to someone else's shoulders.... and the strong desire of society to improve its situation will not suffice to guarantee achieving this goal by a shorter, than constitutional, means of buying off an accusation.... This is just a question of degree.
