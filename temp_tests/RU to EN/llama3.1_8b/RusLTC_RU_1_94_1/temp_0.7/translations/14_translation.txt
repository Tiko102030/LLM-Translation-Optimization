The fears of the conservatives led to the U.S. Constitutional Convention (1787) limiting the power of regional governments to intervene in transactions. Later, the Fifth Amendment was added to prevent confiscation of property. The process of seizing property did not involve due process and offered no compensation (see Raykov 1996, p. 314-5; Eli 1998, p. 43-54; Jacobs 1954, p. 4-5).
