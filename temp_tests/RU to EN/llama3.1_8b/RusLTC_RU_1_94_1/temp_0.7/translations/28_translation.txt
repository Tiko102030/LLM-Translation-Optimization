A new approach was not a general criticism of property rights or an unconditional affirmation of these rights. Instead, through redistribution of the rights it only asserted some property in comparison to another. According to one of the historians of law, Villard Hall, this approach is one of the working principles of the US legal order - "the liberation of all human creative energy". Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have the opportunity to implement profitable projects. In theory, if a project's cost was higher than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase even if the entrepreneur had to pay for the damage caused. However, in practice all the burden is shifted onto the plaintiff's side, which only contributes more to the number of entrepreneurs' projects, while all the risk and operational costs are borne by the neighbors. If property rights were observed to a greater legal extent, high operational costs would have hindered the implementation of entrepreneur's plans. Thus, with cost savings on risks and operational costs for entrepreneurs, this new principle only contributed more to the development of entrepreneurship.
