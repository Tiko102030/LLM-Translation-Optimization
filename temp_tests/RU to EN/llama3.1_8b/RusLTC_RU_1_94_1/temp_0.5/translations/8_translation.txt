A certain form of property as an instrument may be convenient, but it will never be necessary. However, Locke does not defend property rights as resolutely as one might understand from the well-known excerpt above (see Horn 1990; Katzelson 1998, p. 77). In this excerpt, he addresses the question of the right of English Stuart monarchs to levy taxes without Parliament's consent. But besides this particular issue, Locke recognizes a social definition of property, according to which property rights must be limited by other natural rights. Locke said that each person should have as much as they can use.
