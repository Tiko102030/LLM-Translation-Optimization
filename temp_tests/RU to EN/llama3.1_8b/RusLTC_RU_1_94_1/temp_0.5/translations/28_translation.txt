A new approach was not a general critique of property rights or a general confirmation of these rights. Instead, through the redistribution of rights it only asserted some property in comparison to other property. According to one of the historians of law, Wilard Hurst, this approach is one of the working principles of the United States' legal order - "the release of all human creative energy." Despite the fact that the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have the opportunity to start projects that bring profit. In theory, if a project's cost was higher than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would still increase even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage caused. However, in practice all the burden is shifted to the injured party, which only contributes to an even greater increase in entrepreneurs' projects and puts all the risk and operational costs on neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were observed at a higher legal level, high operational costs would have hindered entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by economizing on cost of risk and operational expenses for entrepreneurs, this new principle has only contributed to an even greater development of entrepreneurship.
