Citing the Bible, Locke asserts that the same natural law which gives us property through this process also limits its extent. "God gave us all in abundance" - this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But for what purpose did He give it to us? For enjoyment. A man has the right to turn his labor into property as much as he can use for any necessities of his life, before that thing will perish. And anything beyond these limits exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or ruin. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "disproportionate and unequal ownership of land", exercise power over others. Therefore, restrictions on property accumulation are necessary to guarantee access to the wealth of society for all. This part of Locke's theory of property contributed to discussions about natural rights as limits on individual accumulation, which were later adopted by other authors, including the well-known American radical Thomas Paine.
