It appears that the underlying views of J. Locke regarding absolute property rights are at the basis of this case. The Mann case was also mentioned in this case, and it was decided by Chief Justice Waite. However, attention should be drawn to the last statement considered earlier: "it is only a question of degree." This limitation written by Judge Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr. once again consciously re-established an instrumental view of property rights that declared unconstitutional fifty years of previous judicial practice in the Lochner case. 

Holmes had already expressed his opinion on this matter. Beginning his speech with the statement that "the decision was made according to the principles of economic theory, which however does not please a large part of the population," he added: The right of each individual to act as they see fit for as long as they wish, provided that the rights of others are not violated, does not correspond to the basic postulates of legal schools, postal management, laws of any state or municipal institution that take their money to achieve goals that seem necessary regardless of whether he wants them or not. The Fourteenth Amendment does not take into account Mr. Herbert Spencer's Social Statics. Unlike Lochner who despoticly defends intensive property rights, Holmes considered competing interests require a balance to be achieved.
