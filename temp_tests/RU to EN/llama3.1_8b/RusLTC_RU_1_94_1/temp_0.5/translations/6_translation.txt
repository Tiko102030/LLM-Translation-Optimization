There exists an "undisputable" right of property to the worker because: Although the land and all lower beings belong to all people in common, yet... the labor of his body and the work of his hands are owed to him by strict account. What he then extracts from that state which nature has created and preserved is combined with his labor and joined to something belonging personally to him, and thereby makes it his property... Since this labor is an undisputable property of the worker, no one else can have rights to what he once joined to it, at least in those cases where sufficient quantities and qualities remain for others' common use.
