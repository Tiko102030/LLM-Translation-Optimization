Calder v. Bull was a landmark case in which the Supreme Court of the United States established the principle that legislative immunity protects state legislators from lawsuits for actions taken while performing their official duties. The case involved a dispute between Thomas Calder, who claimed to have suffered damage to his home as a result of repairs to a street in Boston, and the State of Massachusetts. Calder's attorney cited English common law precedents and principles in an effort to secure compensation, but the defense rejected these arguments, citing the need for road maintenance due to circumstances specific to America, saying that if such a suit were allowed "then we must examine into the correctness of every highway in our country."
