However, after these liberal icons retired in 1990 and 1991, the Court introduced additional restrictions on state regulation. For example, in Lucas v. South Carolina Coastal Council (1992), the Court required the owner of property in South Carolina to be compensated for damage to his property caused by a new "Coastal Zone Management Act" that suspended construction on his land. Again expressing distrust in elected lawmakers, Scalia warned, as he had in Nollan, that judges must carefully scrutinize ordinances to protect against the risk that private property will be used in public purposes under the guise of reducing serious harm to society.
