The U.S. Supreme Court did not rush to criticize state regulation, but Judge Stephen Field questioned ideas about police powers as early as 1872 in the case "New Orleans Butchers' Benevolent Association v. Crescent City Livestock Landing & Slaughter-House Co." The case arose after Louisiana's legislative body had restricted cattle slaughter in New Orleans. Since public health was not threatened, this law was similar to ordinances prescribed earlier in many other cities.
