Considering the boundaries of public authority in a democratic society more closely, Waite acknowledged several limitations on state regulation. "You can get compensation for using regulated property," and Waite argued that the final decision on compensation should be made by the legislative body. "Any law is assumed to be constitutional," he said. Judges cannot declare which laws are unconstitutional, unless it's clear, of course. If there is doubt, then the expressed will of the legislative body must be supported by a majority."
