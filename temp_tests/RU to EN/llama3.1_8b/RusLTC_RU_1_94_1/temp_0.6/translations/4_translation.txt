Also at each level of governance there is a clear division of power between the executive, legislative and judicial branches, each of which is intended to respect and ensure compliance with the norms of the Constitution in which they are also interpreted. The reconciliation of these differences in interpretation was the cause of the subsequent division of written constitutions, codes of law and sometimes legally effective "Common Law" that emerged in England and was described by several English legal scholars. Different sources offer a rich material for judicial interpretation. Oliver Wendell Holmes was not the first to recognize that judges in American legislation are independent players, and this is all due to various ways of interpreting one or another case. As interpreters of norms of law, they use their experience to use law as an instrument to promote their ideas about an ideal society (see Fisher, Horwitz and Reed 1993). American property rights were established by judges and politicians through civil proceedings caused by specific institutions of the American government, legal tradition, and political conflict.
