New property paradigm acknowledges various definitions of ownership, however, still considers "property rights" as relationships between people and objects (see Barzel 1997; Liebhart 1989). Thus, property rights exist independently of other social circumstances. As a relationship between people and objects, property exists without human society; for Robinson Crusoe also has property rights. Like the relationships between people and objects they create, property acquires moral legitimacy and economic value because it is a reward for labor that shapes the non-human material world.
