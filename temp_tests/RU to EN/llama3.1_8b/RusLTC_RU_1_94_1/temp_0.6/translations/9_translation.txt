Citing the Bible, Locke argues that the same natural law which gives us property also limits its extent. "God has given us all in abundance" - this is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But for what purpose did he give it to us? For our enjoyment. Man has the right to turn his labor into property up to the point at which it can be used for some necessity of his life, before the object deteriorates. What goes beyond that limit exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or decay. However, to Locke's dismay, the invention of money as a store of wealth allowed some to monopolize "unequal and disproportionate ownership of land," acquire power over others. Therefore, restrictions on property accumulation are necessary to ensure that everyone has access to the society's wealth. This part of Locke's property theory contributed to discussions about natural rights as limitations on individual accumulation, which were later developed by other authors, including Thomas Paine, an American with radical views.
