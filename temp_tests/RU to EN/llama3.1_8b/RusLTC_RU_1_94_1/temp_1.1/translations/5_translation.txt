New property paradigm recognizes various definitions of ownership, yet it still considers "property rights" as relations between people and things (see Barzel 1997; Leibknecht 1989). Thus, property rights exist independently of other social circumstances. As a relation between people and things, property exists without human society; for Robinson Crusoe also has property rights. Like the relations between people and things they create, property acquires moral legitimacy and economic value because it is a reward for the labor that shapes the non-human material world.
