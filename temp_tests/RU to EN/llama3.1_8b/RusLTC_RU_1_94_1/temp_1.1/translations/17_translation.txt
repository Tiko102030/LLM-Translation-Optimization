The Supreme Court of the United States also indicated that the right to compulsory expropriation of private property is based on public benefit and instrumental considerations, and in the case of 1875 "Cohens v. United States" it ruled that the right to compulsory expropriation of private property is "a result of political necessity, and it cannot be separated from supreme power". The right to compulsory expropriation of private property was one example of the use of government authority to promote the development of a "well-organized society," to improve the quality of the "public good" or "welfare" by means of legislation concerning interventions into property rights.
