According to the court ruling: "The landowner has no absolute and unlimited right to alter the natural composition of the soil in order to use it for purposes that infringe on the rights of other people, and which could not be achieved by its initial composition." The court acknowledged that zoning rules for environmental protection "should be reasonable", but "we believe that preventing violations of public rights through the introduction of restrictions on private property in its original uses is justified". There was no "taking" of property as there had been in Pennsylvania Coal Co. This was because "using property without changing its natural soil composition is permitted by law". Property rights advocates achieved greater success in litigation regarding "takings" after Ronald Reagan became President in 1980, headed by Reagan's attorney, Antonin Scalia.
