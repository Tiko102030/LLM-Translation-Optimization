Instrumental, competing "rights" to property can never be absolute, nor can they ever be an end in themselves. Because some rights must take precedence, new conditions are necessary to consider and weigh competing claims. Some still believe that a return to the discussion of natural rights will occur, but contemporary judicial conservatives have not changed US law in any fundamental way. It would require courts to completely reverse their legal pedigree, according to which property is considered a social institution rather than an end in itself.
