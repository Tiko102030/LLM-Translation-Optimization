Property rights in the United States are considered a way of manifesting an individual's personal identity as owner; on the other hand, controlling economic activity and determining public goals for the benefit of owners of property and protection from external influence factors (polluters) are considered essential aspects of majority rule. However, it is also necessary to understand how property rights can restrict the rights of others by granting state-authorized monopolies over resources and giving some individuals an advantage in enforcing their rights against other people.
