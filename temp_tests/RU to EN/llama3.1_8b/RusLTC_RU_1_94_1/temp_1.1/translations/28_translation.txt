A new approach was not a general criticism of property rights nor a general affirmation of these rights. Instead, by redistributing rights it only asserted some property in comparison to other property. According to one of the historians of law, Villard Hall, this approach is one of the working principles of the order of law of the United States of America - "freeing all human creative energy". Although the burden of paying compensation to neighbors for indirect losses remains on entrepreneurs' shoulders, they still have an opportunity to start projects that bring profits. In theory, if a project's cost was higher than the amount of damage caused, its profitability would increase anyway, even if the entrepreneur had to pay the amount of damage suffered by the other party. However, in practice all the burden is shifted to the plaintiff's side, which only adds more significantly to an increase in the number of entrepreneurs' projects, and the entire risk and operational costs fall on neighbors' shoulders. If property rights were observed to a greater extent legally, high operational costs would hinder the execution of entrepreneurs' plans. Thus, by saving on cost of risk and operational expenses entrepreneurs' new principle only contributed more significantly to the development of entrepreneurship.
