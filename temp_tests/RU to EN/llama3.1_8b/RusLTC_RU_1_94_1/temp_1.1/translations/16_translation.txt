Local authorities have used the right of eminent domain to achieve public purposes at the expense of private property owners. The concept "the right of eminent domain" was first coined by Dutch scholar Hugo Grotius in 1625, and then written into English law through John Locke, who placed it on a foundation of "natural law", which equated the will of all citizens to having given all powers to the state legislative bodies (see Eagle 1996, p. 8). American judges and jurists expanded the concept merely for instrumental reasons, denying any respect for private property rights altogether.
