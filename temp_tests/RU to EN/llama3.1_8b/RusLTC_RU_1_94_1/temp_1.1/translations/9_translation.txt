Citing the Bible, Locke argues that the same natural law that grants us property through labor also limits its scope. "God gave us all in abundance" is the voice of reason confirmed by inspiration. But what was given to us for? For enjoyment. Man has a right to turn his labor into his property as much as he can consume during his life, before it deteriorates. Anything that exceeds these bounds exceeds his share and belongs to others. Nothing was created by God for destruction or ruin. However, unfortunately for Locke, the invention of money as a reserve of wealth allowed some to monopolize "an excessive and disproportionate holding of land", gaining power over others. Therefore, limits on property accumulation are necessary to ensure all have access to society's wealth. This part of Locke's property theory contributed to discussions about natural rights as restrictions on individual accumulation, which were later developed by other authors, including the American radical thinker Thomas Paine.
