I will bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep amazement and a dull meaningless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not in delirium," he thought, "it seems this is really...". After two minutes, Nastasya turned back with soup and announced that now tea would also be served. Alongside the soup came two spoons, two plates and all the utensils: a saltshaker, a peppermill, mustard for beef and other things that had not been seen in such an order for a long time. The tablecloth was clean. "Not bad, Nastasya," I said, "if Praskovya Pavlovna ordered two bottles of beer. We'll drink it." "Oh, you're quite the connoisseur!" Nastasya muttered and went to carry out my order.
