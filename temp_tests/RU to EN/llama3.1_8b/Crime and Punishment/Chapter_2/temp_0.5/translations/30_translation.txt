Ilya Petrovich! — the clerk began to say carefully, but stopped to wait for a moment, because you can't hold back a boiling officer except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the lush lady, at first she trembled violently from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and fierce her curses became, the more charming her appearance became, the more enchanting was her smile directed to the stern ensign. She was hopping on one foot and incessantly squatting, impatiently waiting for someone to finally let her say something, and she waited. — There will be no noise or fight at my place, Mr. Captain, she suddenly babbled like a burst of beans with a strong German accent, although speaking quickly in Russian, — and there won't be any scandal, and they came drunk, and I'll tell you all about it, Mr. Captain, but I'm not to blame... My home is noble, Mr. Captain, and my behavior is noble, Mr. Captain, and I always didn't want any scandal. They completely came drunk and then asked three times for a drink, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his feet, which is very unpleasant in a noble house, and he's breaking the piano and there's no manner at all here, and I said so. And he took a pipe and pushed everyone behind him with it. And then as soon as I quickly called the porter and Karl came, he grabbed Karl and pinched his eye, and Henriette too pinched her eye, but they hit me five times on the cheek. It's not delicate in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I screamed. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood in it like a little pig, squealing; and that's shameful.
