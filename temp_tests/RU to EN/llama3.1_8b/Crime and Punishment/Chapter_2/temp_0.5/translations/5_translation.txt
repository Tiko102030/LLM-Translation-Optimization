Fragments of torn fabric would never arouse any suspicion; it seemed so, seemed so! he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a painful attention began to search around again, on the floor and everywhere, had he forgotten anything else? The certainty that everything – even memory, even simple reasoning – left him was starting to torment him unbearably. "What if it's really beginning, what if this is actually the execution?" he exclaimed, gazing around. Indeed, there were scraps of braid from his pants lying on the floor in the middle of the room, so that anyone would see them first! "What's happening to me!" he cried out again like a lost man. Then a strange idea came into his head: what if all his clothes are covered in blood, what if there are many stains, but he just can't see or notice them because his reasoning has weakened, is fragmented... his mind is clouded... Suddenly he remembered that the wallet had also been bloody. "Ah! So it means there must be blood in the pocket too, because I put a wet wallet in my pocket then!" He suddenly turned out his pocket and – just as he thought – on the lining of the pocket there were stains, spots! "So it means I haven't lost all reason completely, so there is still reasoning and memory, since I even remembered and figured it out myself!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep breath with his whole chest. "Just a weak attack of fever, just a minute's delirium," he thought, and he ripped the lining out of the left pocket of his pants.
