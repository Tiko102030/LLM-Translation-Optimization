He didn't even have matches. No, it would be better to go out somewhere and throw everything away. Yes! Better throw it all away! — he repeated as he sat down on the couch again — right now, this very minute, no delay... But instead of that his head leaned back onto the pillow once more; a unbearable chill seized him again; he put on his greatcoat once more. And for a long time, several hours, it kept haunting him in fits and starts that "if only I could go somewhere right now, without putting it off, and throw everything away so it would be gone, finally, quickly!" He tried to get up from the couch several times; he wanted to stand up but couldn't anymore. A strong knock at the door finally woke him up. — Open up! Are you alive or not? And all day long, like a dog, he's sleeping! — Nastasya shouted, knocking on the door with her fist — whole daytime days, as if nothing's wrong, sleeping!
