His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "It's a trick! They're trying to lure me in with tricks and then knock me out of it," he thought to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "What's wrong is that I'm almost out of my mind... I can lie about some nonsense..."

On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings scattered around - "and now they'll probably search without me", he recalled and stopped. But such desperation and cynicism took hold of him, as if it was a fatal blow, that he waved his hand in dismissal and continued on.

"Just get it over with!"

Back out on the street, the heat was unbearable once again; just one drop of rain would have been welcome these past few days. Again came the dust, brick, and plaster; again the stench from the shops and bars; again the drunkards, the Chuvash peddlers, and the half-ruined cab drivers. The sun blazed in his eyes so intensely that it hurt to look at it, and his head spun completely around - a familiar feeling of someone coming out into bright sunlight after being ill with fever. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked anxiously down it, towards that house... and immediately turned away.

"If they ask me, I might just say so," he thought as he approached the office building.
