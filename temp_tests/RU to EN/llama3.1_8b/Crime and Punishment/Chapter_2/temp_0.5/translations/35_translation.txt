Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but anyway! It's well known that gunpowder can't withstand insults. You probably got annoyed with him too and couldn't hold back, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with courtesy - but it's pointless: he's the most insignificant person, I'm telling you, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He burst, boiled, burned out - and that's it! And as a result, only gold in his heart remained! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-gunpowder"...

Oh, what a great regiment was! exclaimed Il'ya Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so pleasantly tickled, but still half-awake. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to all of them. - Please forgive me, Captain, began he quite freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich unexpectedly, - take an interest in my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if on my part there was any fault.
