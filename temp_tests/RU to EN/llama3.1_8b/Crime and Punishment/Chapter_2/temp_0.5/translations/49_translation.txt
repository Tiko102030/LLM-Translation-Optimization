He was becoming extremely absent-minded and forgetful and knew it himself. Resolutely he had to hurry! He went towards the Neva on V - Boulevard; but on his way a sudden thought came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into water? Wouldn't it be better to leave somewhere very far away, again perhaps to the Islands, and there in some deserted place, in the woods, under a bush - bury everything and the tree, perhaps notice it?" And although he felt that he was not capable of discussing everything clearly and sensibly at this moment, but the thought seemed faultless to him. But he wasn't destined for the Islands either, and something else happened: coming out onto the square from V - Boulevard, he suddenly saw a gate leading into a courtyard on the left, completely surrounded by walls. To the right, immediately after entering through the gates, far into the courtyard stretched a blank unbegun wall of a neighboring four-story house. Left, parallel to the blind wall and also now from the gates, went a wooden fence, twenty steps deep into the courtyard, and then it made a turn to the left. This was a secluded enclosed place where some materials lay. Further, in the depression of the courtyard, a corner of a low, blackened stone shed peered out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
