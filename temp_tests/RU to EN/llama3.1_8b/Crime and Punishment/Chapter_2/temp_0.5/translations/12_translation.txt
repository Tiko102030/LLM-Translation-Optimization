He didn't respond and held a piece of paper in his hand, not printing it out. — Yes, don't get up, Nastasya continued to pity him and see that he was putting his feet on the couch. — Sick, so don't go: you won't burn yourself. What's he got in his hands? He looked: in his right hand there were cut-off pieces of braid, a sock, and scraps torn from a pocket. And he slept with them. Later, thinking about it, he remembered that, half-asleep in the heat, he tightly clenched everything in his hand and fell asleep again. — And look at all these rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, exactly like a treasure... — Nastasya let out her nervously sick laugh. In an instant he stuck it all under his greatcoat and intensely stared at it with his eyes. Even though he could only barely understand what was happening in that moment, he felt that people wouldn't be treating him like this when they came to take him away.
