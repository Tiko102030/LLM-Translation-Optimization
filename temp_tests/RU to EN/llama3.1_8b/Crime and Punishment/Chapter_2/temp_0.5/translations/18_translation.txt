The air was stifling. Sweepers went up and down with notebooks under their arms, haggard-looking people of both sexes - visitors. The door to the inner office was also wide open. He entered and stopped in the entrance hall. Everyone stood there waiting for some men. Here too the air was stifling, and besides, a wave of nausea hit his nose from fresh, still wet paint on rooms that had just been repainted with rancid linseed oil. After waiting a bit, he thought it was time to move forward again, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A terrible impatience drove him further and further on. Nobody noticed him.
