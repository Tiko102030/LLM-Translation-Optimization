"Yes, but she is my mistress, isn't she?" "So what's the matter with your mistress?" The clerk looked at him with a condescending smile of pity and triumph, as if he were a novice who had just been bombarded: "How do you feel now, I suppose?" But what did it matter to him now about the loaned letter, about the debt? Was it worth any anxiety, in turn, even the slightest attention! He stood, read, listened, replied, and even asked questions himself, but all this was automatic. The triumph of self-preservation, the salvation from crushing danger - that's what filled his entire being at this moment, without forethought, without analysis, without future speculations and riddles, without doubts or questions. This was a moment of complete, direct, purely animal joy. But in that very same moment, something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by the impertinence, still burning with anger and clearly wanting to support the injured pride, attacked the unfortunate "puffy lady" with all his might, who had been looking at him since he entered with a pitifully foolish smile. - And you, such-and-such and so-and-so, he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the tragic lady had already left), what happened to you there last night?
