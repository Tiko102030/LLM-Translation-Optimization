The plump lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and began to squat down with a particular kind of excitement; but the officer paid no attention to her whatsoever, and she was no longer able to sit next to him. This was a lieutenant, assistant supervisor, with reddish whiskers that stood out horizontally on both sides of his face and extremely small facial features that expressed nothing in particular except for a certain boldness. He glanced at Raskolnikov askance and somewhat with indignation: Raskolnikov's suit was too shabby, and despite the humiliation he still didn't have the right posture; Raskolnikov looked at him straight on by accident, for too long, so that the lieutenant even took offense. — What do you want? — he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin wouldn't even try to duck from his lightning-fast glance. — They demanded... according to the summons... — Raskolnikov replied somehow awkwardly. — This is about collecting money from them, from the student, — the clerk hastened, tearing himself away from the papers. — Here! — and he handed Raskolnikov a notebook, pointing out the place in it, — read!
