"Is this how they hide things? My mind is truly leaving me!" He collapsed onto the sofa in exhaustion and was immediately seized by an unbearable chill. Mechanically, he pulled over his old student winter coat, which was warm but already almost in tatters, and covered himself with it; sleep and delirium seized him once more. He dozed off. No more than five minutes later, he leapt up again and, in a state of frenzy, rushed back to his clothes. "How could I fall asleep without doing anything? It's true, it's true: the noose is still under my arm! I forgot, I completely forgot about this! Such evidence!" He pulled off the noose and quickly began to tear it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in his bedding. "Shreds of torn fabric will never arouse suspicion; it seems so, it seems so," he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with intense attention began to search around again, on the floor and everywhere, if he hadn't forgotten anything else?
