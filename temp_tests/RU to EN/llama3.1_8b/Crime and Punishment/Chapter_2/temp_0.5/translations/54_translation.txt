Not what I thought at all..."

He suddenly stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question simultaneously threw him off balance and bitterly amazed him:

"If indeed everything was done consciously, not carelessly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how did you manage to not even glance into the wallet and not know what you got, which is why you've been suffering all this time and deliberately went for such a vile, disgusting, low act? You were just about to throw the wallet with everything in it into the water, together with all the other things you haven't seen yet... How is that?"

Yes, that's right; that's all true. He, however, knew that before, and this wasn't even a new question for him; and when it was decided to throw everything into the water at night, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, just as if that was how it had to be, as if it couldn't be otherwise... Yes, he knew all that and remembered it; perhaps it was almost decided yesterday itself, in that very moment when he sat on top of the trunk and rummaged through the cases... And so! "It's because I'm extremely ill," he finally resolved gloomily, "I've tormented myself and tortured myself, and I don't even know what I'm doing... And yesterday, and two days ago, and all this time I've been tormenting myself... I'll recover and... won't torment myself anymore... But if I don't recover at all? God! How tired I am of it all..."

He kept walking without stopping. He terribly wanted to somehow distract himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to try. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking hold of him more and more with almost each passing minute: it was a boundless, almost physical revulsion towards everything that met his eyes and surrounded him, persistent, malicious, hateful. All the people he met were disgusting to him – their faces, gait, movements were disgusting. He would have just thrown up on someone if they had spoken to him...
