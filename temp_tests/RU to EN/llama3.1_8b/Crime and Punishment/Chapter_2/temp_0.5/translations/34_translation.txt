Louisa Ivanovna with a benevolent smile began to squat on all sides and, squatting, reached the doors; but in the doorway she bumped her back into one of the prominent officers, with an open fresh face and with magnificent thick fair mustaches. This was none other than Nikodim Fomich, the quarter-master overseer. Louisa Ivanovna hastened to squat almost up to the floor and with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. — Oh, again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — said Nikodim Fomich kindly and affably to Ilya Petrovich, — again they have agitated my heart, again it has boiled over! Even from the staircase he heard it. — What? — with noble nonchalance replied Ilya Petrovich (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!"), moving with some papers to another table and artistically swinging his shoulders with each step; wherever he stepped, there his shoulder was; — here you see, if you please: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, does not pay money, gave out bills of exchange, does not clean the apartment, endless complaints are submitted against them, and they have taken it upon themselves to make a complaint, that I lit a cigarette among them! They themselves are complaining, but look at them: here they are in their most attractive appearance now. — Poverty is not a vice, comrade, well, what's done is done!
