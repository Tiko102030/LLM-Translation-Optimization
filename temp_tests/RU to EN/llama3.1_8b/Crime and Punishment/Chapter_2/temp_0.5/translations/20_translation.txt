This was some sort of particularly disheveled man with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You can't learn anything from him because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, — said the scribe and pointed forward with his finger, showing the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), cramped and tightly packed with people – common folk, somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Between the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at a table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and red-faced, spotted, visible woman, and what's more, lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest, the size of a tea saucer, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. He glanced briefly at it, said: "wait" and continued working with the lady in mourning. He breathed more freely.
