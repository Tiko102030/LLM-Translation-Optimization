And suddenly, to his greatest astonishment, he caught the sound of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming and sobbing, rushing, hurrying, spewing out words that couldn't be made out, begging for something - certainly for them to stop beating her, because she was being beaten mercilessly on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrible from malice and fury that it could only rasp, but still the beater too was saying something, and just as quickly, incoherently, rushing and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! Ilya Petrovich is here and beating the landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, banging her head against the step - that's clear, that can be heard from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is happening? Has the light turned upside down? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, rising, knocking, slamming doors, rushing out. "But why, why, and how can this be?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had completely gone mad. But no, he is hearing it too clearly!
