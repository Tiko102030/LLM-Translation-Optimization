He would have simply spat on someone and bitten them if anyone had spoken to him...

He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "This is where he lives in that house - but how did I come here myself? The same old story as before... But it's very interesting: was I coming to see him or just happened to stumble upon his place by chance? Doesn't matter; I said I'd come the day after tomorrow, so I'll go. As if I couldn't possibly drop in now..."

He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor apartment. He was at home, in his own little room, and was writing at that moment, and he himself let him in. It had been four months since they'd last seen each other. Razumikhin sat in a tattered dressing gown, with slippers on one foot, disheveled, unshaven, and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
