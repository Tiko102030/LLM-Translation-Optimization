The sun was shining brightly, and he quickly glanced over himself from head to toe, examining his clothes for any signs of anything out of the ordinary. But it couldn't be done like that: shivering with cold, he took everything off and began to inspect him all over again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there seemed to be nothing, no signs of anything; only on the part where his pants had torn at the bottom and hung in fringes, there remained thick stains of dried blood on the fringe itself. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. There was probably nothing more now. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and things he'd pulled out from the old woman's trunk were still all in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out yet, let alone hide them! He hadn't even thought of them as he inspected his clothes! What is this?
