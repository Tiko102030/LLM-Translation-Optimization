A small depression formed under the stone; he immediately started throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and yet there was still room for more. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one rotation to its original side, and it fit perfectly back in its original place, except that it seemed a bit higher. But he pushed some earth under it and pressed down with his foot on both sides. Nothing could be seen. He then went out and headed towards the square. Once again, a strong, barely bearable joy, just like yesterday in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are buried! And who would ever think to look underneath this stone? It might have been lying here since the house was built and will lie there for just as long."
