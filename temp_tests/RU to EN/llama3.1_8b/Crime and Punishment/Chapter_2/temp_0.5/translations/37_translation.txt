— All these sensitive details, sir, don't concern us in the least — Ilya Petrovich cut him off with a brazen remark — you must give your review and assurance, and as for what you were so pleased to be in love with and all those tragic places, we don't care about any of that. — Well... cruelly... — muttered Nikodim Fomichev, sitting down at the table and also taking up a pen to sign. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write on, then, said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What's there to write? — he asked, gruffly enough. — And I'll dictate it to you, said the clerk. Raskolnikov thought that the clerk had become more careless and contemptuous towards him after his confession, but strangely enough — he suddenly felt utterly indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change came somehow in one moment, in a single minute. If he had wanted to think about it for a bit, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak with them like that just a minute ago, and even impose his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
