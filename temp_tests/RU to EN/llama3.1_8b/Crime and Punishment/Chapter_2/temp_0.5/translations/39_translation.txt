The clerk began to dictate to him the ordinary phrase in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise later, I won't leave the city, I won't sell or give away my property and so on. — You can't even write, your pen is slipping out of your hands, — observed the clerk, looking at Raskolnikov with curiosity. — Are you ill? — Yes... My head is spinning... Go on talking! — Yes, everything; sign it. The clerk took back the paper and busied himself with others. Raskolnikov handed over his pen but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head in his hands. It was as if they were hammering a nail into his forehead. A strange idea suddenly occurred to him: get up now, approach Nikodim Fyodorovich and tell him everything that happened yesterday, down to the smallest detail, then go with them to the apartment and point out their belongings, in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already stood up from his seat to carry it out.
