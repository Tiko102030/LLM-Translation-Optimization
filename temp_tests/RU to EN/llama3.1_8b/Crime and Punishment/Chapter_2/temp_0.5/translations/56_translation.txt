His face expressed amazement. — What's wrong with you? he shouted, examining the entered comrade from head to toe; then fell silent and whistled. — Is it really that bad? Oh, brother, you've outdone our brother, he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. — Come on, sit down, you must be tired! — And when the latter collapsed onto a worn-out Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. — Are you seriously ill, do you know it? He began to feel Raskolnikov's pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. — Don't need, I said... I came... well, I wanted to... but I don't need lessons at all... — What do you know?
