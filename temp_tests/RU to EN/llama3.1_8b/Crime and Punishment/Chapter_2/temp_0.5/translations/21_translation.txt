He took a deeper breath more freely. "Not quite!" He gradually began to recover, he tried by all means to pick himself up and come to his senses. "Some foolishness, some smallest carelessness, and I can deceive everyone! Hmm... it's a shame there is no air here - stuffiness... My head spins even more... and my mind too..."

He felt a terrible disorder in everything within him. He was afraid of not being able to control himself. He tried to cling to something and think about something completely unrelated, but it didn't work out at all. The clerk greatly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure out what was going on. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his age, dressed in the latest fashion and foppishly, with a part on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with many rings and cuffs on his white-gloved fingers and gold chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner who was here he even said two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down, he said briefly to the partly dressed red-brown lady, who stood as if she dared not sit down herself, although a chair was nearby.
