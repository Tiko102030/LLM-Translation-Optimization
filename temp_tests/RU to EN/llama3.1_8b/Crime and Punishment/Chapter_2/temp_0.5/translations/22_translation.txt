Louisa Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said to the slightly disheveled red-haired lady, who was standing as if not daring to sit herself down, although a chair was nearby. — I thank you, she said and quietly, with a silk rustling sound, sat down on the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and took up almost half the room. She wafted perfumes. But the lady clearly felt uneasy about taking up half the room and that her perfume was drifting so strongly from her, although she was smiling timidly and shamelessly at the same time, but with obvious anxiety. The mourning lady finally finished speaking and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially rotating his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his hat with a badge on the table and sat in the armchair. A puffy lady jumped out of her seat at the sight of him and took on a certain special excitement, began to squat; but the officer didn't even notice her slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit down near him.
