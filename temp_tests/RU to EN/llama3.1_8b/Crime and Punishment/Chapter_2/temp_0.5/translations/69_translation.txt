But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, apparently, they will come to him now if so, "because… well, all this is from the same... because of yesterday... God!" He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand didn't rise... and what's the use anyway! Fear, like ice, wrapped itself around his soul, tormented him, petrified him... But finally, after that whole din, which had been going on for a faithful ten minutes, began to gradually subside. The landlady was still moaning and shrieking, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But finally, it seems, he calmed down; it's not even possible to hear him anymore; "has he left? God!" Yes, the landlady is leaving too, still sobbing and crying... and her door has slammed shut... And the crowd is dispersing through the apartments on the stairs, exclaiming, arguing, calling out to each other, sometimes raising their voices to a shout, sometimes lowering them to a whisper. There must have been many of them; almost the whole house had gathered. "But God, can all this be possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov fell weakly onto the divan, but already couldn't close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of boundless horror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a bowl of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he wasn't asleep, she put the candle on the table and began to lay out what she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
