Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed with a nervous, high-pitched, almost inaudible, long laugh, and he was laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Street Boulevard, where two days ago he ran into that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts began to crowd into his head. He realized then too that it was incredibly repulsive for him now to walk past that bench on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be just as hard to run into that scoundrel again who had given him a two-kopeck coin: "Damn him!" He walked, looking around dazedly and spitefully. All his thoughts were now spinning around some main point, and he himself felt that this really was the main point and that now, exactly now, he was face to face with it for the first time in these two months.
