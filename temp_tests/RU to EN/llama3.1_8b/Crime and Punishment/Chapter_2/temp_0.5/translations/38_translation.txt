Where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, if his room were suddenly filled with his first friends rather than the quartermasters, he would not have had a single human word for them, so suddenly empty was his heart. A gloomy feeling of painful and endless isolation and alienation suddenly became conscious to him. It wasn't the baseness of his confessions to Ilya Petrovich, nor the quartermaster's triumph over him that turned his heart around suddenly. Oh, what did it matter to him now about his own baseness, about all these ambitions, quartermasters, Germans, pursuits, offices, and so on! If they had even sentenced him to be burned at the stake in this moment, he wouldn't have stirred, and maybe not even listened carefully to the sentence. Something was happening to him that was completely unfamiliar to him, new, sudden, and never before experienced. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt with all his might that he could no longer address these people, the quartermasters in the office, with anything, whether it was sensitive expansiveness as before or something else; even if they were his own brothers and sisters instead of quartermasters, he had no need to address them at all, not even in life; he had never experienced such a strange and terrifying feeling before. And what's most tormenting is that it was more than just a feeling, more than just an idea - it was a direct, the most tormenting feeling of all those experienced by him so far in his life. The clerk began to dictate to him the usual form of response in such cases, namely "I'm unable to pay, I'll promise something (someday), I won't leave the city, and I won't sell or give away my property, etc."
