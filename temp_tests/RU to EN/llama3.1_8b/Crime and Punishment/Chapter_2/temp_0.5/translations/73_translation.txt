He remembered only how he had taken one sip of cold water and spilled some from the cup on his chest. Then came a state of amnesia. III

However, he was not entirely in a state of amnesia throughout his illness; it was a delirious condition with hallucinations and partial consciousness. He recalled much later. It seemed to him that there were many people gathered around him, wanting to take him away somewhere, arguing about him heatedly and quarreling among themselves. Suddenly he would be alone in the room, everyone would have left and be afraid of him, only occasionally slightly opening the door to look at him, threatening him, whispering something to each other, laughing and taunting him. He often remembered Nastasya beside him; he could distinguish another person nearby who seemed very familiar, but couldn't figure out who it was and yearned for this person, even crying. At times it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; in other cases, the same day would seem to be passing. But he completely forgot about what exactly - about that, while constantly remembering that he had forgotten something that couldn't be forgotten - he was tormented, suffering, recalling, groaning, falling into frenzy or an unbearable, paralyzing fear. Then he tried to get up from his place, wanted to run away, but someone always held him back by force, and he again fell into powerlessness and amnesia.
