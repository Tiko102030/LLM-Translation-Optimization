— And where, if I may ask? — Down the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, curtly, entirely pale as a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes from Illya Petrovich's gaze. — He barely stands on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fomich noticed. — Nothing! — somehow said Illya Petrovich particularly. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the clerk, who was also very intently looking at him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
