He left the room this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt that he was completely broken, but his consciousness remained with him. He feared pursuit, he feared that in half an hour, maybe even a quarter of an hour, the instructions would come out to follow him; therefore, no matter what, he had to bury the ends before it was too late. He had to get through while there were still some forces and some reasoning left... Where to go? This had been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends in the water, and that's it." He had decided this even during the night, in a delirium, in those moments when he remembered it several times and was about to get up and leave: "as soon as possible, as soon as possible, and throw everything out." But throwing everything out turned out to be very difficult. He had been walking along the Yekaterininsky Canal embankment for over half an hour, maybe even more, and he several times glanced at the sewers where they met.
