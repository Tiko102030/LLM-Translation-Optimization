The summons was so strong that he had already risen from his seat to carry it out. "Shouldn't I at least think for a minute?" flashed through his head. "No, better not think and be done with it!" But suddenly he stopped as if rooted to the spot: Nikodim Fomich was speaking with fervor to Il'ia Petrovich, and his words reached him:

— It's impossible that both of them will be released! First of all, everything contradicts; consider this: why would they summon a janitor if it were their affair? To denounce themselves, perhaps? Or for cunning? No, it would be too cunning! And finally, the student Pestyakov was seen by both janitors and the woman at the very gates at the exact moment when he entered: he walked with three friends and parted from them right at the gates and inquired about their residence while still with his friends. Would he inquire about their residence if he had such an intention?
