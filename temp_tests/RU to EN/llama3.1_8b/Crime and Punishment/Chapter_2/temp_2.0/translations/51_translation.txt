Under the stone a small indentation had formed; at once he began throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and still there was room in the indentation. Then he caught the stone again and with one rotation turned it back to its previous side, and just as he expected it fit exactly back into its former place, except slightly, just a little higher. But he scraped up earth and pressed down on both sides with his foot. There wasn't anything visible anymore. Then he went out and headed for the square. Once again strong and hardly bearable joy took hold of him for a moment, just like in the office before, "The ends are buried! And to whom could it occur that anyone would look for something under this stone? It's here probably since the house was built and will still be lying there for as long."
