Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved over to him on the sofa, clumsily like a bear, grabbed his head with his left hand, despite the fact that he himself could have sat up, and with his right hand held a spoonful of soup to his mouth, several times first blowing on it so that he wouldn't burn. But the soup was still quite warm. Raskolnikov swallowed one spoonful greedily, then another, third. But after placing a few spoons, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and announced that regarding further steps we should consult with Zosima. Nastasya entered carrying two bottles of beer. — Would you like tea? — I'd like it. — Hurry up with the tea, Nastasya, because as for tea, it seems we can do without a doctor.
