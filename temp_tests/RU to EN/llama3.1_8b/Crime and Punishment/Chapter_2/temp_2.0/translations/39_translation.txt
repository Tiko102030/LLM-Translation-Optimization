The scribe began to dictate a standard letter of refusal in such cases, namely that he was unable to pay, promising someday, leaving the city not, not selling nor giving away any property etc. — Can you even write? You're letting your pen slip out of your hand," observed the scribe, intently eyeing Razumikhin with curiosity. — Are you ill? — Yeah... my head is spinning... go on talking! — Indeed; sign below it. The scribe took back the paper and busied himself with others. Razumikhin handed over the pen but instead of standing up and leaving, he put both elbows on the table and grasped his head tightly in his hands. It felt like a nail was being driven into his temples. A strange thought suddenly came to him: stand up now, go up to Nikolay Ivanovich and tell him everything from yesterday's events down to the smallest detail, then together with them go to the flat and point out their belongings on the wall, in the corner, in a hole. The urge was so strong that he already got up from his seat to carry it out.
