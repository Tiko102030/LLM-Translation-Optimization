Louisa Ivanovna, would you please sit down - he said with a fleeting glance at the partly dressed crimson woman who stood there as though she dared not sit herself down, although there was a chair right beside her. I thank you,[17 - Thank you (German) – Ed.] — she replied and quietly, with the soft rustling of silk, lowered herself onto the chair. Her brightly blue dress with white crocheted trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon, occupying almost half the room. There was a whiff of perfume in the air. However, it seemed the woman felt uncomfortable about occupying such space and being surrounded by the strong scent from her, although she also smiled shamefully and provocatively at the same time with evident distress. The mourner lady finally stopped making small talk and began to rise. Suddenly, making a somewhat loud noise, boldly and somehow especially twitching her shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade onto the table and sat down in an armchair. The plump lady leapt off her feet upon seeing him and enthusiastically started bobbing up and down, but the officer didn't pay her any attention whatsoever, while she no longer dared sit beside him afterwards.
