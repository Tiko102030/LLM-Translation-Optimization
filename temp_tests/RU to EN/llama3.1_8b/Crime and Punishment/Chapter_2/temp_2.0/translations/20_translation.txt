This was some sort of particularly disheveled person with an immovable idea in their gaze. "You won't learn anything from that because it doesn't matter to him," thought Raskolnikov. - Go there, to the clerk, said the copyist and pointed a finger forward showing the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), cramped and packed tightly with people - the crowd was slightly better dressed than those in the other rooms. Between visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning attire, poorly dressed, sat at a table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, extremely plump and reddish-pink with spots, a noticeable woman, and extravagantly dressed, with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea saucer, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. He briefly glanced at it, said "wait" and continued to work with the lady in mourning.
