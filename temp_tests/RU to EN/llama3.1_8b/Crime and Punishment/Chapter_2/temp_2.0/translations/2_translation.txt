The light was plentiful and he hastily glanced himself over from head to foot in his entire garment: are there any signs? But one couldn't do that: trembling with chills, he began to remove everything from himself and again examine it around him. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and not trusting himself, he repeated the examination three times. But there was nothing, as it seemed, no signs of anything; only at the place where his pants below were frayed and hung with a braid, on this braid remained thick spots of congealed blood. He grabbed a folding large scissors and trimmed the braid. Maybe there was nothing more left. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and things which he pulled out from the old woman's trunk, all still lie at him in his pockets! And not even thought to pull them out and hide them yet! Hadn't even thought of them now while examining the clothing! What's this?
