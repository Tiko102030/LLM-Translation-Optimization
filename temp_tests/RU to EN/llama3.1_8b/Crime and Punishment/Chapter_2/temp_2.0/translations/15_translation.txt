He thought to himself: "Put on the sock!—only that will scrape against the dust some more, and the footprints will be gone!" But as soon as he put it on, he immediately tore it off in disgust and terror. He tore it off, but then realized there was no other one, so took and put it back on – and laughed again. "It's all conditional, relative, everything is just a form –" he thought fleetingly with the edge of his mind, while shaking all over—“because look I've put it on! Because I ended up putting it on!" His laughter, however, was immediately replaced by despair. "No, not possible…", he thought to himself. His legs were trembling. "From fear", he muttered to himself. His head was spinning and aching from the heat.
