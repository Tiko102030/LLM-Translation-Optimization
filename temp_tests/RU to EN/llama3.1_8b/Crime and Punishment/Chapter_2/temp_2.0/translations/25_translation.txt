— Don't shout! I'm not shouting, but speaking calmly; it's you who are shouting at me. As a student, I won't allow anyone to raise their voice against me. The clerk was so enraged that he could hardly speak in the first minute and only spluttered out words from his mouth. He leapt up from his seat. — Outrageous! You are in my presence. Don't swear, fellow! — And you too are in my presence, said Raskolnikov loudly, shouting down to the clerk; besides what's more, you shout while smoking a cigarette, so you're all disgracing us. — As soon as he finished speaking, Raskolnikov felt an inexpressible sense of enjoyment. The scribe looked on with a smile.
