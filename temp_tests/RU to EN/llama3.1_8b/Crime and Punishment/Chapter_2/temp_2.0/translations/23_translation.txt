Plump woman suddenly leaped out of her seat when she saw him and, with an extraordinary enthusiasm, began to squat down; but the officer did not pay any attention to her, and she was no longer bold enough to sit next to him. He was a lieutenant, the assistant chief guard officer, with horizontally standing reddish-brown whiskers on both sides and with extremely small facial features, expressing nothing special, except for some impudence, in fact. He glanced at Raskolnikov from the side and partially with indignation: his outfit was just too shabby, and despite all condescension, he still did not hold himself well; Raskolnikov, by mistake, looked straight at him too long and openly, which made the other take offense. —What's this? — he shouted to Raskolnikov, apparently surprised that such a ragamuffin didn't tremble at his swift glance. — Asked for... as per summons... — Raskolnikov replied in an awkward manner. — It's about collecting money from them on the account of a student, — the clerk hastened to explain, looking away from the papers. — See! — and he handed over a notebook to Raskolnikov, pointing to its relevant page, — read this!
