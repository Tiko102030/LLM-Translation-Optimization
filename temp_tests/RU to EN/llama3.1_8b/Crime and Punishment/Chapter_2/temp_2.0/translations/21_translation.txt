He breathed more freely. "Not so!" Gradually he began to pick up spirits, he was trying with all his might to calm down and regain control. "Any nonsense, any minor carelessness, and I can totally betray myself! Hmm... pity there's no air here - stuffiness... Head spins even more... and the mind too..."

He felt a terrifying chaos in himself everywhere. He was afraid not to keep his wits about him. He tried to latch onto something and think about anything completely unrelated, but it wasn't working at all. The scribe strongly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, read between the lines. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a tanned and lively physiognomy that seemed older than his years, dressed in style and fashions of the day, with a pompadour on his head, brushed and perfumed, wearing many rings and cuffs on white-gloved fingers and gold chains on his vest. He even said two words to one of the foreigners present, and spoke them correctly - in French. "Luisa Ivanovna, would you like to sit down?" he said briefly to a elegantly dressed red-haired lady who had been standing stiffly as if unsure whether to sit down or not, despite a nearby chair being available.
