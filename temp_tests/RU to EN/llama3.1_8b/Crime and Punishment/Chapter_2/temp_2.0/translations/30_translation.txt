— Ah, Ilya Petrovich! — the secretary began with care, but stopped to wait for a moment, because one couldn't hold back the heated lieutenant otherwise than by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the flamboyant lady, at first she did tremble like a leaf at the thunder and lightning; but a strange thing: as the insults grew louder and more numerous, her appearance became more charming, her smile more captivating, directed towards the gruff lieutenant. She kept jumping around in place, constantly bowing down impatiently, awaiting the moment when finally it would be allowed to let loose her own words. And she did. — No noise or fights here at my house, Mr. Captain! she burst out suddenly, like a legume that had sprouted under a jolt, with a firm German accent, although speaking quickly in Russian — and no hooliganism whatsoever, and they all got drunk here and I'll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, but it's not my fault… I have a noble home, Mr. Captain, and noble manners, Mr. Captain, and I always never wanted any kind of trouble. But then they all completely got drunk and afterwards started hitting me with a pipe again, asking me questions, and then one lifted his feet onto the piano and started banging at it with his feet; this is very unpleasant in a noble home and he smashed the piano terribly and completely there's no sense here, and I'm saying. Then I had to call a porter quickly and Carl came up, he grabbed Carl by the collar, Genoche was hit as well, but five times he punched me in the cheek. And this is not tasteful in a noble home, Mr. Captain! So I screamed! —And then he opened the window onto the canal and stood there, squealing like a small pig, in the window; this is scandalous.
