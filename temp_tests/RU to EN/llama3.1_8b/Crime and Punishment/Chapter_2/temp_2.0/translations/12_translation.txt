He wasn't responding and was holding a piece of paper, not having printed it yet.—Go back to sleep, for Pete's sake,—Nastasya continued, sympathizing and seeing him put his feet down from the sofa—he's ill, just don't go out: nothing will get burned.Why do you have all that in your hands? He glanced at it—a pair of clipped remnants of braid, a sock, shreds of torn-off pocket—all strewn together.He slept with them still. Afterwards, racking his brains and half-awake from the heat, he tightly clasped everything in his hand once more, and went back to sleep.Nastasya laughed at her nervously-hysterical laugh.Seeing that he was collecting this rag-and-bone man’s load in bed like it was a hoard… In an instant he tucked everything under his greatcoat and pierced the material with his gaze.Oh so little did he have the wit to understand what he was up to at that exact moment, but he sensed how people wouldn't treat someone with such indifference when they came for him.
