Louisa Ivanovna sat down with politeness in all directions and, sitting, almost reached the doors; but at the doors she bumped her back into one notable officer with an open fresh face and excellent thick blond mustaches. It was Nicholas Fomich himself, a quarter master sergeant. Louisa Ivanovna quickly sat down to nearly half and trotted out of the office with frequent small steps, leaping, saying "Again thunderstorm, again noise and lightning, whirlwind, hurricane!" - Nice and friendly addressed Nicodemus Fomich to Ilya Petrovich - again ruffled heart, again boiling over! He heard it even from the stairs. "-What what?"-With a noble lack of concern said Ilya Petrovich (and even not "what" but: "Da- a shta- a!"-), passing with some papers to another table and elegantly wobbling with each step his shoulders, where was a step - there were also shoulder;-Well, can you see: Mr. writer, i.e. the student, former, he doesn't pay money, issues warrants, hasn't cleaned the apartment, persistent complaints are being brought on them, and they have dared to enter in claim that I puffed up a cigarette amongst them! They bring themselves up, but - look here at them: behold them in their attractive view now!- Poverty is not vice, friend, ah, yes enough already.
