--Well, but she is my mistress after all. --So what about it, then? The clerk looked at him with a condescending smile of sorrow, and at the same time some triumph, as if he were a novice who had just been bombarded: "How do you feel now?" But how was he concerned with borrowed documents or reimbursement right now? Wasn't this all a waste of anxiety in return, or even attention for that matter! He stood, read, listened, responded, and even asked questions himself, but everything was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, rescue from the overwhelming danger – that is what filled his entire being at this moment, without consideration, without analysis, without future speculation and guesswork, without doubts or questions. This was a moment of pure, immediate, animal joy. But at that very same moment, something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by the impertinence, still ablaze, and obviously wanting to support the injured ambition, threw himself with all his might on the unfortunate "flourishing woman" who had been looking at him since he entered with a stupidly obsequious smile. --And you, such-and-such-and-what, it was something that happened there last night?
