On the other hand, now, if suddenly the room filled up with his first-rate friends, not mere quartalniks, there would be no human words left for them; his heart would have become so desolate in an instant. A dark feeling of excruciating, endless isolation and estrangement had suddenly expressed itself to his soul. It was not the lowliness of his heart's outpourings before Ilia Petrovich, nor his lieutenant's triumph over him that turned his heart upside down all at once. Oh, what concern does he now have with his own meanness, with all these ambitions, lieutenants, German women, and chancelleries, and the rest of it? If he were even condemned to be burned alive in this very moment, he would not even budge; he probably wouldn't listen attentively to the verdict. Something was happening to him that had never happened before, something completely unfamiliar, new, unexpected and unforeseen. He didn't really understand, but he clearly felt with all his might that not only could he no longer express his sensitive expansiveness in the way he used to, but it would be utterly pointless for him to address these people - the quartalniks in the office, even if they were his own brothers and sisters, rather than quartalnik officers - and he wouldn't have any use for them whatsoever; that was now utterly superfluous, even unthinkable. What made everything even more torturous was that this was a feeling, not a consciousness, not an idea, but a direct feeling, the most torturing of all the sensations experienced by him in his life so far.
