These sensitive details, sir, do not concern us in the least – Ilya Petrovich snapped off with gross impertinence. You must give us your opinion and statement of fact, what you were indulging yourselves in this love affair, and all those tragic places we simply don't know anything about them.

No... how brutal... murmured Nikodim Fyodorovich to himself as he sat down at the table and also began signing his name. He felt some shame somehow. 

Write on, the clerk told Raskolnikov.

What to write? asked him with some unusual roughness. 

I will dictate it to you. To Raskolnikov, it seemed that after his confession, the clerk became even more careless and contemptuous towards him, but, curiously, he suddenly began to feel utterly indifferent to everyone's opinion, and this change happened somehow all at once, in a single moment, one minute. If only he had thought about it for a little while, then of course he would have been surprised at how he could talk with them like that just a minute ago, and even intrude with his feelings? Where did these feelings come from?
