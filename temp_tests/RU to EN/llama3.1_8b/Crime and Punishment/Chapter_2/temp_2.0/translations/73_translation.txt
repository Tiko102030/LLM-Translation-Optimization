He remembered only that he had taken one gulp of cold water and spilled some from the cup on his chest. Then came forgetfulness. III

However, he was not entirely in a state of delirium all the time: it was an intermittent feverish state with madness and partial consciousness. He recalled much later. Sometimes it seemed to him that many people were gathering around him, wanting to take him away somewhere, arguing about him fiercely, shouting at each other. Then suddenly he would find himself alone in the room, everyone gone and frightened of him, barely opening the door occasionally to look at him and threaten him, whispering something among themselves, laughing and teasing him. He often recalled Nastasya nearby; he distinguished another person as well, very much like someone familiar, but couldn't quite figure out who it was and would pine for them, even crying. At one time, he thought he had been lying in bed for a month; on another occasion, it seemed the same day went by. He forgot about all that; yet every minute he remembered what he could not forget to remember – tormented himself, suffering, trying to recall, whimpering, descending into rage or an unbearable fear. At that point, he was driven mad with desperation, wanted to get up and run away, but someone would always stop him physically, and he would succumb once more to powerlessness and amnesia.
