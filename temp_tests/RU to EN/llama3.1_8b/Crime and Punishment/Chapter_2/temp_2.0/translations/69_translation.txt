The crowd is dispersing through the apartments, moaning, arguing, calling out, sometimes raising their voice to a shout, sometimes lowering it to a whisper. There must have been many of them; hardly the whole house gathered together. "But God, can all this be possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov was exhausted and fell onto the sofa, but already couldn't close his eyes; for half an hour he lay in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of boundless horror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a bowl of soup. Looking at him carefully and noticing that he wasn't sleeping, she put the candle on the table and began to lay out what she brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
