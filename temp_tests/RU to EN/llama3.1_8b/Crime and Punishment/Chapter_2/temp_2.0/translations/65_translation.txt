But in that moment as he stood by the railing and still stared hatefully at the receding carriage, rubbing his back, he suddenly felt someone slip something into his hands. He looked down: an elderly businesswoman, with a hat and goat hide shoes, and beside her a girl, with a hat and a green umbrella, probably her daughter. "Take it, Father, for Christ's sake." He took it, and they passed by. It was two groshy. By their dress and appearance they might have taken him for a beggar, a real coin-collector on the streets, and he was doubtless due for another lash with a whip that had moved them to pity. He clutched the two groshy in his hand, took ten steps and turned towards the Neva, in the direction of the palace. The sky was cloudless and there were hardly any clouds at all, and the water was almost blue, which rarely happens on the Neva. The cathedral dome shone out brightly, as it never does from any point except when viewed from this place, from the bridge, not twenty paces before the chapel; through the clear air every one of its ornaments could be clearly made out. The pain of the whip had receded, and Raskolnikov had forgotten all about the blow; he was now entirely preoccupied by a troubled and hardly clear thought.
