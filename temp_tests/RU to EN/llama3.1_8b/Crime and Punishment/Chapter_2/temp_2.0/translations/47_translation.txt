He then stepped out of the room, this time leaving it wide open. He was walking quickly and firmly, even though he felt like a broken man, but his consciousness was still with him. He was afraid of being pursued, afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, maybe, there would already be instructions to follow; so whatever the case might be, he had to stash away his ends for now. He needed to get through it before what strength and reason he had left ran out... Where should he go? The decision on that was made a long time ago: "Toss everything into the ditch and get rid of them once and for all." That's how he had resolved things last night, in a delirium, at those moments when he recalled it and several times felt like getting up to leave quickly and throwing it all out. But it was too hard to throw away. He'd been walking along the Embankment of Catherine Canal for over half an hour now, maybe more, and several times caught sight of people standing at the canalside, where he was met. It was impossible to even think about carrying out his intention: either there were boats moored right next to the canals and washerwomen were washing clothes on them, or sailing-boats were tied up, and all sorts of people swarmed everywhere, and from everywhere along the embankments, from all sides, one could see: suspiciously, that a person intentionally disembarked, stopped, and was throwing something into the water.
