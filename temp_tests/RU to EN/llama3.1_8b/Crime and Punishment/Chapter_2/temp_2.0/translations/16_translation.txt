His head spun and hurt from the heat. "This is a trap! They want to trick me into letting my guard down," he thought to himself as he went up the stairs. — It's terrible that I'm almost out of my mind... I can come up with some stupid lie..."

On the staircase, he remembered leaving everything in a heap – "they would be sure to search for him without his stuff" - and stopped, but a feeling of despair and a cynicism about his fate overtook him so completely that he shook it off and continued. "As long as it's over with quickly..."

Outside, the heat was just as unbearable; no drop of rain had fallen in days. There was dust, bricks, and plaster everywhere; a stench came from the food stalls and pubs; drunkards and hucksters constantly swarmed the streets; the sun shone brightly into his eyes so he hurt to look at it, and his head started spinning - just an ordinary symptom of being out in the hot sun as one was leaving a sickbed. When he reached the corner that led back to yesterday's street, he peered anxiously down it, into the house... and immediately looked away again. "I might even tell them so if they ask," he thought, approaching the office building.
