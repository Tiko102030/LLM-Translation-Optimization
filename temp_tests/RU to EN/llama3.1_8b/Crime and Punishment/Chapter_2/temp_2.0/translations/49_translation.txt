He became extremely absent-minded and forgetful, he knew it himself. Resolutely, one had to hurry! He walked along the V-street avenue towards the Neva; but on his way a new idea suddenly occurred to him: 'Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to go somewhere very far away, maybe back to the Islands again, and there in some deserted place, in a forest, under a bush - bury everything here and notice the tree perhaps?' And although he felt that at this moment he was not capable of clearly and sensibly discussing everything, the idea seemed infallible to him. But it was not meant for him to reach the Islands, instead something else happened: exiting from the V-street avenue onto the square, he suddenly saw on the left entrance into a yard surrounded by absolutely blind walls. On the right, just after entering through the gate, in the far part of the courtyard, there was an unbolted wall of a neighboring four-story house. On the left, parallel to the blind wall and at once next to the gate, ran a wooden fence, about twenty paces deep into the yard, then it turned to the left. This was a secluded fenced area where some materials were lying. Further on, in a recess of the yard, peeped out from behind the fence a corner of a low, sooted, stone shed that obviously was part of some workshop.
