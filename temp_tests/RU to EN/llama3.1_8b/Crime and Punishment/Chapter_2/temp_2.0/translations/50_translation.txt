Further into the courtyard, a corner of a low, soot-blackened stone shed peeped out over the fence, apparently part of some workshop or another. Clearly there was something like a carriage factory or a smithy here; everywhere, almost from the very gates, coal dust hung in clouds. "If only I could drop all this and disappear," he suddenly thought to himself. Not noticing anyone around, he strolled right up to the gates and at that moment, just next to the gates, saw, affixed to the fence a groove (which is often found in such houses where there are many factory workers, members of trade unions, cabmen and other), above the groove on the fence was written with chalk the usual phrase in such cases: "Here, beggar, do not come back". And well might he think it lucky that no one had spotted him entering or stopping. "Here I can just as easily pick up all this somewhere and disappear."

Glancing around once more, he was already slipping his hand into his pocket when he suddenly noticed between the outer wall and the groove, just at the entrance, where there was only about a forearm's width of space, a big, roughly hewn stone that probably weighed an ounce or so less than one and a half pounds in weight. This stone was stuck fast right against the stonewall that surrounded the courtyard on this side. On the other side of this wall was the street, the sidewalk, and he could hear the pedestrians shuffling along – it seemed like there were always a lot of them here. But since he wouldn't be seen if someone entered the yard from outside (and that too was a possibility), there wasn't any time to waste.

He bent down to the stone, grasped it firmly by its upper end with both hands and put all his strength into turning the stone over. As the stone turned over, a small depression appeared underneath; he quickly started shoveling everything from his pocket into it.
