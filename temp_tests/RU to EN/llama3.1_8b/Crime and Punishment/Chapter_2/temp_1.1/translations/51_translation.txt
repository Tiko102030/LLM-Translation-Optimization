Under the stone a small depression formed; immediately he started throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top, and still there was room for more in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one rotation to its original side, and it fit perfectly back in its old place, except that it seemed slightly higher by a little bit. But he kicked some dirt under it and crushed it down with his foot on either side. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed towards the square. Again strong and barely bearable joy took hold of him for just an instant, just like before in the office, "Buried at last! And who would think to look for them under this stone? Maybe it's been lying here since the house was built and will stay there just as long."
