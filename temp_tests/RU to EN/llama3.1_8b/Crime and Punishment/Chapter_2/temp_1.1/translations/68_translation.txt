And then, to his greatest amazement, he suddenly caught a glimpse of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming and whimpering, speaking in such rapid succession that it was impossible to make out what she was saying, imploring them to stop beating her, because they were mercilessly pounding her on the staircase. The beatings became so savage with anger and madness that all that could be heard was a low growl, but still both of them were shouting something in a jumbled mess, speaking quickly and gasping for air. Suddenly Raskolnikov shuddered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was the voice of Ilya Petrovich. It's Ilya Petrovich here beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his legs, banging her head against the step - this is clear from the sounds, wails and blows! What on earth is going on? The lights seem to have turned around. It could be heard as a crowd gathered from all floors, along the entire staircase, voices, outcries, footsteps, knocks and bangs on doors were coming from everywhere, people were rushing down. "But why, why can this happen?" he repeated seriously, thinking that he must have gone completely mad. But no, he's hearing everything far too clearly!...
