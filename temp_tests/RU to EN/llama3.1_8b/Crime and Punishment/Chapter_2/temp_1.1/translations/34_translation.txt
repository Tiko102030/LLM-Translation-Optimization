Louisa Ivanovna, with exaggerated politeness, sat down on all sides and, sitting down, reached the doors; but in the doorway she bumped her back into a prominent officer with an open fresh face and thick light blond whiskers. This was none other than Nikodim Fomichev, the district overseer. Louisa Ivanovna hurriedly sat down almost to the floor and quickly ran out of the office on tiptoes. —Again thunder, again storm and lightning, hurricane and tornado! — greeted Nikodim Fomichev with friendliness and benevolence towards Ilya Petrovich, — again it's stirred up my heart, again it's boiling over! I could hear it from the stairs even. —What's what? — said Ilya Petrovich with a lordly nonchalance (and not "what," but somehow: "Da-aa sha-a!" ), moving to another table with some papers and dramatically tossing his shoulders with each step, where he stepped, there his shoulder; — look here, you see: the gentleman writer, that is the student, who used to be owes money, issued IOUs, doesn't clean up the apartment, constant complaints are coming in about them, and they decided to enter a claim that I smoked a cigar with them! They go around begging themselves, but see here, take a look at them: there they are in their most attractive appearance now! —Poverty is not a vice, my friend, oh well what can you do!
