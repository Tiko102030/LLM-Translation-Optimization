Nastasya muttered and went to execute the order. Raskolnikov watched her wildly and with tension. Meanwhile Razumikhin moved over to him on the divan, awkwardly, like a bear, grasped his head with his left hand, although he could have easily got up himself, and held a spoonful of soup to his mouth, blowing gently on it first so as not to burn him. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov devoured one spoonful, then another, and a third. But after bringing a few more spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that they should consult with Zosima about further arrangements. Nastasya came in carrying two bottles of beer. "Would you like some tea?" she asked. "I'd like it." "Hurry and get the tea, Nastasya," because it seems we can probably do without a doctor for the tea.
