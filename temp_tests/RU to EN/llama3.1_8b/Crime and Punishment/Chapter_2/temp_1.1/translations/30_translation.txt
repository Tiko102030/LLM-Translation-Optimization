Ilya Petrovich! — began the clerk carefully, but stopped to wait for a moment, because one couldn't hold onto the heated-up lieutenant otherwise than by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the pompous lady, at first she was indeed shaken by the thunder and lightning; but it's a strange thing: as the number of her insults grew more numerous and stronger, so did her appearance become lovelier, her smile more charming, addressed to the menacing lieutenant. She pattered in place and incessantly bent low with impatience waiting for the moment when she would finally be allowed to say her piece, and she waited it out. — There won't be any noise or fight, I'm responsible, Captain, she suddenly began to babble, like peas being sprouted, with a strong German accent, although speaking fluently in Russian: 'and no scandal whatsoever, none at all; but they came here drunk, and that's what I'll tell you about, Captain, and I'm not guilty... my house is noble, Captain, and our way of living is noble too, and I never wanted any scandal. But they completely got drunk here, and then asked three times where things were, and one of them lifted his feet and started banging away on the piano with his feet, which isn't at all proper in a respectable house, and he smashed the piano, and it's just awful here without any manners; and I said so. And then he grabbed the whiskey bottle and started hitting everyone from behind with it. And when I quickly called out to the doorkeeper to call Karl, he picked up Karl and poked him in the eye, and Henriette too; and he hit me on the cheek five times. It's just not nice at all in a respectable house, Captain; and I shouted. But then he opened the window onto the canal and stood there, like a little pig squealing, it's shameful.
