Where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly his room were filled with his first and dearest friends, he wouldn't have a single human word for them either; so suddenly empty had become his heart. The dismal feeling of agonizing, endless loneliness and alienation has suddenly become conscious in him. It's not the baseness of his emotional outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, nor is it portly officer's triumph over him that turned his heart upside down like this just now. Oh, what business does he have with his own baseness, with all these ambitions, officers, Germans, searches, offices and so on! If they were even to sentence him to be burned at the stake right now, he wouldn't budge, and probably wouldn't even listen to the verdict carefully either. Something was happening to him that was utterly unfamiliar, new, sudden and never-before experienced. Not as if he understood anything, but he clearly felt with all the force of feeling that not only were his sensitive expansiveness, like before, impossible for these people in the apartment house office, but it didn't matter what it might be - he simply couldn't address them anymore; if they weren't quartermaster officers, but even his own blood brothers and sisters, then too there was no need to talk to them at all, nor in any case in life. He had never experienced anything like this strange and terrifying feeling before. And what's most agonizing - it was more an experience than a realization, a concept; an immediate experience, the most agonizing of all his experiences so far lived through in life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual formula for such cases, namely that he couldn't pay, promised something sometime, wouldn't leave the city, and wouldn't sell or give away his property either, etc.
