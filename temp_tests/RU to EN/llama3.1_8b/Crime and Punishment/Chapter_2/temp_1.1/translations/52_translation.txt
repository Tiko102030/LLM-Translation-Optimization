There are no clues!" – and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he laughed nervously, quietly, barely audible, prolonged laughter, and he laughed all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Street Boulevard, where he met her three days ago, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts came into his head. It seemed to him that it was very repulsive to pass by that bench now, on which he had sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be just as difficult to meet again that sly fellow to whom he gave a two-grivna coin: "Damn him!"
