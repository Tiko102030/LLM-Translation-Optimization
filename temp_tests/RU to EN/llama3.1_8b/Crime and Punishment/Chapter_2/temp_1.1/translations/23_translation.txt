The plump lady so jumped up from her place as soon as she saw him and with some special excitement began to squat down; but the officer paid no attention at all to her, and she didn't dare sit next to him again. This was a lieutenant, an assistant quarter-master sergeant, with horizontally sprouting reddish sideburns and extremely small facial features that expressed nothing peculiar except for a certain impudence. He glanced askance and partly in indignation at Raskolnikov: the latter's clothes were far too shabby, and despite all the condescension, his posture was not fitting; Raskolnikov had looked straight at him for too long, so that the officer became offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin did not bow to his lightning-fast gaze. "Asked for... by summons...," Raskolnikov stammered in response. "This is about collecting money from them, from the student," the clerk hastened, tearing himself away from the papers. "Here's your chance!" he said and handed Raskolnikov a notebook with a place marked in it.
