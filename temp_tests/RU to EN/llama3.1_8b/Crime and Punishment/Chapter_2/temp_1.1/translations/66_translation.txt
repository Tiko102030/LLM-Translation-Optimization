The pain from the whip subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one anxious and not quite clear thought occupied him exclusively now. He stood and looked far away for a long time, carefully; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to university, it often happened, more often than not, on his way home, that he would stop at this very same spot, gaze attentively at this truly magnificent panorama, and almost wonder every time at one unclear and insoluble impression of his own. An inexplicable cold wind blew on him always from this magnificent panorama; the spirit was mute and deaf to him in this lush picture… He marveled every time at his gloomy and mysterious impression, putting off its solution, not trusting himself, until later. Now suddenly he recalled all these former questions and doubts of his, and it seemed to him that he hadn't remembered them by chance now. One thing struck him as strange and wonderful - that he had stopped at the very same spot as before, as though he truly imagined that he could think the same way now as before, with the same old themes and pictures interesting him, just like he was... still so recently. Even it almost became funny to him, but at the same time it constricted his chest in pain. In some depth, below, somewhere barely visible underfoot, this whole past, and former thoughts, and former problems, and former topics, and former impressions, and the entire panorama, and he himself, and everything... seemed to be fleeing somewhere upwards, disappearing from his eyes… Having made a sudden movement with his hand, he suddenly felt in his fist something clenched; it was the double-rouble coin. He loosened his hand, looked carefully at the coin, swung it back and forth and threw it into the water; then turned around and went home. It seemed to him as though he had just cut himself off from everything and everyone with a pair of scissors in that very moment.
