He remembered only that he had taken one gulp of cold water and spilled some from the cup on his chest. Then came a period of unconsciousness. III

However, he was not completely unconscious throughout his illness; it was a state of delirium with hallucinations and partial awareness. He remembered much later. It seemed to him at times that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him somewhere, arguing heatedly about him and quarreling among themselves. Suddenly he would be alone in the room, and everyone had left, afraid of him, only occasionally opening the door to look at him with a threatening expression, whispering something to each other and laughing or teasing him. Nastasya often came to mind beside him; he could distinguish another man who seemed very familiar but couldn't figure out who it was, feeling sad about it and even crying. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying for a month; in another case - that the same day was going on. But one thing he had completely forgotten; however, every minute he remembered what he forgot, something important, and could not forget, tormented, suffered, tried to remember, moaned, lost control or fell into an unbearable terror. At those moments, he tried to get up, wanted to run away, but someone always stopped him physically, and he would fall back into weakness and unconsciousness again.
