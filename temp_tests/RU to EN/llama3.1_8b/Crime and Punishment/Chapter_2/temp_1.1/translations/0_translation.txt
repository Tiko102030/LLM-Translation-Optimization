He lay there for a very long time. Sometimes it seemed to him as though he was waking up, and at these moments he noticed that it had been night for a long time, but the idea of getting up did not occur to him. Finally, he noticed that it was bright like day. He lay on the couch on his back, still dazed from the recent forgetfulness. The terrible and desperate cries from the street reached him sharply, which, however, he listened to every night under his window at three o'clock. They were what woke him now. "Ah! And there go the drunkards from the drinking dens," he thought, - "Third hour" - and suddenly jumped up as though someone had yanked him off the couch. — What! It's already the third?" He sat on the couch, and then it all came back to him!
