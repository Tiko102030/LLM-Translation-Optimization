Further in, in the courtyard, a low, blackened stone shed appeared behind the fence, apparently part of some workshop. Here, indeed, was some kind of establishment, a carriage or locksmith shop, or something similar; everywhere, almost from the very gates, there was much coal dust on the ground. "Wouldn't it be nice to throw [something] away and leave?" he thought suddenly. Not noticing anyone in the courtyard, he strode into the gates and just saw, right near the gates, a gutter (just as often arranged in houses where there are many factory workers, artisans, cabmen, etc.) attached to the fence; and above the gutter, right on the fence, was written with chalk the usual sharpness: "Stealing is forbidden here". So it's good that he didn't arouse any suspicion by going in and stopping. "Everything would be fine if I could just gather all this somewhere and leave!"

He looked around again and had already stuck his hand into his pocket when, suddenly, near the outermost wall, between the gates and the gutter - where all the distance was only a width of an arshin - he noticed a large uncut stone, weighing roughly about one and a half poods, lying flat against the stone sidewalk. Behind this wall was the street, with a pedestrian path; he could hear people walking by, which there always is a lot of here; but no one behind him would be able to see, unless someone from the street were to come in, which, however, could very well happen, and therefore he needed to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top with both hands firmly, gathered all his strength, and turned over the stone. Under the stone a small depression formed; immediately he started throwing everything from his pocket into it.
