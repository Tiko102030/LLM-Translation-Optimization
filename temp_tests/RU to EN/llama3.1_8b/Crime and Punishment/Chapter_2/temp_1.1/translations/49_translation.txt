He became extremely distracted and forgetful and knew it. It was high time for him to hurry! He walked towards the Neva along the Boulevard; but on his way suddenly a new idea occurred to him: "Why Neva? Why the water? Would it not be better to go somewhere very far away, perhaps back to the Islands, and there in some solitary place, in the woods, under a bush - bury all this and possibly notice the tree?" Although he felt that he couldn't clearly and reasonably discuss everything at this moment, the idea seemed infallible to him. But it wasn't destined for him to reach the Islands, and something else happened: while leaving the Boulevard onto the square, he suddenly saw a gate leading into a courtyard on the left-hand side, surrounded by completely solid walls. To his right, just next to the gate, a distant, unwhitewashed wall of a four-story neighboring house stretched into the courtyard. Left, parallel to the solid wall and also immediately behind the gates, a wooden fence ran for twenty steps deep into the courtyard and then turned sharply left. This was a secluded fenced-in place where some materials were lying about. Further in, in a depression of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooted, stone shed poked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop or other.
