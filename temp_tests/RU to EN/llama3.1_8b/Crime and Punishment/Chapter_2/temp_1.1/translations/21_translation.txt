He took a deeper breath more freely. "Not quite!" Gradually, he began to brighten up, he encouraged himself with all his might to lift his spirits and come to his senses. "Some kind of foolishness, some very trivial carelessness, and I can deceive myself entirely! Ah... too bad there is no air here - stuffiness... My head spins even more... and my mind too..."

He felt a terrible disorder in himself at all times. He was afraid not to have control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about something completely unrelated, but it didn't work out at all. The clerk greatly, by the way, interested him: he wanted very much to guess something from his face, figure it out. This was a young man of twenty-two years old, with a dark-skinned and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than its age, dressed according to the fashion, fat, with a hair parting on his neck, combed and pomaded, with many rings and bracelets on white, cleaned fingers and gold chains on his vest. With one of the foreigners who was here he even said two words in French and very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down, he said briefly to a half-dressed red-haired lady, who stood all the time as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair next to her.
