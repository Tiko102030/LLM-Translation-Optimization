All these sensitive details, milord, don't concern us at all – Illia Petrovich snapped brazenly – you must give an opinion and a promise, and as for what you happened to be in love with and all those tragic places, we have no business with any of that. Well, you... are cruel... Nikodim Fomichev muttered under his breath, sitting down at the table and starting to sign too. He felt somehow ashamed. Write it down, the clerk told Raskolnikov. What is there to write? he asked somewhat gruffly. I'll dictate it to you, the clerk replied. Raskolnikov thought that the clerk began speaking with him more carelessly and scornfully after his confession, but strangely enough, he suddenly felt utterly indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change came somehow in one moment, in a minute. If only he had wanted to think a little about it, of course, he would have been amazed at how he was talking to them like that just a minute ago, even forcing his feelings upon them? And where did those feelings come from?
