The clerk looked at him with a condescending smile of pity and triumph, as if he were a novice who had just been attacked: "What is your current feeling now?" But what did he care about the borrowed letter, about the pursuit? Was it worth any anxiety, in turn, not even attention! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked himself questions, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, the salvation from the pressing danger - that filled him at this moment with his entire being, without foresight, without analysis, without future speculations and riddles, without doubts and questions. It was a moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But in this very moment something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by impoliteness, all inflamed and, apparently, wanting to support the injured pride, attacked the unfortunate "fluffy lady" with all his might, looking at her since he entered with a sickeningly sweet smile. —And you, what happened there last night?
