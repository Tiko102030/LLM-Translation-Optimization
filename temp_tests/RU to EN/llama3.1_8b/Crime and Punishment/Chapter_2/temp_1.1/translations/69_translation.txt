But no, he's too clearly audible! But it seems they'll come to him now if so, "because... well, all this is from the same thing... because of yesterday... Lord!" He wanted to lock himself on a hook, but his hand did not rise... and what's the use anyway! Fear, like ice, enveloped his soul, tormented him, paralyzed him... But finally, that whole din, which had been going on for a faithful ten minutes, began to subside gradually. The landlady was crying out and screaming, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But finally, it seems, he too fell silent; no longer can you hear him; "perhaps he's gone! Lord!" Yes, the landlady is going out now, still sobbing and crying... and her door slammed shut. And the crowd disperses from the staircase to the apartments - they sigh, argue, call out to each other, sometimes raising their voice to a shout, sometimes lowering it to a whisper. There must have been many of them; almost the whole house gathered. "But Lord, is all this possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov was unable to withstand his helplessness and fell onto the sofa, but already couldn't shut his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of boundless horror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya came in with a candle and a soup bowl. Looking at him carefully and noticing that he wasn't asleep, she placed the candle on the table and began to unfold what she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
