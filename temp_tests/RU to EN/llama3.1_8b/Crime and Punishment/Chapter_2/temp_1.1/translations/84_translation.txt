I will bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep astonishment and a dull meaningless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not mad, I think," he thought, "it really is..." Two minutes later Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would be served now as well. For the soup two spoons, two plates, and the entire set of tableware arrived: a saltshaker, a pepper mill, mustard for beef and other things that had not been seen in such an order for a long time. The tablecloth was clean. "Not bad, Nastasya, if Praskovya Pavlovna would send two beer bottles," I said. "We'll drink to it." "Go ahead and try to get some, clever one!" Nastasya muttered under her breath and went off to fulfill my command.
