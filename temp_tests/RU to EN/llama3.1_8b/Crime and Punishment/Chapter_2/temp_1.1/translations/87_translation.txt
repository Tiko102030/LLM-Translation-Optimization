— Any tea? — Tea, perhaps. — Pour some. Wait, I'll pour it myself; sit down at the table. He immediately set about pouring, then poured another cup, threw away his breakfast and resettled on the divan again. Holding onto the sick man with his left hand as before, he lifted him up and began to give him tea with a teaspoon, blowing persistently and especially diligently on the spoon, as if in this process of blowing were the main and life-saving point of recovery. Raskolnikov was silent and did not resist, despite feeling very enough strength to sit up on the divan without any external help, and not only manage his hands well enough to hold a teaspoon or cup, but even possibly walk. But out of some strange, almost beastly cunning it suddenly came into his head to conceal his strength for now, hide, pretend, if necessary, that he was still completely bewildered, yet in the meantime listen and pry into what is happening here? Anyway, he couldn't resist his aversion: having drunk ten spoonsful of tea, he suddenly freed his head, capriciously pushed the spoon away, and fell back onto the pillow.
