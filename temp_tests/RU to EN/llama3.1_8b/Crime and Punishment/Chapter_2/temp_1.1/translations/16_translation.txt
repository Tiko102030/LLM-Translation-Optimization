His head spun and hurt from the heat. "This is a trap! They're trying to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out on the spot," he muttered to himself as he went up the stairs. - It's terrible that I'm almost at my wit's end... I can tell some stupid lie..." On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings exactly where they were, "and maybe they'll search for them just because I wasn't there," he thought and stopped. But a sense of despair and cynicism about his impending doom suddenly took hold of him, so he waved his hand in resignation and continued on. "Just hurry up!" Again, the heat on the street was unbearable; just one drop of rain in these days would have been welcome. Again there was dust, brick, and lime plaster everywhere; again the stench from shops and bars filled the air; again there were drunkards, Chuvash pedlars, and ruined taxi drivers milling about every minute. The sun blazed in his eyes so intensely that it hurt to look, and his head spun even more - a familiar feeling of having a fever, which suddenly hits you as soon as you step out into the bright sunlight. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he anxiously glanced down it at that house... and immediately looked away again. "If asked, I might say something," he thought to himself, approaching the office.
