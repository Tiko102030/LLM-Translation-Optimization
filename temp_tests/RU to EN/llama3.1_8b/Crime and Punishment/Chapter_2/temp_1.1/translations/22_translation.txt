— Louisa Ivanovna, would you sit down — he said with a nod to the somewhat elegantly dressed red-skinned woman who stood as if she couldn't bring herself to sit, despite there being a chair right next to her. — I thank you,[17 - Thank you (German) - Ed.] — she replied and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down in the chair. Her pale blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread out around the chair and took up almost half the room. It smelled of perfume. But it was clear that the woman was nervous about occupying so much space and the scent coming from her, although she was smiling timorously and shamelessly together, but with evident distress. The mourning woman finally finished speaking and stood up to leave. Suddenly, with some noise, very gallantly and in a certain way particularly throwing their shoulders back with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with the cockade onto the table and sat down in the armchair. The elegant woman jumped so high out of her seat when she saw him, and with particular excitement began to bob up and down; but the officer didn't even notice her, and she was no longer able to sit down in front of him anymore.
