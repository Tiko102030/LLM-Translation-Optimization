He was some kind of especially tousled man with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this because it's all the same to him," Raskolnikov thought. — Go there, to the clerk, said the scribe and pointed ahead with his finger, showing to the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in line), cramped and tightly packed with people - a crowd of somewhat better-dressed folk than those in the other rooms. Between visitors were two ladies. One in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, extremely fat and florid, with spots, a visible woman, and rather opulently dressed, with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea saucer, stood off to the side and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. The latter glanced at it briefly, said: "wait" and continued working with the lady in mourning. He took a breath more freely.
