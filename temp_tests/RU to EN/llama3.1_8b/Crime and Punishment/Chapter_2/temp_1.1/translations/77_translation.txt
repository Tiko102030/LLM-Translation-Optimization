Well, and you who are you? — I am an ardent at our office, from the merchant Sholopov's, and I came here on business, that is to say. — Do sit down on this chair, — Razumikhin sat down on another one, on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to come to your senses, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov — you'd barely been eating and drinking for four days. Rightly so, tea with a spoon was being given. I brought Zossimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zossimov? He looked at you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense, — what hit you on the head like that? Some nervous nonsense, says he, bad food, and not enough beer or garlic were given to you, so it must have been that which caused your illness; but nothing, it will pass and be over.
