Then he left the room this time even leaving it wide open. He was walking quickly and firmly, although he felt that he was completely broken, but consciousness was with him. He feared pursuit, he feared that in a quarter of an hour, perhaps half an hour already, there would come out an instruction to follow him; therefore, whatever happened, one had to hide the ends in time. One had to settle things while still having at least some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This decision had been made long ago: "To throw everything into the gutter, and that's it". So he decided last night, in a delirium, during those moments when, he remembered it, he several times was about to get up and go: "As quickly as possible, and throw everything away". But it turned out very hard to throw. He had been walking along the Yekaterininsky Canal Embankment for over half an hour, or maybe more, and several times looked at the points where they met, but it was impossible even to think about carrying out his intention: either pontoons stood right at the points of entry and laundrywomen were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and everywhere people swarmed, and from all directions one could see that a person intentionally came down, stopped, and threw something into the water. And what if the covers won't sink but will float?
