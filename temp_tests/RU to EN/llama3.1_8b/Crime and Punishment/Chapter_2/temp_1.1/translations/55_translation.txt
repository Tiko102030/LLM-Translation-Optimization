He would have just bitten him if someone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva on Vasilevsky Island, next to a bridge. "Here he lives in this house - but how did I come here? What's going on again, just like last time... But it's very interesting: did I really come to see him or was I just passing by and happened to stop here? Doesn't matter; I said three days ago that I'd go see him the next day, well, I'll go. As if I couldn't go there now..."

He went up to Razumikhin's on the fifth floor. He was home in his room, writing, and let him in himself at this moment. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat in a tattered robe, with slippers on one foot, disheveled, bearded and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
