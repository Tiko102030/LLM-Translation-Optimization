His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out completely," he thought to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "It's terrible that I'm almost delirious... I can tell some silly lie..."

On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his things scattered around, "and here they might just search for him on purpose", he recalled and stopped. But such despair and cynicism about death suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand in resignation and continued further. "Just get it over with quickly!"

Outside, the heat was unbearable again; not a single drop of rain had fallen in these days. Again there was dust, brick, and plaster, again the stench from the shops and bars, again drunkards, Chuvash vendors, and half-ruined cab drivers. The sun blazed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely around - a common feeling of someone with a fever suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked anxiously into it, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might just say so," he thought as he approached the office.
