Don't shout! I'm not shouting, I'm speaking quite calmly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and won't allow myself to be shouted down. The clerk was so flustered that in the first minute he couldn't get a word out, and only some spittle flew from his lips. He jumped up from his seat. Let him m-m-mutter! You're in my presence. Don't g-g-growl, sir! — Yes, and you are too in my presence, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides what you're shouting about, you're smoking a cigar, so we're all equally guilty.
