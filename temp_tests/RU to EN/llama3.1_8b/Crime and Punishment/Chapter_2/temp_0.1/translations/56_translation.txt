His face expressed surprise. "What's wrong?" he shouted, examining the entered comrade from head to foot; then fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad? You've outdone our brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when the latter collapsed onto a worn-out Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. "You're seriously ill, don't you know it?" He began to feel Raskolnikov's pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. "Don't bother," he said, "I came... well, I wanted to... but I don't need any lessons anyway..." "Do you know what?"
