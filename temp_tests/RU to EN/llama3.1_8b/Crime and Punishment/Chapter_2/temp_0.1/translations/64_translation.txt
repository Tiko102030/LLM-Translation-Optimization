But Raskolnikov had already stepped out onto the street. On Nikolsky Bridge, he was forced to wake up again completely due to a rather unpleasant incident for him. A coachman from one of the carriages struck him sharply with his whip on the back because he almost got under the horses' hooves, despite the fact that the coachman had shouted at him three or four times. The blow from the whip infuriated him so much that he, jumping away to the railing (he was walking in the middle of the bridge, where vehicles pass, not pedestrians), maliciously clicked his teeth and growled. Of course, laughter echoed all around: "And what's the matter?!" "Some kind of beggar." "It's known, a drunkard will present himself and intentionally run under the wheels; and you'll be responsible for him." "They make their living that way, sir, they make their living..." But at that moment, as he stood by the railing and still looked after the departing carriage with a senseless and angry gaze, rubbing his back, he suddenly felt someone shoving money into his hands.
