There they are, writers! - and he cast a scornful glance at Raskolnikov. - There was a story about him in the tavern three days ago: he ate and doesn't want to pay; "I'll describe you in satire for that," he says. On the steamer too, another one last week, a respectable family of a civil servant, his wife and daughter, he insulted with vile words. From the confectionery recently they threw out one man in a fit. That's what writers are like, literati, students, heralds... ugh! You go away! I'll come to you myself... then watch yourself! Did you hear? Louise Ivanovna began to curtsy all around with a sarcastic politeness and, curtsying, reached the doors; but in the doorway she bumped her backside on one prominent officer, with an open fresh face and magnificent thick fair hair.
