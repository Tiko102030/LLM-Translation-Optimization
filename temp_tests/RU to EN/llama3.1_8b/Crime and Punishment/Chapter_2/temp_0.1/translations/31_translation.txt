And he was opening the window onto the canal and stood in the window like a little pig squealing; and this is shameful. And how can one stand at the window on the street like a little pig squealing; and this is shameful. Fie upon it, fie upon it, fie! And Karl behind him pulled his frock from the window and then, that's true, Mr Captain, he tore off his jacket. And then he shouted that he had to pay fifteen whole marks in fines. And I myself, Mr Captain, paid him five whole marks for a torn jacket. And this is an impolite guest, Mr Captain, and makes all sorts of trouble! I say, I'll have you know, Mr Captain, that I'm a big satirist and will write about you in all the newspapers. — From writers, then? — Yes, Mr Captain, and what kind of impolite guest is this, Mr Captain, when he's in a noble house… — Ah-ah-ah!
