He took a deeper breath more freely. "Not quite!" He gradually began to regain his composure, he tried with all his might to calm down and come to his senses. "Some foolishness, some smallest carelessness, and I can deceive myself completely! Ah... it's a pity there is no air here - the stuffiness... My head spins even more... and my mind too..."

He felt an awful disorder in everything about him. He himself was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything, but that didn't work at all. The scribe strongly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure out what was going on with him. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and fatigued, with a parting on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white-gloved fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner here he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to the brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood as if she didn't dare to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her.
