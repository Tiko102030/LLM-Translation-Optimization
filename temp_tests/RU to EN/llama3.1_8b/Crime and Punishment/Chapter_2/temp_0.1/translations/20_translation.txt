This was some especially disheveled person with a motionless idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from him because it's all the same to him," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, he said and pointed forward with his finger, showing the last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), cramped and packed tightly with people - a crowd of people, somewhat better dressed than those in the other rooms. Between the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at a table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and red-faced, with spots, a noticeable woman, and extremely lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest, as big as a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. He glanced briefly at it, said: "wait" and continued working with the mourning lady. He breathed more freely.
