—But she is my mistress, after all? —Well, what about it, then, mistress? The clerk looked at him with a condescending smile of pity and triumph at the same time, as if he were a novice who had just been bombarded: "How do you feel now?" But what did he care now about the borrowed letter, about the pursuit? Was it worth any anxiety, in turn, even some attention! He stood, read, listened, replied, even asked himself questions, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the pressing danger - that's what filled him at this moment with a complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But in that very moment, something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by the impertinence, still burning and obviously wanting to support the wounded ambition, attacked the unfortunate "puffy lady" with all his might, who had been looking at him since he entered with a stupidly silly smile. —And you, such-and-such and so-and-so, —he suddenly shouted from the bottom of his lungs (the tragic lady had already left), —what happened to you last night?
