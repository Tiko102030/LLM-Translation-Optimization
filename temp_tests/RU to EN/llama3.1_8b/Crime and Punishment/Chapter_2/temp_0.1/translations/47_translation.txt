Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, although he felt that he was completely broken, but his consciousness remained with him. He feared pursuit, he feared that in half an hour, perhaps in a quarter of an hour, instructions would already come out to follow him; therefore, no matter what, he had to bury the ends for now. He had to manage while there were still some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This was decided long ago: "Throw everything into the gutter, and the ends in the water, and that's it." So he had resolved last night, in a delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered it, he several times tried to get up and go: "as quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and throw everything out." But throwing everything out turned out to be very difficult. He had been walking along the embankment of the Ekaterininsky canal for half an hour already, or maybe more, and several times he looked at the stairs into the gutter, where they met him. But it was impossible even to think about carrying out his intention: either rafts stood right next to the stairs and washerwomen washed laundry on them, or boats were moored, and everywhere people swarmed, and from all sides you could see that a person had deliberately gone down, stopped, and thrown something into the water. And what if the boxes don't sink, but float?
