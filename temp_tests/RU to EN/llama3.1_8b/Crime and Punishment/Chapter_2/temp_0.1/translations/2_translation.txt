The sun was shining brightly, and he quickly began to examine himself from head to toe, checking his clothes for any signs of blood. But that wasn't possible: shivering with cold, he started taking off everything and examining it all over again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and, not trusting himself, repeated the examination three times. But there seemed to be nothing; only on the place where his pants had torn at the bottom and hung in fringes, the fringe itself still had thick dried bloodstains. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. There didn't seem to be anything else. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and things he'd pulled out of the old woman's trunk were still stuck in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out yet, let alone hide them! He hadn't even thought about them while examining his clothes! What was this?
