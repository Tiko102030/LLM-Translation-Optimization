There are no clues!" — and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed nervously, with a small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and was laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Street Boulevard, where he met that girl three days ago, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts came into his head. It seemed to him too that it was terribly unpleasant for him now to pass by that bench on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard to meet that old man again who he had given two grivnas to: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and sullenly. All his thoughts were now revolving around one main point, — and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and existed, and that now, exactly now, he was left alone face to face with this main point, — and that it was even for the first time after these two months.
