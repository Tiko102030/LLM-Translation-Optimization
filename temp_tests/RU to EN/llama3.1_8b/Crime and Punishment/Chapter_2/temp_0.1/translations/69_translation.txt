But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, it seems, they will come to him now, if so, "because... yes, all this is from the same... because of yesterday... God!" He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand didn't rise... and what's the use anyway! Fear, like ice, wrapped itself around his soul, tormented him, petrified him... But finally, after that whole commotion, which had been going on for a faithful ten minutes, began to gradually subside. The landlady was still wailing and screaming, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But finally, it seems, he has calmed down; now you can't even hear him; "has he left! God!" Yes, the landlady is leaving too, still wailing and crying... and her door slammed shut. And the crowd disperses from the stairs to the apartments, exclaiming, arguing, calling out to each other, sometimes raising their voices to a shout, sometimes lowering them to a whisper. There must have been many of them; almost the whole house had gathered. "But God, is all this possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov was overcome and fell onto the divan, but he couldn't even close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of boundless horror that he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a bowl of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he wasn't sleeping, she put the candle on the table and began to unpack what she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
