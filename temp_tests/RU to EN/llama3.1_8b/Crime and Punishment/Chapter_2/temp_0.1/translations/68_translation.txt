And suddenly, to his greatest astonishment, he caught a glimpse of the voice of his landlady. She was wailing, screaming and lamenting, rushing, hurrying, uttering words so indistinct that they couldn't be made out, begging for something - of course, for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it could hardly be heard anymore, but still he too was saying something, also quickly and indistinctly, in a hurry and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was the voice of Ilya Petrovich. Ilya Petrovich is here and beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the stairs - that's clear, it can be heard from the sounds, screams, blows! What is this, has the world gone mad? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, on the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, rising, knocking, banging doors, rushing out. "But why, why, and how can this be?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he is hearing it too clearly!
