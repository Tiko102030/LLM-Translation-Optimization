"Don't bother," he said, "I came... well, I have no lessons to give... I was thinking of... but I don't need any lessons anyway."

"Do you know what?" Razumikhin observed, watching him closely. "You're rambling!" "No, I'm not rambling..." Raskolnikov stood up from the sofa. As he rose to face Razumikhin, he hadn't even thought that he would have to confront him face-to-face. Now, in an instant, he realized, with experience, that at this moment he was least disposed to meet anyone face-to-face in the whole world. All his bile rose up in him. He almost choked on his own anger at himself for having just stepped into Razumikhin's apartment. "Goodbye!" he said suddenly and headed towards the door.
