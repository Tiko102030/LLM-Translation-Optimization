— And where, may I ask? — Down the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov replied curtly, abruptly, his whole face as white as a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes from Ilia Petrovich's gaze. — He is barely standing on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fomich had noticed. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said somehow especially. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the clerk, who was also looking very intently at him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
