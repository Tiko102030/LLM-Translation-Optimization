Louisa Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said to the hastily dressed red and pink lady who was standing as if she dared not sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke, I thank you, she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down in the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half the room. It wafted scents of perfume. But the lady was clearly nervous about occupying half the room and that her perfume was so overpowering, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly at the same time, but with obvious distress. The mourning lady finally finished speaking and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially swinging his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. The plump lady jumped right out of her seat at the sight of him and with some special excitement began to curtsy; but the officer didn't even glance at her, and she no longer dared sit down next to him.
