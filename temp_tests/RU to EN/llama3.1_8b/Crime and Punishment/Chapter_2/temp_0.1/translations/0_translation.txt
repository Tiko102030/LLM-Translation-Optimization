He lay there for a very long time. Sometimes it seemed as if he had even woken up and in those moments noticed that it was already late at night, but the thought of getting up didn't come to him. Finally, he realized that it was already light like during the day. He was lying on the couch upside down, still dazed from recent forgetfulness. The terrible, desperate screams from the street reached him sharply, which, however, he listened to every night under his window at three o'clock. They were what woke him up now. "Ah! And it's already out of the bars that drunk people are coming out," he thought - "third hour" - and suddenly jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. "What! It's already the third hour!" He sat on the couch, and then everything came back to him!
