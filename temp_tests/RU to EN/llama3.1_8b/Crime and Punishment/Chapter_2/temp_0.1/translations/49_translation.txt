He became extremely absent-minded and forgetful and knew it. He had to hurry resolutely! He went towards the Neva along Voznesensky Prospekt; but on his way a sudden thought came to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to leave somewhere very far away, perhaps even to the Islands again, and there, in some solitary place, in the forest, under a bush - bury everything and the tree, maybe notice it?" And although he felt that he was not capable of clearly and sensibly discussing everything at this moment, but the thought seemed infallible to him. But he was not destined for the Islands, and something else happened: coming out onto the square from Voznesensky Prospekt, he suddenly saw a gate to the left leading into a courtyard surrounded by completely blank walls. To the right, immediately after entering through the gates, far into the courtyard stretched a blank unwhitewashed wall of a neighboring four-story house. Left, parallel to the blank wall and also now from the gates, went a wooden fence, twenty steps deep into the courtyard, and then it made a turn to the left. This was a secluded place, where some materials lay. Further, in the depression of the courtyard, a corner of a low, blackened stone shed stuck out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
