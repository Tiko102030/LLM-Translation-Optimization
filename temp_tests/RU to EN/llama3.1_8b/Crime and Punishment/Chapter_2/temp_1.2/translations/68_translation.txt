And suddenly, to his utmost amazement, he distinctly heard the voice of his landlady. She was screaming, shrieking, and wailing, rushing, hurrying, uttering words that were almost impossible to make out, begging for something - certainly, for them to stop beating her, because she was being beaten mercilessly on the stairs. The voice of the beater had grown so terrifying from rage and fury that it could hardly be heard anymore, but the beater himself still said something, also quickly, indistinctly, and choking on his words. Suddenly Razumikhin quivered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice. And Ilya Petrovich is beating up the landlady here! He beats her with his feet, knocks her head against the stairs - that's clear from the sounds, the cries, and the blows! What's going on, has the light gone out or something? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, along the entire staircase, voices were heard, exclamation followed one another, people rushed to get out, knocked at the doors, slammed them shut, ran away. "But why is this happening, what can they be thinking of?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone mad completely. But no, he's hearing it all too clearly...
