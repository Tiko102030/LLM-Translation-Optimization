I will bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep amazement and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "Apparently I'm not crazy," he thought, "apparently this is really...".

In two minutes Nastasya returned with soup and announced that now tea would be served too. The table was set with two spoons, two plates, and all the utensils: saltshaker, pepper shaker, mustard for beef and so on, which had not been used in such an order for a long time already. The napkin was clean. "Not bad, Nastasya," I said, "if Praskovya Pavlovna ordered two bottles of beer to be brought. We'll drink up." "You're just being facetious," grumbled Nastasya and went to fulfill my command.
