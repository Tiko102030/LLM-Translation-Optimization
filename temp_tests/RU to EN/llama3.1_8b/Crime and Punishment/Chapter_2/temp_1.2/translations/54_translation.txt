He had no sooner said this than he stopped; a new, quite unexpected and extremely simple question suddenly befuddled him and bitterly amazed him: "If indeed everything was done deliberately, not by accident, if you really did have a definite and firm purpose, then how did you still manage to glance into the wallet and not know what you had received, for which reason you took all your troubles upon yourself and deliberately went to such a vile, disgusting, low act? And didn't you just now want to throw the wallet, along with all those things you haven't seen yet, into the water? This is how it is, indeed; this is all so. But he knew this already; in fact, it wasn't even a new question for him; and when it was decided at night to throw everything into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that's just how it should be, as if otherwise it wouldn't be possible... Yes, he knew all this and remembered it well; yes, it almost felt like yesterday, in that very moment when he sat on a trunk and was pulling out cases from it... Ah, indeed! "It's because I'm extremely ill," he gloomily decided at last, "I've tormented myself, tortured myself, and don't even know what I'm doing... And yesterday, and the day before that, and all this time I've been tormenting myself... I'll recover and won't torture myself anymore... But what if I never do? God! How I'm getting tired of it all!..." He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to distract himself somehow, but he didn't know what to do or what to try. One new, insurmountable feeling had taken hold of him more and more with almost every passing moment: a sense of endless, almost physical revulsion towards everything that was encountered and surrounded him, an obstinate, spiteful, hateful feeling. All the people he met were disgusting - their faces, walk, movements. He wouldn't have bothered to take notice of anyone; it seemed like he would want to bite someone if they spoke to him...
