"From this you won't learn anything, because it doesn't matter to him," thought Raskolnikov. - Go there to the scribe, he said and pointed ahead with his finger, indicating the last room. He entered that room (the fourth in order), a narrow and overcrowded one filled with people, somewhat better dressed than those in the other rooms. Among the visitors were two women. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at a table facing the scribe and was writing something under his dictation. The other woman, very plump and flushed red with blotches, visible woman, and that was very elegantly dressed, with a locket on her chest, as big as a tea plate, stood aside waiting for something.
