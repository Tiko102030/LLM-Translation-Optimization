He then left the room, this time leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt like he was completely broken inside, but his consciousness remained with him. He was afraid of being chased, afraid that in half an hour, perhaps even a quarter of an hour later, an instruction might come out to keep track of him; therefore, no matter what, it was necessary to dispose of the ends before time ran out. He had to manage somehow while there were still some strength and at least some reasoning left... Where to go? This decision had been made a long time ago: "Throw everything into the canal, and that's it." He had come to this conclusion even in his delirium last night, in those moments when he remembered it several times tried to get up and leave: "as quickly as possible, and all over with it". But throwing everything away turned out to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of Ekaterininsky Canal for half an hour already, maybe more, and several times glanced at the points where they met him in the canal. But you couldn't even think about carrying out his intention: either rafts were standing right next to these points and on them laundry was being washed, or boats were moored, and everywhere people are swarming, and from all sides, from the embankments, one can see and suspect: suspiciously, that a person has intentionally descended, stopped and is throwing something into the water. And what if the cases float away instead of sinking?
