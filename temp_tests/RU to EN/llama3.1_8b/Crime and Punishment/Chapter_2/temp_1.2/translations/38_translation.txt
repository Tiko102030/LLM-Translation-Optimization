Where did these feelings come from? Conversely, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with his neighbors but with his closest friends, there still wouldn't be a single human word for them; his heart had become so empty all of a sudden. The dismal feeling of tormenting and endless isolation and estrangement suddenly consciously reflected itself in his soul. It wasn't the baseness of his confessions to Ilia Petrovich or the port officer's triumph over him that turned his heart upside down, but something completely different. Oh, what does he care now about his own baseness, about all these ambitions, officers, Germans, searches, offices and so on? If he had been sentenced even to be burned in this moment, he wouldn't have batted an eyelid, and barely might have paid attention to the sentence. Something was happening to him that was completely new and unfamiliar, sudden and unprecedented. It wasn't as if he understood anything, but he clearly felt with all his being that not only couldn't he talk expansively about his feelings like before, but he also could no longer address these people in the neighborhood office in any way, whether they were his own siblings or mere neighborhood officers; it wouldn't be necessary for him to address them even if it were a matter of life and death; he had never felt anything similar in all the feelings experienced by him up to this moment. And what is most tormenting – it was more than a feeling, more than an awareness, more than an understanding; it was a direct and most tormenting feeling of all those previously experienced by him. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form for such a reply, that is, I don't have the money, I promise then sometime, I won't leave the city, I won't sell or give away any property, etc.
