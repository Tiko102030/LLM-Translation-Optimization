He was becoming incredibly distracted and forgetful and knew it himself. One thing was clear: they had to hurry! He went towards the Neva along V Street; but on his way he suddenly thought of something else: 'Why go to the Neva? Why to the water? Wouldn't it be better to leave somewhere far, far away – again on the Islands and there, in a solitary place, in the woods, under a bush – bury everything and maybe even mark the tree?' Even though he felt that he couldn't clearly and sensibly discuss everything at this moment, the thought seemed infallible. But it wasn't meant to be – instead, while exiting V Street onto the square, he suddenly saw a gate to the left into a yard surrounded by completely solid walls. To the right, immediately next to the entrance gates, a distant dark wall of a neighbouring four-storey house stretched deep into the courtyard. Left of the gate, parallel to the solid wall and now too from the entrance gates, ran a wooden fence for twenty paces deep in the yard and then bent off to the left. This was an isolated area walled off, where some materials were lying about. Further along, recessed in the courtyard, part of a low, smutty stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, clearly belonging to some kind of workshop.
