Further in, at the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, soot-stained, stony shed looked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop or other. There was probably some kind of establishment there, a carriage-making or locksmith's shop, or something like that; everywhere, almost from the very gates, a thick layer of coal dust was visible. "Just to throw it somewhere and get away!" he suddenly thought to himself. Not noticing anyone in the courtyard, he strolled through the gates and caught sight of this just as he was leaving near the gates, a small trough had been fixed up against the fence (just like they often do in houses with many factory workers, association members, cabmen and so on), and above the trough, immediately next to it on the fence, there was written in chalk the usual pithiness in such cases: "Standing here is forbidden". Well, that was even better; no suspicion at all of him having gone inside or stopped. "Here everything would be so easily cleared up and thrown into a heap somewhere and I'd get out!" He glanced around one more time and just as he was putting his hand back in his pocket, when suddenly, right next to the outer wall, between the gates and the trough, where all the space was only an arm's length wide, he noticed a large unworked stone, roughly perhaps a pood and a half in weight, lying flat against the stonewall. On the other side of this wall was the street, with its sidewalk; one could hear passersby scurrying by - there were always plenty of them here; but nobody would be able to see him behind the gates unless someone from the street happened to enter, which, as it turned out, was quite possible and therefore he should hurry. He bent down to the stone, seized its upper edge tightly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned over the stone. A small hollow formed beneath the stone; at once he began throwing everything from his pocket into it.
