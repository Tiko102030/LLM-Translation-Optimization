His face expressed surprise. "What's the matter?" he shouted, inspecting his friend from head to foot; then fell silent and whistled. "Not so bad, surely? You, brother, outdid our brother in raggedness," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when the guest collapsed onto the cloth Turkish divan that was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. "Seriously ill, do you know it?" He began to feel for his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. "No need," he said, "I came... here's the thing: I don't have any lessons at all... I wanted to... but in fact I don't need lessons..."
