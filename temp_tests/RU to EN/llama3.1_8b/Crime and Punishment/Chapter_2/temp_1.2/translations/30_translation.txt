— Ilya Petrovich! — began the secretary solicitously, but stopped to wait for a moment because the bubbling-officer was impossible to hold back without grabbing him by the arms, which he knew from his own experience. As for the lush lady, at first she did tremble with thunder and lightning; but strangely: as her insults grew more numerous and stronger, so did her appearance become lovelier, her smile turning charmed toward the portly officer. She was quivering in place and continuously squatting, impatiently waiting for the moment when finally they would let her say something, and she waited. — There won't be any noise or fight with me, Mr Captain, she suddenly shouted like corn sprouting up with a thick German accent, although speaking rapidly in Russian—there won't be any scandal at all, but they came drunk, and I'll tell you all about it, Mr Captain, and I'm not guilty… My noble house, Mr. Captain, has a noble reception, Mr. Captain, and I have always, always myself wanted no scandals. But they came altogether drunk and then again three of them asked me to go somewhere, and one of them stood up on his legs and began playing the pianoforte with his feet, which is very unseemly in a noble house; he started smashing the pianoforte, completely out of all manor in this place, and I shouted. He took the bottle and began pushing everyone from behind with it. And just as I quickly called the janitor to order Karl, he took Karl and glared at him, then he also glared at Henriette; but they five times slapped me on the cheek. It is so indecent in a noble house, Mr. Captain, that's why I shouted. But he opened the window on the canal and stood at the window like a small pig, squealing; it's an insult.
