He would have cared little about anyone, it seemed he would bite, if only someone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he got out onto the Malaya Neva embankment on Vasilevsky Island near a bridge. "That's where he lives in this house," he thought. "What is this? But how did I get here myself? The same story as before... Although, very interesting: did I come here or just happened to be walking by?" He shrugged it off. "I said I'd come back the third day, so I'll go. It's not like I can't visit now..."

He went up to Razumikhin in the fifth floor apartment. He was at home, in his little room, writing and he himself let Raskolnikov in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat at his threadbare table, wearing a tattered dressing gown, with one foot bare, tousled and unshaven. His face showed surprise.
