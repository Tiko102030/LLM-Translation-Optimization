His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is trickery! They want to lure me in with cunning and then suddenly knock me down completely," he muttered to himself as he exited onto the stairs. - It's bad that I'm almost out of my mind... I can tell some stupid lie..."

On the staircase, he remembered leaving all his belongings scattered around like this - "and there's a chance they might search for them without me", he recalled and stopped. But a sense of desperation and such cynicism suddenly took hold of him, that he waved his hand in resignation and continued further. "Just hurry up!"

On the street, the heat was unbearable once again; not even a drop of rain in these days. Again dust, brick and plaster powder, again the stench from food stalls and taverns, again drunkards, Chuvash peddlers and broken-down cab drivers every minute. The sun blazed in his eyes so brightly that it became painful to look at him; his head spun completely around - a typical sensation of a fever-ridden person who suddenly comes out onto the street on a bright sunny day. Reaching the turn into yesterday's street, he looked nervously into it, towards that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might say something," he thought, approaching the office.
