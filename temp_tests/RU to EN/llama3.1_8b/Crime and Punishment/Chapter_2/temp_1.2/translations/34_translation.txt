Louisa Ivanovna with benevolent good humor got up and began to squint around, crouching down to the doors; but in the doorway she ran into one prominent officer from behind with an open fresh face and magnificent thick light-blond whiskers. This was Nicholas Fomich himself, the district supervisor. Louisa Ivanovna hastily squatted almost to the floor and rapidly hopping on her toes flew out of the office. — Once again thunderclap, once again thunderstorm and lightning, whirlwind and hurricane! — Nicholas Fomich benevolently and friendly turned to Ilya Petrovich, — once more agitated the heart, once more it boiled over! He even heard from the staircase. — What! — said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and not what, but: "Da-а sh-ta-а!") , moving with some papers to another table and comically swinging his shoulders with each step where a step was, there was also a shoulder; — here's an example, if you see fit: the writer, in other words, the student, who is past his due payment of money, gave out bonds, didn't clean up the apartment, constant complaints are coming to them, and as it happens they came up with a claim that I lit a pipe near them! They themselves flatter and look at these: — Poverty is not a vice, comrade, well what can you do anyway.
