Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down, he said to the partly dressed red and pink lady, who was standing like she wasn't daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair next to her.

—I thank you, — she said and quietly, with a silk sound, sat down in the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair, like an air balloon, almost taking up half the room. It smelled of perfume. But the lady seemed nervous about occupying half the room and smelling of perfume, although she was timidly and impudently smiling at the same time, but clearly in a state of distress.

The mourning woman finally finished and stood up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and as if especially twisting his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat down in an armchair. The full-figured lady jumped out of her place upon seeing him and with particular excitement began to curtsy; but the officer didn't even notice her, and she no longer dared to sit down next to him.
