But just then the door swung open wide again and Razumikhin, slightly leaning forward because of his height, came in. — What a room it is like a cabin! — he exclaimed as he entered, — I always bump my forehead against it; after all, that's what we call a flat! And you, brother, are you awake yet? — he shouted to her. — I've just come from Pashenka. — Yes, I'm coming around now, Nastasya said. — We're coming around too, Razumikhin again echoed with a smile. — And who might you be then? — asked Razumikhin, suddenly turning to him. — Here is Vrazumikhin; not Razumikhin as everyone calls me, but Vrazumikhin, a student and a noble's son, and he is my friend.
