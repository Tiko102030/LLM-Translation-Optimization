But no, he hears everything too clearly!... But if so, then probably they'll come to him now, "because... that's true, all this is because of yesterday... Lord!" He wanted to lock himself up, but his hand didn't rise... and it wouldn't do any good anyway! Fear had frozen over him like ice, tormented him, chilled him to the bone… But at last, after a whole ten minutes of this din, which was gradually subsiding. The landlady kept wailing and moaning, Ilya Petrovich continued to threaten and swear... But finally, it seemed, he too quieted down; now there's no sound from him; "has he gone? Lord!" Yes, the landlady is leaving, still making a loud noise and crying… the door slammed shut behind her... The crowd has dispersed into the apartments, they're shouting, arguing, talking to each other in raised voices one moment and whispering the next. There must have been quite a few of them; almost the whole house came running out. "But Lord have mercy! Could all this be possible? And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov collapsed on the sofa in powerlessness, but couldn't close his eyes anymore; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of infinite horror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya came in with a candle and a tray of soup. Looking at him carefully and noticing that he wasn't asleep, she put the candle on the table and began to unpack the things she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
