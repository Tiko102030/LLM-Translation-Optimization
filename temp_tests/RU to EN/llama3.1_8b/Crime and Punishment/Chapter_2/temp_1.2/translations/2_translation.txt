The daylight had come to an end, and he began more quickly than ever to look over himself from head to foot in his whole attire: were there any traces? But that couldn't be done; trembling with the shivers, he took off everything from himself again and looked round. He turned everything inside out, to the last thread and shred, and didn't trust himself, repeating the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no sign of anything; only on that spot where the trousers had frayed down at the bottom and hung in tassels, those tassels left thick streaks of dried blood. He caught hold of a folding large knife and cut off the tassel. There didn't seem to be any more now. Suddenly he remembered that the purse and things which he had pulled out from the old woman's trunk were all still in his pockets! He hadn't even thought about them yet, pulling out my own clothes and inspecting them so thoroughly!
