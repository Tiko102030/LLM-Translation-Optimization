— Don't shout! I'm not shouting, I'm speaking very calmly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and won't permit myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so flustered that in the first minute he couldn't say anything intelligible; only some spittle escaped from his lips. He jumped up from his seat. — Allow yourself to MA-ALCH-CH-CH! You are present. Do not GR-R-RU-BI-A-N-I-T-E, sir! — And you are present too, — screamed Raskolnikov, — and besides that you're shouting, you smoke a cigarette; therefore we all offend everyone. — After uttering this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
