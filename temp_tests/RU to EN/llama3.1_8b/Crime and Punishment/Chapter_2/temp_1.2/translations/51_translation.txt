A small depression had formed under the stone; he immediately began throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet fell right on top and yet there was still room for more in the depression. He then grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one movement to its original side, and it landed precisely where it originally was, slightly higher than before, but almost imperceptibly so. But he swept away some dirt and crushed it down with his foot on either side. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed towards the square. Again a strong, barely bearable joy, just like yesterday in the office, took hold of him for an instant. "Buried at last! And who would even think to look under this stone? It might be lying here since the house was built and will probably lie here just as long."
