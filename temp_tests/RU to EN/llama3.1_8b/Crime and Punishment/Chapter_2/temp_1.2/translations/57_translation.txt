"Don't," he said, "I came... to say this: I don't have any lessons anyway... I was going to... but it doesn't matter at all."

"You know what?" observed Razumikhin, who had been observing him intently, "You're talking nonsense!" 
"No, I'm not being ridiculous..." Rascolnikov stood up from the couch. As he rose towards Razumikhin, he hadn't even thought about the fact that they were now face to face with each other. Now, in a single instant, having gained experience, he realized that at this moment, he was least inclined to meet face to face with anyone in the whole world. A surge of indignation rose up in him. He almost choked on his own anger towards himself, who had just stepped over Razumikhin's threshold. 
"Goodbye!" he said suddenly and went towards the door.
