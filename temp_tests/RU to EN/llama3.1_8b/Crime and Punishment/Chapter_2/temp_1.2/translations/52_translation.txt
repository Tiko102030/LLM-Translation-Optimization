It's all over, there are no proofs!" — and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he laughed nervously, faintly, almost inaudibly, for a long time, as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard where two days ago he met that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts crawled into his head. It also seemed to him now incredibly disgusting to pass by that bench again on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be also very difficult to meet that old man again whom he gave a two-grivna tip to: "Damn him!"

He was walking around, glancing listlessly and malevolently. All his thoughts were now swirling around one single main point, — and he himself felt that indeed this is such a main point and exists and that now, precisely now, he's left alone face to face with it for the first time in two months. "Damn all of this!"
