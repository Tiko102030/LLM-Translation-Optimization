All these sensitive details, my sirs, do not concern us - Illya Petrovich snorted, — you must give a review and a commitment, and as for what you've deigned to fall in love with and all those tragic places, we don't care about it at all. 

Well... You're... merciless... — Nikodim Fomich muttered, sitting down at the table and also starting to sign. He felt somehow ashamed. 

Write on, — said the scribe to Raskolnikov. 
What to write? — he asked somehow particularly roughly. 
And I'll dictate it to you, — Rascoolnikov was told. 
Raskolnikov thought that the scribe became less attentive and contemptuous towards him after his confession, but strangely enough, — suddenly he began to feel entirely indifferent to anyone's opinion at all, and this change occurred somehow in one moment, in one minute. If only he wanted to think for a little while, he would have certainly been surprised by how he could speak with them like that just a minute ago, and even impose his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
