If asked, I might tell him so, he thought as he approached the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away from him. It had just moved to a new apartment, in a new house, on the fourth floor. He had been there briefly once, but very long ago. As he entered through the gates, he saw a staircase to the right down which went an old man with a book in his hands: "a janitor, that's what; so the office must be here," and he began to climb up randomly. He did not want to ask anyone anything about anything. "I'll enter, get on my knees, and tell everything..." he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and full of filth. All kitchens in all apartments in all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood there almost the whole day. That's why it smelled terrible.
