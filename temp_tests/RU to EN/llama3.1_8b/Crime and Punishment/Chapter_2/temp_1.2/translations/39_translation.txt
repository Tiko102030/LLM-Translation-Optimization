The clerk began to dictate a standard dismissal letter, meaning that he couldn't pay, promised something someday, wouldn't leave the city, wouldn't sell or give away his property and so on – "You can't write," remarked the clerk, looking at Razumikhin with curiosity, "your pen is slipping out of your hands." "Are you ill?" "Yes... my head is spinning..." "Go on, then!" The clerk took back the paper and attended to others. Razumikhin handed over his pen but instead of standing up and leaving, he put both elbows on the table and clasped his head with his hands. They were hammering a nail into his forehead. A strange idea suddenly came to him: stand up now, approach Nikodim Fomich and tell him everything that had happened yesterday, every detail, then go together to their flat and point out things there in the corner in a crack. The urge was so strong that he had already risen from his seat to carry it out.
