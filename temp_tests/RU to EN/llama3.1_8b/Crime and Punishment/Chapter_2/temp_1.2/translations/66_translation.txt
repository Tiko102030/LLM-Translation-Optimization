The pain from the flogging had subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one anxious and not very clear idea occupied him now exclusively. He stood there looking far away for a long time, scrutinizing the place closely; it was especially familiar to him. When he used to go to university, he would often - almost always, in fact - return home by this route, stopping on this same spot, intently studying the truly magnificent panorama, and each time almost marveling at his own vague and inexplicable impression. A mysterious cold seemed to blow over him from this magnificent panorama; a silent and deaf spirit seemed to be poured into this lush picture... He marveled every time at his own gloomy and enigmatic impression, putting off an explanation for the future, not trusting himself. Suddenly he remembered these past questions and perplexities of his and thought it was no accident that they came back to him now. One thing struck him as strange and incredible - that he had stopped on this very spot, just like before, as if he really believed he could think the same thoughts now as before, be interested in the same old topics and pictures as... so recently. He even felt it was almost laughable, at the same time constricting his chest with pain. In some depth, below, somewhere vaguely visible underfoot, it seemed to him that all this past had now emerged from a distant recess; old thoughts, old problems, old themes, and all these impressions and the panorama itself - he himself, everything... It was as if he were flying upwards and it all disappeared from his eyes. One involuntary movement of his hand made him suddenly feel something tightly clutched in his fist. He relaxed his fist, intently gazed at the coin, swung his arm, and threw it into the water; then turned around and went home. It seemed to him that he had cut himself off from everything and everyone with scissors just now.
