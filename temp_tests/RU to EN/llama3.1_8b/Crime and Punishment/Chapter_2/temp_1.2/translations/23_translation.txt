The plump lady had nearly jumped up from her seat when she saw him and with a special excitement started squatting down; but the officer didn't even notice her slightest attention, and she was no longer allowed to sit next to him. This was an ensign, the assistant inspector, with his reddish-brown sideburns horizontally protruding on both sides and incredibly small facial features, which expressed nothing particularly unusual except for a certain boldness. He glanced aside, partly indignant, at Raskolnikov: the suit on him was too shabby, and despite all the humility, he still didn't have an unremarkable posture; Raskolnikov looked at him, unfortunately directly and for too long, so that he even took offense.

"Want something?" - He shouted probably surprised that such a ragamuffin doesn't even try to hide from his lightning-fast gaze. 

"For the letter... of summons... "- Raskolnikov answered awkwardly. 

"This is about collecting money from them, from the student," the clerk hurried to explain while taking a break from writing.
