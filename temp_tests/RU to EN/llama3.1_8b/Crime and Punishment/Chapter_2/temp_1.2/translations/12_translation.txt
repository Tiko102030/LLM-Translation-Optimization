He wasn't responding and was holding a paper, not printing it out. "Don't get up," Nastasya continued, commiserating and seeing that he was sliding his legs off the sofa. "If you're sick, don't get up: it won't burn down. What's in your hands?" He glanced over; in his right hand there were cut-off pieces of fringe, a sock, and tatters from a ripped pocket. And so he slept with them. Later on, thinking about this, he remembered that when he was half-asleep in the heat, he had clutched everything tightly in his hand and gone back to sleep again. "And look at all these rags he's gathered up and sleeps with, just like a treasure trove...". And Nastasya burst out laughing her hysterical laugh. In an instant he stuffed it all under the greatcoat and fixed his eyes on it. Although he could hardly make any sense of things at that moment, he felt that people wouldn't be treating him kindly when they came to take him away.
