The plump lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and took on a certain special enthusiasm for squatting; but the officer paid no attention to her whatsoever, and she was already afraid to sit next to him again. This was an ensign, assistant to the district inspector, with reddish-gray sideburns sticking out horizontally in both directions and extremely small facial features that expressed nothing particularly noteworthy except perhaps a certain brashness. He glanced at Raskolnikov from the side and partly with indignation: his uniform was just too shabby, and despite all the humiliation, his posture was still not up to par; Raskolnikov, through carelessness, looked at him straight on for too long, so that the other even felt offended. "What's it to you?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin didn't even think to cower before his lightning-fast glance. "The summons... was required..." Raskolnikov replied somehow awkwardly. "It's about recovering money from them – from the student," the clerk hurried along, breaking away from the papers. "Here!
