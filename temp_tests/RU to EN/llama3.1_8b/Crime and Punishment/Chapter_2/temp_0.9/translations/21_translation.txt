He took a freer breath. "Not quite!" he thought to himself slowly recovering his spirits and trying to clear his head. "Some trivial mistake, some slight carelessness, and I will give myself away! Ah... pity that there's no air here - it's stifling... my head is spinning even more... and so is my mind..."

He felt a terrible disorder in all of himself. He was afraid he wouldn't be able to control himself. He tried to grasp something and think about something completely unrelated, but it wasn't working at all. The scribe greatly interested him: he wanted to make some sort of educated guess from the look on his face, to unravel him. This was a very young man of twenty-two years, with a swarthy and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than its years, dressed according to the latest fashion and slim, with a hair parting on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and bracelets on his white, polished fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one of the foreigners who had been there he even spoke a couple of words in French and did so very satisfactorily. — Louis Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to the elegantly dressed redhead standing nearby, who seemed hesitant to sit herself although there was an empty chair next to her.
