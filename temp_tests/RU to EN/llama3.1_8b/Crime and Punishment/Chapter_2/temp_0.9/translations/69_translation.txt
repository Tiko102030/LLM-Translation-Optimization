No, he hears it too clearly! But, apparently, they will come to him now, if so, "because... right, everything is because of yesterday... Oh Lord!" He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand did not rise... and what's the use! Fear, like ice, covered his soul, tormented him, chilled him... But finally this whole scene, which had been going on for exactly ten minutes, began to subside. The landlady was still groaning and screaming, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But finally, it seemed, he too calmed down; now you can't even hear him; "has he left! Oh Lord!" Yes, and the landlady is leaving too, still groaning and crying... and her door has just slammed shut. And now the crowd disperses through the apartments from the stairs, they sigh, argue, shout to each other, sometimes raising their voice to a scream, sometimes lowering it to a whisper. There must have been many of them; almost the whole house gathered there. "But God, is all this possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov collapsed in despair onto the divan, but couldn't even close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour, suffering and experiencing unbearable feelings of infinite horror that he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a plate of soup. Having looked at him carefully and noticing that he wasn't sleeping, she put the candle on the table and began to lay out what she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, and a spoon.
