Further into the courtyard, peering out from behind the fence was a low, blackened stone shed, apparently part of some kind of workshop. There, undoubtedly, was some sort of business, a carriage or locksmith's shop, or something similar; everywhere, almost right up to the gates, coal dust was scattered about. "How nice it would be to throw this somewhere and leave!" he suddenly thought. Not noticing anyone in the courtyard, he walked through the gates and just happened to see at that moment, near the gates, a groove (as is often set up in such houses where there are many factory workers, artisans, cab drivers, etc.) next to the fence; above the groove, right on the fence, was written in chalk the usual sharpness of this kind: "Stealing here is forbidden". Well, it's good that he didn't arouse any suspicion that he had come and stopped. "Everything is so neat all at once and I can just throw this somewhere and leave!"

Glancing around one more time, he already stuck his hand in his pocket when suddenly, right next to the outer wall, between the gates and the groove, where the distance was only about an arshin wide, he noticed a large unworked stone, weighing roughly a pound and a half, lying flat against the stone city wall. Beyond this wall was the street, the sidewalk; it could be heard that people were scurrying along here, as there are always many of them here; but no one could see him behind the gates, unless someone from the street happened to come in, which, however, was very possible, and so he had to hurry. He bent down next to the stone, grabbed it at its top with both hands, gathered all his strength, and turned the stone over. Underneath the stone a small depression formed; immediately he started throwing everything from his pocket into it.
