—Well, she is my mistress, isn't she? —And what about it? The clerk looked at him with a condescending smile of pity and triumph at the same time, as if at a novice who had just been bombarded: "How do you feel now?" But what business did he have with this borrowed letter, this debt collection! Was it worth even any concern, let alone attention? He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked himself, but all of this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the pressing danger - this filled him at that moment with a complete, direct, pure animal joy. But in that very minute, something like thunder and lightning struck in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by his impertinence, full of indignation and obviously wanting to support the wounded pride, attacked the unfortunate "puffy lady" who had been looking at him with a ridiculously stupid smile since he entered, with all his might. —And you, such-and-such-a-one and so-and-so-a-one, —he suddenly shouted to the top of his lungs (the mournful lady had already left), what happened to you last night?
