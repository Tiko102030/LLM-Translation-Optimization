He wasn't answering and was holding paper in his hands, not printing it out. — Yes, don't get up, Nastasya continued, sympathizing and seeing that he was pulling his legs off the couch. — If you're sick, just stay there: you won't burn down. What's all this he has in his hands? He looked: in his right hand he had cut-off pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps torn from a pocket. That's how he slept with them. Then later, thinking about it, he remembered that even while half-asleep in the heat, he had tightly clenched everything in his hand and fell asleep again. — And look at all these rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, exactly like treasure... — Nastasya burst into a nervous laugh. He immediately stuffed everything under his greatcoat and intensely stared at it with his eyes. Even though he could barely make sense of things in that moment, he felt that people wouldn't treat him the way they would someone else when they came to take him away.
