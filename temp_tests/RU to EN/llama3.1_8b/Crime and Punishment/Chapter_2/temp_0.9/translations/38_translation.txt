But where did these feelings come from? On the other hand, if the room were suddenly filled with his closest friends instead of petty officials, he would not have a single human word for them, to such an extent had his heart suddenly emptied out. A dismal feeling of painful and endless isolation and alienation suddenly became aware in his soul. It was not the baseness of his emotional outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, nor the lieutenant's triumph over him that turned his heart around so suddenly. Oh, how much he didn't care now about his own baseness, all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, demands, offices and so on, and so on! If they were to sentence him to be burned in this moment, he wouldn't have even stirred, and probably wouldn't have listened carefully to the verdict. Something was being done with him that was completely unfamiliar, new, sudden and never before experienced by him. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt with all the force of feeling that not only could he no longer be sensitive in his expansive way, as before, but that he could no longer even address these people at the quarter office in any way, whether they were his own brothers and sisters or petty lieutenants, and it would then have been completely unnecessary for him to address them and even at all costs of life; he had never experienced a similar strange and terrifying feeling before. And what was most tormenting – this was more than a consciousness, more than an idea; a direct and most tormenting sensation, the most terrible of all sensations that had ever been felt by him in his life. The scribe began to dictate to him the form of an ordinary response in such cases, i.e., I cannot pay, I promise sometime (somewhere), I won't leave the city, I won't sell or give away my property and so on.
