There weren't even any matches. No, it would be better to get up and throw everything out somewhere. Yes! It's better to throw it all out! — he repeated, sitting down on the sofa again, — right now, this very moment, no delay!... But instead of that his head leaned back against the pillow once more; an unbearable chill came over him once more; he pulled his coat up around himself once more. And for a long time, several hours, it still kept occurring to him in fits: "If only I could go somewhere right now and throw everything out, so as to get rid of it finally, as soon as possible, quickly!" He made an effort to get up from the sofa several times, wanted to stand up, but couldn't anymore. A strong knock on the door finally woke him up. — "Open up, are you alive? And you're sleeping all day like a dog! You sleep away whole days!" cried Nastasya, knocking her fist against the door.
