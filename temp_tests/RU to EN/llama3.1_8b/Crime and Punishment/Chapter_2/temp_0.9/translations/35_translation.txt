Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but well, what can one do! It's known that powder couldn't take an insult. You probably annoyed him somehow and you yourselves couldn't help it, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Razumikhin in a courteous manner – but it's all pointless: the most -i-bla-ga-a-ar-r-rod-nest human being, I'll tell you, but powder, powder! He burst out, boiled over, burned out – and there's nothing left! And everything has passed! And as a result, only one piece of gold in the heart remains! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

— And what an excellent regiment he was in! exclaimed Il'ia Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so pleasantly teased, but still stirring Razumikhin awake. Suddenly, Razumikhin wanted to say something unusually pleasant to them all. – Ah, forgive me, Captain, began he quite freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly – take an interest in my situation... I'm even willing to ask them for forgiveness if there was some failure on my part.
