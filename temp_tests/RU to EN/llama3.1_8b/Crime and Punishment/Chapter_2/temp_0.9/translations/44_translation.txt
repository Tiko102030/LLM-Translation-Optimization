— And where, may I ask? — On the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered curtly, hastily, entirely pale like a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes to Ilya Petrovich's gaze. He barely stood on his legs, and you... — Nikodim Fyodorovich had noticed. — Nothing! — Ilia Petrovich spoke somehow especially. Nikodim Fyodorovich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the clerk, who also looked very intently at him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
