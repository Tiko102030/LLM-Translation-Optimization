His face expressed astonishment. "What's the matter?" he shouted, examining from head to foot the entering comrade; then he fell silent and whistled. "Really that bad?" He added, looking at Ragachev's tatters, "You've outdone our brother, you know." "Come sit down, you must be exhausted!" - And when the other collapsed onto a torn Turkish divan which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. "Are you seriously ill, do you realize?" He began to feel his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. "No need, I came... here's the thing: no lessons for me at all... I wanted to... well, I don't need any lessons..." "And know what?"
