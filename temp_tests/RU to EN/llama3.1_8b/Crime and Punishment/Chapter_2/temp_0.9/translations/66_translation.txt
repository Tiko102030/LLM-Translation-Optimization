The pain from the whip had subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one anxious and not quite clear thought now occupied him exclusively. He stood there and gazed far off for a long time, attentively; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to the university, it often happened that, returning home, he would stop right at this very same spot, intently gaze at this indeed magnificent panorama and every time almost be astonished by his own obscure and unresolved impression. An inexplicable chill always blew over him from this magnificent panorama; the spirit was silent and deaf for him in this lush picture... He marveled each time at his own gloomy and mysterious impression and put off solving it, not trusting himself to do so, until later. Now suddenly he sharply remembered his old questions and perplexities and it seemed to him that he hadn't recalled them by chance. Just one thing struck him as strange and wonderful: that he had stopped in the same spot again just like before, as if he really believed that now he could think about the very same things and be interested in the very same topics and images as before... even so recently. It almost seemed funny to him and at the same time his chest constricted with pain. Somewhere down deep, below, where one can barely see beneath their feet, it seemed to him that all this past was now visible - his old thoughts, his old problems, his old topics of interest, and his old impressions, and the whole panorama, and he himself, and everything... It seemed as though he was flying up somewhere, and everything was disappearing from his eyes. Having made an involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a two-ruble coin gripped in his fist. He relaxed his hand, intently looked at the coin, swung his arm back, and threw it into the water; then turned around and headed home. It seemed to him that he had somehow cut himself off from everything and everyone with scissors just now.
