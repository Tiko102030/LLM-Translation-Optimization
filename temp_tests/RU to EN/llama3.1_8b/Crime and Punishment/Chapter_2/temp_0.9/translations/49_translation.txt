He was becoming increasingly absent-minded and forgetful and he knew it. He had to hurry! He walked along the V-sky Prospect towards the Neva; but on his way another thought suddenly occurred to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to go somewhere far, far away, maybe back to the Islands, and there in some solitary spot, in a forest, under a bush, bury everything and probably mark the tree?" Although he felt that he was unable at this moment clearly and sensibly to discuss everything, the idea seemed infallible to him. But it wasn't his fate to get to the Islands; something else happened: as he came out onto the square from the V-sky Prospect, he suddenly saw to the left an entrance into a courtyard surrounded by completely solid walls. To the right, immediately after the entrance through the gates, far into the courtyard stretched a solid unbegreened wall of a neighboring four-story house. Left, parallel to the solid wall and now at the gate, ran a wooden fence, twenty steps deep into the courtyard, then turning left. It was a secluded fenced area where some materials were lying. Further in the depression of the courtyard, peeped out from behind the fence the corner of a low, soot-stained, stone shack, obviously part of some workshop.
