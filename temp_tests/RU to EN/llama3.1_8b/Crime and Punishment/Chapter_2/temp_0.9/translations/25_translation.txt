For goodness' sake, don't shout! — I'm not shouting at all, but speaking very calmly and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and won't allow myself to shout. The clerk was so worked up that in the first minute he couldn't say anything and only some sort of spittle flew out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat.

Let mal-eech-ka, let mal-eech-ka! You're present here. Don't grrr-grrr-groan, sir! — Yes, and you are present too, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and apart from shouting, you smoke a pipe, therefore we all make fools of ourselves.

— Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable delight. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
