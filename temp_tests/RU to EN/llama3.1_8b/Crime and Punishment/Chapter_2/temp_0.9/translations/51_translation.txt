Under the stone a small depression had formed; he immediately began throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and still there was room left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it around with one rotation to its original side, and it fitted exactly back in its original place, perhaps just a little higher than before. But he pushed dirt under the stone and pressed down on both sides with his foot. There was nothing noticeable. Then he left and headed towards the square. Again strong and barely bearable joy seized him for a moment, as it had yesterday in the office. "The ends are buried! And who would even think of looking for them under this stone? It's probably been lying here since the house was built and will still lie there for just as long."
