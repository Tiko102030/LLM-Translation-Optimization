And lo, to the greatest astonishment, he suddenly caught a glimpse of his landlady's voice. She was howling, shrieking and wailing in haste, rushing out words so fast that they could hardly be distinguished, begging for something - undoubtedly begging them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater became so frightful with malice and fury that he could only grunt, but still, the beater too said something of sorts, in a similar rush, incomprehensibly. Suddenly Raskolnikov quivered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! And Ilya Petrovich is here beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, banging her head against the steps - that's clear from the sounds, the cries and the blows. What is happening, has the light turned upside down? It could be heard how a crowd gathered in all the floors, on the entire staircase; voices, exclamations, footsteps were rising, knocking and doors slamming shut. "But why, but why is this possible?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he's hearing it too clearly...
