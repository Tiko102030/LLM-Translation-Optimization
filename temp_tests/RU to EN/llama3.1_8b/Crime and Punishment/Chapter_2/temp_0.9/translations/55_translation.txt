Just stepped on someone if he had talked to him somehow... He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge. "Here he lives in this house - what is it, and I didn't come here by chance! Again the same story as then... But very interesting: did I come myself or just happened to be there? Doesn't matter; I said... three days ago... that I would go see him the next day, well, and I will go! As if I couldn't have gone in now..." He went up to Razumikhin's place on the fifth floor. The latter was at home in his cell, busy writing, and let Raskolnikov in himself. It had been four months since they hadn't seen each other. Razumikhin sat in his tattered dressing gown, slippers on one foot, disheveled, unshaven and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
