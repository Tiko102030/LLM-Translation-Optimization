Then he tried to get up from where he was, wanted to run away, but always someone would stop him by force, and he would fall into powerlessness and a daze again. Finally, he came to himself completely. This happened in the morning, at ten o'clock. At this hour of the morning, on clear days, the sun would always pass through his right wall in a long strip and illuminate the corner near the door. Next to his bed stood Nastasya, and another person who was very curiously looking at him and completely unknown to him. This was a young man in a caftan with a beard, and he looked like an artralist from afar. The landlady was sticking her head out of half-opened door. Raskolnikov raised himself up. — Who is this, Nastasya? he asked, pointing to the young man.
