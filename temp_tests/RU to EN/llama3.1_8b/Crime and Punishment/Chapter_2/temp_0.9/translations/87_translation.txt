— Would you like some tea? —Tea, I suppose. — Pour it then. Hold on, I'll pour it myself; sit down at the table. He immediately gave orders to have it poured, and then he poured another cup too, threw his breakfast aside and sat back down on the sofa again. Still holding the patient's head in his left hand, he lifted him up and began feeding him tea from a teaspoon, blowing repeatedly and especially vigorously on the spoon as if this blowing were the most crucial point of recovery. Raskolnikov was silent and did not resist, despite feeling quite capable of sitting up on the sofa without any outside help and not only holding his hands well enough to hold a cup or spoon but even walking. But by some strange, almost bestial cunning it suddenly occurred to him to conceal his strength for now, hide, pretend if necessary to be barely comprehending, and yet listen in and find out what was going on here. However, he couldn't overcome his aversion: after swallowing ten spoons of tea, he suddenly freed his head, capriciously pushed the spoon away and fell back onto the pillow again.
