Then he came out of the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and though he felt that he was completely broken, his consciousness was still with him. He feared being chased, he feared that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour perhaps, an instruction would come out to follow him; therefore, no matter what, the ends had to be hidden in time. One had to take care of it while there was still some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This decision was made long ago: "Throw everything into the sewer, and the end into the water, and that's the end." He had decided this even the night before, in a delirium, during those moments when he remembered it several times wanted to get up and go: "Quickly, quickly, and throw it all away." But throwing it away proved very difficult.
