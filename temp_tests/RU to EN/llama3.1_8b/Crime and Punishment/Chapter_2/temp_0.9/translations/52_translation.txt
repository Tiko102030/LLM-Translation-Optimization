There are no leads!” — and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed nervously, with a high-pitched, inaudible, drawn-out laugh, and kept laughing the whole time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Street Boulevard, where he ran into that girl three days ago, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts popped up in his head. It occurred to him too that it was horribly revolting for him now to pass by that bench again on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would also be very hard to meet that old man again who he had given two groats to: "Damn him!" He walked looking around carelessly and sullenly. All his thoughts were swirling around one main point now — and he himself felt that this really was a major point and that there existed one, and that now, exactly now, he stood face-to-face with this main point for the first time in two months. "Damn it all!"
