This was some especially unkempt person with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this because it doesn't matter to him," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, he said, and pointed forward with his finger, showing the very last room. He entered that room (the fourth one in order), a cramped and packed one full of people - the common folk, slightly better dressed than those in the other rooms. Between the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning dress, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and red-faced with freckles, a visible woman, and extremely lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest as big as a saucer, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said: "wait" and continued to work with the mourning lady. He breathed more freely.
