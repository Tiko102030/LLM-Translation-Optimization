Louisa Ivanovna with an embarrassed politeness went to sit down on all sides and, sitting, reached the doors; but in the doorway she bumped her backside into a prominent officer, with an open fresh face and excellent thick blonde whiskers. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the night watchman. Louisa Ivanovna hastily sat down almost to half-length and quickly hopping with frequent small steps flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again loud and lightning, hurricane, whirlwind! — Nikodim Fomich kindly and affably addressed Ilya Petrovich, — again they stirred up my heart, again it boiled over! Even from the stairs he heard. — What's what? — with noble carelessness spoke Ilya Petrovich (and even not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!") , moving with some papers to another table and artistically flinging his shoulders with each step; wherever the step, there was also a shoulder; — here, you're seeing: sir writer, that is student, former in fact, doesn't pay money, gave us IOUs, doesn't clean up an apartment, constant complaints about them arrive, but they chose to enter an objection, that I lit a cigar! They themselves provoke it, and behold-s, allow yourselves to look at them: here they are in their most attractive appearance now-s! — Poverty is not a vice, comrade, well yes already what!
