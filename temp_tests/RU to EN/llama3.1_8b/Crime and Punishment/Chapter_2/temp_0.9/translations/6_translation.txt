He thought to himself with triumph, taking a deep and joyful breath - just a weak bout of feverish delirium for a minute,' he said, and tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants. At this moment, a sunbeam illuminated his left boot: on the sock sticking out of the boot, it seemed like signs were appearing. He kicked off the boot: 'really signs! The whole tip of the sock is soaked with blood'; probably he had carelessly stepped into that puddle... 'But what to do now? Where to get rid of this sock, fringe, pocket?' 

He gathered everything in his hand and stood in the room. 'In the fireplace? But first of all they will start rummaging through it there. Burn it? And how to burn it? There aren't even any matches.'
