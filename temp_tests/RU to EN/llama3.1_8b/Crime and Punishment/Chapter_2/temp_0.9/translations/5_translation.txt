Fragments of the torn hem won't arouse any suspicions; it seems so, it seems so! — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with an attention that bordered on agony again began to scrutinize everything around him, on the floor and everywhere, not having forgotten something. The certainty that all—memory, even simple consideration—would leave him was beginning to torment him intolerably. "What if it's already starting, what if this is already punishment? Ah, look, see! — he exclaimed again as lost. Indeed, pieces of braid that he had cut off from his trousers were lying on the floor, in the middle of the room, so that anyone could see them! "What's gotten into me?" — he cried out once more as if he'd lost his mind. Then a strange idea came to him: maybe all his clothes are stained with blood, maybe there are many spots, but he doesn't see or notice them because his thinking has weakened, is shattered... his mind is clouded... Suddenly he remembered that the wallet also had blood on it. "Ah! So therefore, there must be blood in the pocket too, because I stuffed a still damp wallet into the pocket then!" He rummaged through the pocket for an instant, and — yes, indeed — there were stains, spots on the lining of the pocket! "So therefore, I haven't completely lost my reason yet, so therefore there's still thinking and memory, since I myself snapped out of it and figured it out! — he thought triumphantly, breathing deeply and joyfully through his whole chest — just a slight weakness from fever, a fit of madness for one minute," and he tore the lining off the left pocket of his trousers.
