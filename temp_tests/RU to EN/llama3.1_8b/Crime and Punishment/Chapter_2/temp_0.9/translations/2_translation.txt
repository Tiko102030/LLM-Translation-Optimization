The sun was out and he quickly glanced over himself, from head to foot, all his clothes: were there any traces? But that wouldn't do: shivering with cold, he began to take everything off and examine it again. He turned everything inside out, to the last thread and patch, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But nothing seemed to be there; only on that spot where the pants had torn apart at the bottom and hung in fringes, there remained thick stains of dried blood on this fringe. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. There was probably nothing more. Suddenly he remembered that his wallet and things which he took out from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't thought to take them out yet and hide them! Not even now, as he examined his clothes! What is this?
