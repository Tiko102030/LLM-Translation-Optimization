Meanwhile Razumikhin moved over to him on the divan, clumsily like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, despite the fact that he could have gotten up himself, and with his right hand brought a spoon of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand so he wouldn't burn himself. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov swallowed one spoonful greedily, then another, a third. But after bringing him several spoons, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and announced that as for further action they needed to consult with Zosimov. Natasha entered, carrying two bottles of beer. —Do you want tea? —I do. —Hurry and bring the tea, Natasha, because it seems we can get by without a doctor for the tea.
