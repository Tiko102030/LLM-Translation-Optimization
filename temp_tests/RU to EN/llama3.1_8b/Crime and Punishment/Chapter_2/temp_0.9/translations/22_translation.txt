Louisa Ivanovna, you should sit down, he said to the partly dressed red and crimson lady, who stood as if not daring to sit down herself, although a chair was nearby. — I thank you,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said and quietly, with a silk rustling sound, sat down in the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread out around the chair and occupied almost half of the room. It was carrying a scent. But the lady, apparently, felt uneasy about taking up half of the room and that she was being carried away by the perfume, although she was smiling timidly and shamefully together, but with obvious concern. The mourning lady finally finished speaking and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very boldly and somehow particularly rolling his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. A puffy lady jumped right out of her place seeing him, and with a special enthusiasm she began to squat; but the officer didn't notice her at all, and she was no longer able to sit next to him.
