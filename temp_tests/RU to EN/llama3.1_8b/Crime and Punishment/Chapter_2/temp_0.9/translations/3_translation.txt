What's this? In a flash he rushed to pull out and throw everything on the table. Having picked up all of it, even rummaging through pockets to make sure nothing else was left behind, he moved this whole pile over to the corner. There, in the corner itself, down below, in one place the torn-off wallpaper from the wall had been: immediately he started stuffing everything into that hole under the paper: "it's gone! Everything out of sight and even my wallet!" - he thought joyfully, straightening up and blankly staring at the corner, where the hole was now even bigger. Suddenly he shuddered with horror: "My God," he whispered in despair, "what's happened to me? Is this really hidden? Do they hide things like this?" Indeed, he hadn't been counting on things; he had thought there would only be money, and so he hadn't prepared a place ahead of time - "but now, what am I happy about? Do they hide things like this?"
