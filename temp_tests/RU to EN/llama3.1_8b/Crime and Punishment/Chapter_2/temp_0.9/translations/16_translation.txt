His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is trickery! They're trying to lure me in with deceit and then suddenly knock me out - continued he to himself, going up the stairs. - It's bad that I'm almost out of my mind... I can just tell some stupid lie..."

On the staircase, he remembered leaving everything as is, in a state of disarray, - "and here, maybe they're deliberately looking for it without me," - and stopped. But such despair and cynicism overcame him, if one could call it that, that he shook his hand off and continued further. "Just hurry up..."

On the street, the heat was just as unbearable; not a single drop of rain in these days. Again dust, brick, and plaster; again stench from the shops and bars; again drunkards, Chuvash vendors, and semi-dilapidated cabmen. The sun brightly blinded him in the eyes, so it hurt to look, and his head spun completely around - a common feeling of a fever-stricken person who has just come out into bright sunlight. Having reached the corner of the street he was on yesterday, he anxiously glanced at her, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might as well say," thought he, approaching the office.
