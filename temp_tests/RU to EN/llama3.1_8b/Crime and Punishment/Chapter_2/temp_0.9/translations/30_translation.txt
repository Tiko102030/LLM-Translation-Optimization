"Ilya Petrovich! " the clerk began carefully, but stopped to wait a moment, because it was impossible to hold back the boiling port captain without holding him by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the dashing lady, she trembled at first like a leaf from thunder and lightning; but what was strange: the more frequent and harsh her swearings became, the lovelier her face looked, the more enchanting her smile became when addressed to the stern port captain. She stood rooted to the spot and constantly bent forward, impatiently waiting for someone finally to let her speak, and she waited. — "No noise or fighting will happen at my house, Captain," she suddenly rattled on like a sack of peas with a strong German accent, although speaking quickly in Russian - no scandal whatsoever, and they came here drunk, and I'll tell you all about it, Captain, but I'm not to blame... My family is noble, Captain, and our manners are noble too, Captain, and I have always been so myself, never wanting any scandal. But they came here completely drunk and then asked again what way the toilet was, and one of them got up and started playing with his feet on the piano like a madman, which is absolutely not nice in a noble house, and he smashed the piano keys, and there's no decent behavior whatever here, and I've said it. And then, as I quickly called for the doorman Karol to come in, he took Karol and gave him a punch on the face, and Henriette too was punched on the face, while I got slapped five times myself. And that's not nice at all in a noble house, Captain, and I've shouted."
