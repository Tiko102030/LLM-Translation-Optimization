He only remembered taking a single gulp of cold water and spilling it from his cup onto his chest. Then came the haze of forgetfulness. III

However, he was not entirely in a state of delirium throughout his illness: it was an agitated condition with fits of frenzy and semi-consciousness. He later recalled much. It seemed to him that many people were gathering around him, wanting to take him away somewhere, arguing and quarreling over him. Then suddenly he would be alone in the room, everyone having left and being afraid of him; they would only occasionally open the door a little to look at him, threatening him, whispering among themselves, laughing and teasing him.

Nastasya was often remembered standing nearby; he could also distinguish another person who seemed very familiar, but he couldn't guess who it was and felt a pang of sadness for this, even crying. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times – that the same day was going on. But as for what exactly had happened – completely forgotten. On the other hand, he remembered every minute that he had forgotten something important and couldn't forget it – tortured himself, suffered, tried to recall it, groaned, fell into frenzy or an awful, unbearable fear.

At such times he would struggle to get up, wanted to run away, but someone always held him back with force, and he would fall back into powerlessness and forgetfulness.
