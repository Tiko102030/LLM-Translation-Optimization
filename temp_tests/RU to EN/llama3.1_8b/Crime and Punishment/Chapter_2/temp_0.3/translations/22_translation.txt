Louisa Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said to the hastily dressed red-haired lady who stood there as if she dared not sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — I thank you, she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down in the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half the room. It carried perfume. But the lady, clearly nervous about taking up so much space and being carried away by such strong perfume, despite smiling fearfully and impudently together, was visibly troubled. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially turning her shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. The plump lady jumped out of her seat at the sight of him and, with a particular excitement, began to curtsy; but the officer did not even glance at her, and she was no longer able to sit down next to him.
