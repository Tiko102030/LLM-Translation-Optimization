— And where, may I ask? — Down the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered abruptly, curtly, entirely pale as a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes before Ilia Petrovich's gaze. — He is barely standing on his feet, and you... — noticed Nikodim Fomich. — Nothing! — somehow especially said Ilia Petrovich. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the clerk, who was also looking very intently at him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
