And suddenly, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly caught a glimpse of the voice of his landlady. She was wailing, screaming and lamenting, hurrying, rushing, uttering words so quickly that they couldn't be made out, begging for something - of course, for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it could only rasp, but the beater himself was also saying something, and soon, incoherently, hastily, and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was the voice of Ilya Petrovich. Ilya Petrovich is here and beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the stairs - that's clear, it can be heard from the sounds, screams, blows! What is this, has the light turned upside down? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, rising, knocking, banging on doors, rushing. "But why, why, and how can it be?" - he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone completely mad. But no, he is hearing everything too clearly!
