His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me down at any moment," he thought to himself as he went out onto the staircase. "It's terrible that I'm almost delirious... I can tell some silly lie..."

On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings just as they were, in a corner - "and here, perhaps, they're deliberately searching for me", he recalled and stopped. But such despair and cynicism about death suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand and continued on. "Just get it over with..."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; just one drop of rain in all these days. Again dust, brick and cement, again the stench from the shops and drinking dens, again drunkards, Chuvash hawkers and half-ruined cabdrivers. The sun flashed brightly into his eyes, so it hurt to look, and his head spun around completely - a familiar feeling of feverishness that suddenly comes on when going out into the bright sunlight. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked anxiously into it, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might say so," he thought as he approached the office.
