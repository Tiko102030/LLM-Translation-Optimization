Ilya Petrovich! — began the clerk carefully, but stopped to wait for a moment, because the boiling officer could not be restrained otherwise than by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the lush lady, at first she trembled so much from thunder and lightning; but what a strange thing: the more numerous and harsh her curses became, the more charming her appearance became, the more enchanting her smile turned out to be, directed at the stern officer. She was hopping on one foot and constantly squatting, impatiently waiting for them finally to let her speak, and she did wait. — There will be no noise or fight in my house, Mr. Captain, — she suddenly began to chatter, like a sprouted pea, with a strong German accent, although speaking quickly in Russian, — and there won't be any scandal, not at all, but they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, and I'm not guilty... My house is noble, Mr. Captain, and my manner of behavior is noble, Mr. Captain, and I always, always did not want any scandal. They came completely drunk and then asked three times for a pipe, and then one lifted his legs and started playing the piano with his feet, which is very bad in a noble house, and he's breaking the piano, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I said so. And he took a pipe and started pushing everyone behind him with it. And then as soon as I quickly called the janitor to call Karl, he grabbed Karl and poked his eye, and Henriette too poked her eye, but they hit me five times on the cheek. It's not nice at all in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I shouted. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood in the window like a little pig, squealing; and that's shameful.
