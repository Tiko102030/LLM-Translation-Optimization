Ah, the writers! — and he cast a scornful glance at Raskolnikov. — There was a story about him in the tavern just three days ago: he ate his fill but refused to pay; "I'll write you up in satire for it," he claimed. On the steamer too, last week, he insulted a respectable family of a civil servant, his wife and daughter, with vile words. Just yesterday they threw one out of the confectionery. That's what writers are like, literati, students, heralds... ugh! Off you go! I'll come to see you myself... then watch yourself! Did you hear? Louise Ivanovna launched into a courteous bowing down all around and, as she bowed, made her way to the doors; but in the doorway she bumped into one of the prominent officers from behind, with his face fresh and open and magnificent thick blond sideburns.
