This was some particularly disheveled man with a motionless idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from him because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, said the scribe and pointed forward with his finger, showing the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), cramped and packed tightly with people - a crowd of people, somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One was in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite to the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and red-faced, with spots, a noticeable woman, and extremely lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest, as big as a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. He glanced briefly at it, said: "wait" and continued working with the mourning lady. He took a freer breath.
