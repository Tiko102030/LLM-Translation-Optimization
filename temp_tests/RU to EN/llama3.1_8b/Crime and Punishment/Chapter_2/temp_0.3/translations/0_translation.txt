He lay there for a very long time. Sometimes it seemed as if he was waking up and in those moments he noticed that it had been night for a long time, but getting up didn't come to his mind. Finally, he realized that it was already daylight. He was lying on the couch upside down, still dazed from recent forgetfulness. The terrible, desperate cries from the street reached him sharply, which, however, he listened to every night under his window at three o'clock. They were what woke him up now. "Ah! And here the drunkards are already coming out of the bars," he thought - "third hour" - and suddenly jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. "What? Already the third!" He sat on the couch, and then everything came back to him!
