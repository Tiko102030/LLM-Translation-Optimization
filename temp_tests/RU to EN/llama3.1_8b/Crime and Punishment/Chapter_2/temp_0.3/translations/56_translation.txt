His face expressed amazement. "What's wrong?" he shouted, examining the entered comrade from head to foot; then fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad?" He added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes, "You've outdone our brother, you know." "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when he collapsed onto a worn-out Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. "Are you seriously ill, do you know it?" He began to feel Raskolnikov's pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. "No need," he said, "I came... here's the thing: I don't have any lessons... I wanted to... but I don't need lessons at all." "Do you know what?"
