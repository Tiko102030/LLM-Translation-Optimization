Under the stone a small depression had formed; he immediately started throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and yet there was still room left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one rotation to its original side, and it just fit back into its original place, slightly higher than before. But he pushed some earth under it and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again a strong, barely endurable joy, as yesterday in the office, took hold of him for an instant. "The ends are buried! And who could think to look for them under this stone? It may have been lying here since the house was built and will lie here just as long."
