The plump lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and with a peculiar excitement began to squat; but the officer paid no attention to her whatsoever, and she was no longer able to sit next to him. This was a lieutenant, an assistant warden's aide, with horizontally protruding reddish sideburns and extremely small facial features that expressed nothing in particular except for some arrogance. He glanced at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his costume was too shabby, and despite all the condescension, he still didn't have a proper posture; Raskolnikov had looked at him straight on for too long by accident, so that the lieutenant even felt offended. — What do you want? — he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin wouldn't even bow to his lightning-fast glance. — They demanded... according to the summons... — Raskolnikov replied somehow awkwardly. — This is about collecting money from them, from the student, — the clerk hastened, tearing himself away from the papers. — Here! — and he handed Raskolnikov a notebook, pointing out the place in it, — read!
