He became extremely absent-minded and forgetful and knew it himself. He had to hurry up resolutely! He went towards the Neva along V - Street; but on his way a sudden thought came to him: "Why the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to go somewhere very far away, perhaps to the Islands again, and there, in some solitary place, in the forest, under a bush, bury everything and the tree, maybe notice it?" And although he felt that at this moment he was not capable of discussing everything clearly and sensibly, but the thought seemed infallible to him. But it was not destined for the Islands, and something else happened: coming out onto the square from V - Street, he suddenly saw a gate leading into a courtyard on his left, enclosed by completely blank walls. To the right, immediately after entering through the gates, far into the courtyard stretched a white-washed wall of a four-storeyed neighboring house. Left, parallel to the blank wall and also now from the gates, ran a wooden fence, twenty steps deep into the courtyard, and then turned left. This was a secluded place, where some materials lay. Further on, in the depression of the courtyard, a corner of a low, blackened stone shed protruded from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
