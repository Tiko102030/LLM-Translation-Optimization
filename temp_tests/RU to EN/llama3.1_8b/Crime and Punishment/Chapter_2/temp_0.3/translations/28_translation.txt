—Yes, but she is my mistress, after all. —Well, what about it, then, mistress? The scribe looked at him with a condescending smile of pity and triumph, as if he were a novice who had just been bombarded: "How do you feel now?" But what did he care about the borrowed letter, about the demands! Was it worth any anxiety, in turn, even some attention! He stood there, read, listened, answered, even asked himself questions, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the crushing danger - that's what filled him at this moment, without forethought, without analysis, without future riddles and guesses, without doubts or questions. This was a moment of complete, direct, pure animal joy. But in that very moment, something like thunder and lightning struck in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by his lack of respect, still burning with anger, and obviously wanting to support the wounded pride, attacked the unfortunate "proud lady" with all his might, who had been looking at him since he entered with a silly smile. —And you, whatever-you-are-and-whatever-you-do, —he suddenly shouted from the top of his lungs (the tragic lady had already left), —what happened to you there last night?
