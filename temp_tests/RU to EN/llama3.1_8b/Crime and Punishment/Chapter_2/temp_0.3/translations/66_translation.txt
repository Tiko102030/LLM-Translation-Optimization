The pain from the whip had subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one anxious and not quite clear thought now occupied him exclusively. He stood and looked far off for a long time, intently; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to university, it often happened that, returning home, he would stop precisely at this same spot, gaze intently at this indeed magnificent panorama, and every time almost be amazed by one unclear and insoluble impression of his own. An inexplicable chill always blew on him from this magnificent panorama; the spirit was mute and deaf to him in this lush picture... He marveled each time at his gloomy and enigmatic impression and put off its solution for later, not trusting himself. Suddenly he remembered now all these previous questions and doubts of his own, and it seemed to him that he had not recalled them by chance. One thing struck him as wild and strange: that he had stopped in the same place as before, just as if he really imagined that he could think about the same things now as before, and be interested in the same old themes and pictures as he was... still so recently. It even became almost funny to him, and at the same time it squeezed his chest with pain. In some depth, below, somewhere barely visible underfoot, everything this past seemed to him now: the past itself, former thoughts, former problems, former themes, former impressions, and all this panorama, and he himself, and everything... It seemed that he was flying upwards somewhere, and everything disappeared from his eyes... Having made one involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a two-kopeck coin clenched in his fist. He opened his hand, looked intently at the coin, swung it back and forth, and then threw it into the water; after that, he turned around and went home. It seemed to him as if he had cut himself off from everything and everyone with scissors in this very moment.
