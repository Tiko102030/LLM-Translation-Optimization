All these sensitive details, sir, don't concern us at all, — Ilya Petrovich brutally cut in, — you must give a review and an undertaking, and what you were pleased to be in love with and all those tragic places, we're not interested in any of that. — Well, you... cruelly... — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign as well. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write on, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What am I supposed to write? — he asked somewhat especially harshly. — And I'll dictate it to you, — said the clerk. Raskolnikov thought that the clerk had become careless and contemptuous with him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly felt utterly indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change came somehow in one moment, in one minute. If only he had wanted to think a bit, he would certainly have been surprised by how he could talk to them like that just a minute ago, and even impose his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
