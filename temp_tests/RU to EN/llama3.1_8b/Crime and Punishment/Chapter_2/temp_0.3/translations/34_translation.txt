Louisa Ivanovna with a benevolent smile began to squat down in all directions and, squatting, reached the doors; but at the doors she bumped her backside into one of the prominent officers, with an open fresh face and with excellent thick light blond sideburns. This was Nikodim Fomich, the quarter's overseer. Louisa Ivanovna hastily squatted almost to the floor and with frequent small steps, hopping, flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — Nikodim Fomich kindly and affably addressed Ilya Petrovich, — again they have disturbed my heart, again it has boiled over! I even heard it from the stairs. — What? — with noble carelessness said Ilya Petrovich (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!"), moving with some papers to another table and dramatically flinging his shoulders with every step, wherever he stepped, there was his shoulder; — look here, you can see: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, doesn't pay money, issued IOUs, doesn't clean the apartment, constant complaints come in about them, and they came forward with a claim that I lit a cigarette among them! They are whining, but look here at them: see how attractive they now look! — Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, what's it to us!
