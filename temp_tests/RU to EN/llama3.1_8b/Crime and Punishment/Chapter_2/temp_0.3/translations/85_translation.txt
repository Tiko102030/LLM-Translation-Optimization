Meanwhile, Razumikhin sat down next to him on the divan, awkwardly, like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, despite the fact that he himself could have gotten up, and held out a spoonful of soup to his mouth with his right hand, blowing on it several times beforehand so as not to burn him. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov swallowed one spoonful greedily, then another, third. But after bringing a few spoons, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and announced that they should consult Zosimov about further action. Nastasya entered with two bottles of beer. — Do you want tea? — Yes, I do. — Hurry up and bring the tea, Nastasya, because it seems we can even do without a doctor for the tea.
