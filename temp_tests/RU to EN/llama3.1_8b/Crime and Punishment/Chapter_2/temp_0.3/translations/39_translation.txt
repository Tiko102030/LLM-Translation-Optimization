The scribe began dictating to him the usual form of such a statement, that is, I am unable to pay, I promise to do so sometime, I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property and so on. — You can't even write, your pen is slipping from your hands, remarked the scribe, gazing at Raskolnikov with curiosity. — Are you ill? — Yes... my head is spinning... go on talking! — Yes, everything; sign it. The scribe took back the paper and busied himself with others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head in his hands. It was as if a nail was being hammered into his forehead. A strange idea suddenly came to him: to stand up now, approach Nikodim Fyodorovich and tell him everything from yesterday, every detail, then go with them to the apartment and point out the things, in the corner, in a hole. The urge was so strong that he had already stood up from his seat to carry it out.
