And where did these feelings come from? On the other hand, now, if suddenly the room was filled with his first friends, rather than quarterlies, then even then, it seems, he would not have found a single human word for them, so suddenly empty had become his heart. A dismal feeling of painful and endless isolation and alienation suddenly consciously affected his soul. Not the baseness of his heart's outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, nor the poruchik's triumph over him turned his heart around so suddenly. Oh, what a matter now for his own baseness, for all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, demands, offices and so on! If he had been sentenced even to be burned at this moment, then he would not have stirred, and probably would not have listened to the verdict carefully either. Something was being done with him that was completely unfamiliar to him, new, sudden, and never before experienced. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt, with all the force of feeling, that not only could he no longer address these people in the quarter's office with his sensitive expansiveness as he had before, but even anything else; if they were all his own brothers and sisters, rather than quarterlies, then he would have absolutely no need to address them, and it would be pointless in any case. He had never experienced such a strange and terrifying feeling before this moment. And what is most agonizing - it was more of an experience than a consciousness, or a concept; a direct experience, the most agonizing experience of all those he had experienced so far in life. The clerk began to dictate to him the usual form of response in such cases, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (someday), I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away property and so on.
