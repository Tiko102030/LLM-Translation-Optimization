He remembered only how he had taken one gulp of cold water and spilled some from the cup on his chest. Then came a state of forgetfulness. III

However, he was not completely in a state of forgetfulness throughout his illness: it was an intermittent feverish condition with delirium and semi-consciousness. Much later he remembered many things. It seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him somewhere and carry him off; they argued about him and quarreled. Suddenly, he would be alone in the room, everyone had left and were afraid of him, only occasionally slightly opening the door to look at him, threatening him, whispering something between themselves, laughing and teasing him. He often remembered Nastasya beside him; he could distinguish another person, very familiar-looking, but who exactly - he couldn't guess and longed for this person, even crying. Sometimes it seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times - that the same day was going on. But about what - about what he completely forgot; however, every minute he remembered that he had forgotten something important, something that couldn't be forgotten - he tormented himself, suffered, tried to remember, groaned, fell into madness or an unbearable, terrifying fear. Then he would try to get up from his place, wanted to run away, but always someone would stop him with force, and he would fall back into powerlessness and forgetfulness again.
