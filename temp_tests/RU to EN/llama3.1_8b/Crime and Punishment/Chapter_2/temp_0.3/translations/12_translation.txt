He wasn't answering and held a piece of paper in his hands, not even printing it out. — Yes, don't get up, Nastasya continued, pitying him and seeing that he was pulling his legs off the couch. — If you're sick, just stay there: you won't burn yourself. What's all this in your hands? He looked down: in his right hand were cut-off pieces of fringing, a sock, and scraps torn from a pocket. And so he slept with them. Later, thinking about it, he remembered that, even half-awake in the heat, he had tightly clenched everything in his hand and fallen asleep again. — Look at all these rags he's collected and sleeps with them, just like treasure... — Nastasya let out her sickly-nervous laugh. In an instant he stuffed it all under his greatcoat and fixed his eyes on it intently. Though he could hardly grasp the situation fully in that moment, he felt that they wouldn't treat a person like this when they came to take him away.
