He took a deeper breath more freely. "Not quite!" He gradually began to pick himself up, he exhorted himself with all his might to pull himself together and come to his senses. "Some foolishness, some smallest carelessness, and I can fool myself completely! Ah... pity that there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head spins even more... and my mind too..."

He felt a terrible disorder in everything about him. He was afraid of not being able to control himself. He tried to cling to something and think about something entirely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The clerk interested him greatly: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure out what was going on with him. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and fat, with a parting on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white-fingered fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner who was here he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louisa Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to the brightly dressed red-haired woman, who stood as if not daring to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her.
