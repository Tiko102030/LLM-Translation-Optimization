Not at all!..."

He suddenly stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question struck him and bitterly amazed him:

"If indeed everything was done consciously, not carelessly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how did you still not even glance into the wallet and don't know what you've gotten, which is why you're suffering so much and deliberately went to such a vile, disgusting, low deed? You wanted to throw the wallet in the water now, along with all the things you haven't seen yet... How is this?"

Yes, it's like that; everything is like that. He, however, knew this before and it wasn't even a new question for him; and when they decided to throw it into the water at night, they decided without any hesitation or objection, as if it was supposed to be so, as if otherwise it wouldn't be possible... Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; maybe it was already decided yesterday in that very moment when he sat on the trunk and pulled out the cases from it... And indeed! "It's because I'm very sick," he finally decided gloomily, "I've tormented and tortured myself, and don't even know what I'm doing... I've been tormenting myself for three days now, and all this time... I'll get well and... won't torture myself anymore... But if I don't recover at all? God! How tired I am of it all..."

He walked without stopping. He terribly wanted to somehow dissipate himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to try. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical disgust for everything that met his eyes and surrounded him, stubborn, malicious, hateful. All the people he met were disgusting to him - their faces, gait, movements. He would have spat on someone, bitten them, it seemed, if anyone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the Malaya Nevka embankment, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
