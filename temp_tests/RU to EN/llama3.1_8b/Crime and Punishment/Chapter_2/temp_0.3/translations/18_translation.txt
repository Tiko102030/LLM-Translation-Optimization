The air was stifling. Sweepers with notebooks under their arms, pedestrians and people of all kinds - visitors - were going up and down. The door to the very office was also wide open. He entered and stopped in the entrance hall. Here everyone stood waiting for some men. It was also incredibly stuffy here, and besides that, a fresh, still not dried paint on old oil in newly painted rooms hit his nose with nausea. After a while, he decided to move forward again, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. A terrible impatience pulled him further and further. No one noticed him.
