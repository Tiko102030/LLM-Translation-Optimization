Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt completely broken, but his consciousness was still with him. He feared being chased, he feared that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps instructions would already be given to follow him; therefore, no matter what, he had to bury the ends for now. He had to manage it while there were still some strength and at least some reasoning left... Where to go? This was decided long ago: "Throw everything into the gutter, and the ends in the water, and that's it." So he had resolved last night, in a delirium, in those moments when he remembered it several times and wanted to get up and go: "as quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and throw everything out." But throwing it all out turned out to be very difficult. He had been walking along the Embankment of Catherine's Canal for half an hour already, maybe more, and a few times he looked at the stairs into the canal where they met him. But one couldn't even think about carrying out his intention: either the barges stood right next to the stairs and washerwomen were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and everywhere people swarmed, and from all sides you could see, notice: suspiciously, that a person deliberately stepped down, stopped and threw something into the water. And what if the boxes don't sink, but float?
