He took a deeper breath. "Must have been mistaken!" Slowly he regained his composure, he endeavored with all his might to pull himself together and collect his thoughts. "Some ridiculous thing, some smallest carelessness, and I can disguise myself completely! Ah... too bad there is no fresh air here - stuffiness... Head spins even more... and the mind as well..."

He felt a dreadful disorder in everything. He was afraid of losing control of himself. He tried to grasp onto something and think about something completely unrelated, but that didn't work out either. The scribe greatly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure him out. It was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a swarthy and agile face seeming older than its age, dressed in the latest fashion and fat, with a fringe on his back of the head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white-fingered fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner he even spoke two words in French, quite satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, you'd better sit down, he said briefly to a half-dressed lady of bright red dress standing there as if afraid to sit down herself, although there was a chair next to her.
