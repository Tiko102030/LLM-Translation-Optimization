He was some kind of particularly disheveled man with an unyielding idea in his gaze. "From this you won't learn anything because it doesn't matter to him," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, said the scribe and pointed forward with his finger, showing him the very last room. He entered that room (the fourth in order), cramped and overcrowded with people - a crowd of individuals somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One was in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at a table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, extremely plump and ruddy, spotted, visible, and very lavishly dressed with a brooch on her chest, the size of a tea saucer, stood off to one side and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed the scribe his summons. The clerk glanced briefly at it, said "wait" and continued working with the lady in mourning. He took a freer breath.
