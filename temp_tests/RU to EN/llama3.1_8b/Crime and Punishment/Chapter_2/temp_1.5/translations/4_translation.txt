Was he hiding it like this? Truly, my reason leaves me! In exhaustion, he sat down on the divan, and was immediately shaken by an unbearable shiver. Mechanically, he pulled towards him a winter coat lying beside him on a chair, one that had been his student coat, warm but already almost in tatters, covered himself with it, and once more both sleep and delirium seized hold of him. He fell into a faint. Barely five minutes later he leapt to his feet again and at once, in a state of frenzy, rushed back to his clothes. "How could I have fallen asleep again, when nothing had been done! Yes, yes, it's true: the noose under my armpit has still not been removed! I've forgotten about that matter altogether! Such evidence!" He pulled off the noose and set to work at once tearing it into pieces, shoving them under his pillow in his clothes. "Torn strips of fabric will never arouse suspicion; it looks like that, looks like that!" he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, with his attention strained to pain as he again searched all around, on the floor and everywhere, if he hadn't forgotten anything else?
