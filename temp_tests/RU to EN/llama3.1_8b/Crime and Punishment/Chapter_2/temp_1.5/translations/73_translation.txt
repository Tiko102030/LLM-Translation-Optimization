He remembered only how he had taken a gulp of cold water and spilled some from his cup on his chest. Then came forgetfulness. III However, he wasn't completely in a state of confusion throughout the illness: it was an epidemic feverish state, with delirium and half-consciousness. Much later he recalled much. Sometimes it seemed to him that many people were gathering around him and wanted to take him somewhere and haul him away, quarreling and fighting among themselves about him. Then suddenly he was alone in the room; everyone had left and they were afraid of him; only occasionally they would open the door a little to look at him, threatening him, whispering something to each other, laughing and teasing him. He often remembered Nastasya standing near him; he distinguished and still another person, someone who seemed very familiar, but whom he couldn't guess in any way and pined for him even weeping sometimes. It also seemed to him that he had been lying there for a month; at other times it was the same day all over again. But as for what exactly, of that he had completely forgotten: however, every minute he remembered that he had forgotten something which one shouldn't forget – he tormented himself with this recollection, howling in agony, becoming deranged or falling into a state of unspeakable and unbearable fright. Then he tried to get up from his place, wanted to run away, but someone always held him back by force, and he fell into weakness and loss of memory again.
