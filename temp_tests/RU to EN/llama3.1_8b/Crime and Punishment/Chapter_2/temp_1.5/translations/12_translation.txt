He didn't answer and held a piece of paper in his hands, not unfolding it. — Yes, don't get up, — Nastasya continued to pity him and see that he was slipping his feet off the couch. — You're ill, so just don't move: you won't catch on fire. What's that in your hands? He glanced over: there were cut-off fringes, a sock and tatters of ripped-out pocket lining in his right hand. He slept with them too. Later, thinking about it, he remembered how, half-waking up in the heat, he had tightly clutched all this in his hand and fell asleep again. — And look at all these rags you've accumulated and sleep with them, just like a treasure... — Nastasya let out her own sickeningly nervous laugh. In an instant he pushed it all under his coat and intensely buried himself in the fabric with his eyes. Though he could only barely make sense of things at that moment, but he felt that they would treat him differently when they came to take him away.
