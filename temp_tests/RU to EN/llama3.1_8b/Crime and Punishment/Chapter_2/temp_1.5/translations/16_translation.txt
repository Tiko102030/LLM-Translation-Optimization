His head spun and hurt from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then catch me off guard," he muttered to himself as he climbed up the stairs. - It's bad that I'm almost out of my mind... I can come up with some silly lie..."

On the staircase, he remembered leaving all his belongings just like they were, "and here, perhaps on purpose without me searched", and he stopped. But such desperation and, if one can say so, cynicism overcame him after a while that he waved his hand in despair and continued further. "Just hurry up..."

Outside again, the heat was unbearable; it hadn't rained even once in these days. Again dust, brick and lime, again the stench from the shops and drinking dens, again drunkards and drunken street sellers and half-broken cab drivers at every step. The sun glinted brightly in his eyes, so it hurt to look and his head spun completely around - a usual feeling of feverish, suddenly stepping out into the bright sunlight. Reaching the turn for yesterday's street, he looked down it with painful anxiety and into that house... and immediately averted his gaze. "If they ask, I might say maybe", he thought, approaching the office.
