Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, although feeling completely broken, but his consciousness was still with him. He was afraid of being chased, feared that in half an hour, quarter of an hour maybe even, there would come out instructions to follow them; therefore, at any cost, the ends had to be hidden before it was too late. One had to manage while there were still some strength and some reason left... Where to go? This decision had been made a long time ago: "To throw everything into the gutter, and end with it". So he decided last night, in delirium, in those moments when he remembered this, several times burst out of bed to get up and go: "As quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and get rid of everything." But getting rid of things proved to be very difficult. He was walking along the Catherine Canal embankment for over half an hour, maybe even more, and a few times looked at the openings into the canal, where he met them. But it wasn't even worth thinking about carrying out his intention: or boats stood right at the opening and washerwomen washed clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people crowded everywhere, and from all sides you can see suspiciously that a person deliberately stepped off, stopped, and threw something into the water. And what if the boxes don't sink, but float?
