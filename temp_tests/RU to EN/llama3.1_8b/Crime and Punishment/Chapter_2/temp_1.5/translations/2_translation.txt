The light had gotten in, and he quickly glanced himself over from head to foot, his entire clothes: were there any marks? But it couldn't be done like that; shaking with a chill, he began removing everything from himself and inspecting all around again. He turned everything over, down to the last thread and piece, and didn't trust himself as he repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no signs of anything at all; only on that place where the pants had bunched up and hung with fringes below, on those very fringes remained dense traces of congealed blood. He grabbed a small folding knife and cut off the fringe. Nothing else, probably, would be left now. Suddenly he remembered that his wallet and things, which he had pulled out from the old woman's trunk, were still lying in his pockets this whole time! And he hadn't thought to remove them yet, not even when he was inspecting his clothes just then! What was wrong with him?
