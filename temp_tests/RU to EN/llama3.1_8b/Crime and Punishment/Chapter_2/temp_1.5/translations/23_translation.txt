The plush lady suddenly leapt up from her place the moment she saw him and, with some peculiar excitement, began to squat; but the officer did not even glance at her, nor could she dare sit beside him anymore. He was a lieutenant, assistant head of the watchman, with rusty red sideburns jutting out horizontally in all directions and very fine features on his face, which expressed nothing unusual other than some coarseness; he glanced sideways and partly with indignation at Raskolnikov: his clothes were too shabby, and despite everything, there was still no match between them - his posture. By carelessness, Raskolnikov looked straight and for too long at him, so that the officer became indignant. — What do you want? he bellowed, probably marveling that such a beggar would not show deference to his lightning-fast glance. — We... were summoned... by order of summons... — replied Raskolnikov vaguely. — This is about collecting money from them - the student, — hasty writing clerk quickly got distracted from his papers. - Here! - and he threw a notebook at Raskolnikov, pointing to a place in it, — read this!
