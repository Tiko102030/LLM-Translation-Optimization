All these sensitive details, sir, have nothing to do with us – brutally interrupted Ilya Petrovich – you must give a statement and an affidavit, and what you two had the pleasure of being in love with and all these tragic places, don't matter to us at all. — Well, you... cruelly… muttered Nikodim Fomichev, sitting down at the table and also taking out a pen to sign. He somehow felt ashamed. — Write, said the scribe to Raskolnikov. — What am I supposed to write? – he asked, somewhat coarsely. — And I'll dictate it to you, the scribe replied. Raskolnikov thought that the scribe had become with him more careless and contemptuous after his confession, but strangely enough – suddenly he himself became utterly indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in one moment, in one minute. If only he wanted to think about it for a bit, he would undoubtedly be surprised by how he could speak with them like that just minutes ago, and even impose his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
