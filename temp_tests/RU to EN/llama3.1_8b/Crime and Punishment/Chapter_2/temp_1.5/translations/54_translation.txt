He was utterly amazed and perplexed; he stopped dead in his tracks. A brand new question came into his mind, one that he had never thought of before - a very simple and extremely straightforward one:

"If I really did do this on purpose, without just being careless, if I truly knew what my aim was all along, how could I not have checked the purse yet, not even seen what's in it, due to which I'm now suffering like this and intentionally gotten myself into such a dirty and base business? You were actually thinking of throwing everything into the river right at that moment, including the purse with all those other things you hadn't seen yet... What's this nonsense?"

This was how it was; this is just what he was used to. However, he had known all of this already; it wasn't a new thought for him at all; and when it had been decided to throw everything into the river that night, there was no hesitation or any objection, it was simply something they felt they must do, as if there was no other way.

Yes, he knew it all well, just like he remembered it. And almost yesterday... He seemed to have come close to making this decision at exactly the same moment when he was sitting on top of a trunk, rummaging through its boxes...

He actually felt that way! It's because I'm extremely ill... and so it is because of my sickness that I've been tormenting myself without knowing what I'm doing. And all the time - yesterday too and the day before, and always... and it's not just that, I'm self-tormenting, torturing myself needlessly...

He'll get well... and stop this sort of nonsense! But if I don't recover completely... God have mercy on me! How I'm sick and tired of all this! He kept walking without stopping.

He was terribly longing for some kind of distraction from his thoughts. He had no idea what to do or how to act. One more, quite insurmountable feeling grew stronger in him with each passing moment - a boundless, almost physical repulsion for everything that surrounded him and met the eye, an obstinate and angry hatred for everyone he saw.

Everyone who crossed his path seemed loathsome, and their faces, steps and movements disgusted him. He would not even have bothered to take care of someone who approached him, and perhaps would have bitten them if they tried talking to him... 

He stopped in his tracks suddenly when he reached the bank of Malaya Neva on Vasilevsky Island near a bridge.
