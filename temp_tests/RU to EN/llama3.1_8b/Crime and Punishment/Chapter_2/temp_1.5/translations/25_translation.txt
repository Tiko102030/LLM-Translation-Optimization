— Don't shriek! I'm not screaming, I'm speaking quite calmly, and it's you who are shouting; I'm a student and won't let anyone shout at me. The assistant was so flustered that in the first minute he couldn't utter a word, and only some spittle was flying out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Make ma-a-aa-atches! You're in my presence. Don't g-r-r-rumble, sir! — Yes, and you're in my presence too, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides that you're shouting, smoking a cigar, which means we're all slacking off somehow. — After saying this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable delight. The clerk was smiling at them.
