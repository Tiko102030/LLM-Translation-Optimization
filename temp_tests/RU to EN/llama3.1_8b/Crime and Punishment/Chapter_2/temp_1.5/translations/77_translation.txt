Well, and who are you then? — I am a fellow labourer in our office, come from the merchant Shelpayev's place, on business - here on business. — Please, sit down on that chair, said Razumikhin, sitting down on another chair on the other side of the little table. — You did well, brother, to regain your senses, he continued addressing Raskolnikov. — You hardly ate or drank for four days now. Actually, they even gave you tea by the spoonful. I brought Zossimov twice to see you. Do you remember Zossimov? He took a close look at you and right away said it was all nonsense - maybe something did hit your head in some way. Some nervous foolishness, he says, a bad ration of food, too little beer and mustard served, that's why the illness, but nothing to worry about, will pass on its own.
