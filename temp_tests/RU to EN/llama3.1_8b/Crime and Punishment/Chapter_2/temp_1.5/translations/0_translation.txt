He had been lying there for a long time. It would sometimes happen that he as if woke up and noticed that it was already late at night, but getting up didn't come to mind. Finally, he realized that it was light day-like. He lay on the couch on his back, still dazed from recent forgetfulness. The terrible, desperate cries from the street reached him sharply, which, however, he listened to every night under his window in the third hour. These ones, and they woke him up now. "Ah! And it's finally the drunkards come out of the bars," he thought, "the third hour — and suddenly jumped up as if someone had yanked him off the couch. — How? The third one already?" He sat on the couch, and then remembered everything all at once.
