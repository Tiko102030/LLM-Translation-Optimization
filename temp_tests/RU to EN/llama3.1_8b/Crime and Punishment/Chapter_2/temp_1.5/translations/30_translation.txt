Ilya Petrovich! the secretary began carefully but stopped to wait for a moment, because you can't hold back a boiling officer other than by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the flamboyant lady, at first she shook so hard with thunder and lightning, but a strange thing: the more numerous and vehement the abuse was, the more charming her expression became, the more enchanting was her smile directed towards the stormy lieutenant. She was hopping about in place and continually squatting impatiently waiting for finally being allowed to enter her word and she waited.

"No noise or brawl at my place, Mr. Captain," she burst out suddenly, like oats that had been poured over with a firm German accent although speaking fluently in Russian - "and no trouble, not a bit of it, but they came here drunk and I will tell you everything about it, Mr. Captain, but I'm not at fault... I have a noble house, Mr. Captain, and noble manners, Mr. Captain, and I've always, always not wanted any bother myself. But they're completely drunk and then three times asked what the matter was, and then one of them picked up his legs and started playing foot-piano on the table, which is just awful in a noble house, and he's destroying the foot-piano, and there's no proper behavior here at all, and I said... And he took the beer glass and began pushing everyone with it from behind. And then I quickly called the butler to chase him away and Karl came, too, and he took Carl by the eye, and also Henry by the eye, while they slapped my cheek five times. Which is so indecent in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I screamed. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood in it like a little pig squealing; which is shameful.
