The air was stiflingly thick. Street sweepers and passersby of both sexes kept coming up and going down the stairs, each carrying a notebook under their arm. The door to the office was wide open too. He entered and stopped in the hall, where all the men who were waiting for something stood around. It was hot as well, and apart from that, a fresh and not yet dry paint fume on a stale linseed oil in freshly painted rooms stung his nose terribly. After a little wait he decided to move further ahead, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A dreadful impatience drove him ever further and further. No one took any notice of him.
