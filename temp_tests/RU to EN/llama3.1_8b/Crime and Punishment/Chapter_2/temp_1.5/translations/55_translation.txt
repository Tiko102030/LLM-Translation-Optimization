He would simply have spit in the face of anyone, bitten someone, it seems, if anybody spoke to him... He stopped suddenly when he emerged onto the Embankment of the Little Neva on Vasilevsky Island, near a bridge. "This is where he lives, in this house - what's that? And I couldn't just have come here myself! The same old story as before... But it's very intriguing: was it really I who came or did I simply stroll and ended up here? Oh well; I said three days ago that I would go see him the day after tomorrow, so I'll go anyway! As if I couldn't go now...". He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. Razumikhin was at home in his little room, writing and busy with some task, and he himself let Raskolnikov in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat slumped in a torn to shreds dressing gown, in slippers on one foot, disheveled, unshaven, and unwashed.
