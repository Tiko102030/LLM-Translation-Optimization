He thought to himself with triumph, deeply and joyfully sighing all over his chest - simply a weak and delirious condition for one minute', — and he pulled out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment the sun's rays illuminated his left boot: on the tip which was protruding from the boot, as if signs appeared. He kicked off the boot: "indeed, signs! The whole tip of the laces are soaked in blood"; probably he accidentally stepped into that puddle... "But what to do now with this? Where to dispose of this sock, tuft, pocket?"

He gathered it all in his hand and stood in the room. "Into the stove? But first the thieves will search there. Burn it? And how to burn it? There are no matches either."
