He became extremely absent-minded and forgetful and knew it. Decisively he needed to hurry! He walked along the Nevsky Prospect; but on the way a new thought came to him: "Why the Neva? Why into water? Would it not be better to go somewhere very far, perhaps to the Islands again, and there in some secluded spot, in the woods, under a bush – bury all this and the tree too, maybe notice it?" And although he felt that he was not able to discuss everything clearly and soundly at this moment, the idea seemed faultless to him. But he would not be destined for the Islands either, and something else happened: exiting from the V - Prospekt onto the square, he suddenly saw on the left an entrance into a courtyard walled up completely with solid walls. To the right, immediately after entering through the gate, a distant wall of the neighboring four-story house stretched far back in the courtyard. Left, parallel to the blind wall and also directly next to the gate, was a wooden fence, about twenty paces deep into the yard, which then turned sharply left. This was a secluded place, where some materials lay. Further, in an indentation of the yard, peeped out from behind the fence the corner of a low, charred, stone shed, obviously part of any workshop.
