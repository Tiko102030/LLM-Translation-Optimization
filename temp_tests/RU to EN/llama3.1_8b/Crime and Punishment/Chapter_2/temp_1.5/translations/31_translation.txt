And he was opening the window onto a ditch and jumping through it like a little pig, squealing; and that's a shame. And how is one to jump out of the window onto the street, like a little pig, squealing; and that's a shame. Boo-hoo-hoo! And Charles pulled him from behind by his coat at the window, and this, truly, Captain, he ripped his undershirt. And then he was crying out that he would have to pay fifteen groschen to the man in mufti as a fine. And I myself, Captain, paid him five groschen on account of it. And this is an impolite guest, Captain, and every kind of ruckus he's making! I say, speaking for myself, there will be big trouble between us, Captain, because I can write about all of this in all the newspapers. —  Is that a writer, then? —Yes, sir, the Captain, and what is it to be an impolite guest, the Captain, when one jumps out of the window of a noble house… — Oh no, oh no, oh no!
