Yes, she's my mistress after all? Ah, then what about being a mistress, eh? The scribe looked at him with a condescending smile of sorrow and at the same time some triumph, as if at a novice who was just now being fired at: "What, they say, how do you feel now?" But what business did he have with borrowed papers and complaints now! Was it worth any anxiety, even a little attention! He stood, read, listened, replied, even asked questions himself, but all this was done mechanically. The triumph of self-preservation, the salvation from the pressing danger - that's what filled his entire being at this moment, without thinking ahead, without analysis, without future speculations and guesses, without doubts and questions. This was a moment of pure, immediate, animal joy. But in the same moment, something like thunder and lightning struck in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by impertinence, burning with passion, and apparently wanting to support injured pride, hurled himself at the unfortunate 'gaudy woman', who looked at him since he entered with a dim-witted smile. — And you, whatever-your-name-is-and-whatever-you-are - he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the tragic woman had already left) - what happened to you there last night?
