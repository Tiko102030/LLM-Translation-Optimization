Louis Ivanovna, would you please sit down — he said with a glimpse to the elegantly dressed damsel in reddish-pink who had been standing there as though she dared not sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her — Ich danke,[17  - Thanks (Ger.)] — she said and quietly, with a soft rustling of silk, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white crochet trimming, like an inflated balloon, spread out around the chair and took up almost half the room. It wafted perfumes. However, the lady obviously blushed at occupying half the room and at the perfume wafting from her, though she smiled cowardly and impudently together but with obvious worry. The mourning lady finally finished and was about to stand up. Just then, with some noise, quite dashing and somehow particularly swiveling her shoulders with every step, an officer entered, tossed his cap with a braid on the table and sat in the armchair. The voluminous dame jumped out of place as soon as she saw him and with especial enthusiasm began to crouch down; but the officer didn't pay any attention to her at all, while she was no longer daring to sit down near him.
