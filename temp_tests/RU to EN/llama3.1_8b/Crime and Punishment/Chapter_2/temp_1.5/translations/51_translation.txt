A small depression formed under the stone; at once he began to toss everything from his pocket into it. The wallet was centered on top and yet there still remained space in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one spin to its previous side, and it landed exactly in its former place, only slightly, almost imperceptibly seeming higher. But he gathered dirt around it and stepped down on its edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed for the square. Again a strong, barely manageable joy, as it was yesterday at the office, overpowered him for an instant. "The ends have been hidden! And to whom could it even come to mind to look under this stone? He may be lying here from when the house was built and will stay just as long."
