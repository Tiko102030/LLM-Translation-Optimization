And suddenly, to his greatest astonishment, he caught a faint sound of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming, and crying out in distress, speaking so quickly that it was impossible to make out what she was saying - no doubt about begging them to stop beating her because they were mercilessly pounding her on the stairs. The beaters' voice became so horrible with malice and madness that it was nothing more than a hoarse croak, but still he was saying something indistinct, also quickly, and almost choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled all over like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! And Ilya Petrovich is beating the landlady here! He is kicking her with his legs, battering her head against the stairs - that's clear from the sounds, cries and blows. Has reality gone mad? It was audible how people gathered in all the rooms, on every floor of the staircase, voices were raised, people shouted, banged on doors, stampeded out. "But why then, but why this?" he repeated to himself seriously, thinking that he had gone completely crazy. But no, he is hearing everything too clearly!
