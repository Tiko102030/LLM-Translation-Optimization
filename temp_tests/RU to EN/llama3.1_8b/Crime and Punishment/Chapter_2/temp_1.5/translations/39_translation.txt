The scribe began to dictate the customary response in such a case, namely that he was unable to pay and promised it someday, that he would not leave the city and would neither sell nor give away any of his possessions, etc. -"You can't write; your pen is slipping from your hand," observed the scribe, eyeing Raskolnikov with curiosity."Are you ill?" "Yes... my head is spinning... go on!"-"All right; sign it. The scribe took back the paper and occupied himself with other tasks. Raskolnikov gave up his pen but instead of getting up and leaving he put both elbows on the table and gripped his head in his hands. They were hammering a nail into his skull. A strange idea suddenly came to him: get up now, approach Nikodim Fyodorovich, and tell him everything from yesterday - all the details - then go together with them to the apartment and show them the things, which were hidden away somewhere. The urge was so strong that he had already risen from his seat in order to carry it out.
