But whence did these feelings come from? Conversely, now if the room suddenly filled with first-rate friends of his, there would probably be no human word for them in him; to that extent, his heart has suddenly emptied itself. A somber feeling of agonizing, endless solitude and alienation has suddenly made its presence felt to his soul. Not the baseness of his emotional confessions to Ilya Petrovich, nor the poruchik's triumph over him have so suddenly turned his heart around. Oh, what concern is it to him now about his own baseness, about all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, demands, offices and etc.! If he were condemned even in this moment to be burned at the stake, he would not even stir, and maybe wouldn't even listen to the verdict carefully. Something strange and unfamiliar was happening with him, new, sudden, and never-before-happened. Not that he understood anything, but he clearly felt with all the force of feeling that he could no longer approach these people, the ward office workers, with anything – not only with sensitive and expansive emotions as before, but even with anything at all; if they were his own closest brothers and sisters rather than the ward's poruchiks, it would be utterly pointless for him to turn to them and wouldn't make any sense in life. He had never experienced a feeling so strange and terrible before. And what is most agonizing – this was more of an experience than a consciousness, than a notion; direct sensation, the most agonizing sensation that he had ever experienced in his life. The scribe began dictating to him the form of an ordinary letter written on such an occasion, namely that he could not pay, promises something sometime later, won't leave the city, won't sell or give away property, and so on.
