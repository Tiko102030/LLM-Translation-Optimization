Poverty is no vice, my friend, but in any case. It's well known that he couldn't take offense. You probably upset him with something, and yourself as well didn’t hold back – continued Nikodim Fomich, courteously addressing Raskolnikov – but it was all for nothing: a truly unimportant person, I assure you, but powder, powder! He exploded, boiled over, burnt out - and that's it! And everything passed by! And as a result only one thing remains: gold of the heart. They even nicknamed him in the regiment "lieutenant-powder"... How nice was that regiment! - exclaimed Ilia Petrovich, very pleased that they were pleasantly teasing him, but still waking him up. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to all of them. – Oh, for mercy's sake, captain, please – began he very openly addressing Nikodim Fomich again – pay attention and understand my situation... I'm even willing to ask their forgiveness if I made any mistakes from our side.
