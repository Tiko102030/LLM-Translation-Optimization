—"Pardon me, Captain," he began quite freely, addressing himself to Nikolai Fyodorovich unexpectedly — get into my position... I'm even willing to ask them for forgiveness if I've failed in any way on my part. I am a poor and sick student, downtrodden by poverty. I was a student once, but now I can no longer support myself; however, I will receive some money. My mother and sister are in the province — I'll get it sent to me, and I... will pay it back. My landlady is a good woman, but she's so infuriated that I've lost my lessons and haven't been paying her for four months - not even sending me food... And I simply don't understand this bill at all! Now she's demanding the loaned money from me through this letter, what am I supposed to say? — But it has nothing to do with us..." 
—"Please, please let me explain, I completely agree with you," Rashkolyov took over again, addressing himself not to the clerk but to Nikolai Fyodorovich exclusively, though trying with all his might also to address himself and Ilya Petrovich, despite the latter obstinately pretending to rummage through papers and disdainfully ignoring him. —Please let me explain myself on my own part that I've been living at her place for almost three years now, from when I first arrived in the province and before... Before anything, really, it was an understanding between us that I would marry her daughter - a verbal agreement completely free of any conditions. That young girl... Well, she suited me well too, actually, even if I wasn't in love with her, so you could say it was just a youthful mistake; however, what's essential is this: my landlady used to make loans to me back then and I lived somewhat carelessly... 
—We're not asking for that sort of intimacy, sir, and we don't have the time," rudely cut in Ilya Petrovich with a triumphing tone. But Rashkolyov suddenly stopped him as it became extremely difficult for him to speak on his own account. —But please, just let me tell part of this story... how things were... in my turn, although it's pointless and excessive, yet I'm willing to recount... Last year the girl died from typhus; I stayed a tenant, just as I was before; but the landlady, once she'd moved into her current apartment, told me... spoke with friends' feelings towards me... that she had every trust in me and everything would be... But that if I would like to give her this loaned document for 115 rubles - what she believed I owed her at the time — she'd start crediting me all over again as much as needed, and never once use it while waiting until I paid it off myself. These were her very words... She said these: 'whenever you hand this bill to her,' so that I could give her a written promise of repayment, just for a mere nothing... But now, when I've lost lessons and I don't have any money, she has my paper presented in payment... What am I supposed to say? 
—These sensitive details, sir, are irrelevant to us — harshly cut off Ilya Petrovich. —You need to give your comments and a promise of repayment; as for you getting entangled with those women and these tragic matters - we simply have nothing to do with this.
