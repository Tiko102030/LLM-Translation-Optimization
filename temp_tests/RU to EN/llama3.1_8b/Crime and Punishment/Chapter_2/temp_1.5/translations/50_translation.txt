Further in the courtyard, peeking out from behind a fence was the corner of a low, sooted, stone shed, obviously part of some workshop. There, probably, there was some kind of establishment, a carriage shop or a blacksmith's shop, or something like that; everywhere, almost right at the gates, swirled a lot of coal dust. "How nice it would be to dump everything and get out!" - suddenly flashed in his mind. Not noticing anyone in the courtyard, he took a few strides through the gates and saw just now near the gates, attached to the fence was a ditch (as is often arranged in such houses where there are many factory workers, work-gangs, cab drivers, etc.), and above the ditch, right on the fence, written in chalk was always the sharp remark that one finds in similar cases: "It is forbidden to linger here". Good that he didn't arouse any suspicion that he had gone inside and stopped. "All of this can be quickly bundled up somewhere and left!" Again glancing around, he already had his hand in his pocket, as suddenly near the outer wall, between the gates and the ditch, where all the space was only a width of an ell, he noticed a large unworked stone, approximately weighing about one and a half pounds, leaning directly against the stone city wall. Behind this wall was the street, a sidewalk, it could be heard how people were scurrying past, of which there is always a multitude here; but behind the gates, no one could see him, unless someone came from the street, what could happen, and so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top firmly with both hands, gathered all his strength and overturned the stone. Under the stone a small depression formed; at once he started throwing everything from his pocket into it.
