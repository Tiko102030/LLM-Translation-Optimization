Louisa Ivanovna hurriedly sat down to almost half a floor and with frequent small steps jumped up, flying out of the office. — Again thunder, again loud noise and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — The quartermaster Nikodim Fyodorovich addressed Illya Petrovich in an affable and friendly manner: — Again you disturbed my heart, and it boiled over! I even heard from the stairs. — What's the matter? — replied Illya Petrovich with noble carelessness (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!"), moving some papers to another table and melodramatically swaying with each step, where he stepped, there was his shoulder; — just see: a writer, namely a student, who is no longer paying money, gives us vouchers, doesn't clean the apartment, non-stop complaints arrive about them, and they themselves appeared in protest, that I lit a cigarette among them! They accuse you, and look - take a glance at them: look at how attractive they are now. — Poverty is not a fault, comrade, but come on!
