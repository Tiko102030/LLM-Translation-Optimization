Then he tried to get up, wanted to run away, but always someone would stop him by force and he'd fall back into powerlessness and a daze again. Finally he came to himself completely. It happened in the morning at ten o'clock. At this time of day on clear days, the sun would pass through his right wall in a long strip and light up the corner near the door. Next to his bed stood Nastasya, and another man was standing nearby, scrutinizing him with great curiosity and entirely unknown to him. This was a young fellow in a kaftan, with a beard, and he looked like an artisan on first glance. The landlady looked out from behind the half-open door. Raskolnikov raised himself up. — Who's this, Nastasya? — he asked, pointing to the young man.
