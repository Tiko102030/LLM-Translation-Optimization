He remembered only how he had taken one gulp of cold water and spilled some from the cup on his chest. Then there came a state of oblivion. III

But however, he was not altogether in a state of complete unconsciousness all the time: it was a delirious condition with hallucinations and semi-consciousness. Much later he remembered many things. Sometimes it seemed to him that a lot of people were gathering around him and wanted to take him away somewhere, arguing about him and quarreling with each other. And then suddenly he would be alone in the room, and everyone had left, and they were afraid of him, only occasionally slightly opening the door to look at him, threatening him, whispering something to each other, laughing and teasing him. Nastasya was often by his side; he also remembered another person who seemed very familiar, but couldn't guess who it was and was pining for it, even crying. Sometimes it seemed to him that a month had passed since he lay down; at another time, it seemed like the same day was going on. But about one thing or another, he completely forgot; however, every minute he remembered that he had forgotten something that couldn't be forgotten - he tormented himself, suffered, trying to remember, groaned, fell into a frenzy or an unbearable, insufferable fear. Then he tried to get up from the bed, wanted to run away, but someone always held him back by force, and he would fall into powerlessness and unconsciousness again.
