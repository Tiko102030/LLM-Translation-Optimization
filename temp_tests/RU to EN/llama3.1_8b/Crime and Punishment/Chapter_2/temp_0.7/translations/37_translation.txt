All these sensitive details, sir, do not concern us at all — Ilya Petrovich snapped bluntly — you must give a review and an undertaking, and what you were pleased to be in love with and all those tragic places, they are of no interest to us whatsoever. — Well, you... cruel... — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write, then, said the scribe to Raskolnikov. What am I supposed to write? he asked somewhat coarsely. — And I'll dictate it to you, the scribe replied. It seemed to Raskolnikov that the scribe had begun to be careless and contemptuous of him after his confession, but strangely enough — he suddenly felt utterly indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change came somehow all at once, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little longer, he would certainly have been amazed at how he could speak with them like that just a minute ago, and even impose his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
