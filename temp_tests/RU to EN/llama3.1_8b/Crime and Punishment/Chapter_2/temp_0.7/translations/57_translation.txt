"Don't bother," he said, "I came... well, what is it: I have no lessons... I was going to... anyway, I don't need any lessons at all."

"You know something?" observed Razumikhin, who was staring at him intently, "you're babbling!"

"No, I'm not," said Raskolnikov standing up from the sofa. As he stood up to face Razumikhin, he hadn't thought that he would be meeting him face to face. But now, in an instant, he realized, with experience, that he was least inclined to meet anyone, in this whole world, face to face at this moment. All his bile rose up in him. He almost choked on his own anger towards himself for having just stepped into Razumikhin's house. "Goodbye!" he suddenly said and headed for the door.
