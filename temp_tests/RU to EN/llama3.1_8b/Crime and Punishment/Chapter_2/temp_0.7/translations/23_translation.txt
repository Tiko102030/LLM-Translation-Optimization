The plump lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and began to squat down with a look of peculiar excitement; but the officer paid no attention to her whatsoever, and she didn't dare sit beside him again. This was a lieutenant, assistant supervisor's aide, with reddish sideburns that stuck out horizontally in all directions and extremely small facial features that expressed nothing particularly special except for some audacity. He glanced at Raskolnikov askance and partly with indignation: the young man's clothes were too shabby, and despite his own lowly status, he still didn't have a proper posture; Raskolnikov looked at him directly and for too long, so that the lieutenant was even offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin wouldn't cower from his swift glance. "Required... by summons...," Raskolnikov stammered in response. "This is about collecting money from them, from the student," the clerk hastened to explain, tearing himself away from his papers. "Here!
