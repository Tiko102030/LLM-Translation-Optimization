Where did these feelings come from? On the other hand, if the room were to be suddenly filled with his most intimate friends, he wouldn't have a single human word for them, so empty had become his heart. A dismal feeling of agonizing, infinite loneliness and isolation suddenly manifested itself in his soul. It wasn't the baseness of his confessions to Ilya Petrovich or the port officer's triumph over him that turned his heart around like this; no, not even the triviality of it all - his ambitions, the officers, Germans, demands, offices, etc. etc. What was he now concerned with? If they were to sentence him to be burned at the stake right now, he wouldn't budge, nor would he likely even listen carefully to the verdict. Something completely alien and new was happening to him, something that had never happened before. He didn't understand it, but he clearly felt, with all his might of feeling, that not only was he unable to address these people at the quarter's office - and they were no ordinary people, but rather the officers who resided there - but even if they were his own brothers and sisters, he wouldn't need to address them or consider it necessary in any case; he had never experienced anything like this strange and terrifying feeling before. And what was most agonizing about it all was that it was a sensation rather than a realization, more than an idea; a direct, agonizing sensation, the most painful of all the sensations he had ever experienced in his life.
