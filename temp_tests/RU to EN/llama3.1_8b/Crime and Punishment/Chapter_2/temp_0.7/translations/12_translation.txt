He didn't respond and held a piece of paper in his hands, not printing it out. — Oh, don't get up, he said, continued Nastasya, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was pulling his legs off the couch. — You're sick, so just stay there: you won't burn anything. What's all that in your hand? He looked at it: in his right hand were torn pieces of fringing, a sock, and scraps of a ripped pocket. And he slept with them like that. Later, thinking about this, he remembered that, half-awake in the heat, he had tightly clenched everything in his hand and fell asleep again. — And look at all these rags he's collected and sleeps with them, exactly like treasure... — Nastasya let out her hysterical laughter. In an instant, he stuffed it all under his greatcoat and stared intently into the folds of it. Although he could only grasp very little in that moment, he felt that they wouldn't treat a man this way when they came to take him away.
