—"Have mercy on me, captain," he began in a very free and easy way, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fyodorovich - "pay attention to my situation... I'm even willing to ask them for forgiveness if I've been remiss in some way. I am a poor and sick student, overwhelmed by poverty. I used to be a student, because now I can't take care of myself, but I'll get the money... My mother and sister are living in - y gubernia. They will send me the money and I... I'll pay it off. My landlady is a good woman, but she's so angry that I've lost my lessons and haven't paid her for four months, not even giving me food... And I don't understand at all what this promissory note is! Now she's demanding payment on this loaned letter, what am I supposed to do?!" 

—"But this isn't our concern..." the clerk observed again...

—"Let me, let me," Raskolnikov interrupted once more, addressing not the clerk but Nikodim Fyodorovich, while trying his best to address also Ilia Petrovich, although he persistently pretended to be digging through papers and contemptuously ignored him - "let me explain on my side... I've been living with her for about three years now, since I arrived from the province and before that... well, why not tell the truth? From the very beginning I gave her a verbal promise to marry her daughter, a completely free promise... She was a girl... well, she suited me, though I wasn't in love with her. It's just youth, I mean to say that my landlady made me loans then and I lived partly such a life... I was very reckless at the time..."

—"We're not asking for such personal details from you, charitable person, and there's no time for it," Ilia Petrovich roughly interrupted again, but Raskolnikov stopped him with fervor, though he found it extremely hard to speak suddenly - "But let me please tell some of the story... how it was and... on my side... although it's unnecessary, I agree with you that it would be better not to talk about it..." 

—"A year ago this girl died of typhus, and I remained a lodger just as before. When she moved to her current apartment, she said to me... in a friendly tone... that she was completely confident in me and everything else... but that maybe I would like to give her this promissory note for 115 rubles, considering it my debt. Please note: she said exactly that if I gave her this paper, she would start lending me as much as I wanted again, and that she would never use this letter herself - at least not until I paid off the debt myself... And now, when I've lost my lessons and have nothing to eat, she's sending it to collection. What am I supposed to say? "

—"All these sensitive details are irrelevant to us," Ilia Petrovich cut him off nastily, "you must give a response and a commitment, and what you were in love with and all those tragic scenes - that has nothing to do with our business."
