Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though feeling that he was all broken up inside, but his consciousness was still with him. He feared being chased, feared that in half an hour, perhaps in a quarter of an hour already, instructions would come to watch over him; therefore, no matter what, the ends had to be disposed of for now. He had to manage while there were still some strength and at least some reasoning left... Where to go? This decision had been made long ago: "Throw everything into the gutter, and end with it." So he decided last night, in a delirium, during those moments when, he remembered this, he several times felt like getting up and going: "as quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and throw everything away." But throwing things away proved to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the Yekaterininsky Canal embankment for over half an hour, possibly even longer, and a few times he glanced at the openings into the canal where they met him.
