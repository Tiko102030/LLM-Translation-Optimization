And lo, to his greatest amazement, he suddenly caught a glimpse of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming, and sobbing, rushing, hurrying, spitting out words so incoherently that they couldn't be understood, begging for something - obviously begging them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrible with malice and frenzy that it was already nothing but a mere croak, but the beater too was saying something, also incoherently, quickly, and choking. Suddenly Rascolnikov shuddered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! Ilya Petrovich is here beating his landlady! He's beating her with his feet, knocking her head against the step - that's clear, it can be heard from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What's this? Has the world turned upside down? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, on the whole staircase, voices were heard, shouts, people were rushing up and down, knocking on doors, slamming them shut, running out. "But why, why, what is this?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he must have gone mad. But no, he's hearing it too clearly...
