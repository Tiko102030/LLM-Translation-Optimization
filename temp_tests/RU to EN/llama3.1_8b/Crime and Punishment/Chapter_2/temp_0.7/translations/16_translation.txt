His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is deceit! They want to lead me astray with trickery and then get rid of me," he continued to himself as he walked up the stairs. "It's bad that I'm almost out of my mind... I can tell some foolish lie..."

On the staircase, he remembered leaving all his belongings in a hole in the wall - "and it's probably intentionally left without me during the search", he thought, and stopped. But such despair and cynicism about his impending doom suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand dismissively and continued on. "Just as long as I get out of here quickly..."

Outside again, the heat was unbearable; not a single drop of rain in all these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime plaster; again the stench from the shops and bars; again drunkards and half-collapsed cab drivers. The sun flashed brightly into his eyes so that it hurt to look, and his head started spinning again - a familiar feeling for someone who had been out of bed and into the bright sunlight without acclimatizing first. Having reached the corner onto the street from yesterday, he looked anxiously at it, at that house... and immediately turned away his gaze. "If they ask me, I might just say so," he thought as he approached the office building.
