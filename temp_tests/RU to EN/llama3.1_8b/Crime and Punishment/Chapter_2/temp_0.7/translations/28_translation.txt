"Yes, well she's my mistress anyway?" The clerk looked at him with a condescending smile of pity and at the same time some triumph, as on a novice who was just starting to be bombarded: "What do you think now, how are you feeling?" But what business did he have now to care about the borrowed letter, the claims? Was it worth any concern, in turn, even any attention! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked himself questions, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from crushing danger - that's what filled his entire being at this moment, without forethought, without analysis, without future guesses and riddles, without doubts or questions. This was a moment of complete, immediate, pure animal joy. But in the very same moment something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The lieutenant, still shaken by the lack of respect, full of anger and apparently wanting to support the wounded ambition, attacked the unfortunate "puffed up lady" with all his might, who had been looking at him since he entered with a stupidly smug smile. - And you, that one and this one," he suddenly shouted from the depths of his throat (the tragic lady had already left), "what happened to you last night?"
