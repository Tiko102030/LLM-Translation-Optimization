The light was on and he quickly began to examine himself from head to toe, all his clothes: were there any signs? But it couldn't be done that way: shivering with cold, he started taking everything off and examining it again. He turned everything over, even the last thread and patch, and not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no signs at all; only on the spot where the pants had torn down below and hung in fringes, on this fringe remained thick traces of dried blood. He grabbed a folding big knife and cut off the fringe. There seemed to be nothing more. Suddenly he remembered that his wallet and things, which he pulled out from the old woman's trunk, were still all in his pockets! He hadn't even thought about taking them out and hiding them yet! Not even now, while examining his clothes, did he remember them. What was this?
