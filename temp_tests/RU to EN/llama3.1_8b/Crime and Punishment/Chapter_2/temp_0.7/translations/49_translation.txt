He was becoming extremely absent-minded and forgetful and he knew it. There was no time to lose! He went towards the Neva along Voznessensky Avenue; but on his way a sudden thought struck him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to leave somewhere very far, perhaps even in the Islands again, and there, in some solitary spot, in the forest, under a bush, – bury everything and the tree, probably, mark?" And although he felt that at this moment he was not capable of discussing everything clearly and sensibly, the thought seemed to him infallible. But it was not destined for him to reach the Islands; instead something else happened: stepping out onto the square from Voznessensky Avenue, he suddenly saw a gateway into a courtyard to the left, completely surrounded by walls on all sides. To the right, immediately after entering through the gates, far down the courtyard stretched a blank, unwhitewashed wall of the adjacent four-storey house. Left, parallel with the blank wall and at once after the gate, ran a wooden fence for twenty steps into the courtyard, then it made a turn to the left. This was a closed-off place, where some materials lay. Further in the depression of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooted stone shed stuck out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
