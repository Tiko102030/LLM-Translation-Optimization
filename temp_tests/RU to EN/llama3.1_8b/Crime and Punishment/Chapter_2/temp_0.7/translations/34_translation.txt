Louisa Ivanovna jumped around with an amused politeness, jumping from one place to another, and when she reached the doors, she bumped into a prominent officer, who had an open fresh face and thick, fair mustaches. This was Nikolay Fyodorovich, the quarter's overseer. Louisa Ivanovna quickly sat down almost on the floor and jumped out of the office with quick little steps, like a bird in flight. "Again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane!" said Nikolay Fyodorovich to Ilya Petrovich, friendly and politely, "again you've disturbed my heart, it's boiling over! I heard it from the staircase." "What?" replied Ilya Petrovich with noble nonchalance (and even not what, but something like: "Da-a sh-ta-a!" as he walked to another table with some papers, dramatically throwing his shoulders with each step), "look here, please: the writer, that is, the student, used to be, doesn't pay money, issues promissory notes, and doesn't clean the apartment. They keep sending us complaints about them, but they came in a claim that I lit a cigar near them! They're complaining themselves, but look at them now - see how attractive they are!" Poverty is not a vice, comrade, oh well, it's something anyway.
