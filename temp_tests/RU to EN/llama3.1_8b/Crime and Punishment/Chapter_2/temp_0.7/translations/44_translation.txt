— Where to, if I may ask? — Down the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered curtly, brusquely, his whole face as white as a handkerchief, and not lowering his black inflamed eyes from Ilia Petrovich's gaze. — He can hardly stand on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fomich noted. — Nothing! — Ilia Petrovich said somehow particularly. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but looking at the clerk, who was also very attentively watching him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
