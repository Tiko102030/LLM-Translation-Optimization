A small depression had formed under the stone; he immediately started throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and yet there was still room left in the depression. He then grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one spin to its previous side, and it just fit back into its original place, slightly higher only by a tiny bit. But he scraped up some earth and pressed it down with his foot around the edges. There was nothing visible anymore. Then he left and headed towards the square. Again, a strong, barely manageable joy seized him for an instant, just like in the office yesterday. "The ends are buried! And who could possibly think of looking under this stone? It may be lying here since the house was built and will lie here for just as long again."
