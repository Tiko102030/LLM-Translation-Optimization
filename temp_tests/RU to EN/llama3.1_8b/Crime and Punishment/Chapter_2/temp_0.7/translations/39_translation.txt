The scribe began to dictate to him the customary form of such a reply: that he could not pay, that he promised to do so some day, that he would not leave the city, and that he would neither sell nor give away his property, etc. — You cannot write, your pen is slipping from your hand, observed the scribe, looking at Raskolnikov with curiosity. Are you ill? — Yes... my head is spinning... go on! — That's all; sign it. The scribe took the paper and began to attend to others. Raskolnikov handed over his pen, but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clasped his head in his hands. A nail was being hammered into his temples. A strange idea suddenly came to him: to stand up now, approach Nikodim Fyodorovich and tell him everything that had happened yesterday, down to the last detail, then go with them to the apartment and point out their things, which were scattered about everywhere. The impulse was so strong that he had already gotten up from his seat, ready to carry it out.
