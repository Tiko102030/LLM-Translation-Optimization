He simply wouldn't have paid any attention to anyone else, and he probably would have bitten him if someone had started talking to him...

He stopped suddenly when he stepped out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "Here he lives in that house," he thought. "What's it, but I myself came here by chance! The same old story as before... But very interesting: did I come here or was I just passing and happened to stop? No matter; I said on the third day... that I'd go see him after that the next day, well, I'll go! As if I couldn't go in now..."

He climbed up to Razumikhin's place on the fifth floor. He was home, in his tiny room, and at this very moment he was working, writing, and himself let him in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat in a tattered dressing gown, barefoot slippers, tousled, unshaven and unwashed.
