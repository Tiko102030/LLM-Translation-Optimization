He drew a breath more freely. "Maybe it's not that!" Little by little he began to regain his composure, persuading himself with all his might to calm down and collect himself. "Some foolishness, some trivial carelessness, and I can make myself completely unrecognizable! Ah... too bad there is no air here - stuffiness... My head spins even more... and my mind too..."

He felt an awful disorder in everything about him. He was afraid of not being able to control himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything, but it wasn't working out at all. The clerk was very interesting to him: he wanted to guess something from his face, unravel the threads. This was a young man of twenty-two years old, with a swarthy and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his age, dressed in style and fashionably thin, with a part on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white and polished fingers and golden chains on his vest. With one former foreigner here he even said two words in French, very successfully. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to a half-dressed red-haired lady who was standing there as if she were afraid to sit down herself, although a chair was next to her.
