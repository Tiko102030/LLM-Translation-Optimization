— Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said to the momentarily dressed-up red and pink lady who stood there as if not daring to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke,[17 - I thank (Ger.) — Ed.] she said and quietly, with a silk rustle, sat down on the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread out around the chair and occupied almost half of the room. It wafted scents. But the lady, clearly nervous about taking up so much space and being swept by such strong scents, although she was smiling cowardly and shamelessly together, showed obvious distress. The mournful lady finally finished speaking and stood up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow turning her shoulders in particular with every step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a badge on the table, and sat down in armchairs. The lush lady jumped right out of place when she saw him and took on a particularly excited posture; but the officer paid no attention to her at all, and she dared not sit down next to him anymore.
