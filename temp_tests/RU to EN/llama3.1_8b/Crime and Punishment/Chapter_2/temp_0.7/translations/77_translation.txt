Well, and who are you? — I'm an artisan from our office, hired by Mr. Shelopaev, and I've come here on business. — Please have a seat on that chair, — Razumikhin sat down on another chair across from Raskolnikov. — You did the right thing, brother, coming to your senses, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You haven't eaten or drunk for four days. Honestly, they were giving you tea with just a spoonful of sugar. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense, that something had hit your head, some kind of nervous rot, the food was bad, he says they weren't giving you enough beer or vinegar, which is why you were sick, but there's nothing to worry about, it'll pass and get better.
