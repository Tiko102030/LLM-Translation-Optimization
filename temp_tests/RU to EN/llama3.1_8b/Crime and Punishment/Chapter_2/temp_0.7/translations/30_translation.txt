Ilya Petrovich! — began the clerk carefully, but stopped to wait for a moment, because the boiling officer could not be held back otherwise than by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the lush lady, she was initially trembling with thunder and lightning; but what a strange thing: as more numerous and stronger her insults became, so did her appearance become lovelier, her smile to the menacing lieutenant becoming more charming. She was hopping on one foot and continuously squatting, impatiently waiting for someone finally let her say her word, and she waited. — There's no noise or brawl at my place, sir captain, — suddenly gushed forth a torrent from her, as if sprouted corn with a strong German accent, although speaking briskly in Russian, — and no schoolgirl nonsense at all; they came drunk and this is what I'll tell you, sir captain, but I'm not to blame… My family has noble standing, sir captain, and noble upbringing, sir captain, and I have always, always never wanted any schoolgirl nonsense. But they are completely drunk and then they ask for another drink, and then one of them lifted his legs and began playing a piano foot with the keys, which is not good at all in a respectable house, and he was smashing the piano with his feet, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I told him. And then as I quickly called the doorman and Karl came, he took Karl and poked his eyes out, and Henriette too poked her eyes out, but they hit me five times on the cheek. Which is not dignified in a respectable house, sir captain, and I shouted. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood in it like a small pig, squealing; which is shameful.
