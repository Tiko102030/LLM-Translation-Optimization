This was some especially disheveled person with an immobile idea in their gaze. "You won't learn anything from this because it's all the same to him," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, — said the scribe and pointed ahead with his finger, showing the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth one by order), a narrow and overcrowded space filled with people - a crowd, somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, dressed in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at a table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and red-faced, spotted, and quite prominently dressed, with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea saucer, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. The latter glanced briefly at it, said "wait" and continued working with the lady in mourning. He breathed more freely.
