There are no clues!" — and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed nervously, quietly, almost inaudibly, for a long time, and kept laughing the whole time as he passed through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard, where three days ago he ran into that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts began to occupy his mind. It also seemed to him now that it was extremely unpleasant to pass by that bench, on which he sat and pondered after the girl left, and it would be extremely hard to run into that old man again, to whom he gave a two-grivna tip: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around listlessly and spitefully. All his thoughts were now revolving around one central point, — and he himself felt that this was indeed such a central point and that there existed and that now, precisely now, he had been left alone face to face with it, — and that it was even for the first time in two months.
