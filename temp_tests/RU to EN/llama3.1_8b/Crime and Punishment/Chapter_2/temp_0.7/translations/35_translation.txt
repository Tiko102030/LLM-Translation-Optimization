Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but anyway! It's well known that he couldn't take an offense. You probably offended him somehow and you yourself couldn't restrain yourselves, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with courtesy - but it's all pointless: the most blabbering, I'm telling you, person, but powder, powder! He burst forth, boiled over, burned out – and that's it! And everything passed by! And in the end only gold of his heart remains! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

And what a company he was part of! exclaimed Ilya Petrovich, very pleased that they had been teased so pleasantly, but still prodding Raskolnikov awake. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something uncommonly pleasant to them all. - Oh, forgive me, Captain, began he very freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly, - take a look at my situation... I'm even ready to ask for their apologies if on our part we made any mistakes.
