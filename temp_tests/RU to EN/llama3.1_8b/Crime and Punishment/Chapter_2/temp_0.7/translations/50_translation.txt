Further on, in the courtyard's recess, a corner of a low, soot-covered stone shed was visible behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop or other. There must have been some kind of establishment there - a carriage works or a locksmith's shop, or something similar; almost from the very gates, coal dust was scattered everywhere. "If only I could just shove it somewhere and leave!" he thought suddenly. Not noticing anyone in the courtyard, he strode through the gates and happened to glance down by the gate itself, where there was a trough (as is often arranged in such houses with many factory workers, artisans, cabdrivers, etc.) next to the fence, and on the trough, right above it, just near the fence, was scrawled in chalk: "Here standing is forbidden." Well then, that's even better - no one would suspect that he had entered or stopped. "Everything here is so neatly arranged, I could just shove everything into a pile somewhere and leave!" He looked around once more, already stuffing his hand into his pocket, when suddenly, right next to the outer wall, between the gates and the trough, where all the space was barely an arshin wide, he noticed a large uncut stone, weighing about one and a half poods, leaning directly against the stone city wall. Behind that wall was a street, a sidewalk; you could hear passersby hurrying along - there are always plenty of them here; but no one would be able to see him behind the gates, unless someone came from the street itself - which was possible, and so he needed to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed it firmly with both hands at its top edge, collected all his strength, and turned the stone over. Underneath the stone a small depression formed; immediately he began throwing everything out of his pocket into it.
