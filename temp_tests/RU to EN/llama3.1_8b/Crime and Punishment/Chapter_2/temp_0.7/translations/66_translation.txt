The pain from the whip had subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one disturbing and not quite clear thought now occupied him exclusively. He stood and gazed far off for a long time, intently; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to university, it often happened, perhaps even ten times, that on his way home he would stop at exactly this same spot, intently gaze at this truly magnificent panorama, and each time almost be amazed by one obscure and unsolved impression of his own. An inexplicable chill always seemed to blow over him from this magnificent panorama; the spirit was dumb and deaf for him in this rich picture... He marveled every time at his own gloomy and enigmatic impression and postponed its solution, not trusting himself, into the future.

Now suddenly he sharply remembered these old questions and doubts of his, and it seemed to him that he had not recalled them by chance. One thing struck him as wild and strange: he had stopped at exactly the same spot as before, just as if he really did imagine now that he could think about the same things as before, with the same themes and images that interested him... still so recently. Even it almost became funny to him, but at the same time his chest felt constricted in pain.

In some depth, below, somewhere just visible underfoot, everything from his past seemed to have appeared before him now: all his old thoughts, problems, topics of interest, and impressions, and this entire panorama, and he himself, and everything, everything... It seemed as though he was flying off upwards and everything was disappearing from his eyes. Making a sudden involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt the double ruble clutched in his fist. He relaxed his hand, intently looked at the coin, swung his arm back and threw it into the water; then turned around and went home.

It seemed to him as though he had cut himself off from everything and all with scissors in this very moment.
