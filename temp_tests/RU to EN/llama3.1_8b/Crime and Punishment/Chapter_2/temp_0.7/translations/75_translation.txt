He pointed at the boy and asked, "Is he awake?" She said, "They're waking up." The master replied, "We've woken up." Guessing that they had woken up, the landlady, who was peeking in from behind the door, immediately closed it and hid. She was always shy and endured conversations and explanations with difficulty; she was forty years old and fat, with a black complexion and dark eyes; kind due to her weight and laziness; even excessively coy. Shy beyond necessity. "You... who are you?" he continued questioning the master himself. But at that moment the door swung open again, and Raskolnikov's brother Razumikhin, slightly bent because of his height, entered.
