He wasn't responding and held a piece of paper in his hands, not printing it out. — Yes, don't get up, Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was pulling his legs off the couch. — You're sick, so just stay there: you won't catch fire. What's all this in your hand? He looked down: in his right hand were cut-off pieces of braid, a sock, and scraps torn from the pocket. And he slept with them like that. Later, thinking about it, he remembered that, half-waking up in the heat, he gripped everything tightly in his hand and fell asleep again. — Look at all these rags he's collected and sleeps with them, just like a treasure... — Nastasya let out her sickeningly nervous laughter. At once, he pushed it all under his greatcoat and stared intently into it with his eyes. Even though he could only vaguely understand what was going on in that moment, he felt that they wouldn't treat the man so well when they came to take him away.
