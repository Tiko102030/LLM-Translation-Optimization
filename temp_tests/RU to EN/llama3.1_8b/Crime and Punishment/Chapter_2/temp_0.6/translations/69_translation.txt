But no, he hears it all too clearly!... But then, it seems, they will come to him now as well, if so, "because... correctly, all this is from the same... because of yesterday... God!" He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand didn't rise... and what's the use anyway? Fear, like ice, enveloped his soul, tormented him, petrified him... But finally, after this whole commotion that had been going on for ten faithful minutes, it began to gradually subside. The landlady was still wailing and screaming, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and scolding... But finally, it seems, he has calmed down; now you can't even hear him; "has he really left! God!" Yes, the landlady is leaving too, still with a groan and tears... and her door has closed. The crowd is dispersing from the stairs to the apartments, exclaiming, arguing, calling out, sometimes raising their voices to a shout, sometimes lowering them to a whisper. There must have been many of them; almost the whole building gathered. "But God, is all this possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov was powerless and collapsed onto the divan, but couldn't even close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of infinite horror that he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a bowl of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he wasn't sleeping, she put the candle on the table and began to unpack what she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
