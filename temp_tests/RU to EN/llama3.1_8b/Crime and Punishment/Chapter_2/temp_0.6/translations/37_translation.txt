All these sensitive details, good sir, are of no concern to us — Ilya Petrovich brutally cut in — you must give a review and a commitment, and what it is that you were so pleased with being in love and all those tragic places, we don't care about any of this. "You...are cruel..." muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also taking up signing. He felt somehow ashamed. "Write," said the clerk to Raskolnikov. "What am I supposed to write?" he asked somewhat especially roughly. "I'll dictate it to you." It seemed to Raskolnikov that the clerk began to be less careful and more contemptuous of him after his confession, but strangely enough — he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in one moment, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little bit, he would certainly have been surprised by how he could speak with them like that just a minute ago, and even impose himself and his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
