Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved over to sit next to him on the divan, awkwardly like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, although he himself could have gotten up, and with his right hand brought a spoonful of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand so that he wouldn't burn himself. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov devoured one spoonful greedily, then another, and a third. But after bringing several more spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and declared that as for further action, they should consult with Zosima. Nastasya entered carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want tea? — Yes, I do. — Hurry up and bring some, Nastasya, because when it comes to tea, it seems we can get by without a doctor.
