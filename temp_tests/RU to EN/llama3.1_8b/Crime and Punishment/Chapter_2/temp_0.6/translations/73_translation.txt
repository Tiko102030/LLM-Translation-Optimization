He remembered only how he had taken one gulp of cold water and spilled some from the glass on his chest. Then came a state of confusion. III He was not entirely in a state of delirium throughout his illness; it was an agitated condition, with hallucinations and partial consciousness. Much later he recalled many things. It seemed to him that a crowd was gathering around him and they wanted to take him away somewhere, arguing fiercely about him and quarreling among themselves. Then suddenly he would be alone in the room, everyone having left and afraid of him, only occasionally opening the door slightly to glance at him, threatening him, whispering something to each other, laughing and mocking him. Nastasya was often near him; he could distinguish another person as well, one who seemed very familiar but whose identity he couldn't figure out and whom he yearned for, even crying. Sometimes it seemed to him that a month had passed since he lay down; at other times it felt like the same day was passing over again. But of what – of what – he had completely forgotten; whereas every minute he remembered that he had forgotten something that couldn't be forgotten – and he tormented himself, suffering, recalling, groaning, falling into fury or an unbearable, unendurable fear. Then he would try to get up, want to flee, but someone always held him back by force, and he would fall into powerlessness and delirium again.
