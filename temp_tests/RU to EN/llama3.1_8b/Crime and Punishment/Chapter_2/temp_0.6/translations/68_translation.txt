And suddenly, to the greatest amazement, he suddenly caught a glimpse of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, shrieking and crying out in haste, uttering words so rapidly that they were almost indistinguishable, pleading for something - obviously begging them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The beatings had become so frightful with malice and fury that only a hoarse growl could be heard, but the beater too was saying something, also rapidly and incoherently, choking on his words. Suddenly Raskolnikov shuddered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! Ilya Petrovich is here beating the landlady! He is kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the stairs - that's clear from the sounds, the screams, and the blows. What's going on? Has the light turned upside down? It could be heard how a crowd gathered in all the floors, along the entire staircase, voices were heard, exclamations rose, people knocked, doors slammed shut, they ran out. "But why, why, how is this possible?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone mad. But no, he can hear everything too clearly!
