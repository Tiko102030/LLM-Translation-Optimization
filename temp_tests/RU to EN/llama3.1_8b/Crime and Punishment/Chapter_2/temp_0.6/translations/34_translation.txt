Louisa Ivanovna with a benevolent smile began to sit down on all sides and, sitting, reached the doors; but in the doors she bumped her back into one prominent officer, with an open fresh face and with excellent thick fair mustaches. This was Nikodim Fomich, the quarter guard inspector. Louisa Ivanovna hastily sat down almost to the floor and with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — kindly and affably addressed Nikodim Fomich to Ilya Petrovich, — again disturbed the heart, again boiled over! He even heard it from the stairs. — What? — with noble nonchalance said Ilya Petrovich (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!"), passing with some papers to another table and dramatically swaying with each step his shoulders, wherever he stepped, there and the shoulder; — here you see: the gentleman writer, that is the student, namely, does not pay money, issued checks, does not clean the apartment, constant complaints come against them, and they have entered a claim that I lit a cigarette among them! They themselves make excuses, but look at them now in their most attractive appearance: — Poverty is not a vice, friend, well yes what!
