He would have simply spat on someone and bitten him if anyone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he got out onto the Malaya Nevka embankment, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "That's where he lives, in that house," he thought. "What is it? But I didn't come here myself! It's the same old story as before... And actually, it's very interesting: did I really come here or was I just walking and happened to stop by? Doesn't matter; I said I'd go see him three days ago... so let's get this over with!"

He went up to Razumikhin's place in the fifth floor. He was home, in his little room, and at that moment he was busy writing and opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat at a table in a tattered dressing gown, with slippers on one foot, tousled, bearded and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
