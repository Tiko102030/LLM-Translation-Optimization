He kept repeating to himself: "Torn remnants of fabric won't arouse any suspicion; it seems that way, it seems that way!" as he stood in the room and with a painful intensity began searching around again, on the floor everywhere, whether he had forgotten something else? The certainty that everything, even his memory, even simple reasoning, left him, was starting to torment him unbearably. "What if this is really it, what if this is the execution?" He exclaimed, pointing out the scraps of braid from his pants lying on the floor in the middle of the room for anyone to see! "What's happening to me!" he cried again, as lost as before. Then a strange thought occurred to him: maybe all his clothes are covered in blood, maybe there are many stains, but he just can't see them, can't notice them because his reasoning has weakened, is fragmented... his mind is clouded... Suddenly he remembered that the wallet was also bloody. "Ah! So then it must be bloody inside too, because I put the wet wallet into my pocket at the time!" He rummaged through his pocket for a moment and – yes, there were indeed stains on the lining of his pocket! "So, therefore, not entirely lost to reason, so there is still some reasoning and memory, since he himself remembered and figured it out!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep breath with all his chest, and then ripped off the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
