The scribe began to dictate to him the usual formula in such a case, that is, I can't pay, I promise to do so someday, I won't leave the city, I won't sell or give away my property, etc. — You can't write, your pen slips out of your hands, observed the scribe, looking at Raskolnikov with curiosity. Are you ill? — Yes... My head is spinning... go on! — Yes, everything; sign it. The scribe took the paper and busied himself with others. Raskolnikov gave back his pen but instead of standing up to leave, he placed both elbows on the table and clenched his hands around his head. They were hammering a nail into his skull. A strange idea suddenly occurred to him: get up now, go over to Nikodim Fyodorovich and tell him everything that happened yesterday, every detail, then go with them to the apartment and point out the things in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already stood up from his seat to carry it out.
