His face showed surprise. "What's the matter?" he shouted, examining his friend from head to foot; then he fell silent and whistled in disapproval. "Is it really that bad?" he said, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "You've outdone our brother," he added, glancing at him. "Sit down, you must be exhausted!" And when Raskolnikov collapsed onto the thin Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. "Are you seriously ill?" he asked. He began to feel Raskolnikov's pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. "Don't bother," he said. "I came... look, I don't have any lessons... I wanted to... well, I don't need lessons at all..."
