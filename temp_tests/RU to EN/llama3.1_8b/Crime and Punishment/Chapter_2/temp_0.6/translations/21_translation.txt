He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "Not quite, probably!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to pull himself together and regain his composure. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness, and I'll be caught for sure! Ah... too bad there's no air here - the heat... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt a terrible disorder in everything about him. He was afraid of not being able to control himself. He tried to cling to something and think about something completely unrelated, but it just wouldn't work out. The clerk greatly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure him out. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and agile physiognomy that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and fatigued, with a part on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white-gloved fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner who had been here, he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louisa Ivanovna, would you sit down, said he briefly to the brightly dressed red-haired lady, who was standing as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was next to her.
