— And where, if you please? — Along the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered curtly, abruptly, his whole face as white as a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes from Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He barely stands on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fyodorovich had noticed. — Nothing! — somehow especially said Ilya Petrovich. Nikodim Fyodorovich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the clerk, who was also very intently looking at him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
