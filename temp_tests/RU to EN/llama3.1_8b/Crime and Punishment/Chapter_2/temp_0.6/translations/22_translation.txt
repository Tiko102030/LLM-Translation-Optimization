Louisa Ivanovna, you sit down, said he to the slightly dressed-up red and pink lady, who stood all the time as if she didn't dare to sit herself, although a chair was next to her. — I thank you, said that one and quietly, with a silk sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half of the room. It smelled of perfume. But the lady, apparently, felt awkward about taking up half of the room and smelling so strongly of perfume, although she was smiling timidly and shamelessly together, but with a clear sign of distress. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially turning her shoulders with every step, an officer entered, threw his hat with the cockade on the table and sat in an armchair. The plump lady jumped out of her seat at the sight of him and took a special delight in squatting; but the officer didn't even glance at her, and she was no longer able to sit down next to him.
