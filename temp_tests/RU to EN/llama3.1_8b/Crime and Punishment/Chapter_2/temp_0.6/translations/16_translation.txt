His head was spinning and throbbing from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out completely," he thought to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "It's terrible that I'm almost out of my mind... I can make up some silly story..."

On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his things just as they were, in a heap - "and now, maybe on purpose without me they'll search through everything," he thought and stopped. But despair and cynicism about his impending doom suddenly took hold of him, so he waved his hand dismissively and continued on. "Just get it over with..."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; not a single drop of rain had fallen in these days. Again the dust, brick, and plaster; again the stench from the cafes and bars; again drunkards, Chuvash peddlers, and half-ruined cab drivers everywhere. The sun flashed brightly in his eyes, so it hurt to look, and his head spun completely around - a familiar feeling of feverishness that suddenly hits someone when they step out into the bright sunlight. He reached the turn onto yesterday's street, glanced nervously at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask me, I might say so," he thought as he approached the office building.
