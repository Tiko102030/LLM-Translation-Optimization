He lay there for a very long time. It sometimes happened that he seemed to wake up and in those moments noticed that it was already late at night, but the thought of getting up did not come to his mind. Finally, he noticed that it was light as during the day. He lay on the couch upside down, still dazed from recent forgetfulness. The terrible, desperate screams from the street reached him sharply, which, however, he listened to every night under his window at three o'clock. They were what woke him up now. "Ah! And here the drunkards are leaving the bars," he thought - "third hour - and suddenly jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. - What! Third already?" He sat on the couch, - and then everything came back to him!
