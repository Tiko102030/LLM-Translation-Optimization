Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt that he was completely broken, his consciousness remained with him. He feared pursuit, he feared that in half an hour, perhaps even a quarter of an hour, the instructions to follow him would be issued; therefore, no matter what, it was necessary to bury the ends in time. It was necessary to manage while there were still some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This had been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the gutter, and the ends into the water, and that's it." He had resolved this even last night, in a delirium, in those moments when he remembered it several times, he was on the verge of getting up and going: "as quickly as possible, quickly, and get rid of everything". But it turned out to be very difficult to get rid of. He had been wandering along the embankment of Yekaterininsky Canal for half an hour already, maybe even more, and several times he glanced at the openings into the ditch where they were met.
