Ilya Petrovich! — began the clerk carefully, but stopped to wait for a moment, because the boiling officer couldn't be held back otherwise than by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the lush lady, she trembled at first like a leaf at thunder and lightning; but what's strange: the more numerous and fierce her curses became, the more charming her appearance became, the more enchanting her smile addressed to the formidable officer became. She was hopping on one foot and constantly squatting, impatiently waiting for someone to finally allow her to speak up, and she waited. — There won't be any noise or fight here at my place, Mr. Captain, — she suddenly babbled like a bean sprouting, with a strong German accent, although speaking rapidly in Russian, — and there won't be any scandal, no, not a bit of it! And they came drunk, and I'll tell you all about it, Mr. Captain, but it's not my fault... My house is noble, Mr. Captain, and so is the upbringing, Mr. Captain, and I have always, always not wanted any scandal. But they were completely drunk and then asked three questions again, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his feet, which is absolutely not good in a noble house, and he's breaking the piano, and there's no manners at all here, and I said so. And he took the pipe and pushed everyone behind him with it. And then, as soon as I quickly called the doorman to summon Karl, he grabbed Karl and hit me five times on the cheek; and Henriette was also hit in the eye. This is not delicate behavior in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I shouted. And he started opening the window onto the canal and stood at the window like a little pig squealing; this is shameful.
