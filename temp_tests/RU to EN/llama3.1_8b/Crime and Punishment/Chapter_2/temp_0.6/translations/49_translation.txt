He became extremely absent-minded and forgetful and knew it. He had to think clearly! He went along the V -th prospect to the Neva; but on his way a sudden thought struck him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Wouldn't it be better to leave somewhere very far away, again maybe to the Islands, and there in some solitary place, in the woods, under a bush, bury all this and the tree, perhaps mark it?" And although he felt that he was not capable of discussing everything clearly and sensibly at this moment, but the idea seemed infallible to him. But it was not destined for him to reach the Islands, and something else happened: coming out onto the square from the V -th prospect, he suddenly saw a gate leading into a yard to the left, surrounded by completely blank walls. To the right, immediately after entering through the gates, far into the yard stretched a whitewashed wall of a neighboring four-story house. To the left, parallel to the blank wall and also now from the gates, ran a wooden fence, twenty steps deep into the courtyard, and then turned sharply to the left. This was a secluded area where some materials lay. Further in the recesses of the yard, peered out from behind the fence an angle of a low, smoke-blackened stone shed, obviously part of some workshop.
