The plump lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and with some special excitement began to squat; but the officer paid no attention to her whatsoever, and she was no longer able to sit down next to him. This was a lieutenant, assistant chief of police, with horizontally sprouting red sideburns and extremely small facial features that expressed nothing in particular, except for a certain boldness. He glanced at Raskolnikov sideways and with some indignation: his uniform was too shabby, and despite all the humiliation, he still didn't have the bearing of a man who has lost everything. Raskolnikov looked at him straight on and for too long, so that the lieutenant got offended. — What do you want? — he shouted, probably surprised that such a beggar did not even think to cower under his lightning-fast glance. — It's about... by summons... — Raskolnikov replied somehow awkwardly. — This is about collecting money from them, from the student, — the clerk hastened, tearing himself away from the papers. — Here! — and he handed Raskolnikov a notebook, pointing to a place in it: read!
