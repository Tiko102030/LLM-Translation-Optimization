The air was stiflingly thick with dust. Street cleaners, wearing notebooks under their arms, and passersby of both sexes - visitors, climbed up and down the stairs. The door to the inner office was also wide open. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Everyone there stood and waited for some men. Here too, the air was stiflingly thick and, besides that, it hit you with a wave of nausea from the fresh, still unhardened paint on the tarry oil used to repaint the rooms. After waiting a bit, he thought it was time to move further forward into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A terrible impatience pulled him ever deeper. No one noticed him.
