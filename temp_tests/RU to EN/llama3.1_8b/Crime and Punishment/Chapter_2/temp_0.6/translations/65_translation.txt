But at that very moment, as he stood by the railing and still looked back with a senseless and malicious gaze at the departing carriage, rubbing his spine, he suddenly felt someone slipping money into his hands. He glanced around: an elderly merchant's wife in a headscarf and goat-skin boots, and with her a girl wearing a hat and a green parasol, probably her daughter. "Take it, father, for Christ's sake." He took it, and they walked by. The coins were two groschen. By their dress and appearance they could have taken him for a beggar, for a genuine street alms-seeker, and he, on receiving the entire two groschen, probably owed them a blow with a whip that had softened them. He clutched the two groschen in his hand, walked ten paces, and turned his face towards the Neva, in the direction of the palace. The sky was cloudless, and the water almost blue, which rarely happens on the Neva. The dome of the cathedral, which is not better outlined from any point than when viewed from here, from the bridge, not even twenty steps short of the chapel, shone brightly, and through the clean air one could distinctly make out every ornament on it.
