Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but really, what's the point! It's well known that he couldn't bear insults. You've probably offended him with something and yourself couldn't help but get angry too, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with a courteous smile - but you're wrong to do so: he's a nephew of the most noble sort, I assure you, but powder, powder! He blew up, boiled over, burned out - and that's it! And everything has passed! And as a result, only gold in his heart! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

— What a company he was part of! — exclaimed Il'ya Petrovich, very pleased that they had tickled him so pleasantly, but still half-asleep. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to them all. — Forgive me, Captain, began he quite freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly - come on and get into my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've been at fault in some way.
