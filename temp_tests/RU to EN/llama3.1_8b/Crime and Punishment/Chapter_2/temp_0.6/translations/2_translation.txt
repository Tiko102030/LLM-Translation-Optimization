It was quite light out and he quickly glanced over himself from head to foot, checking his clothes for any signs. But it couldn't be done like that: shivering with cold, he began to remove everything from himself and examine the area around him again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there seemed to be nothing; only on the place where his pants had frayed and hung in tassels at the bottom, there were thick dried bloodstains left on the tassels. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the tassels. There was probably nothing else. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and things he had pulled out of the old woman's chest were still in his pockets! He hadn't thought to take them out yet, let alone hide them! He didn't even think about it now as he inspected his clothes! What's this?
