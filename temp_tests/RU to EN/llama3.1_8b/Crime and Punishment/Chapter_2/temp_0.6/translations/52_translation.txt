He's probably been lying here since the house was built and will lie here just as long. And even if they find him: who would think of me? It's all over, I have no alibi! — and he laughed. Yes, later on, he remembered that he had laughed nervously, weakly, inaudibly, and for a long time, and was laughing the whole time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Street Boulevard, where two days ago he ran into that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts rushed into his head. It suddenly seemed to him too disgusting to pass by that bench again, on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be just as difficult to run into that old man again, whom he had then given two rubles: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and maliciously. All his thoughts were now swirling around one main point — and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and existed, and that now, exactly now, he was left alone face to face with it, and that it was even for the first time after these two months.
